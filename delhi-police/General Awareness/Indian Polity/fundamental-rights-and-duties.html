Fundamental Rights & Duties

Indian Polity – Delhi Police/SSC Exams

1. Introduction – Why Are Fundamental Rights Important?

The Fundamental Rights (FRs) are the cornerstone of Indian democracy. They guarantee freedom, equality, and dignity to every citizen and act as a shield against arbitrary state action.

Memory Trick: Rights make citizens strong, Duties make the nation strong.

A. Fundamental Rights – Overview

Part III (Articles 12–35) of the Constitution guarantees Fundamental Rights. Inspired by the USA’s Bill of Rights and are justiciable — i.e., can be enforced in court.

Trick: 3-Part Freedom: Articles 12 to 35 → 6 Rights → 1 Constitution.

2. List of Fundamental Rights

Right Articles Description
1️⃣ Right to Equality 14–18 Equality before law, abolition of untouchability and titles
2️⃣ Right to Freedom 19–22 Freedom of speech, movement, assembly, etc.
3️⃣ Right against Exploitation 23–24 Prohibits forced labor & child labor
4️⃣ Right to Freedom of Religion 25–28 Freedom of belief and worship
5️⃣ Cultural & Educational Rights 29–30 Protects minorities’ culture and education
6️⃣ Right to Constitutional Remedies 32 Right to approach courts for protection of rights

Trick to Remember: E-F-E-R-C-R → Equality, Freedom, Exploitation, Religion, Culture, Remedies.

3. Right to Equality (Articles 14–18)

Core Idea: All are equal before law.

Article Provision
14 Equality before law
15 Prohibition of discrimination
16 Equality in public employment
17 Abolition of untouchability
18 Abolition of titles (e.g., Sir, Raja)

Trick: 14–15–16–17–18 = Equality Alive.

4. Right to Freedom (Articles 19–22)

Freedom makes life meaningful. It includes:

Article Freedom
19 Six freedoms – Speech, Assembly, Association, Movement, Residence, Profession
20 Protection in criminal cases
21 Right to life & personal liberty
22 Protection against arrest/detention

Trick: 19 gives life, 21 protects it.

Key Case: Maneka Gandhi vs Union of India (1978) — broadened the meaning of “life.”

5. Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)

Known as the “Heart and Soul of the Constitution” (Dr. Ambedkar). Citizens can approach the Supreme Court or High Courts to protect their rights.

Trick: If Rights are violated, Article 32 is activated!

B. Writs – Tools to Enforce Fundamental Rights

Issued by Supreme Court (Art. 32) and High Courts (Art. 226).

Writ Meaning Purpose
Habeas Corpus “Produce the body” Release from unlawful detention
Mandamus “We command” Order to perform a public duty
Prohibition Stop lower court from exceeding jurisdiction -
Certiorari Transfer a case to higher court -
Quo Warranto “By what authority” – check illegal occupation of public post -

Trick: Happy Men Play Cricket Quickly! → Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Prohibition, Certiorari, Quo Warranto

C. Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSPs)

Found in Part IV (Articles 36–51) — they guide the State to create social and economic justice.

Trick: Rights = Law; DPSPs = Goals.

Category Examples
Socialist Equal pay, right to work
Gandhian Cottage industries, panchayati raj
Liberal-Intellectual Uniform civil code, environment protection
Source: Irish Constitution Ireland
Trick: DPSPs are non-justiciable – cannot be enforced by courts.

Examples of DPSPs (Key Articles)

Article Objective
39Equal pay, right to livelihood
40Panchayati Raj
43Cottage industries
45Free and compulsory education
47Nutrition and public health
48AProtection of environment
Trick: 39–45–47–48A → Welfare through Work, Food, Education, Environment.

D. Fundamental Duties (Article 51A)

Added by 42nd Amendment, 1976 (based on USSR Constitution). They remind citizens of their moral obligations toward the nation.

List of Fundamental Duties (11 Total)

  1. Respect the Constitution, National Flag, and Anthem
  2. Cherish freedom struggle ideals
  3. Protect sovereignty, unity, and integrity
  4. Defend the nation
  5. Promote harmony and brotherhood
  6. Value women’s dignity
  7. Preserve heritage and culture
  8. Protect environment
  9. Develop scientific temper
  10. Safeguard public property
  11. Provide education to children (added by 86th Amendment, 2002)
Trick: 11 Duties – From Respect to Responsibility.

Difference Between Fundamental Rights, DPSPs, and Duties

Trick: Rights protect, DPSPs direct, Duties reflect.
