Local Government of India

Delhi Police / SSC – Polity Master Notes

Introduction

The Local Government system in India brings democracy to the grassroots level — enabling citizens to participate directly in governance and development. It works through two branches: Rural Local Bodies (Panchayati Raj) and Urban Local Bodies (Municipalities & Corporations).

Local = Last Level of Democracy.

A. 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act (1992) – Rural Local Governance

  • Came into effect on 24 April 1993.
  • Added Part IX (Articles 243–243O) to the Constitution.
  • Gave constitutional status to the Panchayati Raj System.
Trick: 73rd = Rural → Remember R for 73rd.

Key Features

Structure3-Tier System (Village, Block, District)

3F → Funds, Functions, Functionaries.

B. Panchayati Raj System (3-Tier Structure)

Gram Sabha

All adults above 18 yrs; Parliament of the Village.
Meets twice yearly, approves plans/budgets.

Gram Panchayat

Village Level; Sarpanch / Pradhan heads; 5-year tenure.

Panchayat Samiti

Block Level; Chairperson heads.
Coordinates Gram Panchayats.

Zila Parishad

District Level; Zila Pramukh heads.
Supervises and allocates funds for district.

  • Village works, Block coordinates, District supervises.
  • Zila Parishad = CEO of District Development.
Level Body Head Main Role
VillageGram PanchayatSarpanchExecution
BlockPanchayat SamitiChairpersonCoordination
DistrictZila ParishadZila PramukhSupervision

C. 74th Constitutional Amendment Act (1992) – Urban Local Governance

  • Came into force on 1 June 1993.
  • Added Part IXA (Articles 243P–243ZG) to the Constitution.
  • Gave constitutional status to Urban Local Bodies (ULBs).
Trick: 74th = Urban → 4 walls of city.

Types of Urban Local Bodies

Type Area Covered Example / Head
Municipal CorporationBig cities (Population >10 lakh)Mayor
MunicipalityMedium townsChairperson
Nagar PanchayatTransition areas (rural→urban)President
Town grows → Panchayat → Municipality → Corporation.

Composition & Tenure

  • Members elected directly by people for 5 years.
  • 1/3rd seats reserved for women.
  • States establish Finance Commission & District Planning Committees.

Functions of Urban Local Bodies

Type of Function Examples
CivicWater supply, sanitation, waste disposal
DevelopmentalRoads, parks, schools
Social WelfareHealth centers, housing for poor
RegulatoryBuilding rules, trade licenses
C–D–S–R → Civic, Development, Social, Regulatory.

D. Role of Local Self-Government in India

  • Promotes grassroot democracy.
  • Citizen participation in development.
  • Local solutions to local problems.
  • Reduces burden on Central and State governments.
  • Encourages leadership from rural & urban communities.
Local = Learn, Lead, Live democracy.
