Ancient Civilisations: Egypt, Mesopotamia, Greece & Rome

Delhi Police Exam History Section

1) Egypt Civilization

Location: Nile River Valley (Africa)

Time Period: ~3000 BCE onwards

Key Features:

Agriculture dependent on Nile Floods

Pharaoh = God-King

Writing system – Hieroglyphics

Famous monuments = Pyramids, Sphinx

Religion: Polytheistic, life after death concept (mummification)

Contribution:

Calendar (365 days)

Astronomy, Mathematics, Surgery

Papyrus sheets

2) Mesopotamia Civilization

Location: Tigris & Euphrates river region (Iraq area)

Time Period: ~3500 BCE onwards

Key Features:

Cities like Sumer, Babylon, Assyria

World's earliest known writing Cuneiform

Hammurabi's Code of Law → earliest legal code

Advanced agriculture irrigation canals

Contribution:

Wheel invention

First City-states

First legal code

3) Greek Civilization

Location: Southeastern Europe (Balkan Peninsula)

Time Period: ~800 BCE – 146 BCE

Key Features:

City states (Polis) – Athens, Sparta

Democracy developed in Athens

Olympics originated in Greece

Strong impact on philosophy, maths, architecture

Contribution:

Philosophers: Socrates, Plato, Aristotle

Early Science → Euclid, Pythagoras, Archimedes

Drama – Tragedy/Comedy

4) Roman Civilization

Location: Italian Peninsula → later huge empire

Time Period: ~500 BCE – 476 CE (Fall of Western Roman Empire)

Key Features:

Republic → later Empire

Spread Christianity later

Large scale road networks, aqueducts, architecture

Contribution:

Roman Law (basis of modern law)

Latin Language foundation

Engineering construction (Colosseum, roads)

Quick Recap

Civilization River / Region Writing Special Contribution
Egypt Nile Hieroglyphics Pyramids, Mummification
Mesopotamia Tigris-Euphrates Cuneiform First Law Code, Wheel
Greece Balkan Region — Democracy, Philosophy
Rome Italy & Europe Latin Roman Law, Engineering

Sample Question

Compare the contributions of Greek and Roman Civilizations to modern society.

Greek contributions: Democracy, philosophy (Socrates, Plato, Aristotle), scientific principles, Olympic Games. Roman contributions: Legal systems, engineering (roads, aqueducts), Latin language, architectural innovations.
