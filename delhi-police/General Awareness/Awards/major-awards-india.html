Delhi Police & SSC Exams – GK Section

Introduction to Awards & Honors

Awards in India honor excellence, service, and contribution in diverse fields. From civil and military bravery to outstanding achievement in art, literature, sports, and social work, they encourage recognition and inspiration for all.

Memory Trick: B-P-G-D-K-A-F-S-J-B → Bharat, Padma, Gallantry, Dadasaheb, Khel, Arjuna, Film, Sahitya, Jnanpith, Bravery.
Think: “Big People Give Dedicated Kudos And Fairly Shine Justly & Bravely.” (Mnemonic for all major awards)

Bharat Ratna (India’s Highest Civilian Award)

  • Established: 1954
  • Given For: Exceptional service in art, literature, science, or public service
  • Presented By: President of India
  • Shape: Peepal leaf medal with sun engraving
  • Notable Recipients:
  • Dr. S. Radhakrishnan, C.V. Raman, Sachin Tendulkar, Atal Bihari Vajpayee, Lata Mangeshkar
  • Number of Recipients (till 2025): 48+

Mnemonic: Bharat Ratna – Brightest Jewel of India.

Padma Awards (Civilian Honors)

  • Established: 1954
  • Categories:
    • Padma Vibhushan – Exceptional & distinguished service
    • Padma Bhushan – High order distinguished service
    • Padma Shri – Distinguished service in any field
  • Fields: Arts, Literature, Science, Sports, Public Affairs, etc.
  • Presented By: President of India (on Republic Day)
Recent Recipients (2024–25): Dr. M.S. Swaminathan (Padma Vibhushan), Rakesh Jhunjhunwala (Padma Bhushan), Rameshwar Nath Kao (Padma Shri)

Mnemonic: Padma – People's Appreciation for Distinguished Merit & Achievement.

Gallantry Awards (Bravery in Defence)

War-Time Gallantry Awards

Award Established Purpose
Param Vir Chakra 1950 Highest military decoration for bravery
Maha Vir Chakra 1950 Second-highest war-time gallantry
Vir Chakra 1950 Third-highest war-time gallantry

Mnemonic: Param > Maha > Vir — 1, 2, 3.

Peace-Time Gallantry Awards

Award Purpose
Ashok Chakra Highest peace-time gallantry award
Kirti Chakra Second-highest
Shaurya Chakra Third-highest

Mnemonic: Ashok – Always Safe Heroes On Kindness.

Dadasaheb Phalke Award (Cinema)

  • Established: 1969
  • Field: Outstanding contribution to Indian Cinema
  • Given By: Government of India
  • Named After: Dadasaheb Phalke – Father of Indian Cinema
  • Recent Winners:
  • 2021: Asha Parekh
  • 2022: Waheeda Rehman
  • 2023: Amitabh Bachchan (Lifetime Honor)

Mnemonic: Phalke Award – Film Pioneer's Pride.

Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award (Sports)

  • Established: 1991 (as Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, renamed 2021)
  • Given For: Highest sporting honor in India
  • Presented By: President of India
  • Criteria: Spectacular performance over 4 years
Notable Winners: Neeraj Chopra, M.S. Dhoni, Virat Kohli, P.V. Sindhu, Mary Kom

Mnemonic: Khel Ratna – India's Sporting Crown.

Arjuna Award

  • Established: 1961
  • Given For: Consistent excellence in sports
  • Award Includes: Bronze statuette of Arjuna, certificate, and cash prize
Notable Winners: R. Ashwin, Bajrang Punia, Lovlina Borgohain

Mnemonic: Arjuna – Achiever on the Sports Battlefield.

National Film Awards

  • Established: 1954
  • Presented By: Directorate of Film Festivals, Ministry of I&B
  • Categories: Best Feature Film, Best Actor, Best Actress, Best Director, etc.
Recent Highlights (2023): Best Film – Rocketry; Best Actor – Allu Arjun; Best Actress – Alia Bhatt, Kriti Sanon

Mnemonic: National Film Awards = Nation's Cinema Celebration.

Sahitya Akademi Award

  • Established: 1954
  • Given By: Sahitya Akademi (National Academy of Letters)
  • Purpose: Literary excellence in 24 Indian languages
Recent Winners: English – Anuradha Roy; Hindi – Gyan Chaturvedi

Mnemonic: Sahitya = Storytelling with Excellence.

Jnanpith Award

  • Established: 1961
  • Given By: Bharatiya Jnanpith Organization
  • Purpose: India's highest literary award
Recent Winners: Amitav Ghosh (2018, English), Damodar Mauzo (2021, Konkani), Prabha Varma (2022, Malayalam)

Mnemonic: Jnanpith – Journey of Indian Literary Genius.

National Bravery Awards (Children)

  • Established: 1957 (by ICCW)
  • Purpose: Honors children below 18 who display outstanding bravery
  • Awards Include: Bharat Award, Geeta Chopra Award, Sanjay Chopra Award
  • Presented On: Republic Day

Mnemonic: Bravery Awards = Boldness Beyond Age.

PYQs – Awards in Competitive Exams

Q1. Which is the highest civilian award in India?

A) Padma Vibhushan   B) Bharat Ratna   C) Jnanpith Award   D) Param Vir Chakra

Show Answer

B) Bharat Ratna

Q2. How many categories are there in Padma Awards?

A) 2   B) 3   C) 4   D) 5

Show Answer

B) 3 (Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan, Padma Shri)

Q3. The highest wartime gallantry award is —

A) Vir Chakra   B) Param Vir Chakra   C) Ashok Chakra   D) Maha Vir Chakra

Show Answer

B) Param Vir Chakra

Q4. Who gives the Dadasaheb Phalke Award?

A) Ministry of Defence   B) Ministry of Culture   C) Ministry of Information & Broadcasting   D) Filmfare Organization

Show Answer

C) Ministry of Information & Broadcasting

Q5. The Khel Ratna Award is now named after —

A) Major Dhyan Chand   B) Rajiv Gandhi   C) Kapil Dev   D) Milkha Singh

Show Answer

A) Major Dhyan Chand

Q6. The Sahitya Akademi Award is given for —

A) Music   B) Sports   C) Literature   D) Films

Show Answer

C) Literature

Q7. The Jnanpith Award is the highest in —

A) Sports   B) Literature   C) Science   D) Politics

Show Answer

B) Literature

Q8. Param Vir Chakra is equivalent to —

A) Bharat Ratna   B) Gallantry Award   C) Sahitya Award   D) Padma Shri

Show Answer

B) Gallantry Award

Q9. National Bravery Awards are given to —

A) Soldiers   B) Police officers   C) Children   D) Teachers

Show Answer

C) Children

Q10. Dadasaheb Phalke is known as —

A) Father of Indian Cinema   B) Father of Modern India   C) Father of Indian Sports   D) Father of Indian Literature

Show Answer

A) Father of Indian Cinema
