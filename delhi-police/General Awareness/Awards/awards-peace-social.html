Peace & Social Work Awards

Delhi Police & SSC Exams – GK Section

Introduction

Peace and social work awards honor selfless service, non-violence, and humanitarian leadership. These awards celebrate individuals and organizations who promote social justice, human rights, and global harmony.

Memory Trick: M–G–I → Magsaysay, Gandhi, Indira.
Mankind Gains Inspiration.

Ramon Magsaysay Award (International – ‘Asian Nobel Prize’)

  • Established: 1957
  • Named After: Ramon Magsaysay (President of Philippines)
  • Given By: Ramon Magsaysay Award Foundation, Manila
  • Field: Public service, leadership, peace, journalism, community
  • Known As: "Asian Nobel Prize"
  • Frequency: Annual
Notable Indians: Vinoba Bhave (1958), Mother Teresa (1962), Kiran Bedi (1994), Aruna Roy (2000), Ravish Kumar (2019)

Mnemonic: Magsaysay = Model Asians for Service & Society.

Gandhi Peace Prize (India)

  • Established: 1995 (Gandhi’s 125th anniversary)
  • Given By: Govt. of India
  • Administered By: Ministry of Culture
  • Purpose: Peace, non-violence, humanitarian development
  • Award: ₹1 crore, citation, plaque
  • Based On: Gandhian principles
Notable: Julius Nyerere (Tanzania), Baba Amte, ISRO, Yohei Sasakawa (Japan), Gita Press (2021)

Mnemonic: Gandhi Peace = Global Ahimsa & Nonviolence Drive.

Indira Gandhi Prize for Peace, Disarmament & Development

  • Established: 1986
  • Given By: Indira Gandhi Memorial Trust, New Delhi
  • Purpose: International peace, development, disarmament
  • Award: Citation, ₹25 lakh
  • Recognizes: Global cooperation, equality, sustainability
Recipients: UNICEF (1989), Gorbachev, Mandela, Manmohan Singh, UNHCR (2019), CSE (2022)

Mnemonic: Indira Gandhi Prize = Inspiring Global Development.
