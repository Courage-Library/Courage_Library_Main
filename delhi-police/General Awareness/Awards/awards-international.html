International Awards

Delhi Police & SSC Exams – GK Section

Introduction

International awards recognize outstanding contributions worldwide in peace, literature, science, cinema, journalism, and human welfare.
These awards are common topics in competitive exams.

Mnemonic: N–O–P–B–M–T–R–F–A → Nobel, Oscar, Pulitzer, Booker, Magsaysay, Templeton, Right Livelihood, Fields, Abel.
Think: “Nine Outstanding People Bring Meaningful Talent, Recognition, Fame & Achievement!”

Nobel Prize

  • Founded By: Alfred Bernhard Nobel (Sweden, 1895)
  • First Awarded: 1901
  • Categories: Peace, Literature, Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Economics
  • Prize Given In: Stockholm (Sweden), except Peace Prize (Oslo, Norway)

Oscar Awards (Academy Awards)

  • Established: 1929
  • Given By: Academy of Motion Picture Arts & Sciences (USA)
  • Field: Cinema (Best Film, Actor/Actress, Director, Music)
  • Award Trophy: Golden statuette (“Oscar”)
Indian Oscar Highlights: Bhanu Athaiya (Costume, Gandhi, 1982); A.R. Rahman & Resul Pookutty (*Slumdog Millionaire*, 2009); The Elephant Whisperers (Best Documentary Short, 2023)

Mnemonic: Oscar – On Screen Cinema Achievement Recognition.

Pulitzer Prize

  • Established: 1917
  • Founded By: Joseph Pulitzer
  • Given By: Columbia University, USA
  • Field: Journalism, Literature, Music
  • Frequency: Annual
Indians: Gobind Behari Lal (1937, Journalism); Reuters Team with Indian photographers (2022)

Mnemonic: Pulitzer – Press, Pen & Pictures Prize.

Booker Prize

  • Established: 1969
  • Given For: Best original English-language novel published in UK/Ireland
  • Administered By: Booker Prize Foundation, UK
Winner Book Year
Arundhati RoyThe God of Small Things1997
Kiran DesaiThe Inheritance of Loss2006

Salman RushdieMidnight’s Children1981 / 1993

Mnemonic: Roy, Desai & Adiga Booked the Booker!

Ramon Magsaysay Award

  • Named After: Ramon Magsaysay (Philippines President)
  • Established: 1957
  • Given For: Public service, peace, community work
  • Known As: “Asian Nobel Prize”
Name Year Field
Vinoba Bhave1958Community Leadership
Mother Teresa1962Peace
Kiran Bedi1994Government Service
Aruna Roy2000Social Activism
Ravish Kumar2019Journalism

Mnemonic: Magsaysay – Model Asians for Service & Society.

Templeton Prize

  • Established: 1972
  • Given For: Progress in religion, spirituality, or purpose
  • Prize Amount: Usually larger than Nobel Prize
Indian Recipients: Mother Teresa (1973), Dalai Lama (2012)

Mnemonic: Templeton – Thinkers of Theology & Truth.

Right Livelihood Award

  • Established: 1980
  • Given For: Environment, human rights, sustainable development
  • Also Called: “Alternative Nobel Prize”
Indians: Medha Patkar (1991), Vandana Shiva (1993), Asha Hagi (2005)

Mnemonic: Right Livelihood – Real Life for Right Cause.

Fields Medal

  • Established: 1936
  • Given By: International Mathematical Union (IMU)
  • Field: Mathematics
  • Frequency: Every 4 years
  • Known As: “Nobel Prize of Mathematics”
Indo-Canadian Winner: Manjul Bhargava (2014)

Mnemonic: Fields Medal – Figures & Formulas Fame.

Abel Prize

  • Established: 2001
  • Given By: Government of Norway
  • Field: Mathematics (Nobel doesn't include Math)
  • Frequency: Annual, ≈ 1 million USD

Mnemonic: Abel Award – Advanced Brain Excellence in Logic.

Final Concept Recap

  • Nobel Prize – Most prestigious award worldwide (6 disciplines)
  • Oscar – Excellence in cinema
  • Pulitzer – Journalism & Literature
  • Booker – English-language fiction
  • Magsaysay – Asian Nobel for public service
  • Templeton – Religious and spiritual progress
  • Right Livelihood – Human rights and environment
  • Fields Medal / Abel Prize – Mathematics

From Nobel Peace to Fields Math — global awards honor knowledge, service, and humanity.
