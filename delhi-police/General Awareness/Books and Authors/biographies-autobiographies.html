Introduction to Biographies & Autobiographies

Biographies and autobiographies present real-life achievements, struggles and worldview of great personalities. They help you answer exam questions like “Who wrote Wings of Fire?” or “Whose autobiography is Playing It My Way?”

Memory Trick: K–G–R–T–B–M–K = Kalam, Gandhi, Ramdev, Tendulkar, Bhagat, Milkha, Kalam (thrice!)

Wings of Fire – A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

  • Type: Autobiography
  • Author: Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (with Arun Tiwari)
  • Published: 1999
  • Theme: Rameswaram to “Missile Man” and President
  • Key Points: Childhood, ISRO, DRDO, vision for youth
Kalam’s Wings ignited the Nation’s Fire.

The Story of My Experiments with Truth – Mahatma Gandhi

  • Type: Autobiography
  • Author: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi
  • Written In: Gujarati (Translated by Mahadev Desai)
  • Published: 1927
  • Theme: Satya (Truth) & Ahimsa (Non-Violence)
  • Focus: India, South Africa, philosophy of Satyagraha
Gandhi’s Truth = Nation’s Conscience.

My Life, My Mission – Baba Ramdev

  • Type: Autobiography
  • Author: Baba Ramdev (Uday Mahurkar)
  • Published: 2019
  • Theme: Journey of Yoga, Ayurveda, Swadeshi
  • Focus: Patanjali foundation, Indian wellness
Ramdev’s Mission = Yoga + Swadeshi.

Playing It My Way – Sachin Tendulkar

  • Type: Autobiography
  • Author: Sachin Tendulkar (with Boria Majumdar)
  • Published: 2014
  • Theme: Cricket journey, 100 centuries
  • Special: India’s best-selling autobiography
  • Quote: Cricket was never just a game, it was my life.
Tendulkar Played It His Way – Straight and True.

Why I Am an Atheist – Bhagat Singh

  • Type: Autobiographical Essay
  • Author: Shaheed Bhagat Singh
  • Written In: Jail (1930)
  • Theme: Rationalism, rejection of blind faith
  • Purpose: Defending beliefs before execution
Bhagat’s Belief – Reason, not Religion.

My Journey – A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

  • Type: Semi-Autobiographical
  • Author: A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
  • Published: 2013
  • Theme: Life lessons, inspiration for youth
  • Focus: Simplicity, patriotism, innovation
Kalam’s Journey = Lessons for Life.

The Race of My Life – Milkha Singh

  • Type: Autobiography
  • Author: Milkha Singh (with Sonia Sanwalka)
  • Published: 2013
  • Theme: Partition struggle, Flying Sikh
  • Highlight: 1960 Rome Olympics, symbol of determination
Milkha’s Race = From Refugee to Record.

Indomitable Spirit – A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

  • Type: Motivational Book
  • Author: A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
  • Published: 2006
  • Theme: Youth, education, nation-building
  • Focus: Leadership, values, courage
Kalam’s Spirit = India’s Strength.

PYQs (Delhi Police, SSC & State Exams)

Who wrote Wings of Fire?

A) Mahatma Gandhi   B) A.P.J. Abdul Kalam   C) Jawaharlal Nehru   D) C.V. Raman

Show Answer

B) A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Whose autobiography is The Story of My Experiments with Truth?

A) Subhas Chandra Bose   B) Sardar Patel   C) Mahatma Gandhi   D) Jawaharlal Nehru

Show Answer

C) Mahatma Gandhi

Playing It My Way is the autobiography of —

A) Virat Kohli   B) Kapil Dev   C) Sachin Tendulkar   D) Sunil Gavaskar

Show Answer

C) Sachin Tendulkar

Why I Am an Atheist was written by —

A) Chandrashekhar Azad   B) Bhagat Singh   C) Subhas Chandra Bose   D) M.N. Roy

Show Answer

B) Bhagat Singh

The book The Race of My Life is the autobiography of —

A) P.T. Usha   B) Mary Kom   C) Milkha Singh   D) Neeraj Chopra

Show Answer

C) Milkha Singh

Indomitable Spirit was written by —

A) Ratan Tata   B) A.P.J. Abdul Kalam   C) Dr. B.R. Ambedkar   D) Swami Vivekananda

Show Answer

B) A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

My Life, My Mission is authored by —

A) Baba Ramdev   B) Swami Dayananda   C) Sri Sri Ravi Shankar   D) Mahesh Yogi

Show Answer

A) Baba Ramdev

Which of the following books is not written by A.P.J. Abdul Kalam?

A) Indomitable Spirit   B) My Journey   C) Wings of Fire   D) The Discovery of India

Show Answer

D) The Discovery of India (Jawaharlal Nehru)

Who co-authored Wings of Fire with A.P.J. Abdul Kalam?

A) Boria Majumdar   B) Arun Tiwari   C) Sonia Sanwalka   D) Mahadev Desai

Show Answer

B) Arun Tiwari

“Flying Sikh” wrote which autobiography?

A) The Race of My Life   B) My Country, My Life   C) My Experiments with Truth   D) Playing It My Way

Show Answer

A) The Race of My Life

Final Concept Recap

  • Kalam’s Trilogy – Wings of Fire, My Journey, Indomitable Spirit
  • Gandhi’s Truth – Moral and spiritual awakening
  • Sachin’s Story – Cricket passion and perseverance
  • Bhagat Singh’s Essay – Revolutionary rationalism
  • Milkha Singh’s Race – From struggle to glory
  • Ramdev’s Mission – Yoga & Swadeshi movement

From Gandhi’s Truth to Kalam’s Fire — every story lights India’s spirit higher!
