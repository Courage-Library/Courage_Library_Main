Delhi Police Exams - GK Section

Introduction to Religious & Philosophical Texts

Religious and philosophical books guide ethics, values and humanity. Competitive exams regularly ask you to match scriptures, religions and authors — memorize the essentials below.

Memory Trick: G–B–Q–G–D → Gita, Bible, Quran, Granth, Dhammapada (“God’s Books Guide Devotion!”)

Foundational Religious Books & Authors

Bhagavad Gita – Vyasa

  • Author/Compiler: Ved Vyasa
  • Scripture: Bhagavad Gita (Mahabharata – Bhishma Parva)
  • Religion: Hinduism
  • Language: Sanskrit
  • Main Theme: Duty, Dharma, Karma Yoga
Vyasa wrote Gita – the Song of Divine Duty.

Bible – Christianity

  • Scripture: Bible
  • Religion: Christianity
  • Language: Hebrew, Greek, Aramaic
  • Main Figures: Jesus Christ, Apostles
  • Main Message: Faith, love, forgiveness
Bible = Book of Belief & Blessings.

Quran – Islam

  • Scripture: Quran
  • Religion: Islam
  • Language: Arabic
  • Revealed To: Prophet Muhammad
  • Main Teachings: One God, morality, justice
Quran = Questions of Life, Answers by Allah.

Guru Granth Sahib – Sikhism

  • Scripture: Guru Granth Sahib
  • Religion: Sikhism
  • Language: Gurmukhi
  • Compiled By: Guru Arjan Dev Ji
  • Main Message: Equality, devotion, humanity
Granth = Guru’s Living Word.

Dhammapada – Buddhism

  • Scripture: Dhammapada
  • Religion: Buddhism
  • Language: Pali
  • Teachings: Buddha
  • Main Theme: Mindfulness, moral conduct
Dhammapada → Dharma Path of the Buddha.

Final Concept Recap

  • Bhagavad Gita (Vyasa): Duty, Karma, Dharma (Hinduism)
  • Bible: Love, Forgiveness (Christianity)
  • Quran: Peace, Justice, Unity (Islam)
  • Guru Granth Sahib: Equality, Devotion (Sikhism)
  • Dhammapada: Mindfulness & Morality (Buddhism)

All faiths lead to one truth — peace, duty, and compassion for humanity.
