Sports Awards & Honours (India)

Delhi Police Exams - GK Section

Introduction

Sports awards in India recognize athletes, coaches, and institutions for their contributions to Indian sports. The awards are presented annually by the President of India on National Sports Day (29th August) — the birthday of Major Dhyan Chand.

Memory Trick: K–A–D–D–M → Khel Ratna, Arjuna, Dronacharya, Dhyan Chand, MAKA Trophy.

Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award (Highest Sports Honour)

Introduced1991–92
Renamed2021 (Rajiv Gandhi to Dhyan Chand)
Given ForHighest sporting honour, exceptional international performance
Award IncludesMedallion, Certificate, ₹25 lakh
Presented ByPresident of India
FrequencyAnnual (1 or more recipients)
Recent / Famous Winners: Neeraj Chopra (Athletics), Mirabai Chanu (Weightlifting), Ravindra Jadeja (Cricket), Manika Batra (Table Tennis)

Khel Ratna = King of Awards.

Arjuna Award (Consistent Sports Performance)

Introduced1961
Given ForConsistent outstanding international performance (min 4 yrs)
Award IncludesBronze statuette, Certificate, ₹15 lakh
Presented ToMen and women across recognized sports
Recent / Famous Winners: Harmanpreet Kaur (Cricket), Lakshya Sen (Badminton), Rani Rampal (Hockey), Manpreet Singh (Hockey)

Arjuna Aims True – Reward for Consistency.

Dronacharya Award (Coaching Excellence)

Introduced1985
Given ToCoaches for producing international medal winners
Award IncludesBronze statuette, Certificate, ₹15 lakh
CategoriesRegular & Lifetime achievement
Famous Winners: Pullela Gopichand (Badminton), Satpal Singh (Wrestling), R.K. Sharma (Boxing), Radhakrishnan Nair (Athletics)

Dronacharya Trains, Arjuna Gains.

Dhyan Chand Award (Lifetime Achievement in Sports)

Introduced2002
Given ForLifetime contribution and continued promotion post-retirement
Award IncludesPlaque, Certificate, ₹10 lakh
Named AfterMajor Dhyan Chand (“Wizard of Hockey”)
Famous Winners: Trupti Murgunde (Badminton), Harbinder Singh (Hockey), Manpreet Kaur (Athletics)

Dhyan Chand = Dedication Beyond Career.

Maulana Abul Kalam Azad (MAKA) Trophy

Introduced1956–57
Given ToBest performing University in sports
Presented ByPresident of India
Award IncludesTrophy & Certificate
PurposeFoster sports culture in higher education
Recent Winners: Guru Nanak Dev University (Amritsar), Punjabi University (Patiala), Lovely Professional University (Phagwara)

MAKA = Master of Athletic Knowledge in Academia.

PYQs (Delhi Police, SSC & State Exams)

Which is India’s highest sports honour?

A) Arjuna Award   B) Dhyan Chand Award   C) Khel Ratna Award   D) Dronacharya Award

Show Answer

C) Khel Ratna Award

The Arjuna Award is given for —

A) Coaching Excellence   B) Consistent Performance   C) Lifetime Contribution   D) University Sports Performance

Show Answer

B) Consistent Performance

The Dronacharya Award is presented to —

A) Sports Administrators   B) Players   C) Coaches   D) Referees

Show Answer

C) Coaches

The Dhyan Chand Award is given for —

A) Best Coach   B) Lifetime Achievement in Sports   C) Consistent Performance   D) University Sports

Show Answer

B) Lifetime Achievement in Sports

The MAKA Trophy is awarded to —

A) Best University in Sports   B) Best School   C) Best Athlete   D) Best Coach

Show Answer

A) Best University in Sports

The Khel Ratna Award was renamed in 2021 after —

A) P.T. Usha   B) Dhyan Chand   C) Milkha Singh   D) Rajyavardhan Rathore

Show Answer

B) Dhyan Chand

Which of the following awards was instituted first?

A) MAKA Trophy   B) Arjuna Award   C) Dronacharya Award   D) Khel Ratna Award

Show Answer

A) MAKA Trophy (1956–57)

Who presents India’s National Sports Awards?

A) Prime Minister   B) Sports Minister   C) President of India   D) Vice President

Show Answer

C) President of India

Which award is given posthumously or after retirement for continued contribution?

A) Arjuna Award   B) Dronacharya Award   C) Dhyan Chand Award   D) Khel Ratna Award

Show Answer

C) Dhyan Chand Award

Pullela Gopichand has received which award?

A) Arjuna & Dronacharya   B) Only Arjuna   C) Only Khel Ratna   D) MAKA Trophy

A) Arjuna & Dronacharya Awards
