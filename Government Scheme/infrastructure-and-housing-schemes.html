Infrastructure and Housing Schemes

Government initiatives for urban development and connectivity

1. Smart Cities Mission

Feature Details
Objective Improve urban infrastructure and services through smart technology
Key Components Smart governance, energy-efficient buildings, intelligent transport, waste and water management
No. of Cities 100 selected cities
Selection Criteria Based on city-level competition (City Challenge)
Funding Pattern 50:50 (Centre : State/ULB)
Ministry Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs
Launch Year 2015

SSC Tip: Smart Cities = 100 cities = 50:50 funding = City Challenge competition = Housing Ministry

2. AMRUT (Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation)

Feature Details
Objective Improve basic urban services for quality of life
Focus Areas Water supply, sewerage, septage management, urban transport, green spaces
Coverage 500+ cities with population over 1 lakh
AMRUT 2.0 Launched in 2021 with focus on water security and digital governance
Ministry Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs
Launch Year 2015

SSC Tip: AMRUT = Urban services = 500+ cities = AMRUT 2.0 (2021) = Same ministry as Smart Cities

3. Bharatmala Pariyojana

Feature Details
Objective Improve road connectivity, especially in border, tribal, and economically lagging regions
Focus Areas Economic corridors, expressways, inter-corridor roads, feeder routes, border roads
Total Road Length Target Over 83,000 km (in phases)
Importance Boosts trade, logistics, and accessibility
Implementing Agency National Highways Authority of India (NHAI)
Ministry Ministry of Road Transport and Highways
Launch Year 2017

SSC Tip: Bharatmala = Road connectivity = 83,000 km = NHAI = Road Transport Ministry

4. UDAN Scheme (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik)

Feature Details
Objective Make air travel affordable and increase regional connectivity
Focus Connect under-served and unserved airports
Fare Cap ₹2,500 for 1-hour flights on regional routes
Type Viability Gap Funding (VGF) to airlines
Benefits Boosts tourism, regional development, Tier-2/Tier-3 cities
Implemented By Airports Authority of India (AAI)
Ministry Ministry of Civil Aviation
Launch Year 2016

SSC Tip: UDAN = Affordable flights = ₹2,500 fare cap = AAI = Civil Aviation Ministry
