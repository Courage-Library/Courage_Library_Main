Cybercrime

Delhi Police Exam

Cybercrime awareness is essential for Delhi Police Computer Awareness exams. This topic covers common threats like phishing and malware along with prevention measures that frequently appear in competitive exams.

1Ô∏è. What is Cybercrime?

Cybercrime refers to criminal activities that involve computers, networks, or the internet. It includes unauthorized access, data theft, online fraud, hacking, and spreading malware.

Exam-Focused Definition:

"Cybercrime = illegal activities in cyberspace targeting individuals, organizations, or governments."

2Ô∏è. Common Cyber Threats

1

1. Phishing

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information like passwords, bank details, or credit card numbers by pretending to be a trustworthy entity.

Example: Fake emails from "banks" asking for login details.

üí° Exam Trick: If it asks for personal info in email or link ‚Üí Phishing.

2

2. Malware

Malicious software designed to harm, exploit, or steal data from computers.

Virus

Infects files and programs

Worm

Spreads automatically across networks

Trojan

Appears legitimate but has hidden malicious functions

Ransomware

Encrypts data and demands ransom

üí° Exam Trick: Malware = All harmful software.

3

3. Other Threats

Hacking

Unauthorized access to systems

Identity Theft

Stealing personal information

Denial of Service (DoS)

Overloads systems to make them unavailable

3Ô∏è. Prevention Measures

Do not click suspicious links or attachments

Use updated antivirus software

Strong passwords & 2FA

Verify sender's email address before sharing info

Backup important files

Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions

Short Answer Task

Question:

Answer Guidelines:

Use keywords like phishing, malware, ransomware, strong passwords, antivirus

Keep your answer concise ‚Äî 50 words or less as specified

Sample Answer Structure:

Preventive measures include using strong passwords with 2FA and installing updated antivirus software to detect and block threats.
