Famous International Authors & Books

Delhi Police Exams - GK Section

Introduction to International Literature

Global literature opens doors to universal themes like love, courage, freedom and human struggle. Exams frequently quiz on world classics and their authors — knowing the keyword memory is vital for quick revision.

Memory Trick: P–L–A–J–W–D–G–E → Paulo, Leo, Arthur, Jane, William, Dan, George, Ernest. (Please Learn All Just With Dedication, Genius Effort!)

Major International Authors & Their Books

The Alchemist – Paulo Coelho

  • Author: Paulo Coelho (Brazilian)
  • Published: 1988
  • Genre: Philosophical fiction
  • Main Character: Santiago (shepherd boy)
  • Theme: Dreams and destiny
  • Quote: When you want something, the whole universe conspires to help you achieve it.
Coelho’s Alchemist = Dreams + Destiny.

War and Peace – Leo Tolstoy

  • Author: Leo Tolstoy (Russian)
  • Published: 1869
  • Genre: Historical novel
  • Main Characters: Pierre, Natasha, Andrei
  • Theme: Napoleonic wars, Russian nature
  • Significance: Among the longest novels ever written
Tolstoy’s War and Peace = Russia’s Rise and Pain.

The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes – Arthur Conan Doyle

  • Author: Arthur Conan Doyle (British)
  • Published: 1892
  • Genre: Detective fiction
  • Main Characters: Sherlock Holmes & Dr. Watson
Doyle’s Detectives Deduce Danger.

Pride and Prejudice – Jane Austen

  • Author: Jane Austen (British)
  • Published: 1813
  • Genre: Romantic novel
  • Main Characters: Elizabeth Bennet & Mr. Darcy
  • Theme: Love, marriage, social class & independence
Austen’s Pride = Prejudice Against Class.

Hamlet / Macbeth – William Shakespeare

  • Author: William Shakespeare
  • Genre: Tragedy
  • Hamlet Theme: Revenge, indecision, fate
  • Macbeth Theme: Ambition, guilt, downfall
  • Quotes: To be or not to be (Hamlet), Fair is foul, and foul is fair (Macbeth)
Shakespeare Shows Human Shadows.

The Da Vinci Code – Dan Brown

  • Author: Dan Brown (American)
  • Published: 2003
  • Genre: Mystery thriller
  • Main Character: Robert Langdon
  • Theme: Secret societies, religious mysteries
Dan’s Code = Decode the Divine Mystery.

Animal Farm / 1984 – George Orwell

  • Author: George Orwell (British)
  • Published: Animal Farm (1945), 1984 (1949)
  • Genre: Political satire, dystopian fiction
  • Theme: Freedom vs control, dictatorship
Orwell Warns Against Oppression.

The Old Man and the Sea – Ernest Hemingway

  • Author: Ernest Hemingway (American)
  • Published: 1952
  • Genre: Adventure, philosophical fiction
  • Theme: Courage, endurance, struggle against nature
  • Characters: Santiago (old fisherman)
  • Awards: Pulitzer Prize 1953, Nobel Prize 1954
Hemingway’s Old Man = Symbol of Strength.
