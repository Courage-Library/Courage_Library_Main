Constitutional Bodies of India

Polity Master Notes – Delhi Police / SSC Ready

Introduction

To ensure democratic governance, transparency, and accountability, the Indian Constitution has created several Constitutional Bodies that function independently of the executive. These institutions safeguard the Constitution and strengthen democracy.

Every Constitution Needs Fair Watchdogs → E, C, N, F, W = Election Commission, CAG, UPSC, Finance Commission, and more.

A. Election Commission of India (ECI)

Articles 324–329, Part XV

Composition

Established25 January 1950
Constitutional ArticleArticle 324
MembersCEC + 2 Election Commissioners
Appointed ByPresident of India
Tenure6 years or till 65 years of age
HQNew Delhi
Election starts on 25 January – National Voters Day!

Functions

TypeDescription
Conduct of ElectionsParliament, State Legislatures, President, Vice-President
DelimitationFixes boundaries of constituencies
RecognitionAllots symbols to political parties
Model Code of ConductEnsures free & fair elections
E = Election, Equality, Ethics.

Independence

  • Cannot be removed except like Supreme Court judge.
  • Expenses charged on Consolidated Fund of India.

B. Union Public Service Commission (UPSC)

Articles 315–323, Part XIV

Composition

Established1926 (Constitutional in 1950)
Article315
MembersChairman + other members (by President)
Appointed ByPresident
Tenure6 years or till 65 years
HQNew Delhi
UPSC → Union’s Personnel Selection Commission.

Functions

TypeDescription
RecruitmentConducts exams for All India & Central Services
Advisory RoleAdvises on promotions, transfers, disciplinary actions
ReportingSubmits annual report to the President
UPSC selects, advises, and reports.

Independence

  • Removal process same as Supreme Court judges (Chairman/members).
  • Salary & allowances protected by Constitution.

C. State Public Service Commission (SPSC)

Article 315

Composition

Established ByEach State
Appointed ByGovernor
Tenure6 years or till 62 years
Reports ToGovernor of State
UPSC for Centre, SPSC for State.

Functions

  • Conducts exams for State services
  • Advises on State recruitment, promotions, transfers
  • Submits report to Governor annually

D. Finance Commission of India (FC)

Article 280, Part XII

Composition

Appointed ByPresident every 5 years
Members1 Chairman + 4 Members
TenureDecided by President
Current (2025)16th Finance Commission
Finance Commission = 5-year fund adjuster.

Functions

TypeDescription
Distribution of TaxesBetween Centre and States
Grants-in-aidRecommends assistance to States
EvaluationStrengthen financial position of States
Other FunctionsMatters referred by President
Finance = Funds Flow & Fiscal Balance.

E. Comptroller and Auditor General of India (CAG)

Articles 148–151

Composition & Appointment

Article148
Appointed ByPresident of India
Tenure6 years or till 65 years
RemovalSame as Supreme Court judge
HeadquartersNew Delhi
CAG = Watchdog of Public Money.

Functions

TypeDescription
AuditAll receipts and expenditure of Central & State Governments
ReportsTo President and Governors
AccountsEnsures financial accountability
AutonomyIndependent of Government control
CAG – Counts, Audits, Guards.

Reports of CAG

  • Submitted to President (Union) & Governor (State).
  • Examined by Public Accounts Committee (PAC) of Parliament.
CAG reports, PAC inspects.
