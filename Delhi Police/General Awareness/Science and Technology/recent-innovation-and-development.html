Modern Innovations & Technology

Science & Technology – Exam Preparation

Introduction: The Era of Rapid Innovation

The 21st century is an era of rapid innovation and technological transformation — from mRNA vaccines in healthcare to electric vehicles, AI-driven robots, and smart materials shaping a sustainable future.

In the Delhi Police exam, questions from this topic focus on latest scientific breakthroughs, global initiatives, and applications of modern tech in real life.

Memory Trick: "Health Heals, Energy Sustains, AI Thinks, Nano Builds."

A. Healthcare & Medicine Innovations

1. mRNA Vaccines

Aspect Details
Full FormMessenger RNA Vaccine
WorkingTeaches cells to produce a protein that triggers an immune response
ExamplesPfizer-BioNTech (BNT162b2), Moderna (mRNA-1273)
Used ForCOVID-19 prevention
FeatureFast to develop, adaptable to variants
"mRNA – Message for Immunity."

2. CRISPR Therapy

Aspect Details
Full FormClustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats
FunctionGene-editing tool that cuts & replaces faulty DNA segments
UsesTreating genetic diseases like sickle cell anemia and cancer
InventorsJennifer Doudna & Emmanuelle Charpentier (Nobel Prize 2020)
"CRISPR – Scissors of Life."

3. AI in Diagnostics

Aspect Details
UseAI algorithms analyze medical scans, predict diseases, and assist doctors
ApplicationsX-ray analysis, early cancer detection, COVID prediction, telemedicine
ExamplesIBM Watson Health, Google DeepMind
"AI Diagnoses Before Doctor Knows."

B. Energy & Environment Innovations

1. Solar Energy

Aspect Details
DefinitionConversion of sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic cells
Example ProjectsInternational Solar Alliance (ISA – led by India & France)
GoalAchieve clean, renewable power for all
"Solar = Sunlight to Current."

2. Hydrogen Energy (Green Hydrogen)

Aspect Details
DefinitionHydrogen produced using renewable electricity (solar/wind) instead of fossil fuels
UseFuel cells for vehicles, power generation
India's InitiativeNational Green Hydrogen Mission (2023)
GoalIndia to become a global hub for green hydrogen
"Green Hydrogen – Cleanest Fuel for the Future."

3. Electric Vehicles (EVs)

Aspect Details
DefinitionVehicles powered by electric motors and rechargeable batteries
ExamplesTata Nexon EV, Tesla, Ola Electric Scooters
Government SchemeFAME – Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric Vehicles
BenefitReduces air pollution and fuel cost
"EV → Energy Vehicle, Not Exhaust Vehicle."

C. Artificial Intelligence & Robotics

1. Chatbots

Aspect Details
DefinitionAI-based software that simulates human conversation
ExamplesChatGPT, Google Bard, Alexa, Siri
UsesCustomer support, education, healthcare, and law enforcement
"Chatbots talk smart, not hard."

2. Drones

Aspect Details
DefinitionUnmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) controlled remotely or autonomously
UsesAgriculture, delivery, military, disaster management
Indian ExampleDrone Didi Yojana (2023) – drones for women-led farming
"Drones – Eyes in the Sky."

D. Nanotechnology & Advanced Materials

1. Graphene

Aspect Details
DefinitionA single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a honeycomb pattern
PropertiesStronger than steel, excellent conductor, flexible, lightweight
UsesElectronics, batteries, sensors, medical devices
Discovered ByAndre Geim & Konstantin Novoselov (Nobel Prize 2010)
"Graphene – Super Material of the Future."

2. Bio-Plastics

Aspect Details
DefinitionPlastics made from renewable plant sources like corn starch or sugarcane
FeatureBiodegradable and eco-friendly
UsesPackaging, utensils, agriculture
"Bio-Plastics – Plastic that Returns to Nature."
