Government Schemes & Economic Policies

Delhi Police Exams - GK Section

1️⃣ Introduction

India’s development journey has been shaped by government policies, reforms, and flagship schemes. From LPG reforms of 1991 to Digital India and Atmanirbhar Bharat, the focus has been on inclusive growth, employment, innovation, and self-reliance.

Memory Trick: Reforms modernize; Schemes humanize.

A. Major Government Schemes for Development

India's major government schemes drive poverty reduction, job creation, citizen empowerment, and economic growth.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi Launched 2019
Ministry: Agriculture
Objective: ₹6,000/year (3 installments) for small & marginal farmers.
Memory tip: "6K for Kisan"
Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) Launched 2015
Objective: "Housing for All by 2024"
Feature: Urban & Rural, subsidized loans, CLSS benefit.
Jal Jeevan Mission Launched 2019
Ministry: Jal Shakti
Aim: Tap water to every rural household by 2024.
Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) Launched 2014
Aim: Financial inclusion—zero-balance accounts, RuPay card, insurance.
Memory tip: "Jan Dhan – Every Jan, One Dhan"
Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Yojana
Purpose: Economic relief to poor households (DBT), especially during crises.
Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY)
Objective: Skill training for rural youth (15–35 years) for better employment opportunities.
Make in India Launched 2014
Objective: Boost manufacturing, attract FDI, create jobs.
Slogan: "Zero Defect, Zero Effect"

B. Economic Reforms – LPG (1991)

Introduced: 1991 | Lead: PM P.V. Narasimha Rao, FM Dr. Manmohan Singh.
Triggered by a major balance of payment crisis.

Reform Focus Result
Liberalization Freeing economy from control Increased competition
Privatization Selling PSUs to private Efficiency boost
Globalization Integrating world economy FDI inflows rise
Liberalization: Less control, more freedom.
Trick: Liberalization = Less control, more freedom.
Privatization: Private hands, public progress.
Globalization: Go beyond borders.
Impact: Boosted FDI inflow, increased exports, promoted competition, reduced government control.

D. Atmanirbhar Bharat Mission (Self-Reliant India Mission)

Launched: 2020
Objective: Make India self-reliant and competitive post-COVID.

Pillar Description
Economy Quantum jump, not incremental
Infrastructure Modern India vision
System Reforms-driven governance
Vibrant Demography India’s strength
Demand Boost domestic consumption
Trick: 5 EID Pillars → Economy, Infra, Demography, Demand.
Key Initiatives: Vocal for Local, PLI Scheme, MSMEs, Agri Reforms, Solar & Defense push.

E. Skill India, Startup India, Digital India

Skill India Mission
  • Launched: 2015
  • Train 40 crore by 2025
  • Agency: NSDC
Trick: Skill India = Skill, Scale, Speed.
Startup India
  • Launched: 2016
  • Encourage innovation & entrepreneurship
  • Tax exemptions, funding support, easy registration
  • Agency: DPIIT
Trick: Startup India → Standup India.
Digital India
  • Launched: 2015
  • Transform India digitally
  • Broadband, e-Governance
  • UPI, BHIM, online empowerment
Trick: Digital India = Power to Empower.
