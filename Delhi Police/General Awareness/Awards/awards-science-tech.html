Science & Technology Awards

Delhi Police & SSC Exams – GK Section

Introduction

Awards in Science and Technology celebrate innovation, discovery, and contributions to society. Questions on these awards are common in competitive government exams.

Mnemonic: B–T–A–L → Bhatnagar, Turing, Abel, Lasker.
Think: “Brilliant Thinkers Achieve Laurels!”

Shanti Swaroop Bhatnagar Award (India)

  • Named After: Dr. Shanti Swaroop Bhatnagar (founder-director CSIR)
  • Established: 1958
  • Given By: Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR), Govt. of India
  • Field: Scientific research
  • Eligibility: Indian scientists under age 45
  • Categories: Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Engineering, Medicine, Mathematics, Earth Science
  • Prize: ₹5 lakh + citation
  • Frequency: Annual
Example: Dr. Ritu Karidhal (ISRO "Rocket Woman") – honored for space research.

Mnemonic: Bhatnagar = Best Brains Below 45.

Turing Award (International)

  • Named After: Alan M. Turing
  • Established: 1966
  • Given By: Association for Computing Machinery (ACM), USA
  • Field: Computer Science (IT)
  • Known As: Nobel Prize of Computing
  • Prize: USD 1 million
Famous Recipients: Tim Berners-Lee (World Wide Web), John McCarthy (AI), Cerf & Kahn (Internet), Geoffrey Hinton (Deep Learning)

Mnemonic: Turing – Tech Titans Triumph.

Abel Prize (International)

  • Named After: Niels Henrik Abel (Norway)
  • Established: 2001
  • Given By: Government of Norway
  • Field: Mathematics
  • Prize: USD 1 million
  • Nickname: Nobel Prize of Mathematics
Famous Laureates: Jean-Pierre Serre (2003, First Winner), Andrew Wiles (2016, Fermat's Last Theorem), Karen Uhlenbeck (2019, First Woman)

Mnemonic: Abel = Advanced Brains Excel in Logic.

Lasker Award (International)

  • Named After: Albert & Mary Lasker
  • Established: 1945
  • Given By: Lasker Foundation, USA
  • Field: Medicine & Medical research
  • Nickname: American Nobel Prize
  • Categories: Basic & Clinical Medical Research, Public Service
  • Significance: 80+ Lasker winners later received Nobel!
Indian Connection: Dr. Vikram Patel (Psychiatry) – global recognition in mental health.

Mnemonic: Lasker – Life & Science Keep Evolving Rapidly.
