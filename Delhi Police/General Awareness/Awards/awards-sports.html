National Sports Awards

Delhi Police & SSC Exams – GK Section

Introduction

India recognizes outstanding achievements in sports through four major national awards given every year by the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports. These honor exceptional players, coaches, and contributors to Indian sports.

Memory Trick: K–A–D–D → Khel Ratna, Arjuna, Dronacharya, Dhyan Chand.
Keep Athletes Dedicated Daily!

Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award (Highest Sports Honor)

  • Established: 1991 (Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, renamed 2021)
  • Named After: Major Dhyan Chand (Hockey Legend)
  • Given By: President of India
  • Presented By: Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports
  • Purpose: Outstanding 4-year performance in sports
  • Award Includes: Medallion, certificate, ₹25 lakh cash
  • First Recipient: Viswanathan Anand (Chess, 1991–92)
Recent: Neeraj Chopra (Athletics), Mirabai Chanu (Weightlifting), Manika Batra (TT), Rani Rampal (Hockey)

Mnemonic: Khel Ratna = King of Sports Awards.

Arjuna Award

  • Established: 1961
  • Named After: Arjuna – Mahabharata’s archer symbolizing focus
  • Given By: President of India
  • Presented For: Consistent outstanding performance (4 years)
  • Award Includes: Bronze statuette, certificate, ₹15 lakh cash
  • Fields Covered: All recognised sports
Notables: P.V. Sindhu (Badminton), Rohit Sharma (Cricket), Bajrang Punia (Wrestling), Lovlina Borgohain (Boxing)

Mnemonic: Arjuna Award = Archer’s Accuracy in Achievement.

Dronacharya Award (For Coaches)

  • Established: 1985
  • Named After: Guru Dronacharya – legendary teacher of Arjuna
  • Given By: President of India
  • For: Coaches who have trained medal-winning athletes
  • Award Includes: Bronze statue, certificate, ₹15 lakh cash
  • Categories: Lifetime Achievement & Regular Category
Famous: Pullela Gopichand, Satpal Singh, R. K. Sharma, Balachandran Manikkal

Mnemonic: Dronacharya trains the Arjunas of today.

Dhyan Chand Award (Lifetime Achievement)

  • Established: 2002
  • Named After: Major Dhyan Chand (Hockey Legend)
  • Given By: President of India
  • For: Lifetime contribution to sports or sports promotion
  • Award Includes: Certificate, plaque, ₹10 lakh cash
  • Focus: Retired players who inspire generations
Notables: Late Capt. Chand Singh (Hockey), Manpreet Kaur (Athletics), Manoj Kumar (Boxing), Shabbir Ali (Football)

Mnemonic: Dhyan Chand = Dedication Beyond Career.
