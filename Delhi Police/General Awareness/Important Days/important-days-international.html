International Important Days

Global GK Section – Major UN & World Days

Introduction to International Days

International days are celebrated worldwide to promote awareness about global issues — health, peace, environment, literacy, equality and more. Knowing dates, organizers and themes of these days is vital for GK preparation.

Memory Trick: “W–H–E–L → World, Health, Environment, Literacy”

Exam Focus: Learn the organizers and main idea behind each day.

Major International Days by Month & Theme

February – Water & Wetlands
Date Day Organized By Theme (2024) Significance
2 February World Wetlands Day UNEP / Ramsar Convention Wetlands and Human Wellbeing Marks adoption of Ramsar Convention (1971) for wetland protection.
Trick: "Wetlands = Water + Wildlife + Wellbeing."
March – Women & Water
Date Day Organized By Theme (2024) Significance
8 March International Women's Day United Nations Invest in Women: Accelerate Progress Celebrates women's empowerment & gender equality.
22 March World Water Day UN-Water Water for Peace Importance of freshwater and sustainable water use.
Trick: "Women (8th) Need Water (22nd) — Both March for Equality."
April – Health & Earth Awareness
Date Day Organized By Theme (2024) Significance
7 April World Health Day WHO My Health, My Right WHO founding day – health for all awareness.
22 April World Earth Day Earth Day Network / UN Planet vs Plastics Environmental protection and climate awareness.
Trick: "Health before Earth – 7th then 22nd April."
May – Labour & Workers' Rights
Date Day Organized By Theme (2024) Significance
1 May International Labour Day (May Day) ILO Ensuring Safety and Dignity at Work Honours workers' contributions and rights worldwide.
Trick: "May Day = Workers' Way."
June – Environment & Yoga Month
Date Day Organized By Theme (2024) Significance
5 June World Environment Day UNEP Land Restoration, Desertification, and Drought Resilience Largest global environment event; hosted by Saudi Arabia (2024).
21 June International Yoga Day United Nations / India Initiative Yoga for Self and Society Holistic health focus; proposed by India in 2014.
Trick: "Environment Cleans Earth, Yoga Heals Self."
July – Population Awareness
Date Day Organized By Theme (2024) Significance
11 July World Population Day UNDP / UNFPA To Leave No One Behind Global population issues, family planning & equality.
Trick: "7 + 11 = 18 → 1.8 Billion Indians — Population Connection!"
September – Literacy & Ozone Protection
Date Day Organized By Theme (2024) Significance
8 September International Literacy Day UNESCO Promoting Literacy for a World in Transition Promotes education and global literacy.
16 September World Ozone Day UNEP Montreal Protocol: Fixing the Ozone Layer and Cooling the Planet Marks Montreal Protocol (1987) signing.
27 September World Tourism Day UNWTO Tourism and Peace Promoting responsible, sustainable tourism.
Trick: "Read (8th), Breathe (16th), Travel (27th)."
October – United Nations
Date Day Organized By Theme (2024) Significance
24 October United Nations Day United Nations Empowering Global Unity UN formation in 1945.
Trick: "UN Born on 24th October – After World War II."
December – Health & Human Rights
Date Day Organized By Theme (2024) Significance
1 December World AIDS Day WHO / UNAIDS Let Communities Lead Awareness about HIV/AIDS, prevention & treatment.
10 December Human Rights Day United Nations Freedom, Equality, and Justice for All Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948).
Trick: "AIDS (1st) Protects Health; Rights (10th) Protect Humanity."

March → Women, Water
April → Health, Earth
May → Labour
June → Environment, Yoga
July → Population
September → Literacy, Ozone, Tourism
October → UN Day
December → AIDS, Human Rights

Final Concept Recap

  • World Environment Day (5 June) – UNEP's global environment campaign
  • International Yoga Day (21 June) – Indian initiative at UN
  • World Population Day (11 July) – Focus on awareness and planning
  • World Ozone Day (16 September) – Montreal Protocol (1987)
  • United Nations Day (24 October) – UN formation
  • Human Rights Day (10 December) – Universal Declaration of Human Rights
