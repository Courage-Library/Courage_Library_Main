Defence & Armed Forces Days of India

GK Static – Army, Navy, Airforce, Police, Kargil, Coast

Introduction to Defence and Armed Forces Days

India’s Defence and Armed Forces Days salute the heroism and service of our soldiers, airmen, sailors, and police. These dates figure regularly in competitive exams’ GK and Current Affairs.

Memory Trick: A–C–K–A–N–P → Army, Coast Guard, Kargil, Air, Navy, Police

Exam Focus: Know the date, event and what/who is honored.

Army Day – 15th January

Significance First Indian Commander-in-Chief: Field Marshal K. M. Cariappa took command (1949)
Observed Since 1949
Purpose To salute courage and discipline of Indian Army personnel.
Celebrations Military parade, display at HQ Delhi.
Trick: "Army Ascended Command – 15th January."

Coast Guard Day – 1st February

Established 1977
Motto Vayam Rakshamah (“We Protect”)
Purpose Commemorates foundation of Indian Coast Guard, responsible for coastal security.
Trick: "Coast Guard Comes First – February 1st."

Air Force Day – 8th October

Established 1932 (IAF founded)
Purpose Honors achievements of Indian Air Force.
Main Event Shows & parades at Hindon Air Base, Ghaziabad.
Motto Touch the Sky with Glory
Trick: "8th October – Air Warriors Soar Higher."

Navy Day – 4th December

Significance Commemorates Operation Trident success, 1971 war (Karachi Harbor)
Motto Sham No Varunah (“May Lord of Oceans be auspicious”)
Celebrations Naval parade, maritime displays
Trick: "Navy Navigates Victory – 4th December."

Kargil Vijay Diwas – 26th July

Significance Marks Kargil War victory (1999)
Purpose Tribute to Operation Vijay soldiers.
Location of Ceremony Dras War Memorial, Ladakh
Key Hero Captain Vikram Batra (Param Vir Chakra)
Trick: "Kargil Victors Remembered – 26th July."

National Police Commemoration Day – 21st October

Established 1960
Reason In memory of 10 CRPF jawans at Hot Springs, Ladakh (1959)
Purpose Honors police martyrs nationwide.
Event Memorial parade at Chanakyapuri, Delhi
Trick: "Police Protect – Remembered on 21st October."

Quick Revision Mnemonics

Code: “15-1-26-8-21-4” → Army, Coast, Kargil, Air, Police, Navy
Sentence Trick: "15 Soldiers, 1 Coast Guard, 26 Victories, 8 Airmen, 21 Martyrs, 4 Sailors."

Final Concept Recap

  • Army Day – 15 Jan: First Indian Commander-in-Chief, K. M. Cariappa
  • Coast Guard Day – 1 Feb: Foundation of Coast Guard
  • Kargil Vijay Diwas – 26 Jul: Victory in 1999 Kargil War
  • Air Force Day – 8 Oct: IAF Foundation Day
  • Police Commemoration Day – 21 Oct: Tribute to 1959 Hot Springs Martyrs
  • Navy Day – 4 Dec: Operation Trident, 1971 Indo-Pak War
