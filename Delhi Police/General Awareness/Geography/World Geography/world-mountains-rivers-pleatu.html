Important Mountains, Rivers, and Plateaus of the World

World Geography – Exam Preparation

Introduction: Earth’s Majestic Landforms

The Earth’s surface is full of diverse landforms — from towering mountains to mighty rivers and vast plateaus.

These features shape climate, vegetation, and human settlement.

Memory Trick: "Mountains hold height, Rivers give life, Plateaus build might."

Major Mountain Ranges of the World

Mountain Range Continent Highest Peak Key Facts
Himalayas Asia Mount Everest (8,848 m) Youngest fold mountains; “Roof of World”
Andes South America Aconcagua (6,962 m) Longest range (~7,000 km)
Rockies North America Mount Elbert (4,401 m) Backbone of North America
Alps Europe Mont Blanc (4,808 m) Switzerland; tourism
Atlas Africa Toubkal (4,165 m) Sahara vs Mediterranean
Ural Europe–Asia Narodnaya (1,895 m) Europe-Asia division
Great Dividing Australia Kosciuszko (2,228 m) Australia’s main range
Drakensberg Africa (South) Thabana Ntlenyana (3,482 m) S. Africa’s highest
"HARA AUD GAD" → Himalaya, Andes, Rockies, Alps, Ural, Drakensberg, Great Dividing, Atlas

Exam Tricks & Mnemonics

Continental Rivers Trick:

Yankee Nile Amazes Many. (Yangtze, Nile, Amazon, Mississippi)

Highest Peaks Trick:

Every King Knows Many Amazing European Adventures. (Everest, Kilimanjaro, Kangchenjunga, McKinley, Aconcagua, Elbrus, Andes)

Plateaus Trick:

Tall Tibetans Drink Brazilian Coffee. (Tibetan, Deccan, Brazilian)

Revision & Exam Points

Most Repeated Questions: Highest peak (Everest), Longest river (Nile), Roof of World (Tibetan Plateau), Longest range (Andes), Separating range (Ural), Coffee region (Brazilian Plateau)
One Mark Shortcuts: Everest → 8,848 m, Nile → Africa, Amazon → Largest volume, Tibet → Highest plateau, Andes → Longest range
