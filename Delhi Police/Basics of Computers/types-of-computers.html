Delhi Police Exam Exam

1. Definition ‚Äì What are "Types of Computers"?

Computers are classified based on size, processing power, and purpose. There are mainly four major types: Microcomputer, Minicomputer, Mainframe Computer, and Supercomputer.

2. Types of Computers

1

Microcomputer

Also known as a Personal Computer (PC). Used by individuals for daily work like typing, internet browsing, or gaming.

Based on microprocessor technology

Cost: Low

Users: 1 at a time

Speed: Moderate

Examples:

Desktop PC

Laptop

Tablet

Smartphone

Exam Tip: If the question says "used by a single person," answer = Microcomputer.

2

Minicomputer

Larger and more powerful than microcomputers but smaller than mainframes. Support 10‚Äì100 users at once.

Used in small organizations

Cost: Medium

Users: Multiple (10‚Äì100)

Speed: Higher than micro

Examples:

IBM AS/400

PDP-11

VAX series

Exam Tip: If you see "medium organization server," it's Minicomputer.

3

Mainframe Computer

Very large and powerful system used by banks, railways, airlines, and government offices to process huge data.

Supports hundreds of users

High processing speed

Massive storage capacity

Cost: High

Examples:

IBM z Series

Hitachi Z800

Exam Tip: Used by large organizations for central database storage.

4

Supercomputer

The fastest and most powerful computer type used for scientific research, weather forecasting, and space research.

Performs millions of calculations/sec

Cost: Very High

Users: Research teams

Speed: Extremely High

Examples:

PARAM 10000

Pratyush

Summit (USA)

Exam Tip: PARAM ‚Üí Supercomputer of India (Developed by C-DAC).
