Q1. A can finish a work in 10 days. What is his 1-day work?

View Answer

1/10

Q2. B can complete a work in 20 days. What is his efficiency ratio compared to A who works in 10 days?

View Answer

A : B efficiency = 20 : 10 = 2 : 1

Q3. A takes 12 days, B takes 6 days. Who is more efficient and by what ratio?

View Answer

Efficiency ratio = 6 : 12 = 1 : 2, So B is twice as efficient as A.

Q4. A's efficiency is 25% more than B. If B finishes work in 20 days, find A's time.

View Answer

A's efficiency = 125% of B = 5/4, Time ratio = 4 : 5, A's time = 20 √ó (4/5) = 16 days

Q5. A and B can do a job in 12 days and 15 days respectively. How long if both work together?

View Answer

Their 1-day work = 1/12 + 1/15 = 9/60 = 3/20, Total time = 20/3 = 6.67 days

Q6. A can finish a piece of work in 8 days. B is 50% as efficient as A. Find B's time.

View Answer

Q7. A is twice as efficient as B. Together they finish work in 12 days. Find individual times.

View Answer

Efficiency A : B = 2 : 1, Total parts = 3, Total work = 12, A's days = 12/2 = 6 days, B's days = 12/1 = 12 days

Q8. A can do a work in 15 days. After 5 days A leaves. Remaining work is done by B in 10 days. Find B's time.

View Answer

A's 5-day work = 5/15 = 1/3, Remaining work = 2/3, B's 1-day work = (2/3)/10 = 1/15, So B's time = 15 days
