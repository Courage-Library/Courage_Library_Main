Panchayati Raj System

Essential Concepts for SSC GD Exams

1. Introduction

Panchayati Raj is India's rural local government system, meant to bring democracy and administration to villages. It is a 3-tier system established by the 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992. This topic is very important for SSC GD.

Simple Definition

Panchayati Raj is a 3-tier local government system in rural India established by the 73rd Amendment Act to empower villages.

2. Constitutional Status – 73rd Amendment Act, 1992

The Panchayati Raj system became constitutional through:

73rd Constitutional Amendment Act (1992)

  • Implemented on 24 April 1993
  • Added Part IX (Articles 243–243O)
  • Added Eleventh Schedule (29 subjects)

Important Date

24 April = National Panchayati Raj Day

3. Features of 73rd Amendment (Most Important)

Feature Explanation
3-tier system Gram Panchayat → Panchayat Samiti → Zilla Parishad
Regular elections Every 5 years
Reservation SC, ST, OBC & Women (33% minimum)
Gram Sabha Village-level assembly
State Finance Commission Every 5 years
State Election Commission Conducts panchayat elections
Eleventh Schedule 29 subjects assigned to Panchayats

Many states increased women reservation to 50%.

4. Structure of Panchayati Raj (3-Tier System)

India follows a three-tier system: Gram Panchayat → Panchayat Samiti → Zilla Parishad

4.1. Gram Panchayat (Village Level)

The lowest and most important tier.

Gram Sabha

  • Assembly of all adult members (18+) of a village
  • Approves budgets, decisions, schemes

Gram Panchayat

  • Elected body at village level
  • Head: Sarpanch (Pradhan)
  • Term: 5 years

Functions

  • Drinking water supply
  • Street lighting
  • Sanitation
  • Rural roads
  • Registration of births & deaths

4.2. Panchayat Samiti (Block / Taluka Level)

Middle tier covering many village panchayats.

Head

Chairperson (Pramukh)

Functions

  • Implementing government schemes
  • Agriculture development
  • Rural health services
  • Poverty alleviation

4.3. Zilla Parishad (District Level)

Top tier of Panchayati Raj.

Head

Zilla Parishad Chairperson

Vice-Chairperson

Deputy Chairperson

Functions

  • Coordinates Panchayat Samitis
  • District planning
  • Construction of district roads
  • Allocation of funds
  • Supervises development programs

5. Eleventh Schedule – 29 Subjects of Panchayats

SSC GD often asks: How many subjects? → 29

Major Subjects Include:

  • Agriculture
  • Roads
  • Health
  • Education
  • Women & child development
  • Rural housing
  • Drinking water
  • Poverty reduction
  • Social welfare
  • Rural electrification
  • Small-scale industries
  • Fisheries
  • Animal husbandry

Memory Trick:

"A–R–H–E–W–H–P–S–E" (Agriculture, Roads, Health, Education, Women, Housing, Poverty, Social welfare, Electricity)

6. Reservation in Panchayati Raj

6.1. Categories

  • SC/ST → As per population
  • Women → Minimum 33%, many states have 50%
  • OBC → Provided by states

6.2. Applies To

  • Chairperson posts (Sarpanch, Pramukh, Zilla Chairperson)
  • Seat positions

7. Sources of Revenue of Panchayats

Source Details
Taxes House tax, market tax
Grants From State Govt & Central Govt
Share from schemes MGNREGA, PMAY, etc.
Fees & fines Trade license fees

8. State Election Commission (SEC)

  • Conducts panchayat elections
  • Independent body
  • Appointed by Governor
  • Similar role as Election Commission of India, but only for local elections

9. State Finance Commission (SFC)

  • Appointed every 5 years
  • Decides distribution of funds to Panchayats

10. Important Committees Related to Panchayati Raj

Committee Recommendation
Balwant Rai Mehta Committee (1957) 3-tier Panchayat system
Ashok Mehta Committee (1977) 2-tier system
G.V.K. Rao Committee (1985) Strengthened Panchayats
L.M. Singhvi Committee (1986) Constitutional status

Actual constitutional status → 73rd Amendment (1992)

11. Difference Between Gram Sabha & Gram Panchayat

Gram Sabha Gram Panchayat
All adults of village Elected members
Permanent body Elected for 5 years
Approves plans Implements plans
Controls Panchayat Controlled by Sarpanch
