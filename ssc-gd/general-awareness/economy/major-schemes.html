Major Government Schemes

SSC GD Self-Paced Course

Introduction

Government schemes are very important for SSC exams. Questions often ask the full form, year of launch, ministry, and purpose of schemes.

This article includes all major schemes relevant for SSC GD preparation.

1. PM-KISAN

Full Form

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi

Launch Year

2019

Ministry

Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare

Benefits:

  • Provides income support of 6000 rupees per year to farmers
  • Paid in 3 installments of 2000 rupees each directly to bank accounts

Eligibility:

  • Small and marginal farmers
  • Landholding farmers only

Type:

Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) scheme

2. MNREGA

Full Form

Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act

Launch Year

2005

Ministry

Ministry of Rural Development

Features:

  • Guarantees 100 days of wage employment to rural households
  • Focus on manual unskilled work
  • Wages directly transferred to bank accounts
  • Aim: reduce rural poverty and create durable assets

Coverage:

All districts of India except urban areas

3. PMJDY

Full Form

Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana

Launch Year

2014

Ministry

Ministry of Finance

Purpose:

Financial inclusion for every household

Features:

  • Zero balance bank accounts
  • RuPay debit card
  • Overdraft facility
  • Accident insurance
  • Direct Benefit Transfer enabled

Important:

Guinness World Record for maximum bank accounts opened in one week

4. PMAY

Full Form

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana

Launch Year

2015

Two Parts:

  • PMAY-U (Urban)
  • PMAY-G (Gramin)

Objective:

Housing for All

Features:

  • Affordable houses
  • Subsidy on home loans
  • Basic amenities included: water supply, toilet, electricity

5. PMEGP

Full Form

Prime Minister's Employment Generation Programme

Launch Year

2008

Ministry

Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME)

Purpose:

To generate jobs through micro-enterprises in rural and urban areas

Eligibility:

  • Individuals above 18 years
  • No income limit

Implementing Agency:

KVIC (Khadi and Village Industries Commission)

6. PMFBY

Full Form

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana

Launch Year

2016

Ministry

Agriculture and Farmers Welfare

Purpose:

Crop insurance scheme to protect farmers against crop loss due to:

  • Natural calamities
  • Pests
  • Diseases

Premium Rates:

  • 2 percent for Kharif crops
  • 1.5 percent for Rabi crops
  • 5 percent for commercial and horticulture crops

7. Ayushman Bharat

Also Known As

Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PMJAY)

Launch Year

2018

Ministry

Health and Family Welfare

Purpose:

Provide health insurance coverage of up to 5 lakh rupees per family per year

Beneficiaries:

Poor and vulnerable families as per SECC data

Key Feature:

World's largest government health insurance scheme

8. PMUY

Full Form

Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana

Launch Year

2016

Ministry

Petroleum and Natural Gas

Purpose:

Provide free LPG connections to women belonging to poor households

Benefits:

  • Free gas connection
  • Subsidized gas refills

9. PMGKY

Full Form

Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Yojana

Launch Year

2020 (Covid-19 period)

Purpose:

Provide free food grains, cash support, and essential services to the poor during crisis

10. PM-SVANidhi

Full Form

Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor's AtmaNirbhar Nidhi

Launch Year

2020

Ministry

Housing and Urban Affairs

Purpose:

Provide loans to street vendors to resume business

Loan Details:

  • Initial loan: up to 10000 rupees
  • Later up to: 20000 and 50000 rupees

11. PMKVY

Full Form

Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana

Launch Year

2015

Ministry

Skill Development and Entrepreneurship

Purpose:

  • Provide skill training to youth
  • Certified training programs
  • Increase employability

12. NRLM

Full Form

National Rural Livelihood Mission

Launch Year

2011

Ministry

Rural Development

Purpose:

  • Promote Self Help Groups (SHGs)
  • Provide employment opportunities in rural areas
  • Women empowerment program

13. Atal Pension Yojana

Launch Year

2015

Ministry

Finance

Purpose:

Provide pension to unorganized sector workers

Benefits:

  • Pension of 1000 to 5000 rupees depending on contribution

Eligibility:

Age 18 to 40 years

14. Stand-Up India

Launch Year

2016

Ministry

Finance

Purpose:

Provide bank loans to SC, ST, and Women entrepreneurs

Loan Amount:

10 lakh to 1 crore rupees

15. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana

Launch Year

2015

Ministry

Women and Child Development

Purpose:

Savings scheme for girl child

Features:

  • High interest rate
  • Tax free returns
  • Account can be opened for girls up to 10 years of age

16. National Food Security Act (NFSA)

Launch Year

2013

Purpose:

Provide subsidized food grains to poor households

Benefits:

  • Rice at 3 rupees per kg
  • Wheat at 2 rupees per kg
  • Coarse grains at 1 rupee per kg

Coverage:

Covers 75 percent of rural and 50 percent of urban population

17. Jal Jeevan Mission

Launch Year

2019

Ministry

Jal Shakti

Purpose:

Provide piped drinking water to every rural household

Target:

Har Ghar Jal (Water to Every Home)

18. Swachh Bharat Mission

Launch Year

2014

Ministry

Drinking Water and Sanitation; Urban Development

Purpose:

Make India clean

  • Build toilets
  • End open defecation

Two Parts:

  • Swachh Bharat Urban
  • Swachh Bharat Gramin

19. Digital India Mission

Launch Year

2015

Purpose:

  • Promote digital services
  • Cashless payments
  • Internet access for all
  • Digital governance
