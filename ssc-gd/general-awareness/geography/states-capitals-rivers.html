Part 2: Major Rivers of India

Himalayan Rivers (Perennial Rivers)

Ganga River

  • Origin: Gangotri Glacier (Uttarakhand)
  • Empties into: Bay of Bengal
  • Longest River in India
  • Main Tributaries: Yamuna, Ghaghara, Gandak, Kosi

Yamuna River

  • Origin: Yamunotri Glacier
  • Meets Ganga at: Prayagraj
  • Flows through: Delhi

Brahmaputra River

  • Origin: Tibet
  • Enters India through: Arunachal Pradesh
  • Forms: Majuli (Largest river island)

Peninsular Rivers (Rain-fed Rivers)

Godavari River

  • Origin: Nashik (Maharashtra)
  • Longest Peninsular River
  • Also called: Dakshin Ganga

Krishna River

  • Origin: Mahabaleshwar
  • Flows through: Maharashtra, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh

Narmada River

  • Origin: Amarkantak
  • Flows Westward
  • Empties into: Arabian Sea

Tapti River

  • Origin: Satpura Range
  • Flows Westward

SSC GD Important River Facts

  • Longest River in India – Ganga

  • West Flowing Rivers – Narmada and Tapti

Memory Trick for Major Rivers

GBY – GKN

North to South Order of Important Rivers

GBY (Himalayan Rivers)

  • G – Ganga
  • B – Brahmaputra
  • Y – Yamuna

GKN (Peninsular Rivers)

  • G – Godavari
  • K – Krishna
  • N – Narmada

Quick Recall: Himalayan Rivers → Snow-fed (Perennial) Peninsular Rivers → Rain-fed West Flowing → Narmada and Krishna
