Climate & Soils of India

SSC GD Self-Paced Course

1. What is Climate?

India's climate and soils are influenced by its location, relief, oceans, monsoon winds, and physiographic features.

These topics are very important for SSC GD, SSC CGL, CHSL, Railways, and state-level exams.

Simple Definition

Climate refers to the average weather condition of a place over a long period (30+ years).

India has a Monsoon Type Climate with distinct seasons and heavy seasonal rains.

India's climate is mainly controlled by monsoons, showing summer heat, winter cold, and rainy seasons.

2. Features of India's Climate

Feature Explanation
Monsoon Type Rainfall mainly in June–Sept
Seasonal Variation Hot summers, cold winters
Cyclones In Bay of Bengal & Arabian Sea
Diverse Climate Desert, mountains, coastal climates
Uneven Rainfall Highest in Meghalaya, lowest in Rajasthan

3. Factors Affecting India's Climate

✔ Latitude

Tropic of Cancer divides India → Tropical + Temperate climate zones.

✔ Altitude

  • Himalayas → Cold
  • Plains → Hot
  • Plateau → Moderate

✔ Distance from Sea

  • Coastal areas → Mild climate
  • Interior areas → Extreme climate

✔ Mountains & Relief

  • Himalayas stop cold winds
  • Western Ghats cause rainfall on west coast

✔ Monsoon Winds

Main rainfall source

4. Seasons in India (According to Indian Meteorological Department)

Season Months Features
Winter December–February Cool, dry
Summer (Hot Weather) March–May Very hot, loo winds
South-West Monsoon June–September Heavy rainfall
Retreating Monsoon October–November Cyclones in Bay of Bengal

Memory Trick:

"W–S–M–R" → Winter → Summer → Monsoon → Retreating

5. Monsoons in India (SSC GD Most Important Topic)

Monsoon = Seasonal reversal of winds

A. South-West Monsoon (June–September)

Gives 75% of India's rainfall.

Two branches:

Branch Areas Covered
Arabian Sea Branch Western Ghats, Gujarat, Punjab
Bay of Bengal Branch West Bengal, Assam, Bihar

Highest rainfall area: Mawsynram (Meghalaya) – world's wettest place

B. North-East Monsoon (Retreating Monsoon)

  • Winds blow from land to sea
  • Rainfall mainly in Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh

C. Cyclones

Two major cyclone zones:

  • Bay of Bengal (more dangerous)
  • Arabian Sea

Months of cyclones: May–June and October–November

6. Climate Regions of India

Region Climate
Northern Mountains Cold, snowy
Northern Plains Extreme climate
Western Desert Hot & dry
Coastal Areas Moderate climate
South India Humid & warm

7. Soils of India (Very Important for SSC GD)

What is Soil?

Soil = Upper layer of the earth where plants grow.

India has 8 major soil types

8. Soil Types of India

Soil Type Area/Region Features Crops
Alluvial Soil Northern Plains, Rivers Fertile Rice, wheat, sugarcane
Black Soil Maharashtra, MP Clayey, holds moisture Cotton
Red Soil Tamil Nadu, Karnataka Less fertile Millets, pulses
Laterite Soil Western Ghats Red due to iron Tea, coffee
Mountain Soil Himalayas Thin, poor Apples
Desert Soil Rajasthan Sandy, salty Bajra
Forest Soil Forest areas Rich in humus Rubber
Peaty Soil Kerala, Bengal Waterlogged Rice, jute

Memory Trick:

"A–B–R–L–M–D–F–P" → Alluvial, Black, Red, Laterite, Mountain, Desert, Forest, Peaty

9. Alluvial Soil (Most Important for Exams)

Key Features

  • Covers 40% of India
  • Deposited by rivers: Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra
  • Very fertile

Two Types

  • Bangar (old)
  • Khadar (new, more fertile)

Best for crops like:

  • Wheat
  • Rice
  • Sugarcane
  • Oilseeds

10. Black Soil (Regur Soil)

Key Features

  • Found in: Maharashtra, Gujarat, MP
  • Forms black cotton soil
  • Retains moisture
  • Rich in minerals like lime, iron

Best for:

  • Cotton
  • Tobacco
  • Sugarcane

11. Red Soil

Key Features

  • Formed from old igneous rocks
  • Red color due to iron oxide
  • Less fertile
  • Needs fertilizers

Grows:

  • Millets
  • Pulses
  • Groundnut

12. Laterite Soil

Key Features

  • Found in high rainfall areas
  • Formed due to leaching
  • Suitable for plantations

Crops:

  • Tea
  • Coffee
  • Cashew

13. Desert Soil

Key Features

  • Sandy + low moisture
  • Present in western Rajasthan

Crops:

  • Bajra
  • Jowar
  • Barley

14. Mountain Soil

Key Features

  • Present in Himalayas
  • Rich in humus

Used for:

  • Apple
  • Tea
  • Medicinal herbs

15. Peaty & Marshy Soils

Found in:

  • Bengal
  • Kerala
  • Odisha

Very fertile for:

  • Jute
  • Rice
