Agriculture & Minerals of India

SSC GD Self-Paced Course

1. Types of Farming in India

1. Subsistence Farming

  • Done for self-consumption
  • Small land holdings
  • Example: Rural villages in Bihar, Odisha

2. Commercial Farming

  • Crops grown for market
  • Uses fertilizers, HYV seeds
  • Example: Punjab, Haryana

3. Plantation Farming

  • Single crop on large area
  • Tea, Coffee, Rubber
  • Needs heavy capital

2. Types of Crops (Very Important)

A. Kharif Crops

Sowing & Harvesting:

  • Sown: June–July
  • Harvested: September–October
  • Needs: High temperature & rainfall

Important Crops:

  • Rice
  • Cotton
  • Maize
  • Soybean
  • Jute

Largest Producer of Rice: West Bengal

Cotton: Gujarat (Very Important)

B. Rabi Crops

Sowing & Harvesting:

  • Sown: October–November
  • Harvested: March–April
  • Needs: Cool climate

Important Crops:

  • Wheat
  • Mustard
  • Gram
  • Barley

Wheat Producer No.1: Uttar Pradesh

C. Zaid Crops

  • Grown between Rabi & Kharif seasons
  • March–June
  • Examples: Watermelon, Muskmelon, Cucumber

4. Soil Types in India (Very Important)

1. Alluvial Soil

  • Found in: Northern Plains
  • Most fertile soil
  • Good for: Rice, Wheat, Sugarcane

2. Black Soil (Regur Soil)

  • Found in: Deccan Plateau
  • Good for: Cotton
  • Retains moisture

3. Red Soil

  • Found in: Tamil Nadu, Karnataka
  • Rich in iron
  • Less fertile

4. Laterite Soil

  • Found in: Western Ghats
  • Good for: Tea, Coffee

5. Green Revolution (Important)

Key Facts

  • Started in: 1960s
  • Father of Green Revolution in India: M. S. Swaminathan
  • Focus Crop: Wheat
  • High Yielding Variety Seeds (HYV)

Impact

  • Increased food production
  • Made India self-sufficient in food grains
