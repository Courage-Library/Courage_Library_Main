Diseases & Nutrition

Complete SSC GD Syllabus Coverage

1. What Is Disease?

A disease is any condition that disturbs the normal functioning of the body.

Communicable Diseases

Spread from person to person

Non-communicable Diseases

Do not spread

2. Communicable Diseases

Caused by microorganisms (pathogens).

Main types of pathogens:

Viruses

Bacteria

Protozoa

Fungi

Worms

3. Viral Diseases

Caused by viruses.

Examples:

  • Common cold
  • Influenza (flu)
  • Measles
  • Chickenpox
  • Polio
  • Dengue
  • COVID-19
  • Hepatitis A, B, C

Key Points:

  • Viruses are smallest pathogens
  • Antibiotics do not work on viruses

4. Bacterial Diseases

Examples:

  • Cholera
  • Typhoid
  • Tuberculosis (TB)
  • Pneumonia
  • Tetanus
  • Leprosy

Key Points:

  • Treated using antibiotics
  • Bacteria are larger than viruses

5. Protozoan Diseases

Caused by single-celled organisms.

Malaria

Caused by Plasmodium

Spread by female Anopheles mosquito

Other Examples:

  • Kala-azar – Leishmania
  • Amoebiasis – Entamoeba histolytica

6. Fungal Diseases

Examples:

Ringworm

Athlete's foot

Skin infections

7. Worm-Related Diseases

Examples:

  • Tapeworm infection
  • Roundworm infection
  • Filariasis – elephantiasis
  • Spread by Culex mosquito

8. Non-Communicable Diseases

These do not spread by contact.

  • Diabetes
  • Cancer
  • Heart attack
  • Hypertension
  • Asthma
  • Arthritis

9. Deficiency Diseases (Due to Lack of Nutrients)

Deficiency of vitamins and minerals leads to specific diseases.

Nutrient Deficiency Disease
Vitamin A Night blindness
Vitamin B1 Beri-Beri
Vitamin B12 Anemia
Vitamin C Scurvy
Vitamin D Rickets
Vitamin K Bleeding disorders
Iron Anemia
Iodine Goiter
Calcium Weak bones, muscle cramps

10. Nutrition Basics

Nutrients are substances needed by the body for growth and energy.

Types of Nutrients:

Carbohydrates

Proteins

Fats

Vitamins

Minerals

Water & Fibers

11. Carbohydrates

Main source of energy.

Examples:

  • Rice
  • Wheat
  • Potatoes
  • Sugar

12. Proteins

Body-building food.

Examples:

  • Pulses
  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Fish
  • Meat

Protein deficiency:

  • Kwashiorkor
  • Marasmus (in children)

13. Fats

Provide stored energy.

Examples:

  • Ghee
  • Oil
  • Butter
  • Nuts

14. Vitamins

Required in small amounts. Prevent deficiency diseases.

Fat-soluble:

A, D, E, K

Water-soluble:

B, C

15. Minerals

Important minerals:

Iron

Calcium

Potassium

Iodine

Zinc

Sodium

16. Balanced Diet

A diet containing all nutrients in correct proportion.

Important for growth, immunity, and health.

17. Immunity

Immunity is the ability of the body to fight diseases.

Natural Immunity

Present from birth

Acquired Immunity

Developed after vaccination or infection

18. Vaccination

Vaccines help the body develop immunity.

  • Polio vaccine
  • BCG (for TB)
  • MMR (measles)
  • Hepatitis vaccine
  • COVID-19 vaccines

19. First Aid Basics

Fractures

Immobilize the limb

Burns

Wash with cold water

Snakebite

Keep patient still

Bleeding

Apply pressure

20. Common Health Terms

  • Pathogen: Disease-causing organism
  • Antibiotic: Medicine for bacterial diseases
  • Antiseptic: Prevents infection
  • Analgesic: Pain reliever
  • Antipyretic: Reduces fever

21. Human Blood Groups

A, B, AB, O
Positive and negative (Rh factor)

O negative

Universal donor

AB positive

Universal recipient

  • Vitamin A = Eyes
  • Vitamin C = Cuts and wounds

  • Vitamin K = Clotting
