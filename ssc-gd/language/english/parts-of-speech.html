Parts of Speech

SSC GD Self-Paced Course

Introduction

Parts of Speech are the basic building blocks of English grammar. SSC GD asks direct, simple questions mostly related to identifying parts of speech, spotting errors, and choosing the correct word in a sentence.

Exam Tip: Once this topic is clear, error detection and sentence correction become super easy.

Noun

Meaning

A noun is the name of a person, place, thing, animal, or idea.

Examples: Ram, Delhi, Book, Honesty, Dog

Exam Tip: If you can put a/an/the or many before a word — it's often a noun.

Pronoun

Meaning

A pronoun is used in place of a noun.

Examples: He, She, It, They, You, Them, Ours

Common SSC Mistake: "Everyone should do their work."
Correct: Everyone should do his/her work.

Verb

Meaning

A verb shows action or state of being.

Examples: Run, Eat, Play, Is, Are, Have

Types

Action verbs → jump, write

Be verbs → is, am, are

Helping verbs → can, will, has

Adjective

Meaning

Adjectives describe nouns or pronouns.

Examples: Beautiful girl, Tall building, Five apples

Question Trick: Adjectives answer — which? what kind? how many?

Adverb

Meaning

Adverbs describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

Examples: He runs quickly. Very tall. Speaks slowly.

Note: Common adverbs end with -ly, but not always.

Preposition

Meaning

Shows relationship between noun/pronoun and another word.

Examples: in, on, at, under, behind, between, from, to, with

Exam Favourite: "In the morning", "At night", "On Monday"

Conjunction

Meaning

Conjunctions join words, phrases, or sentences.

Examples: and, but, or, because, although

Common SSC Trap: "Either Ram or Shyam is going." (singular verb)

Interjection

Meaning

Words used to express emotions.

Examples: Oh! Wow! Oops! Alas!

Note: These are not common in exams, but good to know.

High-Frequency SSC GD Examples

Noun → Freedom, Rice, Honesty

Pronoun → They, Him, Yours

Verb → Write, Is, Eat, Has

Adjective → Tall, Red, Honest

Adverb → Slowly, Very, Always

Preposition → On, Between, With

Conjunction → But, Because, And

Sample Questions (SSC GD Style)

1

Identify the part of speech: He runs quickly.

Options: (a) Adjective (b) Verb (c) Adverb (d) Preposition

View Answer

Adverb

2

Identify the noun: Honesty is the best policy.

Options: (a) best (b) honesty (c) is (d) policy

View Answer

honesty

3

Choose the correct preposition: He sat ___ the chair.

Options: (a) on (b) in (c) at (d) behind

View Answer

on

4

Choose the conjunction: I tried hard but failed.

Options: (a) tried (b) hard (c) failed (d) but

View Answer

but

5

Identify the verb: They are playing cricket.

Options: (a) They (b) playing (c) cricket (d) are

View Answer

playing (main verb)

Quick Recap

Noun → name

Pronoun → replaces noun

Verb → action or being

Adjective → describes noun

Adverb → describes verb/adjective

Preposition → shows relation

Conjunction → joins

Interjection → emotions

Exam Focus: SSC GD focuses mostly on noun/pronoun/verb/adjective/adverb questions.
