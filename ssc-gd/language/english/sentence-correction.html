Sentence Correction

Full Lesson - SSC GD English Section

Introduction

Sentence Correction means identifying grammar mistakes and choosing the correct form. SSC GD keeps this topic VERY EASY — usually based on basic grammar concepts.

Basic Sentence Structure

Exam Tip: If you already studied earlier chapters → this becomes super easy.

What Is Sentence Correction?

You are given a sentence with a mistake, and you must choose the correct version.

Incorrect Sentence

❌ He don't know me.

Correct Sentence

✔ He doesn't know me.

Most Important SSC GD Rules

1. Subject–Verb Agreement Errors

Very common in SSC GD exams.

❌ He go to school.

✔ He goes to school.

❌ They is late.

✔ They are late.

2. Tense Errors

Watch for incorrect tense usage.

❌ I have saw the movie.

✔ I have seen the movie.

❌ She is go to market.

✔ She is going to market.

3. Pronoun Errors

Pronouns must match properly.

❌ Everyone must bring their book.

✔ Everyone must bring his/her book.

❌ Me and Riya are friends.

✔ Riya and I are friends.

4. Article Errors

a / an / the mistakes are very common.

❌ He is a honest man.

✔ He is an honest man.

(h is silent)

❌ I saw a sun.

✔ I saw the sun.

5. Preposition Errors

Basic SSC mistakes with common prepositions.

❌ She is good in maths.

✔ She is good at maths.

❌ They discussed about the plan.

✔ They discussed the plan.

❌ He is married with her.

✔ He is married to her.

6. Double Negative Error

Two negatives cannot be used together.

❌ I don't know nothing.

✔ I don't know anything.

7. Misplaced Words

Word must be placed correctly for proper meaning.

❌ He almost drove the kids to school every day.

✔ He drove the kids to school almost every day.

8. Comparison Errors

When comparing, use proper words.

❌ She is better than me.

✔ She is better than I.

(but "me" is accepted informally — SSC prefers "I")

Examples (SSC GD Level)

The boys are playing.

The book is on the table.

Each student was given a pen.

I have finished my work.

She is interested in music.

Sample Questions (SSC GD Style)

1

Choose the correct sentence:

Options: (a) He don't like tea (b) He doesn't likes tea (c) He doesn't like tea (d) He not likes tea

View Answer

He doesn't like tea

2

Choose the correctly written sentence:

Options: (a) She is junior than me (b) She is junior to me (c) She is junior with me (d) She is junior from me

View Answer

She is junior to me

3

Find the correct sentence:

Options: (a) Everyone have a book (b) Everyone has a book (c) Everyone are having a book (d) Everyone having a book

View Answer

Everyone has a book

4

Choose the correct option:

Options: (a) I have saw the movie (b) I have seen the movie (c) I seen the movie (d) I have seeing the movie

View Answer

I have seen the movie

5

Correct sentence:

Options: (a) She is good in English (b) She is good at English (c) She is good on English (d) She is good by English

View Answer

She is good at English

Quick Recap

Verb must match subject

Tense must be correct

Articles: a/an/the → correct usage

Prepositions: good at, married to, discuss (no about)

Pronouns: Everyone → his/her

No double negatives

Word order matters

SSC GD asks basic, school-level corrections
