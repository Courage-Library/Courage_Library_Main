Tenses

SSC GD Self-Paced Course

Introduction

Tenses tell us the time of an action. SSC GD doesn't ask deep theory — only basic rules, error spotting, and correct usage.

Present

Current actions

Past

Completed actions

Future

Upcoming actions

Exam Focus: SSC GD focuses mostly on Simple & Continuous tenses.

Present Tense

Simple Present

Used for:

  • Daily habits
  • Universal truths
  • General facts

Examples:

He eats food.

The sun rises in the east.

SSC Rule: He/She/It → verb + s/es | I/You/They → verb (base form)

Present Continuous

Used for:

  • Actions happening right now

Examples:

She is reading.

They are playing.

Rule: am/is/are + verb + ing

Present Perfect

Used for:

  • Actions completed just now
  • Life experiences

Examples:

I have finished my work.

She has visited Delhi.

Rule: has/have + past participle (V3)

Present Perfect Continuous

Used for:

  • Action started in past and still going

Example:

They have been studying for 2 hours.

Past Tense

Simple Past

Used for:

  • Completed actions in the past

Examples:

He went to school.

I saw a movie yesterday.

Rule: Verb 2 (went, ate, wrote)

Past Continuous

Used for:

  • Ongoing action in past

Examples:

They were dancing.

I was sleeping.

Rule: was/were + verb + ing

Past Perfect

Used when:

  • One action happened before another action in the past

Example:

He had left before I reached.

Rule: had + V3

Future Tense

Simple Future

Used for:

  • Predictions
  • Future plans

Examples:

I will go tomorrow.

She will call you.

Rule: will + verb

Future Continuous

Used for:

  • Ongoing action in future

Example:

I will be sleeping at 10 PM.

Future Perfect

Used for:

  • Action completed before a future point

Example:

They will have completed the work by evening.

Most Important SSC GD Tense Rules (Very High Repeat)

Rule 1: He/She/It → verb + s/es

She writes. He reads.

Rule 2: Did + V1

Did never takes V2.
Wrong: Did he went?
Correct: Did he go?

Rule 3: Has/Have + V3

I have seen the movie. She has eaten food.

Rule 4: Was/Were + ing

I was reading. They were playing.

Rule 5: Time Words

Yesterday → Past | Now → Present Continuous | Tomorrow → Future
These help eliminate wrong options quickly.

Sample Questions (SSC GD Style)

1

Choose the correct form: She ____ to school every day.

Options: (a) go (b) goes (c) going (d) gone

View Answer

goes

2

Identify the tense: He is playing cricket.

Options: (a) Present Perfect (b) Present Continuous (c) Past Continuous (d) Simple Present

View Answer

Present Continuous

3

Choose the correct sentence:

Options: (a) He did not went. (b) He did not go. (c) He do not went. (d) He does not went.

View Answer

He did not go.

4

Fill in the blank: They ____ watching TV when I arrived.

Options: (a) was (b) were (c) are (d) will

View Answer

were

5

Correct form: I ____ finished my work.

Options: (a) has (b) have (c) am (d) had

View Answer

have

Quick Recap

Simple Present: daily habits → He plays

Present Continuous: now → He is playing

Simple Past: yesterday → He played

Past Continuous: He was playing

Simple Future: He will play

Did + V1 rule is most important

SSC GD mostly asks from:

Simple Present, Simple Past, Present/Past Continuous, Has/Have + V3
