Main Idea & Inference

Reading Comprehension - SSC GD English Section

Introduction

In Reading Comprehension, SSC GD often asks about Main Idea and Inference questions. These are very easy if you know what to look for.

Main Idea

Central theme of the passage

Inference

Logical conclusion from the passage

Exam Tip: SSC GD keeps them direct and simple — no deep analysis needed.

What Is "Main Idea"?

The central theme or overall message of the passage. It answers the question: "What is the passage mainly talking about?"

Where do you find the main idea?

The first line

The last line

Repeated keywords

Trick:

If 3–4 lines talk about trees, the main idea is importance of trees, NOT a small detail like "trees give shade."

What Is "Inference"?

Inference means a conclusion we can understand from the passage, even if it is not written directly. Not guesswork — logical understanding.

Example:

Passage: "If Riya studies daily, she will score good marks."

Inference → Riya is a hardworking student.

(It's not written, but clearly understood.)

How to Identify MAIN IDEA (Super Shortcut)

✔ Look at the FIRST line

Usually tells the topic.

✔ Look for repeated words

If "health" appears 4 times → the passage is about health.

✔ Ignore small examples

They are supporting details, not the main idea.

✔ Check the LAST line

It usually summarizes the thought.

How to Identify INFERENCE (Super Shortcut)

✔ It should not be EXACTLY written

It must be understood indirectly.

✔ It must be TRUE based on passage

Not imagination.

✔ It must be logical

If the passage says trees give oxygen → we can infer trees are important for life.

Example Passage (SSC GD Level)

PASSAGE

Regular exercise plays a major role in keeping the body healthy. It improves blood circulation, strengthens muscles, and reduces stress. Many doctors advise people to walk for at least 30 minutes every day. Even a simple routine can bring long-term health benefits.

1

Main Idea Question

What is the main idea of the passage?

Options: (a) Doctors give advice (b) Exercise is important for health (c) People walk every day (d) Muscles need strength

View Answer

Exercise is important for health

(Repeated focus = health benefits of exercise)

2

Inference Question

What can be inferred from the passage?

Options: (a) Only gym workout is useful (b) Exercise is not needed for young people (c) Walking daily can improve health (d) Stress cannot be reduced

View Answer

Walking daily can improve health

(Not directly written, but clearly understood)

3

Inference Question

What can be inferred about doctors?

Options: (a) They discourage walking (b) They know exercise helps health (c) They prefer running (d) They dislike exercise

View Answer

They know exercise helps health

(Doctors advising walking → they know its health benefits)

2nd Example Passage (One-Line Inference Style)

PASSAGE

Rohan always completes his homework on time and helps his classmates when they struggle.

Inference:

Rohan is responsible and helpful.

Main Idea:

Rohan's good habits.

Sample Questions (SSC GD Style)

1

Main idea of a passage about pollution:

Options: (a) Trees are green (b) Pollution harms health (c) Cars are fast (d) People are busy

View Answer

Pollution harms health

2

What can you infer?
Passage says: "Meera reads every night before sleeping."

Options: (a) Meera hates reading (b) Meera is regular in her reading habit (c) Meera sleeps late (d) Meera studies only on Sundays

View Answer

Meera is regular in her reading habit

3

Main idea question:
Passage about: "Advantages of drinking clean water."

View Answer

Clean water is important for health

4

Inference:
If passage says: "Animals are losing their homes due to cutting of forests."

View Answer

Deforestation harms wildlife

5

Main idea:
Passage about: "Hard work leads to success."

View Answer

Importance of hard work

Quick Recap

Main Idea

What the entire passage talks about

Found in → first line, last line, repeated keywords

Ignore small details

Inference

Logical conclusion

Not directly written but clearly understood

SSC GD passages are simple and factual

Key Point: No tricky reasoning → answers are straightforward
