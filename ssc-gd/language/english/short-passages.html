Short Passages

Reading Comprehension - SSC GD English Section

Introduction

Short Passages are the most scoring part of SSC GD English. Passages are very simple, usually 80–120 words, with direct questions.

No difficult vocabulary

No tricky inference

Just basic understanding

Direct fact-based questions

Exam Tip: If you can read English normally → 4/4 marks easy.

What Are Short Passages?

A small paragraph followed by 3–5 questions.

Questions are usually:

Direct fact-based

Word meaning

Main idea

True/false type

Synonym/antonym from the passage

Important: SSC does NOT give tough CAT-level comprehension. It's school-level English.

How to Solve Short Passages (Fast Strategy)

1. Read the questions BEFORE reading the passage

You know what to find → saves time.

2. Scan, don't read slowly

Look for keywords from questions.

3. Answers are directly inside the passage

SSC GD never asks out-of-passage questions.

4. No overthinking

If the line is written clearly → that is the answer.

5. Watch for synonyms

Sometimes, the exam changes the wording.

High-Frequency Comprehension Topics in SSC GD

Environment

Daily life

Education

Health

Social values

Science basics

Moral stories

Note: Nothing deep or abstract.

Example Short Passage (SSC GD Style)

PASSAGE

Ravi wakes up early every morning and goes for a walk in the nearby park. He believes that walking keeps him healthy and active. After returning home, he helps his mother with household chores before getting ready for school. Ravi always tries to manage his time well so that he can study, play, and still help his family.

1

Why does Ravi go for a walk?

Options: (a) To meet friends (b) To stay healthy (c) To pass time (d) To prepare for school

View Answer

To stay healthy

2

What does Ravi do after returning home?

Options: (a) Sleeps again (b) Goes to school (c) Helps his mother (d) Plays outside

View Answer

Helps his mother

3

Which word from the passage means "nearby"?

Options: (a) early (b) active (c) manage (d) nearby

View Answer

nearby

4

What does Ravi try to manage well?

Options: (a) Study time (b) Play time (c) His schedule (d) His sleep

View Answer

His schedule

5

The passage mainly talks about —

Options: (a) Ravi's exam (b) Ravi's hobbies (c) Ravi's daily routine (d) Ravi's school

View Answer

Ravi's daily routine

SSC GD Solved Passage (2nd Example)

PASSAGE

Trees play an important role in keeping our environment clean. They absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen which is essential for human life. Trees also provide shelter to birds and animals. Cutting down trees can lead to pollution and imbalance in nature.

1

What do trees absorb?

View Answer

carbon dioxide

2

What do trees release?

View Answer

oxygen

3

Cutting down trees leads to what?

View Answer

pollution / imbalance

4

Trees provide shelter to whom?

View Answer

birds and animals

5

Main idea of the passage:

View Answer

importance of trees

Quick Recap

Read questions first

Only direct answers → no guesswork

Very simple vocabulary in SSC GD

Identify main idea → usually in first or last line

Look for keywords

Practice makes you faster

Key Point: This section gives full marks with minimum effort.
