Reasoning Shortcuts & Common Traps for SSC CGL

Master Time-Saving Strategies and Avoid Common Mistakes

Verbal Reasoning

1. Analogies

Common Traps:

  • Confusing relationships (e.g., part-whole vs. synonym)
  • Ignoring word type or context

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Identify the relationship first (synonym, antonym, category, degree)
  • Eliminate options that don't match the relationship

2. Odd One Out

Common Traps:

  • Picking the word that seems "different" but actually belongs to a pattern
  • Ignoring multiple characteristics of the words

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Look for one clear logical criterion (function, category, meaning)
  • Compare all options before final selection

3. Series Completion

Common Traps:

  • Missing alternating or skip patterns
  • Overlooking sequences involving alphabets, numbers, or combined logic

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Check differences, gaps, or arithmetic/position patterns
  • Predict the next element logically before looking at options

4. Coding-Decoding

Common Traps:

  • Assuming random codes without detecting patterns
  • Ignoring case-sensitivity or position rules

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Identify letter/number shifts, position patterns, or substitution rules
  • Apply the same pattern to all options systematically

5. Blood Relations / Direction Sense / Ranking

Common Traps:

  • Misinterpreting relationships or directions
  • Ignoring relative positions (e.g., "to the left of" vs. "right of")

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Draw a quick diagram/tree for relations
  • Break problems step by step instead of solving mentally

6. Syllogisms / Statement & Conclusions / Cause & Effect / Course of Action / Decision Making

Common Traps:

  • Jumping to conclusions without logical verification
  • Confusing cause-effect direction or course of action priorities

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Use Venn diagrams for syllogisms
  • Analyze cause-effect direction carefully
  • Choose most logical and practical option in decision making

Non-Verbal Reasoning

1. Mirror & Water Images

Common Traps:

  • Confusing left-right and top-bottom flips
  • Forgetting letters that remain unchanged in mirror

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Memorize mirror-resistant letters: A, H, I, M, O, T, U, V, W, X, Y
  • Sketch or mentally flip for complex figures

2. Paper Folding & Cutting

Common Traps:

  • Ignoring folds or symmetry in multi-folded papers
  • Miscounting cuts

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Visualize folds step by step
  • Remember folds multiply cuts symmetrically

3. Figure Series Completion

Common Traps:

  • Missing rotations, reflections, or shading patterns
  • Assuming random sequences without logic

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Look for rotation, repetition, size, or shading patterns first
  • Eliminate options that don't follow the main pattern

4. Embedded Figures

Common Traps:

  • Distracted by extra lines or patterns
  • Overlooking rotated or partially hidden figures

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Focus on edges, vertices, and unique features
  • Scan systematically (top-to-bottom, left-to-right)

5. Counting Figures

Common Traps:

  • Double-counting overlapping/nested shapes
  • Missing small or hidden elements

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Divide complex figures into layers or sections
  • Count systematically: small → medium → large → combined

6. Cube & Dice

Common Traps:

  • Confusing adjacent and opposite faces
  • Forgetting rotation rules or painted faces in cut cubes

Time-Saving Tricks:

  • Use elimination method for opposite faces
  • For painted cubes, classify as corner, edge, or inner cubes
  • Memorize opposite faces sum rule for standard dice (1–6)

General Time-Saving Tips

  • Practice mental visualization instead of drawing every figure
  • Eliminate impossible options first to reduce choices
  • Focus on symmetry, rotation, and repetition — these repeat in most questions
  • Allocate fixed time per question (around 1–1.5 minutes)
  • Skip only if stuck; revisit later with fresh observation
