Spelling Errors & Commonly Confused Words for SSC CGL

Complete English Language Guide

1. Spelling Errors

Definition: A spelling error occurs when a word is written incorrectly. SSC often asks to identify the wrongly spelled word in a group of options.

Tips to Avoid Spelling Errors

Learn common SSC-tested words

Watch out for double letters → e.g., accommodate, committee

Beware of silent letters → e.g., receipt, knight

Common suffix mistakes → -able / -ible, -ance / -ence

High-Frequency SSC Spelling Traps

Correct Wrong
Accommodation Accomodation
Definitely Definately
Embarrass Embarass
Necessary Neccessary
Maintenance Maintainance
Separate Seperate
Occurrence Occurence
Privilege Privilage
Recommend Reccommend
Questionnaire Questionaire

2. Commonly Confused Words

Definition: Words that sound similar or are related in meaning but are used differently. SSC often tests these in error spotting and sentence correction.

High-Frequency Pairs in SSC

Word 1 Word 2 Correct Usage Example
Accept Except Accept → to agree; Except → excluding I accept your proposal. / Everyone came except John
Affect Effect Affect → verb; Effect → noun The weather affects my mood. / The medicine had a good effect
Advise Advice Advise → verb; Advice → noun I advise you to study. / Take my advice
Compliment Complement Compliment → praise; Complement → completes She gave me a compliment. / Blue complements white nicely
Elicit Illicit Elicit → to draw out; Illicit → illegal The teacher tried to elicit answers. / He was arrested for illicit trade
Principle Principal Principle → rule; Principal → head of school / main Honesty is a good principle. / The principal addressed the students
Stationary Stationery Stationary → not moving; Stationery → writing materials The car remained stationary. / Buy stationery for exams
Ensure Insure Ensure → make certain; Insure → cover with insurance Please ensure your safety. / He insured his car
Loose Lose Loose → not tight; Lose → misplace / fail The screw is loose. / Don't lose your ticket
Desert Dessert Desert → dry land; Dessert → sweet dish Sahara is a desert. / I had ice-cream for dessert

3. SSC Exam Tips

Underline tricky letters → double letters, silent letters

Check context → for commonly confused words

Learn top 100 commonly confused pairs → they repeat in SSC

Read carefully → SSC may give all correctly spelled options except one

4. SSC CGL Previous Year Questions

Q1. Identify the wrongly spelled word:

(a) Occurrence (b) Neccessary (c) Recommend (d) Privilege

Answer: (b) Neccessary

Q2. Choose the correct word: I will ___ your suggestion

(a) Accept (b) Except

Answer: (a) Accept

Q3. Choose the correct usage: Blue shirt ___ white pants

(a) Compliment (b) Complement

Answer: (b) Complement

Q4. Identify the wrongly spelled word:

(a) Embarrass (b) Maintenance (c) Questionnaire (d) Accomodate

Answer: (d) Accomodate

Q5. Choose the correct word: Sahara is a vast ___

(a) Desert (b) Dessert

Answer: (a) Desert
