Constitutional & Non-Constitutional Bodies

Reference: Lucent GK, NCERT Class 6–12

Election Commission of India (ECI)

Feature Description
Constitutional Status Article 324, Part XV
Composition (Post 1993) 1 Chief Election Commissioner (CEC) + 2 Election Commissioners
Appointment By the President
Tenure 6 years or until age 65 (whichever earlier)
Removal of CEC Like a SC Judge (by Parliament)
Functions Conduct elections for President, Vice President, Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, State Assemblies; supervise electoral rolls; Model Code of Conduct

Powers: Advisory, quasi-judicial, and administrative (under Representation of People Act, 1950 & 1951

Finance Commission

Feature Description
Constitutional Status Article 280
Composition 1 Chairman + 4 Members
Appointment By the President
Tenure As decided by the President (usually 5 years)
Function Recommend distribution of net tax revenue between Union and States; principles of grants-in-aid to states; any other financial matter referred by the President

Most Recent: 15th Finance Commission Chair: N.K. Singh)( for 2021–26

Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) & State Public Service Commissions (SPSC)

Feature UPSC State PSC
Constitutional Article Art. 315–323 Art. 315–323
Appointment By President By Governor
Composition 1 Chairman + Members (as decided by President/Governor)
Tenure 6 years or up to age 65 (UPSC); 62 (State PSC)
Function Conduct exams (e.g. IAS, IPS); advise govt on promotions, appointments, transfers Same at state level for Group A, B posts
Removal By President (for UPSC) on grounds of misbehavior, proved by SC inquiry

Reports of UPSC must be laid before Parliament; State PSC before State Legislature

NITI Aayog (National Institution for Transforming India)

Feature Description
Formation 1 January 2015 (replaced Planning Commission)
Nature Non-constitutional, non-statutory body
Chairperson Prime Minister
Vice Chairperson Appointed by PM
Members Full-time, part-time, ex-officio (CMs, LGs)
CEO Appointed by PM
Functions Policy think tank, cooperative federalism, bottom-up planning, strategic input for development

It does not allocate funds (unlike Planning Commission); acts as advisory and directional body

National Human Rights Commission (NHRC)

Feature Description
Established 1993 under Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993
Nature Statutory body (not constitutional)
Composition Chairperson (retired CJI) + 4 members + ex-officio members
Appointment By President on recommendation of committee headed by PM
Tenure 5 years or up to age 70
Function Inquire into human rights violations, recommend compensation, suggest reforms, review safeguards

Limitations: Recommendations not binding; cannot punish offenders

CVC, CBI, Lokpal & Lokayuktas

Central Vigilance Commission (CVC)

Feature Description
Status Statutory body (CVC Act, 2003)
Established 1964 (recommended by Santhanam Committee)
Composition Central Vigilance Commissioner + 2 Vigilance Commissioners
Appointment By President on recommendation of Committee
Function Supervises vigilance in central government, advises CBI, anti-corruption activities

Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI)

Feature Description
Established 1963 (via resolution by Home Ministry)
Parent Body Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances & Pensions
Legal Backing DSPE Act, 1946
Function Investigates corruption, economic & special crimes, inter-state crimes, etc.

Lokpal

Feature Description
Act Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 2013
Status Statutory body
Composition Chairperson (retired CJI/judge) + max 8 members
Jurisdiction PM (with safeguards), ministers, MPs, govt servants
Appointment President on recommendation of selection committee
First Lokpal Justice Pinaki Chandra Ghose (appointed in 2019)

Lokayukta

Feature Description
State-Level Body Appointed by Governor
Coverage State CM, Ministers, and public officials
Status Statutory body (created by individual states)

Note: Every state is expected to create Lokayukta under Lokpal Act, 2013 — implementation varies
