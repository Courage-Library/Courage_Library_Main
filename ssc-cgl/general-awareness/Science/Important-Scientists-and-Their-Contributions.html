Important Scientists and Their Contributions

Reference: Lucent GK, NCERT Class 6–12

A. Physics

Scientist Contribution Significance
Isaac Newton Laws of Motion, Law of Gravitation Foundation of classical mechanics
Albert Einstein Theory of Relativity, Photoelectric Effect Modern physics, Nobel in 1921
Galileo Galilei Laws of Falling Bodies, Telescope improvements Father of Modern Science
James Clerk Maxwell Electromagnetic Theory Unified electricity and magnetism
Michael Faraday Electromagnetic Induction Basis of electric motors and generators
Wilhelm Roentgen Discovery of X-rays First Nobel Prize in Physics (1901)
Heinrich Hertz Proof of existence of electromagnetic waves Unit of frequency named after him (Hz)
Ernest Rutherford Nuclear model of atom, discovered proton Father of nuclear physics
Niels Bohr Bohr's model of atom Electron energy levels in shells
C.V. Raman Raman Effect (scattering of light) First Indian Nobel Laureate in Physics (1930)

B. Chemistry

Scientist Contribution Significance
Dmitri Mendeleev Periodic Table (by atomic weight) Foundation of modern periodic classification
Henry Moseley Modern Periodic Law (by atomic number) Corrected Mendeleev's version
Antoine Lavoisier Law of Conservation of Mass Father of Modern Chemistry
John Dalton Atomic Theory Introduced concept of atoms as indivisible
J.J. Thomson Discovery of Electron Plum pudding model of atom
James Chadwick Discovery of Neutron Completed basic structure of atom
Marie Curie Discovery of Radium and Polonium First person to win Nobel in two sciences
Linus Pauling Nature of chemical bond Nobel Prize in Chemistry
Robert Bunsen Invented Bunsen burner Laboratory heating tool

C. Biology & Medicine

Scientist Contribution Significance
Charles Darwin Theory of Evolution by Natural Selection Revolutionized understanding of life
Gregor Mendel Laws of Inheritance (Genetics) Father of Genetics
Louis Pasteur Germ Theory, Pasteurization Developed first vaccine for rabies
Alexander Fleming Discovery of Penicillin First antibiotic, revolutionized medicine
Edward Jenner First smallpox vaccine Father of Immunology
Robert Hooke Discovered cell (in cork) Coined the term "cell"
Watson & Crick DNA Double Helix Model Explained the structure of genetic material
Frederick Banting Discovery of Insulin Treatment for diabetes
William Harvey Blood circulation in humans Laid foundation for cardiovascular study
Hippocrates Father of Medicine Ancient Greek physician

D. Mathematics & Astronomy

Scientist Contribution Significance
Aryabhata Calculated value of π, Heliocentric theory Ancient Indian mathematician-astronomer
Bhaskara II Algebra, calculus concepts Introduced concepts of zero, infinity
Euclid Elements of Geometry Father of Geometry
Pythagoras Pythagoras Theorem Fundamental geometric principle
Johannes Kepler Laws of Planetary Motion Planetary orbits are elliptical
Nicolaus Copernicus Heliocentric model of solar system Sun-centered theory of universe
Tycho Brahe Precise astronomical observations Laid groundwork for Kepler

E. Indian Scientists (Important for SSC CGL)

Scientist Contribution Field
C.V. Raman Raman Effect Physics
Homi J. Bhabha Nuclear Science in India Physics
Vikram Sarabhai Father of Indian Space Program (ISRO) Space Science
A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Missile & space tech, former President Aerospace
Meghnad Saha Saha Ionization Equation (stellar spectroscopy) Astrophysics
Jagadish Chandra Bose Wireless telegraphy, plant physiology Physics, Biology
Satyendra Nath Bose Bose-Einstein statistics Quantum Physics
Har Gobind Khorana Genetic Code, DNA synthesis Biology
Venkatraman Ramakrishnan Ribosome structure (Nobel Prize in Chemistry) Molecular Biology
