Metals and Non-Metals

Reference: NCERT Class 10, Lucent GK

1. Physical and Chemical Properties

Property Metals Non-Metals
Appearance Lustrous (shiny) Dull (except iodine)
State Solid (except mercury) Solid/gas (except bromine - liquid)
Malleability Malleable (can be beaten into sheets) Non-malleable (brittle if solid)
Ductility Ductile (drawn into wires) Not ductile
Conductivity Good conductor of heat and electricity Poor conductor (except graphite)
Sonority Sonorous (produce sound when struck) Non-sonorous
Melting/ Boiling Point Generally high Generally low (except diamond)
Nature (Chemical) Electropositive, form basic oxides Electronegative, form acidic/neutral oxides

Example:
Metals → Iron (Fe), Copper (Cu), Zinc (Zn), Aluminium (Al)
Non-Metals → Sulphur (S), Oxygen (O₂), Carbon (C), Phosphorus (P)

2. Reactivity Series

A list of metals arranged in order of their reactivity from highest to lowest:

Reactivity Level Metals
Highly Reactive K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al
Moderately Reactive Zn > Fe > Pb
Less Reactive Cu > Hg > Ag > Au > Pt

Key Points:
• More reactive metals displace less reactive ones from their compounds.
• Reactivity determines how metals are extracted from ores.

Order of Reactivity (Mnemonic):
K Na Ca Mg Al Zn Fe Pb (H) Cu Hg Ag Au Pt

3. Alloys and Corrosion

Alloys

A homogeneous mixture of two or more metals or a metal with a non-metal.

Purpose: To improve hardness, strength, corrosion resistance.

Alloy Composition Use
Bronze Copper + Tin Medals, statues
Brass Copper + Zinc Electrical fittings, decorative items
Steel Iron + Carbon Construction, tools
Stainless Steel Iron + Chromium + Nickel Cutlery, utensils
Solder Lead + Tin Joining electric wires
Duralumin Aluminium + Copper + Magnesium Aircraft parts

Corrosion

Gradual destruction of metals due to reaction with air and moisture.

Metal Affected Name of Corrosion Reaction Example
Iron Rusting Fe + H₂O + O₂ → Fe₂O₃·xH₂O (Rust)
Silver Tarnishing Ag + H₂S → Ag₂S (black coating)
Copper Green coating Cu + CO₂ + H₂O → CuCO₃·Cu(OH)₂

Prevention Methods:
• Painting, greasing, oiling
• Galvanization (zinc coating)
• Alloying (e.g., stainless steel)
• Electroplating

4. Quick Facts

  • • Gold is the most malleable and ductile metal (1g can be drawn into 2km wire)
  • • Mercury is the only metal liquid at room temperature
  • • Graphite conducts electricity due to delocalized electrons between layers
  • • Aluminium is the most abundant metal in Earth's crust (8% by weight)
  • • Gallium melts in your hand (melting point 29.8°C)
