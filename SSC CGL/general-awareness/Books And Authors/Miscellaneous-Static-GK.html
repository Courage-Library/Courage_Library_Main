1. Booker Prize Winners (with Indian Connection)

Author Book Title Prize Year Remark
Arundhati Roy The God of Small Things 1997 First Indian woman to win the Booker Prize
Geetanjali Shree Tomb of Sand (Ret Samadhi) 2022 First Hindi novel to win International Booker
Kiran Desai The Inheritance of Loss 2006 Daughter of Anita Desai; diaspora narrative
Aravind Adiga The White Tiger 2008 Debut novel, critical of social divide in India
Salman Rushdie Midnight's Children 1981 Won Booker Prize & Booker of Bookers
V.S. Naipaul In a Free State 1971 Of Indian origin, born in Trinidad

SSC Tip: Match the author with the book and year of Booker Prize.

3. Authors of the Indian Constitution & Related Works

Name Contribution Book Title / Note
Dr. B.R. Ambedkar Chairman of Drafting Committee Annihilation of Caste (1936)
B.N. Rau Constitutional Advisor Draft framework before constituent assembly
K.M. Munshi Member, Drafting Committee Known for contribution to fundamental duties
Rajendra Prasad President of the Constituent Assembly Later became India's first President

SSC Tips:

  • "Father of Indian Constitution" → Dr. B.R. Ambedkar
  • "Drafted the first constitutional framework" → B.N. Rau
  • "Presided over Constitution making" → Rajendra Prasad

Quick SSC Revision Points

  • Tomb of Sand = Hindi original: Ret Samadhi → Translated by Daisy Rockwell
  • Booker Prize vs International Booker: Know the difference
  • Annihilation of Caste = Powerful text by Ambedkar against social discrimination
  • Guru Granth Sahib: Compiled by Guru Arjan Dev
  • Tripitaka: Sacred Buddhist scriptures in Pali
