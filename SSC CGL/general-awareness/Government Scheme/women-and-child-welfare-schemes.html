Women and Child Welfare Schemes

Government initiatives for empowerment and development of women and children

1. Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao (BBBP)

Feature Details
Objective Improve Child Sex Ratio and promote girl child education
Focus Areas Prevention of gender-biased sex selection & Education & empowerment of girl children
Strategy Awareness generation, advocacy, and multi-sectoral action
Implementation Convergent effort of 3 ministries: Women & Child Development (WCD), Health & Family Welfare (MoHFW), Education (MoE)
Launch Year 2015
Target Group All Indian districts (initially launched in 100 districts)

SSC Tip: BBBP = Girl child welfare = 3 ministries involved (WCD, Health, Education) = Launched 2015

2. Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)

Feature Details
Objective Provide financial assistance to pregnant and lactating women
Benefit ₹5,000 in 3 installments for first live birth
Purpose Partial wage compensation and to ensure proper nutrition
Target Group Women (18+ years) not in regular employment under central/state government
Mode of Transfer Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to Aadhaar-linked bank accounts
Ministry Ministry of Women & Child Development
Launch Year 2017

SSC Tip: PMMVY = ₹5,000 maternity benefit = First live birth only = DBT transfer = WCD Ministry

3. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

Feature Details
Objective Encourage savings for the education and marriage of girl child
Account Opening Age Girl child below 10 years
Account Maturity 21 years or upon marriage after age 18
Interest Rate Higher than most saving schemes (subject to periodic revision)
Tax Benefit Exempt under EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) category under Section 80C
Minimum Deposit ₹250/year; Maximum ₹1.5 lakh/year
Operated By Post Offices and Scheduled Commercial Banks
Launch Year 2015

SSC Tip: SSY = Girl child savings scheme = Below 10 years age = EEE tax benefit = Post offices & banks

4. POSHAN Abhiyan (National Nutrition Mission)

Feature Details
Objective Achieve "Malnutrition-free India" by improving nutritional outcomes
Target Group Pregnant women, lactating mothers, children (0-6 yrs), and adolescent girls
Key Focus Area The "1,000-day window" - from pregnancy to child's age of 2
Components ICT-based real-time monitoring, convergence with various ministries
Strategy Community-based events, growth monitoring, nutrition awareness
Ministry Ministry of Women & Child Development
Launch Year 2018

SSC Tip: POSHAN Abhiyan = Nutrition mission = 1,000-day window = WCD Ministry = Launched 2018
