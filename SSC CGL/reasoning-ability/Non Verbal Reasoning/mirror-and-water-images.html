Mirror and Water Images for SSC CGL

Master Reflection and Reversal Logic

1. Introduction

Mirror and Water Images are a key part of SSC CGL Non-Verbal Reasoning. These questions test your ability to visualize reflections and reversals.

These questions are scoring because with practice, you can quickly identify patterns and orientations.

2. Definition

Mirror Image: When an object or figure is reflected in a mirror, the left and right sides are swapped.
Water Image: When an object or figure is reflected in water, the top and bottom are reversed.

Example: Original: ABC

Mirror Image: CBA (left-right reversed)

Water Image: Top-bottom flipped representation

3. Types of Mirror and Water Image Questions

Mirror Image Questions:

Object/figure is reflected in a vertical mirror.

Example: Original PQR → Mirror Image RQP

Water Image Questions:

Object/figure reflected in water placed below it.

Example: Original UP → Water Image PU

Combination Questions:

May involve mirror + rotation or water + rotation.

Alphabet and Number Images:

5. Strategy to Approach Mirror and Water Image Questions

  • Visualize carefully: Mentally or sketch the figure
  • Remember flip rules: Mirror → Left ↔ Right, Water → Top ↔ Bottom
  • Focus on symmetry: Letters like A, H, M, T remain the same in mirror
  • Practice alphabets, numbers, and figures: Familiarity reduces errors
  • Check orientation: Always verify if it's vertical or horizontal reflection

6. Quick Recap

  • Mirror Image → left-right reversal
  • Water Image → top-bottom reversal
  • Some letters and numbers remain unchanged due to symmetry
  • Practice improves accuracy and speed
