Relative Speed is used when two objects move towards or away from each other.

It helps in solving problems related to trains, cars, walking persons, boats, etc.

Relative speed = difference or sum of speeds of two moving objects, depending on their direction.

Used when:

  • ‚Ä¢ Two objects move in the same direction
  • ‚Ä¢ Two objects move towards each other (opposite direction)
  • ‚Ä¢ One object is chasing another

2Ô∏è‚É£ Cases of Relative Speed

‚úî Case 1: Same Direction

When two objects move in same direction:

Relative Speed = Faster speed - Slower speed

üëâ Because the faster one is catching up slowly.

‚úî Case 2: Opposite Direction

When two objects move in opposite direction:

Relative Speed = Speed 1 + Speed 2

üëâ Because the distance between them reduces quickly.

‚úî Case 3: One Chasing Another

Same direction ‚Üí difference of speeds

Opposite direction ‚Üí sum of speeds

‚û° Time Taken to Catch or Meet

Time = Distance / Relative Speed

‚û° Distance Covered During Meeting
