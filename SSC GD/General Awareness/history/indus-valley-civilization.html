Indus Valley Civilization

Essential Concepts for SSC GD Exams

1. Introduction

Indus Valley Civilization refers to the ancient urban culture that developed around the Indus River and its tributaries. It is known for planned cities, advanced drainage systems, multi-storey houses, trade & crafts, and highly organized society.

Simple Definition

Indus Valley Civilization was an ancient, well-organized urban civilization that existed around the Indus River, famous for its planned cities and advanced drainage systems.

2. Geographical Spread

The civilization spread across India, Pakistan, and parts of Afghanistan.

Major Rivers

  • Indus
  • Ravi
  • Saraswati (Ghaggar-Hakra)
  • Sutlej

Countries Covered

  • India
  • Pakistan
  • Parts of Afghanistan

4. Town Planning & Architecture

One of the best planned civilizations in ancient world.

Planned Streets

  • Streets in grid pattern
  • Roads intersect at right angles

Houses

  • Made of burnt bricks
  • Had bathrooms, wells, courtyards, toilets

Public Buildings

  • Great Bath (Mohenjo-daro)
  • Granaries (Harappa, Mohenjo-daro)

Advanced Drainage System

  • Covered drains
  • Connected to every house
  • Main drain had inspection holes

Memory Trick:

"SPHD → Streets, Public buildings, Houses, Drains"

5. Political System

  • No evidence of kings or monarchy
  • Likely ruled by merchants or city councils
  • Society was peaceful, no large weapons found

6. Society & Social Life

Aspect Features
Class system Rich merchants + common people
Food Wheat, barley, milk, meat, dates
Dress Cotton clothes (first use of cotton)
Ornaments Beads, bangles, necklaces
Toys Terracotta toys, carts
Script Pictographic (still undeciphered)

Very Important:

Harappans were the first to use cotton in the world.

7. Economy of Indus Valley

Agriculture

Main Crops:

  • Wheat
  • Barley
  • Cotton
  • Dates
  • Mustard

Tools:

Wooden plough (Kalibangan)

Trade

  • Inland & overseas trade
  • Lothal → major port town
  • Trade with Mesopotamia (Sumer)
  • Standardized weights & measures

Crafts

  • Pottery
  • Bead making (Chanhu Daro)
  • Metal work
  • Terracotta figurines

8. Religion

Harappan religion was non-Vedic.

Key Features

  • Worship of Mother Goddess
  • Worship of Pashupati (Proto-Shiva)
  • Worship of animals
  • Sacred symbols: Swastika, Peepal tree

Absent Features

  • No temples found
  • No evidence of worship of gods like Indra or Agni (Vedic gods)

Memory Trick:

"MPPPS → Mother Goddess, Pashupati, Peepal, Proto-Shiva, Symbols"

9. Seals & Scripts

Seals

  • Made of steatite
  • Used for trade, identity
  • Famous seals: Pashupati seal, Unicorn seal

Script

  • Undeciphered
  • Right-to-left writing
  • Mostly pictographic symbols

10. Art & Culture

Sculpture

  • Bronze Dancing Girl (Mohenjo-daro)
  • Bearded Priest (Mohenjo-daro)

Pottery

Red pottery with black designs

Toys

  • Cart toys
  • Animal toys
  • Dice & games

12. Decline of Indus Valley Civilization

Many theories; no single reason confirmed.

Possible Reasons

  • Climate change
  • Floods
  • River shifting
  • Deforestation
  • Invasion theory (less accepted now)
  • Disease outbreak
