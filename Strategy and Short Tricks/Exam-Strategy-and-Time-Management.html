Exam Strategy & Time Management

Mastering SSC CGL Quantitative Aptitude

1. Understanding the SSC CGL Quant Pattern

Exam Structure

  • Total Questions: 25 (Quant Section)
  • Marks: 50 (2 marks each)
  • Negative Marking: 0.50 marks

Topics Weightage

Arithmetic 50-60%
Algebra, Geometry, Trigonometry 30-35%
DI, Miscellaneous 10-15%

2. Smart Topic Prioritization

Category Topics Priority Level
High Weightage Percentage, Profit-Loss, SI-CI, Time-Speed-Distance, Time-Work
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Medium Weightage Algebra, Geometry, Mensuration
⭐⭐⭐
Low but Tricky Trigonometry, Coordinate Geometry, DI
⭐⭐

3. Time Management During Exam

Total Time: 60 minutes (Tier-I)

Easy (10 Qs) 15 min
Moderate (10 Qs) 25 min
Difficult (5 Qs) 15 min
Review 5 min

Pro Tip

Skip and mark — never get stuck on one question

Maintain steady pace throughout the exam

Return to skipped questions in review time

4. Practice Plan (Before Exam)

Day Type Activity Duration
Mon–Fri 2–3 Topics + Daily Practice Set 2-3 hours

Sunday Error Analysis + Revision 2 hours

Weekly Focus Areas

Week 1-2: Arithmetic & Algebra

Week 3-4: Geometry & Trigonometry

Week 5-6: Advanced Topics & Mixed Practice

Week 7-8: Full Mock Tests & Revision

6. Last 10 Days Before Exam

✅ Do's

  • Attempt 5–7 full mocks
  • Focus only on weak areas
  • Don't learn new formulas—revise old ones
  • Practice mental calculation daily for 15 mins

❌ Don'ts

  • Don't start new topics
  • Avoid last-minute formula memorization
  • Don't change your strategy drastically
  • Avoid excessive study hours

7. Bonus Tips

Exam Techniques

  • Use the elimination method for complex options
  • Approximation > Exact calculation (when options differ widely)
  • Maintain accuracy over attempts — 20 correct is better than 25 with 4 wrong

Health & Wellness

  • Sleep well before exam
  • Take short breaks during long study hours
  • Stay hydrated and eat healthy
  • Practice relaxation techniques

Quick Reference

Time per Question: ~2.4 minutes

Target Accuracy: 85%+

Revision Frequency: Daily 30 mins

Mock Tests: Weekly 2-3
