Sentence Correction Strategies for SSC CGL

Complete Error Detection & Correction Guide

1. What is Sentence Correction?

Sentence Correction questions ask you to identify and correct errors in a sentence or choose the most appropriate version from the options.

Tests: Grammar, Vocabulary, Idioms, Sentence Logic

Often appears with 4–5 options; one is correct, others have errors

Example:

He don't like to play football

Corrected: He does not like to play football

2. Strategy for Sentence Correction

1. Read the Full Sentence Carefully

  • Understand the meaning, logic, and context before choosing an answer

2. Check Grammar First

  • Tense, Subject-Verb Agreement, Articles, Pronouns, Prepositions, Conjunctions

3. Check Vocabulary & Idiomatic Usage

  • Ensure words and phrases are used correctly
  • Example: Kick the bucket happily → Wrong usage; kick the bucket = die

4. Look for Logical Errors

  • Even if grammatically correct, sentence may not make sense

5. Eliminate Clearly Wrong Options

  • Usually 1–2 options are grammatically incorrect → rule them out first

3. Common Patterns Tested in SSC

Pattern Strategy Example
Subject-Verb Agreement Ensure verb matches subject She go to school → She goes to school
Tense Match time frame He go yesterday → He went yesterday
Pronouns Correct case, number, gender Every student must submit their report → his/her report
Articles Correct usage of a, an, the I saw a elephant → an elephant
Prepositions Correct idiomatic preposition She is good in math → at math
Conjunctions Correct connectors I will go but if it rains → remove but
Vocabulary Correct word choice He is very apt to mistakes → He is very prone to mistakes
Idioms Proper idiomatic usage He spilled the beans = revealed secret

4. Sample Sentence Correction

Q1. Choose the correct sentence:

1. He don't like apples

2. He does not likes apples

3. He does not like apples

4. He not likes apples

Answer: 3. He does not like apples

Q2. Choose the correct sentence:

1. She is good in painting

2. She is good at painting

3. She is good on painting

4. She is good for painting

Answer: 2. She is good at painting

Q3. Choose the correct sentence:

1. I saw a eagle flying

2. I saw an eagle flying

3. I saw the eagle flying

4. I saw eagle flying

Answer: 2. I saw an eagle flying

Q4. Choose the correct sentence:

1. He was very happy, he won the lottery

2. He was very happy because he won the lottery

3. He was very happy, because he won the lottery

4. He was very happy but he won the lottery

Answer: 2. He was very happy because he won the lottery

Q5. Choose the correct sentence:

1. Every student must submit their homework

2. Every student must submit his/her homework

3. Every student must submit their homeworks

4. Every student must submit him/her homework

Answer: 2. Every student must submit his/her homework

5. SSC Exam Tips

Always read all options fully before choosing

Check grammar first, then logic

Eliminate obviously wrong options → saves time

Pay attention to tense consistency, subject-verb agreement, and idiomatic usage

Practice previous 10–15 years' SSC sentence correction questions → patterns repeat

For tricky options, ask: Does it sound correct and logical?
