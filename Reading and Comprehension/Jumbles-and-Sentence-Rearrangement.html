Para Jumbles & Sentence Rearrangement for SSC CGL

Complete Strategy & Practice Guide

1. What are Para Jumbles?

Para Jumbles are a set of sentences given in random order.

Your task: rearrange them to form a logical and coherent paragraph.

SSC tests logical reasoning, comprehension, and grammar through this

Usually 4–6 sentences are given

Example:

Sentences:

A. He reached the station late

B. He missed the train

C. He woke up late that morning

Correct Order: C → A → B

2. Strategy for Para Jumbles

Step 1: Identify the Opening Sentence

  • Usually introduces the topic or subject
  • Look for:
    • Subject of first action
    • Time or event indicators

Step 2: Identify the Closing Sentence

  • Usually concludes the event or gives result
  • Look for:
    • Words like "finally," "thus," "hence"

Step 3: Look for Connectors

  • Words like: but, because, however, although, then, after that
  • Help establish logical links

Step 4: Identify Sequence of Events

  • Check chronological order if action/event-based

Step 5: Check Pronouns & References

  • Words like he, she, they, it → refer to previous sentence
  • Ensures coherence

3. Common SSC Patterns

Pattern Tips
Event-based story Arrange sentences chronologically
Cause-Effect Identify cause first, then effect
Opinion + Reason Identify main idea sentence, then supporting reason
Dialogue-based Look for who speaks first, then response
Complex paragraph Identify first, last, then connect middle sentences logically

4. Sample Para Jumble

Sentences:

A. She decided to take an umbrella

B. It was raining heavily outside

C. She opened the door to leave

D. She reached the office safely

Solution Order: B → A → C → D

Explanation:

  • B → introduces the situation (raining heavily)
  • A → reaction (take an umbrella)
  • C → action (opens the door)
  • D → conclusion (reaches office safely)

5. Another Sample (Opinion + Reason)

Sentences:

A. Exercise is essential for a healthy life

B. It improves blood circulation and keeps the body fit

C. Many people ignore physical activity

D. Therefore, everyone should include it in their daily routine

Solution Order: C → A → B → D

Explanation:

  • C → problem statement (people ignore activity)
  • A → main idea (exercise is essential)
  • B → supporting reason
  • D → conclusion/advice

6. SSC Exam Tips

Identify opening & closing sentences first → reduces options

Look for connectors → logical flow is key

Check pronoun references → ensures coherence

For time sequence, check tense markers: then, next, after, finally

Practice past 5–10 years' SSC para jumbles → patterns repeat frequently

7. Practice Set

Sentences:

A. He studied hard for the exam

B. He passed with flying colors

C. Exams were approaching fast

D. He revised all chapters thoroughly

Task: Arrange in correct order

Click for Answer

C → A → D → B

Explanation:

  • C → introduces urgency (exams approaching)
  • A → first action (studied hard)
  • D → supporting action (revised all chapters)
  • B → result (passed with flying colors)
