The Staff Selection Commission – Combined Graduate Level (SSC CGL) exam is one of the most prestigious government exams in India. Every year, lakhs of aspirants apply for SSC CGL to secure posts in ministries, departments, and organizations under the Government of India.

If you are planning to appear for SSC CGL 2025, this guide will give you a complete roadmap: from eligibility to exam pattern, detailed syllabus, important dates, and expert preparation tips.

What is SSC CGL?

The SSC CGL exam recruits candidates for Group B and Group C posts such as:

  • Income Tax Inspector
  • Assistant Section Officer
  • Sub-Inspector (CBI, NIA)
  • Auditor (CAG)
  • Preventive Officer (Customs)

It offers:

Job security

Stable career with government benefits

Government perks

Allowances, pensions, and other benefits

Career growth

Promotion opportunities and stability

SSC CGL 2025 Eligibility

To appear for SSC CGL 2025, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Nationality: Indian citizen (or as per official SSC rules).
  • Educational Qualification: Graduation in any discipline from a recognized university.
  • Age Limit: 18–32 years (with relaxation for SC/ST/OBC/Ex-Servicemen).
Pro Tip: Always check the official SSC notification for post-wise qualifications and age limits as they may vary for different positions.

SSC CGL 2025 Exam Pattern

The exam is conducted in two tiers:

Tier Mode Subjects Duration Marks
Tier I Online (CBT) Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, General Awareness, English 1 hour 200
Tier II Online (CBT) Quant, English, Statistics, General Studies (Finance & Economics) Varies 400+
Both tiers are computer-based. Tier-I is qualifying, while Tier-II is merit-based. The final selection depends on Tier-II performance and document verification.
