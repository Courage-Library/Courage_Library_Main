SSC CGL Previous Year Cutoff Analysis – Understand the Cutoff Trends

Analyze cutoff trends to set realistic preparation goals and build a winning strategy for SSC CGL 2025.

Published: May 25, 2024 | 10 min read | By Courage Library Experts
Home Blog Cutoff Analysis
3.2K

Every SSC CGL aspirant wants to know one thing before starting preparation: "What is the safe score to target?" The answer lies in analyzing the SSC CGL previous year cutoffs. By studying cutoff trends, you not only understand the level of competition but also set realistic preparation goals.

In this article, we will break down the SSC CGL cutoff trends, discuss factors affecting them, and explain how you can use this data to build a winning preparation strategy.

What is the SSC CGL Cutoff?

The cutoff is the minimum score required to qualify for the next stage of the exam. SSC releases separate cutoffs for different posts, categories, and tiers.

Tier-I Cutoff

Decides who moves to Tier-II

Tier-II Cutoff

Post-wise and category-wise shortlisting

Final Cutoff

Based on combined marks of all tiers

Knowing cutoffs is crucial because it helps you set a score benchmark rather than preparing blindly.

SSC CGL Previous Year Cutoff (Tier-I) – General Trend

Here's a snapshot of the last few years' Tier-I cutoffs for the General category:

Year Cutoff (General) Trend Remarks
2023 150–155 High Moderate paper, high competition
2022 130–135 Low Paper was slightly tougher
2021 147–153 High Similar to 2023 trend
2020 147 Stable Post-COVID attempt surge
2019 147 Stable Standard difficulty
Observation: The cutoff usually stays between 130–155 marks depending on paper difficulty and number of applicants.

Tier-II Cutoff Trends

Tier-II is the real game-changer because it carries maximum weightage. Some past year highlights:

High Cutoff Posts

  • AAO & JSO posts usually have higher cutoff due to specialization
  • Cutoffs vary widely between UR, OBC, SC, ST, and EWS categories

Cutoff Examples

  • In SSC CGL 2022, UR cutoff for most posts was around 510–520 combined marks
  • In SSC CGL 2021, it was slightly lower, around 480–490 marks

Factors Affecting SSC CGL Cutoff

Competition Factors

  • Number of Applicants – More applicants → Higher cutoff
  • Vacancies Released – More vacancies → Lower cutoff

Exam Factors

  • Exam Difficulty – Tough paper → Lower cutoff, Easy paper → Higher cutoff
  • Normalization Process – Marks are normalized across multiple shifts
Example: If Tier-I is tough, cutoff can drop by 10–15 marks.

How to Use Cutoff Analysis in Preparation

Instead of randomly aiming for high scores, use cutoffs to set practical goals:

Tier-I Strategy

  • Always aim for 20–30 marks above the expected cutoff
  • If cutoff is 140, target 160–170

Tier-II Strategy

  • Focus on strong accuracy in Quant & English
  • Set a goal of 550+ combined marks to stay safe

Time Management

  • Balance subjects according to weightage

Mock Tests

  • Compare your scores with previous year cutoffs to measure readiness

Safe Score to Target for SSC CGL 2025

Based on past data and increasing competition:

Tier-I

Target 160+ marks

Tier-II

Aim for 370–380+ marks

Overall

550–570+ marks for UR candidates

Remember, cutoffs for reserved categories will be slightly lower, but targeting higher ensures safety.

Conclusion

The SSC CGL cutoff analysis proves that while the competition is tough, having a clear score benchmark makes preparation much more effective. Don't prepare blindly—analyze trends, set realistic targets, and stick to your plan.
