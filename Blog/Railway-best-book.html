RAILWAY EXAM GUIDE

Best Books for Railway Exams

Recommended resources for each section of railway exams like RRB NTPC, Group D, ALP, and JE.

10 min read | 1.8K views | By Railway Experts
Home Blog Books & Resources
1.8K

Preparing for Railway exams like RRB NTPC, RRB Group D, ALP, and JE requires not just hard work but also the right resources. With lakhs of candidates applying every year, the competition is intense.

The key to success lies in choosing a few high-quality books and revising them multiple times instead of wasting time on too many sources. This article lists the best books for Railway exams, subject-wise, to help you prepare smarter.

1. Best Books for Mathematics

Mathematics plays a major role in RRB NTPC, RRB Group D, ALP, and JE exams. Strong basics with good speed are crucial.

Quantitative Aptitude

R.S. Aggarwal

Fast Track Objective Arithmetic

Rajesh Verma

NCERT Mathematics

Class 6-10

Tip: Start with NCERTs for basics, then move to Aggarwal for practice, and Verma for shortcuts.

2. Best Books for General Intelligence & Reasoning

Reasoning is a scoring subject since it doesn't involve lengthy calculations. Regular practice helps in accuracy.

Verbal & Non-Verbal Reasoning

R.S. Aggarwal

Analytical Reasoning

M.K. Pandey

PYQ Compilations

Kiran/Arihant

Tip: Focus more on puzzles, seating arrangements, and coding-decoding as they are frequently repeated.

3. Best Books for General Science

Railway exams test candidates on Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (10th standard level).

Lucent's General Science

Compact book covering all basics

NCERT Science

Class 6-10 for conceptual clarity

Tip: Don't try to study advanced topics; focus only on NCERT-level basics.

4. Best Books for General Awareness (GA/GK)

General Awareness carries high weightage in Railway exams, especially NTPC & Group D.

Lucent's General Knowledge

Covers History, Polity, Geography, Economy

Manorama Yearbook

For general knowledge & static awareness

Newspapers

The Hindu/Indian Express for current affairs

Tip: Revise the last 6–8 months of current affairs thoroughly.

5. Best Books for Technical Subjects (For RRB JE & ALP)

Technical subjects require specialized knowledge for RRB JE and ALP exams.

Objective Electrical Engineering

V.K. Mehta

Objective Mechanical Engineering

R.K. Jain

Basic Electronics

B.L. Theraja

Tip: For JE and ALP, rely more on diploma-level standard books + PYQ practice.

6. Best Resources for Current Affairs

Staying updated with current affairs is crucial for scoring well in Railway exams.

Pratiyogita Darpan

Monthly magazine

Monthly Current Affairs PDFs

Vision IAS, Gradeup, etc.

Daily Newspaper Reading

National & Railway-specific updates

7. Practice & Mock Test Resources

Regular practice with mock tests and previous year papers is essential for success.

Previous Year Papers

Kiran Publications, Arihant

Online Mock Test Series

Courage Library, Testbook, Gradeup

Tip: Attempt at least 2–3 full-length mock tests per week to build exam temperament.

Quick Summary – Best Railway Exam Books

Section Recommended Books
Mathematics R.S. Aggarwal, Rajesh Verma, NCERTs
Reasoning R.S. Aggarwal, M.K. Pandey, PYQs
General Science Lucent's Science, NCERTs
General Awareness Lucent's GK, Manorama Yearbook, Newspapers
Technical (JE/ALP) V.K. Mehta, R.K. Jain, B.L. Theraja
Current Affairs Pratiyogita Darpan, Monthly PDFs, Newspapers
Mock Tests & PYQs Kiran, Arihant, Online Test Series

Final Preparation Tips

  1. Stick to one book per subject—avoid multiple sources.
  2. Revise regularly and focus on short notes for quick revision.
  3. Give equal importance to mock tests and PYQs.
  4. Dedicate daily time to current affairs.
  5. Maintain balance—don't ignore PET preparation for RRB Group D.

Conclusion

Choosing the best books for Railway exams is the first step toward success. The right mix of NCERT basics, Lucent for GK, and PYQs for practice will maximize your score.
