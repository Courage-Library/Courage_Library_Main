Fill in the Blanks (English Grammar)

SSC MTS & Delhi Police Exams - English Language

1. What are Fill in the Blanks Questions?

Fill in the Blanks questions test your understanding of grammar, vocabulary, and context. You must choose the most suitable word or phrase to complete the sentence meaningfully.

Definition:
"Fill in the Blanks" questions require selecting the correct word or phrase to complete a sentence grammatically and contextually.

2. Common Grammar Areas Tested

1. Tenses

Past, present, or future forms

2. Prepositions

in, on, at, from, etc.

3. Articles

a, an, the

4. Subject-Verb Agreement

Matching subjects with correct verb forms

5. Conjunctions

and, but, although, because, etc.

6. Vocabulary

Correct word based on meaning

3. Tips for Solving Fill in the Blanks

1. Read the Entire Sentence

Understand the complete context before choosing an answer.

2. Identify Grammar Rules

Look for tense indicators, prepositions, articles clues.

3. Check Subject-Verb Agreement

Ensure the verb matches the subject in number and person.

4. Consider Sentence Structure

Pay attention to parallelism and conjunctions.

5. Eliminate Wrong Options

Remove clearly incorrect choices first.

6. Practice Regularly

Solve previous year questions for better understanding.

Q15. She is good _______ mathematics.

5. Quick Grammar Rules Recap

Grammar Concept Rule Example
Simple Present Use 's/es' for third person singular She goes to school
Prepositions 'for' periods, 'since' points, 'in' directions for 5 years, since morning, in the east
Articles 'a/an' indefinite, 'the' definite an apple, the poor
Comparatives Use 'than' with comparative adjectives taller than me
Past Perfect Had + past participle for earlier past had left before we reached
Subject-Verb Agreement Verb agrees with nearest subject in 'neither...nor' Neither he nor his friends are coming
