Direct & Indirect Speech (Narration)

SSC MTS & Delhi Police Exams - English Language

1. What is Direct and Indirect Speech?

When we report what someone said, we can do it in two ways:

Direct Speech

Quoting the exact words of the speaker.

Indirect Speech

Reporting the meaning of what the speaker said without quoting exact words.

Simple Definition:
• Direct Speech → Exact words spoken by someone.
• Indirect Speech → Same message, but without quotation marks and with tense/pronoun changes.

2. Structure and Examples

Type Structure Example
Direct Speech Speaker + said + "exact words." Ram said, "I am learning English."
Indirect Speech Speaker + said that + reported words Ram said that he was learning English.

3. Rules for Changing Direct into Indirect Speech

(A) Change of Tense

When reporting speech, the tense usually moves one step back:

Direct Speech Tense Indirect Speech Tense
Simple Present → Simple Past
Present Continuous → Past Continuous
Present Perfect → Past Perfect
Simple Past → Past Perfect
Will/Shall → Would/Should

Example:
Direct: She said, "I am tired."
Indirect: She said that she was tired.

(B) Change of Pronouns

Pronouns change according to the subject and object of the reporting verb.

Person Rule Example
1st Person Changes according to the subject "I am happy," said Rina. → Rina said that she was happy.
2nd Person Changes according to the object He said to me, "You are late." → He told me that I was late.
3rd Person No change He said, "He is reading." → He said that he was reading.

(C) Change of Time and Place Words

Direct Indirect
today → that day
tomorrow → the next day
yesterday → the previous day
now → then
this → that
these → those
here → there
ago → before

Example:
Direct: She said, "I will go tomorrow."
Indirect: She said that she would go the next day.

4. Types of Sentences in Reported Speech

(A) Assertive Sentences

Direct: He said, "I like tea."

Indirect: He said that he liked tea.

(B) Interrogative Sentences

Direct: She said, "Do you like apples?"

Indirect: She asked if/whether I liked apples.

(C) Imperative Sentences

Direct: He said, "Close the door."

Indirect: He told me to close the door.

(D) Exclamatory Sentences

Direct: She said, "What a beautiful dress!"

Indirect: She exclaimed that it was a beautiful dress.

5. Real-Life Analogy

Direct Speech

Think of as a voice recording

(exact words)

Indirect Speech

Think of as a summary

(same meaning, different words)

6. Common Delhi Police / SSC Exam Facts

✅

Quotes (" ") → used only in Direct Speech.

✅

"Said to" → becomes told, asked, ordered, etc.

✅

Tenses usually shift back one step.

✅

No change in tense when reporting universal truths.

Example:
He said, "The sun rises in the east." → He said that the sun rises in the east.

7. Quick Recap

Concept Summary
Direct Speech Exact words of the speaker
Indirect Speech Reported meaning, no quotation marks
Tense Change Present → Past
Pronoun Change Depends on subject/object
Reporting Verbs said, told, asked, ordered, exclaimed

✅ Exam Strategy Tip

• Identify the reporting verb and reported speech.

• Remember the tense shift rules (Present → Past).

• Change pronouns according to subject and object.

• Modify time and place words (today → that day, etc.).
