Tenses (English Grammar)

Delhi Police & SSC Exams - English Language

1. Introduction

Tense means time ‚Äî it tells when an action takes place. In English Grammar, Tenses help us express whether an action happened in the past, is happening in the present, or will happen in the future.

Simple Definition: Tense is the form of a verb that shows the time of an action or event.

Past

She worked yesterday.

Present

She works every day.

Future

She will work tomorrow.

2. Types of Tenses

English has three main tenses, and each has four subtypes ‚Üí total 12 Tenses.

Main Tense Subtypes
Present Tense Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous
Past Tense Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous
Future Tense Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous

3. The 12 Tenses ‚Äì Structure & Examples

üü© A. Present Tense

Type Structure Example Usage
1. Simple Present Subject + V‚ÇÅ (+s/es) She plays cricket. Habitual actions, daily routines, universal truths
2. Present Continuous Subject + is/am/are + V‚ÇÅ + ing She is playing cricket. Action happening now
3. Present Perfect Subject + has/have + V‚ÇÉ She has played cricket. Action just completed
4. Present Perfect Continuous Subject + has/have + been + V‚ÇÅ + ing She has been playing cricket for 2 hours. Action started in past and continuing till now

üü® B. Past Tense

Type Structure Example Usage
1. Simple Past Subject + V‚ÇÇ She played cricket. Action completed in the past
2. Past Continuous Subject + was/were + V‚ÇÅ + ing She was playing cricket. Action was happening at a specific time in past
3. Past Perfect Subject + had + V‚ÇÉ She had played cricket before it rained. One past action completed before another
4. Past Perfect Continuous Subject + had been + V‚ÇÅ + ing She had been playing for two hours before sunset. Action continuing up to a point in the past

üü¶ C. Future Tense

Type Structure Example Usage
1. Simple Future Subject + will/shall + V‚ÇÅ She will play cricket. Future plan or intention
2. Future Continuous Subject + will/shall + be + V‚ÇÅ + ing She will be playing cricket at 5 p.m. Action that will be happening at a specific future time
3. Future Perfect Subject + will/shall + have + V‚ÇÉ She will have played cricket by evening. Action completed before a certain future time
4. Future Perfect Continuous Subject + will/shall + have been + V‚ÇÅ + ing She will have been playing for two hours by 6 p.m. Continuous action up to a point in the future

4. Key Rules & Notes

Rule Explanation Example
1. Use Simple Present for universal truths or habits. The sun rises in the east.
2. Use Present Continuous for actions happening now. I am studying.
3. Use Present Perfect for actions just completed. He has finished his work.
4. Use Past Perfect for two past actions (earlier action takes "had"). She had eaten before I arrived.
5. Use Future Perfect to show action completed before a future point. By 2026, he will have completed his degree.
6. Don't use will after if, when, before, after, until (in time clauses). ‚ùå If it will rain... ‚úÖ If it rains...
7. "Since" and "For" are used with Perfect Continuous Tenses. He has been reading since morning.
8. Was/Were + V‚ÇÅ+ing ‚Üí Action continuing in past. They were watching TV.
9. Had been + V‚ÇÅ+ing ‚Üí Action continuing until another past event. He had been sleeping for 2 hours before I called.
10. Will be + V‚ÇÅ+ing ‚Üí Future continuous action. She will be studying at 8 p.m.
