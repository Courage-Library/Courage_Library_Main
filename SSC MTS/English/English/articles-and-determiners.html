Subject‚ÄìVerb Agreement (English Grammar)

Delhi Police & SSC Exams - English Language

1. Introduction

Subject‚ÄìVerb Agreement is one of the most important grammar topics for all competitive exams ‚Äî especially Delhi Police, SSC, and Banking exams. It means the verb (action word) in a sentence must agree with the subject (doer of the action) in number (singular/plural) and person (1st, 2nd, or 3rd person).

Simple Definition: The verb in a sentence should match the subject in number and person.

‚ùå Incorrect: He play cricket.

‚úÖ Correct: He plays cricket.

Here, He (singular subject) ‚Üí plays (singular verb).

2. Basic Rules of Subject‚ÄìVerb Agreement

Rule 1: Singular subject ‚Üí Singular verb

Use verb + s/es for singular subjects

Rule 2: Plural subject ‚Üí Plural verb

Use base form (no s/es) for plural subjects

Subject Verb Example
Singular (He, She, It, Ram) Verb + s/es He plays football.
Plural (They, We, Children) Base form (no s/es) They play football.

3. Detailed Rules & Examples

Let's understand the most common rules one by one üëá

Rule No. Rule Explanation Example
1. Singular subjects take singular verbs, plural subjects take plural verbs. When one person/thing is the subject ‚Üí use singular verb. The boy runs fast. / The boys run fast.
2. Two subjects joined by 'and' take a plural verb. Both subjects act together. Ravi and Sita are friends.
3. If the two subjects refer to the same person or thing, use singular verb. Same identity or unit. Bread and butter is my breakfast.
4. When two subjects are joined by either‚Ä¶or / neither‚Ä¶nor, the verb agrees with the nearest subject. Focus on the noun/subject closest to the verb. Either Ravi or his friends are coming. / Neither the students nor the teacher is ready.
5. Words like each, every, everyone, someone, anyone, nobody always take singular verbs. Treated as one person. Each student has a book. / Everyone is happy.
6. Collective nouns (team, family, jury, committee, audience) take singular verb when the group acts as one unit. Single entity acting together. The team is winning.
7. When the individuals of the group act separately, use plural verb. Members act individually. The jury are divided in their opinion.
8. Uncountable nouns (milk, sugar, news, money, furniture, advice) take singular verbs. Cannot be counted. The news is shocking.
9. Titles of books, movies, and subjects are singular. Always treated as one name/title. "Gulliver's Travels" is a famous book.
10. When a sentence starts with there is / there are, the verb agrees with the real subject that follows. Verb depends on number of nouns after "there". There is a cat on the roof. / There are cats on the roof.
11. Fractions or quantities take the verb according to the noun after "of." Depends on what follows. Two-thirds of the cake is eaten. / Two-thirds of the students are present.
12. Words like none, some, all, most may be singular or plural depending on meaning. Depends on noun. Some of the water is wasted. / Some of the boys are absent.
13. Plural in form but singular in meaning nouns take singular verb. Name of subject or discipline. Mathematics is tough. / Economics is interesting.
14. When a lot of, plenty of, a number of are used, verb depends on the noun. A number of ‚Üí plural, The number of ‚Üí singular. A number of students are absent. / The number of students is high.
15. Distances, time, money, and weight when considered as a single unit ‚Üí singular verb. Quantity acts as one. Five thousand rupees is a big amount. / Ten miles is a long walk.

4. Common Mistakes & Corrections

‚ùå Wrong Sentence ‚úÖ Correct Sentence

My trousers is dirty. My trousers are dirty.

5. Special Cases to Remember

Case Explanation Example
Either/Neither of + plural noun Takes singular verb. Neither of the players is selected.
More than one Always singular. More than one student has passed.
One of + plural noun Singular verb. One of the girls is absent.
With / Along with / Together with / As well as Verb agrees with first subject. The teacher, along with students, is in the class.
Each of the + plural noun Singular verb. Each of the boys has a pen.
None but / But none Singular. None but Ravi is guilty.
Either / Neither / Every / Everyone Always singular. Everyone is welcome.
