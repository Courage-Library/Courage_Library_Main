Active & Passive Voice (English Grammar)

SSC MTS & Delhi Police Exams - English Language

1. What is Voice in English Grammar?

Voice in grammar shows whether the subject performs or receives the action of the verb. There are two types of voice ‚Äî Active Voice and Passive Voice.

Simple Definition:
‚Ä¢ Active Voice: When the subject does the action.
‚Ä¢ Passive Voice: When the subject receives the action.

Active Voice

The teacher teaches the lesson.

Passive Voice

The lesson is taught by the teacher.

2. Difference Between Active and Passive Voice

Basis Active Voice Passive Voice
Focus Doer of the action Receiver of the action
Structure Subject + Verb + Object Object + Verb (be + V¬≥) + by + Subject
Example He writes a letter. A letter is written by him.
Use Direct and strong Formal, polite, or impersonal

3. Structure of Active and Passive Voice

Tense Active Voice Passive Voice
Simple Present He writes a letter. A letter is written by him.
Present Continuous He is writing a letter. A letter is being written by him.
Present Perfect He has written a letter. A letter has been written by him.
Simple Past He wrote a letter. A letter was written by him.
Past Continuous He was writing a letter. A letter was being written by him.
Past Perfect He had written a letter. A letter had been written by him.
Simple Future He will write a letter. A letter will be written by him.
Future Perfect He will have written a letter. A letter will have been written by him.

‚ö†Ô∏è Note: No passive form for Future Continuous, Present Perfect Continuous, and Past Perfect Continuous.

4. How to Change Active Voice to Passive Voice

‚úÖ

Step 1: Identify Subject (S), Verb (V), and Object (O).

‚úÖ

Step 2: Make the object of active voice the subject of passive voice.

‚úÖ

Step 3: Use the correct form of 'be' + past participle (V¬≥) of the main verb.

‚úÖ

Step 4: Use 'by' + subject (optional).

Example:

Active: The police caught the thief.

Passive: The thief was caught by the police.

6. Passive Voice of Imperative Sentences

Type Active Form Passive Form
Simple Command Open the door. Let the door be opened.
Request Please help me. You are requested to help me.
Advice Work hard. You are advised to work hard.
Prohibition Do not touch it. Let it not be touched.

7. Passive Voice of Interrogative Sentences

Active Passive
Did he write the letter? Was the letter written by him?
Is she cooking the food? Is the food being cooked by her?
Has he done the work? Has the work been done by him?
Who broke the glass? By whom was the glass broken?

8. When to Use Passive Voice

‚úÖ When the doer is unknown or unimportant

The thief was arrested.

‚úÖ To sound formal or polite

You are requested to take your seat.

‚úÖ To emphasize the action rather than the doer

The road was repaired yesterday.

10. Quick Recap

Voice Structure Example
