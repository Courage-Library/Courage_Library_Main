Parts of Speech (English Grammar)

Delhi Police Exam - English Language

1. Introduction

In English Grammar, Parts of Speech are the basic building blocks of sentences. Every word in a sentence belongs to one of the 8 parts of speech, based on its function and use. Understanding them helps in grammar, comprehension, and error detection â€” important for Delhi Police and SSC Exams.

2. The 8 Parts of Speech

Part of Speech Function / Use Examples
1. Noun Names a person, place, thing, or idea. Ravi, Delhi, Apple, Honesty
2. Pronoun Replaces a noun to avoid repetition. He, She, It, They
3. Verb Shows action or state of being. Run, Write, Is, Are
4. Adjective Describes a noun or pronoun. Beautiful, Tall, Honest
5. Adverb Describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb. Quickly, Very, Carefully
6. Preposition Shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word. in, on, at, under, between
7. Conjunction Joins words, phrases, or clauses. and, but, or, because
8. Interjection Expresses sudden emotion or feeling. Oh!, Wow!, Alas!, Hurrah!

3. Real-Life Analogy

Think of a sentence like a cricket team:

Noun

Player names

Pronoun

Substitute players

Verb

Actions on the field

Adjective / Adverb

Describe how they play

Preposition

Show positions on the field

Conjunction

Connect players

Interjection

Audience reaction ("Wow!", "Oh no!")

Just like each player has a specific role in a cricket team, each part of speech has a specific function in a sentence.

5. Quick Recap

Part Question It Answers Example
Noun Who / What? Dog, Delhi
Pronoun Who / What (instead of noun)? He, She
Verb What is happening? Runs, Is
Adjective What kind / how many? Tall, Five
Adverb How / when / where? Quickly, Yesterday
Preposition Where / in relation to what? On, Under
Conjunction What connects? And, But
Interjection What feeling? Wow!, Oh!
