Prepositions (English Grammar)

Delhi Police & SSC Exams - English Language

1. What is a Preposition?

A Preposition is a word that shows the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in a sentence. It tells us about place, time, direction, cause, or manner of something.

Simple Definition: A preposition connects a noun or pronoun to another word and shows how they relate in time, place, or direction.

Place

The book is on the table.

Time

He arrived at 5 p.m.

Direction

She walked to the park.

2. Types of Prepositions

Type Function Examples
Prepositions of Time Show when something happens at, on, in, before, after
Prepositions of Place Show where something happens in, on, at, under, between, behind
Prepositions of Direction / Movement Show movement or direction to, into, towards, through, across
Prepositions of Agent / Instrument Show who or what causes something by, with
Prepositions of Reason / Purpose Show why something happens for, because of, due to
Prepositions of Manner Show how something happens with, by, like, as

4. Commonly Confused Prepositions

Confusing Pair Correct Usage Example
in / at In = inside area, At = specific point He is in Delhi. / He is at the airport.
between / among Between = two, Among = more than two Share this between you two. / Discuss among friends.
on / over / above On = touching, Over/Above = not touching The book is on the table. / The fan is over your head.
since / for Since = starting point, For = duration I've lived here since 2010. / I've lived here for 10 years.
