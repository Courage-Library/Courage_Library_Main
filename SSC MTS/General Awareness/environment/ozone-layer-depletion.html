Ozone Layer Depletion

Environment - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is the Ozone Layer?

Ozone Layer = Protective layer in the stratosphere (10–50 km above Earth) containing ozone gas (O₃)

Location

Stratosphere

10–50 km above Earth's surface

Composition

Ozone gas (O₃) molecules

Function

Absorbs harmful UV radiation

Blocks UV-B & UV-C rays from Sun

Earth's Natural Sunscreen

Protects all life forms

Memory Trick: Ozone = Sunshield of Earth

Importance of Ozone Layer

UV Protection

Blocks harmful UV-B & UV-C rays

Skin Cancer Prevention

Reduces risk of skin diseases

Eye Protection

Prevents cataracts

Ecosystem Stability

Protects plants & marine life

What Causes Ozone Layer Depletion?

Main Culprit: Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs)

Sources of CFCs

Refrigerators

Cooling systems

Air Conditioners

AC units

Aerosol Sprays

Spray cans

Foam Packaging

Insulation materials

Fire Extinguishers

Old systems

How CFCs Destroy Ozone

Step 1

CFCs rise to stratosphere

Step 2

UV rays break CFC → releases chlorine atom

Step 3

One chlorine atom destroys thousands of ozone molecules

Other Ozone-Depleting Substances (ODS)

Halons

Carbon Tetrachloride

Methyl Bromide

HCFCs

Memory Trick: CFC = Chlorine Free to Combine (with O₃)

Ozone Hole (VERY IMPORTANT)

Ozone Hole = Region of severe ozone thinning over Antarctica

Why Over Antarctica?

• Very low temperatures
• Polar stratospheric clouds (PSCs)
• Sunlight returns after winter
• Rapid chemical reactions

Characteristics

• First discovered in 1985
• Largest during Antarctic spring
• Size varies seasonally
• Shows gradual recovery

Effects of Ozone Layer Depletion

Skin Cancer

Increased UV radiation exposure

Cataracts

Eye damage & vision problems

Weakened Immune System

Reduced disease resistance

Reduced Crop Yields

Plant damage

Marine Life Damage

Phytoplankton affected

Ecosystem Imbalance

Food chain disruption

Memory Trick: SCIP = Skin cancer, Cataract, Immunity loss, Plant damage

Montreal Protocol (SSC MUST-KNOW)

Montreal Protocol (1987) = International agreement to ban CFCs and ozone-depleting chemicals

Key Features

Year Signed

1987

Purpose

Phase out ozone-depleting substances

Participation

Signed by almost all countries

Success

Most successful environmental treaty

Results & Recovery

Ozone Recovery

Ozone layer is recovering slowly

Expected Healing

Complete recovery expected by 2050

CFC Reduction

Dramatic decrease in CFC production

Global Impact

Model for international cooperation

Greenhouse Effect vs Ozone Depletion

Aspect Greenhouse Effect Ozone Depletion
Main Cause CO₂, CH₄, N₂O CFCs, Halons
Process Traps heat in atmosphere Allows more UV rays
Result Global warming Health/environment damage
Atmospheric Layer Troposphere (lower) Stratosphere (upper)
Key Effect Temperature increase UV radiation increase

Memory Trick: CO₂ = Heating, CFC = Holes

How to Reduce Ozone Depletion

Avoid CFC Appliances

Use CFC-free refrigerators & ACs

No Aerosol Sprays

Avoid spray cans with CFCs

Follow Montreal Protocol

Support international agreements

Public Awareness

Educate about ozone protection

Proper Disposal

Safely dispose old appliances

Support Alternatives

Use ozone-friendly products

SSC Practice Questions

1. Ozone layer is found in which atmospheric layer?

Stratosphere

2. Main cause of ozone depletion?

CFCs (Chlorofluorocarbons)

3. Ozone hole is mainly found over which region?

Antarctica

4. Which protocol banned CFCs internationally?

Montreal Protocol (1987)

5. What does ozone gas protect us from?

UV (Ultraviolet) rays

6. What does CFC release that destroys ozone?

Chlorine atoms

7. Which is NOT an ozone-depleting substance?

CO₂ (it causes global warming, not ozone depletion)

8. Ozone depletion increases risk of?

Skin cancer

9. What is the chemical formula of ozone?

O₃

10. HCFC stands for?

Hydrochlorofluorocarbon (less harmful than CFCs)

Conclusion

Mastering Ozone Layer Depletion is crucial for SSC environment preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Ozone Layer: Stratospheric protective layer
Ozone Formula: O₃ (triatomic oxygen)
Main Threat: CFCs destroy ozone molecules
Ozone Hole: Over Antarctica, seasonal thinning
Montreal Protocol: 1987 agreement banning CFCs
Health Effects: Skin cancer, cataracts, immune damage
Environmental Effects: Crop damage, marine life harm
Recovery: Ozone layer healing, complete by ~2050
Prevention: Avoid CFC products, use alternatives
Difference: Ozone depletion ≠ Greenhouse effect
