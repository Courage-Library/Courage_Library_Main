Climate Change & Its Impacts

Environment - Lesson 8: Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Climate Change?

Climate Change = Long-term change in Earth's climate due to natural or human activities

Climate

Long-term pattern

Temperature, rainfall, wind patterns over decades

Current Situation

Global Warming

Earth warming mainly due to human activities

Causes of Climate Change

Human-Made Causes (Major Focus)

Fossil Fuel Burning

Coal, petrol, diesel combustion

Deforestation

Reduces CO₂ absorption

Industrial Pollution

Factory emissions

Agriculture

Methane from cattle & rice fields

Primary Driver: Enhanced Greenhouse Effect

Natural Causes

Volcanic Eruptions

Release gases & particles

Earth's Orbit Changes

Milankovitch cycles

Solar Radiation

Sun's energy variations

Ocean Circulation

Natural climate patterns

Greenhouse Gases & Climate Change

Enhanced Greenhouse Effect = Human activity increasing GHGs → Extra heat trapped → Climate change

Greenhouse Gas Symbol Major Source Role in Warming
Carbon Dioxide CO₂ Fossil fuels, deforestation Primary contributor
Methane CH₄ Cattle, rice fields, landfills More potent than CO₂
Nitrous Oxide N₂O Fertilizers, industrial processes Long-lasting effect
Chlorofluorocarbons CFCs Refrigerators, ACs, aerosols Ozone depletion + warming

Memory Trick: C-M-N-C = CO₂, Methane, N₂O, CFCs (Main greenhouse gases)

Impacts of Climate Change (SSC MOST IMPORTANT)

Temperature Rise

1.1°C increase since 1900

Melting Ice

Glaciers & ice caps shrinking

Sea Level Rise

Coastal flooding threat

Extreme Weather

Cyclones, heatwaves, floods

Agriculture Impact

• Lower crop yields
• Crop failures
• Increased pests
• Food security risk

Water Resources

• Droughts
• Reduced freshwater
• Rivers drying up
• Water scarcity

Biodiversity Loss

• Coral bleaching
• Species extinction
• Habitat loss
• Food chain disruption

Human Health

• Heat strokes
• Vector-borne diseases
• Respiratory issues
• Waterborne diseases

Memory Trick: CHDF-ACE = Cyclones, Heatwaves, Droughts, Floods, Agriculture loss, Coral bleaching, Extinction

Climate Change Impacts in India

Heatwaves

Increasing in North India

Erratic Monsoon

Unpredictable rainfall patterns

Floods

Assam, Bihar regions affected

Droughts

Maharashtra, Rajasthan

Glacier Melting

Himalayan glaciers shrinking

Coastal Cities

Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata at risk

Solutions to Climate Change

Reduce GHGs

• Renewable energy (solar, wind)
• Reduce fossil fuel use
• Electric vehicles
• Energy-efficient appliances

Increase Absorption

• Plant trees (afforestation)
• Protect forests
• Restore wetlands
• Soil conservation

Policy Actions

• Paris Climate Agreement (2015)
• National Action Plan on Climate Change
• Carbon tax & regulations
• International cooperation

Individual Actions

Reduce Waste

Conserve Water

Use Public Transport

Plant Trees

Melting glaciers + thermal expansion

SSC Practice Questions

1. Main cause of climate change?

Enhanced greenhouse effect

2. Which gas contributes most to global warming?

Carbon dioxide (CO₂)

3. Rising sea level is due to?

Melting glaciers + thermal expansion of oceans

4. Example of extreme climate event?

Cyclone / Heatwave / Flood

5. Methane is produced by?

Cattle & rice fields

6. Paris Agreement deals with?

Climate change mitigation

7. Coral bleaching is caused by?

Rising sea temperature

8. Which area is most affected by sea-level rise?

Islands & coastal regions

9. Himalayan glaciers melting is due to?

Global warming

10. Increased temperature causes?

More heatwaves

Conclusion

Mastering Climate Change & Its Impacts is crucial for SSC environment preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Climate Change: Long-term climate pattern changes
Main Cause: Enhanced greenhouse effect
Key GHGs: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, CFCs
Temperature Rise: 1.1°C increase since 1900
Sea Level Rise: Melting ice + thermal expansion
Extreme Weather: Cyclones, heatwaves, floods, droughts
Agriculture: Reduced yields, crop failures
Biodiversity: Coral bleaching, species extinction
Human Health: Heat strokes, disease spread
Solutions: Renewable energy, afforestation, policies
