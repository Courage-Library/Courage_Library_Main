Biodiversity & Conservation

Biology - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Biodiversity?

Biodiversity = Variety of life forms on Earth – plants, animals, microbes, ecosystems

Levels of Biodiversity (SSC MOST IMPORTANT)

1. Genetic Diversity

• Variation in genes within a species
• Different genetic makeup

Example: Different varieties of rice, mango, dogs

2. Species Diversity

• Variety of different species in an area
• Different types of organisms

Example: Tigers, elephants, snakes in a forest

3. Ecosystem Diversity

• Variety of ecosystems
• Different habitats

Example: Forests, wetlands, deserts, grasslands

Memory Trick: G-S-E = Genes → Species → Ecosystems

Importance of Biodiversity

Ecological Balance

Maintains nature's equilibrium

Food & Medicine

Source of nutrition and drugs

Oxygen Production

Plants produce oxygen

Pollination

Bees, insects pollinate crops

Soil Protection

Prevents soil erosion

Air & Water Purification

Natural filtration systems

Memory Trick: FOPEP = Food – Oxygen – Pollination – Ecological balance – Protection of soil

Threats to Biodiversity

Deforestation

Cutting trees → habitat loss

Pollution

Air, water, soil, noise pollution

Climate Change

Global warming, changing rainfall

Habitat Loss

Urbanization, farming, mining

Overexploitation

Overfishing, hunting

Invasive Species

Non-native species harming natives

Example: Water hyacinth

Conservation of Biodiversity

In-situ Conservation

Definition:

Protecting species in their natural habitat

Examples:

National Parks

Sanctuaries

Biosphere Reserves

Sacred Groves

Memory Trick: In = Inside nature

Ex-situ Conservation

Definition:

Protecting species outside their natural habitat

Examples:

Zoos

Botanical Gardens

Seed Banks

Gene Banks

Memory Trick: Ex = Exit → taken out of original place

Protected Areas (SSC Always Asks)

1. National Parks

• Strict protection
• No human activities allowed

Jim Corbett (Uttarakhand) – Tiger

Kaziranga (Assam) – One-horned rhino

Gir (Gujarat) – Asiatic lion

2. Wildlife Sanctuaries

• Some human activity allowed
• Less strict than national parks

Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

3. Biosphere Reserves

• Large conservation areas
• Three zones: Core, Buffer, Transition
• Conserves biodiversity + culture

Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve

Sunderbans Biosphere Reserve

Memory Trick: NP = strict, WS = flexible, BR = biggest

Red Data Book & Biodiversity Hotspots

Red Data Book

Maintained by:

IUCN (International Union for Conservation of Nature)

Purpose:

Lists endangered & threatened species

Categories:

Endangered

Vulnerable

Extinct

Near Threatened

Biodiversity Hotspots

Definition:

Areas rich in species but under threat

Examples in India:

Western Ghats

Eastern Himalayas

Indo-Burma Region

SSC Practice Questions

1. Biodiversity means?

Variety of life forms

2. Which is an example of in-situ conservation?

National Park

3. Which is an example of ex-situ conservation?

Zoo

4. Kaziranga National Park is famous for?

One-horned rhinoceros

5. Gir National Park protects which animal?

Asiatic lion

6. Red Data Book contains information about?

Endangered species

7. Which is NOT a threat to biodiversity?

Organic farming (SAFE)

8. In biosphere reserves, the strictly protected zone is called?

Core zone

9. Tiger is listed as which type of species?

Endangered species

10. Western Ghats are known as?

Biodiversity hotspot

Conclusion

Mastering Biodiversity & Conservation is crucial for SSC biology preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Biodiversity: Variety of life on Earth
Levels: Genetic, Species, Ecosystem
Threats: Deforestation, pollution, climate change
In-situ: Conservation in natural habitat
Ex-situ: Conservation outside natural habitat
National Parks: Strict protection (e.g., Corbett)
Wildlife Sanctuaries: Flexible protection
Biosphere Reserves: Core, Buffer, Transition zones
Red Data Book: IUCN list of endangered species
Hotspots: Western Ghats, Eastern Himalayas
