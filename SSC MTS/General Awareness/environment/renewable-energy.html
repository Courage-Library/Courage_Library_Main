Renewable Energy

Environment - Lesson 9: Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Renewable Energy?

Renewable Energy = Energy from natural sources that replenish naturally and never run out

Solar

Wind

Hydro

Biomass

Geothermal

Tidal

Renewable vs Non-Renewable Energy

Aspect Renewable Energy Non-Renewable Energy
Supply Unlimited, replenishes naturally Limited, will run out
Environmental Impact Eco-friendly, low pollution Polluting, high CO₂ emissions
Examples Solar, wind, hydropower Coal, oil, natural gas
Sustainability Sustainable long-term Non-sustainable
Cost Low operating cost High & fluctuating cost

Memory Trick: Renewable = Re-fillable, Non-renewable = One-time use

Solar Energy (SSC Favourite)

Key Features

Source

Sunlight

Devices

Solar Panels

Solar Cookers

Solar Heaters

PV Cells

Pros & Cons

Advantages

• Pollution-free
• Abundant in India
• Suitable for households

Limitations

• Needs sunlight
• Requires large area
• High initial cost

National Solar Mission

Objective

Make India a global leader in solar power

Target

100 GW solar capacity by 2022 (part of 500 GW by 2030)

Wind Energy

How it Works

Wind turbines convert wind kinetic energy into electricity

Top States in India

1. Tamil Nadu (largest)
2. Gujarat
3. Rajasthan

Key Points

• Best in coastal areas
• Low operating cost
• Clean & renewable

Hydropower (Hydroelectricity)

Mechanism

Process

Moving water → turns turbines → generates electricity

Storage

Water stored in dams for controlled release

Major Projects in India

Bhakra Nangal

Himachal Pradesh / Punjab

Hirakud Dam

Odisha

Tehri Dam

Uttarakhand

Biomass Energy

Sources

Cow Dung

Crop Residues

Kitchen Waste

Plant Matter

Biogas Composition

Mainly Methane (CH₄) + CO₂

Uses & Initiatives

Applications

Cooking

Lighting

Rural Energy

Electricity

Indian Initiative

Gobardhan Yojana (Biogas & waste-to-energy)

Geothermal & Tidal Energy

Geothermal Energy

Source

Earth's internal heat (hot springs, geysers)

Indian Potential Site

Puga Valley, Ladakh

Use

Electricity generation, heating

Tidal Energy

Source

Sea waves & tides

Best Locations

Coastal areas with strong tides

Indian Potential

Gulf of Kutch, Gujarat

Importance & India's Renewable Energy Targets

Why Renewable Energy is Important

Environmental Benefits

Reduces pollution & greenhouse gases

Resource Conservation

Reduces dependence on fossil fuels

Climate Change Mitigation

Helps combat global warming

Sustainable Development

Long-term energy security

India's Renewable Energy Goals

Overall Target

500 GW renewable energy capacity by 2030

Major Programs

• National Solar Mission
• Wind Energy Mission
• Green Hydrogen Mission

Global Position

One of world's fastest-growing renewable sectors

SSC Practice Questions

1. Solar energy is obtained from?

Sunlight

2. Which renewable energy uses turbines?

Wind energy / Hydropower

3. Biogas mainly contains?

Methane (CH₄)

4. Largest wind energy-producing state in India?

Tamil Nadu

5. Hydroelectricity is produced by?

Flowing/moving water

6. National Solar Mission aims to promote?

Solar energy development

7. Which of the following is non-renewable?

Coal / Petroleum

8. Geothermal energy comes from?

Earth's internal heat

9. Tidal energy is produced in?

Coastal areas

10. Renewable energy helps reduce?

Global warming / Pollution

Conclusion

Mastering Renewable Energy is crucial for SSC environment preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Renewable Energy: Naturally replenishing sources
Solar Energy: From sunlight, uses panels
Wind Energy: Wind turbines, best in coastal areas
Hydropower: Moving water → electricity
Biomass: Organic matter → biogas (methane)
Geothermal: Earth's internal heat
Tidal Energy: Sea waves & tides
India's Target: 500 GW renewable capacity by 2030
National Solar Mission: Solar energy promotion
Benefits: Eco-friendly, reduces pollution, sustainable
