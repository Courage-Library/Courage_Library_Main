Ecosystem & Its Types

Biology - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is an Ecosystem?

Ecosystem = Living (biotic) + Non-living (abiotic) components interacting

Biotic Components (Living)

• Plants
• Animals
• Microorganisms

Abiotic Components (Non-living)

• Sunlight
• Water
• Air
• Soil
• Temperature

Memory Trick: Bio = Living, Abio = Non-living

Types of Ecosystems

1. Natural Ecosystems

Occur Naturally

Formed without human intervention

Examples:

Forests

Deserts

Oceans

Rivers

2. Artificial Ecosystems

Human-made

Created and maintained by humans

Examples:

Crop Fields

Aquariums

Gardens

Zoos

Features: Require human maintenance, Less biodiversity

Terrestrial (Land) Ecosystems

1. Forest Ecosystem

• High rainfall
• Dense trees
• Rich biodiversity
• High oxygen production

Examples: Tropical rainforests, Deciduous forests

2. Grassland Ecosystem

• Moderate rainfall
• Grasses dominate
• Few trees
• Grazing animals

Examples: Savannas, Prairies

3. Desert Ecosystem

• Very low rainfall
• Extreme temperatures
• Sparse vegetation

Plants: Cactus, Thorny bushes

Animals: Camel, Reptiles

Memory Trick: Desert = Dry + Hot

Aquatic (Water) Ecosystems

1. Freshwater Ecosystem

• Low salt content
• Ponds, Lakes, Rivers
• Fish, Algae, Amphibians

Memory Trick: Fresh = River

2. Marine Ecosystem

• High salt content
• Seas, Oceans
• Coral reefs
• Dolphins, Whales, Seaweeds

Covers 71% of Earth's surface

Memory Trick: Marine = Ocean

Food Chain (VERY IMPORTANT)

Food Chain = Flow of energy from Producers → Consumers → Decomposers

1. Producers

• Green Plants
• Make food by photosynthesis

Base of food chain

2. Consumers

• Herbivores (Primary) - Deer, Rabbit
• Carnivores (Secondary) - Lion, Snake
• Omnivores - Humans

3. Decomposers

• Bacteria, Fungi
• Break dead matter
• Return nutrients to soil

Food Web

Network of interconnected food chains. More realistic, increases ecosystem stability.

Memory Trick: P → H → C → D (Producer → Herbivore → Carnivore → Decomposer)

Pyramid of Numbers & Biomass

Pyramid of Numbers

Shows:

Number of organisms at each trophic level

Structure:

Producers (base) → Consumers (top)

Pyramid of Biomass

Shows:

Total mass of organisms at each level

Rule:

Producers have highest biomass → decreases upward

Memory Tricks

"FAB" for Ecosystem Components

F = Flora (plants), A = Animals, B = Bacteria (microbes)

Desert Ecosystem

Dry & Hot

Freshwater vs Marine

Fresh = low salt, Marine = high salt

Food Chain Roles

Producers = Plants, Consumers = Animals, Decomposers = Bacteria/Fungi

Energy Flow

Sun → Plants → Animals

Marine Ecosystem

Covers 71% of Earth

SSC Practice Questions

1. Which is a biotic component of ecosystem?

Plants/Animals

2. Which ecosystem has the highest biodiversity?

Forest Ecosystem

3. Cactus is found in which ecosystem?

Desert Ecosystem

4. Oceans belong to which type of ecosystem?

Marine Ecosystem

5. Example of an artificial ecosystem?

Aquarium/Crop field

6. Producers in food chain are?

Green plants

7. Decomposers include?

Bacteria & fungi

8. Which ecosystem covers 71% of Earth's surface?

Marine Ecosystem

9. Grasslands are characterized by?

Grasses with few trees

10. Energy in food chain flows from?

Producers → Consumers → Decomposers

Conclusion

Mastering Ecosystem & Its Types is crucial for SSC biology preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Ecosystem = Biotic + Abiotic components
Natural vs Artificial ecosystems
Forest: High biodiversity, rainfall
Desert: Dry, hot, sparse vegetation
Grassland: Grasses dominate, few trees
Freshwater: Low salt (rivers, lakes)
Marine: High salt, 71% of Earth
Food chain: Producers → Consumers → Decomposers
Producers: Green plants (photosynthesis)
Decomposers: Bacteria, fungi (nutrient recycling)
