National Parks & Biosphere Reserves

Environment - Lesson 4: Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What Are Protected Areas?

Protected Areas = Regions protected to conserve wildlife, forests, biodiversity & cultural heritage

National Parks

Highest protection level

Wildlife Sanctuaries

Limited human activities

Biosphere Reserves

Ecosystem + culture conservation

National Parks (NP)

Key Features

• Highest level of protection
• No human activities allowed
• Preserve wildlife + habitat
• Strict government rules
• First NP: Jim Corbett (1936)
• Focus on species conservation

Famous National Parks & Key Species

National Park State Famous For
Jim Corbett Uttarakhand Tiger (First NP of India)
Kaziranga Assam One-horned rhinoceros
Gir Gujarat Asiatic lion
Ranthambore Rajasthan Tigers
Sundarbans West Bengal Royal Bengal tiger
Keoladeo Rajasthan Bird sanctuary (UNESCO)
Bandipur Karnataka Elephants, tigers
Periyar Kerala Elephant + tiger reserve
Hemis Ladakh Snow leopard
Silent Valley Kerala Rare rainforest ecosystem

Memory Trick: "C-K-G-R-S" = Corbett, Kaziranga, Gir, Ranthambore, Sundarbans (Most frequently asked)

Wildlife Sanctuaries

Features

Protection Level:

Less strict than National Parks

Human Activities:

Limited activities allowed

Focus:

Primarily protect animals

Examples

Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary

Rajasthan - Migratory birds

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

Kerala - Elephants, tigers

Mudumalai Sanctuary

Tamil Nadu - Elephants, deer

Biosphere Reserves (BR)

Key Features

Core Zone

No human activity

Buffer Zone

Limited research activity

Transition Zone

Human settlements allowed

Famous Biosphere Reserves in India

Biosphere Reserve State Special Feature
Nilgiri BR TN, KL, KA First BR of India
Sundarbans BR West Bengal Bengal tiger + mangroves
Nanda Devi BR Uttarakhand Himalayan flora/fauna
Gulf of Mannar BR Tamil Nadu Coral reefs
Pachmarhi BR Madhya Pradesh Forest biodiversity
Achanakmar–Amarkantak BR MP–CG Rich forests
Simlipal BR Odisha Tiger reserve
Great Nicobar BR A&N Islands Tropical rainforest

Memory Trick: "NGS – NPGAS" = Nilgiri, Gulf of Mannar, Sundarbans – Nanda Devi, Pachmarhi, Great Nicobar, Achanakmar, Simlipal

National Parks vs Biosphere Reserves

Feature National Park Biosphere Reserve
Purpose Protect wildlife & habitat Conserve ecosystem + people + culture
Human Activity Not allowed Allowed in transition zone
Size Smaller Very large
Zones No zones Core, Buffer, Transition zones
Examples Corbett, Gir, Kaziranga Nilgiri, Sundarbans

Memory Trick: BR = Big Region, NP = Smaller strict area

SSC Practice Questions

1. First National Park of India?

Jim Corbett National Park

2. Kaziranga National Park is famous for?

One-horned rhinoceros

3. Asiatic Lion is found in?

Gir National Park, Gujarat

4. Which protected area has core-buffer-transition zones?

Biosphere Reserve

5. Which is India's first Biosphere Reserve?

Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve

6. Sundarbans is famous for?

Royal Bengal Tiger

7. Which protected area has strictly no human activity?

National Park

8. Hemis National Park protects which animal?

Snow leopard

9. Gulf of Mannar BR protects what?

Coral reefs

10. Which of these is an UNESCO Natural World Heritage Site?

Kaziranga / Keoladeo / Sundarbans / Nanda Devi

Conclusion

Mastering National Parks & Biosphere Reserves is crucial for SSC environment preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

National Park: Highest protection, no human activity
Wildlife Sanctuary: Limited human activities
Biosphere Reserve: Core, Buffer, Transition zones
Jim Corbett: First NP, famous for tigers
Kaziranga: One-horned rhino, UNESCO site
Gir National Park: ONLY home of Asiatic lion
Nilgiri: First Biosphere Reserve of India
Sundarbans: Mangroves, Royal Bengal tiger
Hemis: Snow leopard conservation
UNESCO sites: Kaziranga, Keoladeo, Sundarbans, Nanda Devi
