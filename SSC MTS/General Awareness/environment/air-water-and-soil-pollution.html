Air, Water & Soil Pollution

Environment - Lesson 5: Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Pollution?

Pollution = Unwanted changes in the environment caused by harmful substances (pollutants)

Air Pollution

Harmful gases & particles

Water Pollution

Contamination of water bodies

Soil Pollution

Contamination of land

Air Pollution (VERY IMPORTANT)

Major Air Pollutants

Carbon Monoxide (CO)

From vehicle exhaust

Sulphur Dioxide (SO₂)

Burning fossil fuels

Nitrogen Oxides (NOₓ)

Industrial processes

Particulate Matter (PM2.5, PM10)

Dust, smoke particles

CFCs

Refrigerators, ACs

Causes & Effects

Causes:

Vehicles

Industries

Burning

CFCs

Effects:

• Respiratory diseases
• Acid rain
• Global warming
• Ozone depletion

1. Acid Rain

• Caused by: SO₂ + NOₓ + rainwater
• Effects: Damages buildings (Taj Mahal)
• Harms plants & aquatic life

2. Ozone Layer Depletion

• Caused by: CFCs
• Result: More UV radiation
• Effects: Skin cancer, cataract

Water Pollution

Major Water Pollutants

Sewage Waste

Untreated human waste

Industrial Waste

Chemical discharge

Agricultural Runoff

Fertilizers, pesticides

Plastic Waste

Non-biodegradable

Heavy Metals

Lead, mercury, arsenic

Effects of Water Pollution

Waterborne Diseases

Cholera, typhoid, diarrhea

Aquatic Life Impact

Fish deaths, reduced oxygen

Eutrophication

Algal bloom, oxygen depletion

Drinking Water Contamination

Health hazards

Eutrophication (Important Topic)

Cause

Excess fertilizers (nitrates + phosphates)

Process

Algal bloom → Oxygen depletion

Result

Fish deaths, ecosystem damage

Soil Pollution

Major Soil Pollutants

Pesticides

Chemical pest control

Fertilizers

Chemical nutrients

Plastic Waste

Non-biodegradable

Industrial Waste

Heavy metals, chemicals

Effects of Soil Pollution

Reduced Soil Fertility

Loss of nutrients

Contaminated Crops

Health risks

Microbial Life Damage

Disturbs ecosystem

Agricultural Decline

Reduced productivity

Prevention & Control

Air Pollution Control

• Use public transport
• Plant more trees
• CNG/electric vehicles
• Industrial filters
• Reduce CFCs

Water Pollution Control

• Treat sewage before disposal
• Reduce plastic waste
• Safe industrial waste disposal
• Avoid excess fertilizers
• Biodegradable detergents

Soil Pollution Control

• Avoid excessive chemicals
• Promote organic farming
• Proper waste disposal
• Ban single-use plastics
• Crop rotation

Memory Tricks

Air Pollutants

C-S-N-P = CO, SO₂, NOₓ, PM (Particulate matter)

CFCs

Cooling = CFC (Used in AC, fridge → ozone depletion)

Acid Rain

SO₂ + NOₓ + Rain = Acid Rain

Eutrophication

Extra nutrients = Extra algae = Fish die

Soil Pollution

PFP = Pesticides – Fertilizers – Plastics

Smog

Smoke + Fog = Smog

SSC Practice Questions

1. Ozone layer is depleted by which chemical?

CFCs (Chlorofluorocarbons)

2. Acid rain is caused by which pollutants?

SO₂ and NOₓ

3. Eutrophication is caused by excess of?

Fertilizers (nitrates & phosphates)

4. PM2.5 refers to?

Fine particulate matter in air

5. Which gas is the major cause of global warming?

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

6. Example of waterborne disease?

Cholera / Typhoid

7. Major cause of soil pollution?

Pesticides

8. Example of non-biodegradable waste?

Plastic

9. Smog is a mixture of?

Smoke + Fog

10. Which layer protects us from UV radiation?

Ozone layer

Conclusion

Mastering Air, Water & Soil Pollution is crucial for SSC environment preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Air Pollution: CO, SO₂, NOₓ, PM2.5, CFCs
Acid Rain: SO₂ + NOₓ + rainwater
Ozone Depletion: CFCs cause UV increase
Water Pollution: Sewage, industrial waste, fertilizers
Eutrophication: Excess nutrients → algal bloom
Soil Pollution: Pesticides, fertilizers, plastic waste
Waterborne Diseases: Cholera, typhoid, diarrhea
CFCs: Used in ACs, refrigerators
PM2.5: Fine particulate matter, health hazard
Control Methods: Treatment, reduction, alternatives
