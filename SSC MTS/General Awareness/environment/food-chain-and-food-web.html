Food Chain & Food Web

Biology - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is a Food Chain?

Food Chain = Sequence where each organism eats the one before it and is eaten by the one after it

Energy Flow Direction

Sun

Producer

Herbivore

Carnivore

Top Predator

Decomposer

Memory Trick: SPHCTD = Sun → Plant → Herbivore → Carnivore → Top predator → Decomposer

Trophic Levels in Food Chain

Level 1

Producers

Plants (Photosynthesis)

Level 2

Primary Consumers

Herbivores: Deer, Rabbit

Level 3

Secondary Consumers

Carnivores: Frog, Snake

Level 4

Tertiary Consumers

Top Predators: Eagle, Lion

Recyclers

Decomposers

Bacteria & Fungi

Types of Food Chains

1. Grazing Food Chain (GFC)

Starts with:

Green plants

Example:

Grass → Deer → Tiger

Features:

  • • Depends on solar energy
  • • Most common type
  • • Starts with photosynthesis

2. Detritus Food Chain (DFC)

Starts with:

Dead organic matter (detritus)

Example:

Dead leaves → Earthworms → Birds → Eagle

Features:

  • • Decomposers play major role
  • • Works in forests, soils, oceans
  • • Recycles nutrients

Memory Trick: GFC = Green (starts with green plants), DFC = Dead (starts with dead matter)

Food Web (SUPER IMPORTANT)

Food Web = Network of interconnected food chains

Importance

Ecosystem Stability

Makes ecosystem more stable

Survival Assurance

If one organism disappears, others can still survive

Real-life Representation

Shows actual feeding relationships

Comparison

Food Chain

Linear sequence, single path

Food Web

Complex network, multiple paths

Memory Trick: Food chain = Straight line, Food web = Network

Energy Flow: 10% Law

Lindeman's 10% Law: Only 10% energy transfers to next trophic level

Plants

1000 J

(100%)

Herbivores

100 J

(10%)

Carnivores

10 J

(1%)

Top Predators

1 J

(0.1%)

Energy Loss

90% energy lost at each level as heat, movement, growth, and reproduction

Ecological Pyramids

1. Pyramid of Numbers

• Shows number of organisms
• Example: Many grass → few deer → one tiger

Shape: Usually upright, can be inverted

2. Pyramid of Biomass

• Shows mass of living matter
• Usually decreases upward
• Exception: Aquatic (inverted)

Shape: Usually upright

3. Pyramid of Energy

• Shows energy at each level
• ALWAYS upright (never inverted)
• Due to 10% energy loss

Shape: Always upright

Memory Trick: Energy pyramid = Always straight, Biomass = Usually upright, Numbers = Depends

Decomposers - Nature's Recyclers

Role of Decomposers

Nutrient Recycling

Break dead organisms into nutrients

Soil Enrichment

Return nutrients to soil for plants

Waste Cleanup

Decompose organic waste

Examples

Bacteria

Microscopic decomposers

Fungi

Mushrooms, molds

Importance

Without decomposers → ecosystem collapses

Memory Tricks

Food Chain Flow

P-H-C-D = Producer → Herbivore → Carnivore → Decomposer

10% Law

Only 10% energy transfers to next level

Decomposers

Nature's Recyclers

Pyramid Rule

Energy pyramid = Always upright

Food Chain Types

GFC = Green plants, DFC = Dead matter

Food Web

Network of food chains = More stability

SSC Practice Questions

1. What is the first trophic level in a food chain?

Producers (Plants)

2. Example of a primary consumer?

Deer / Rabbit / Grasshopper

3. Food chain that starts with dead matter?

Detritus food chain

4. Food web means?

Interconnected food chains

5. Percentage of energy transferred to next trophic level?

10% (Lindeman's 10% Law)

6. Which ecological pyramid is always upright?

Pyramid of energy

7. Who are decomposers in an ecosystem?

Bacteria & fungi

8. Snake in a food chain is often a?

Secondary consumer

9. Main source of energy for all ecosystems?

Sun

10. Grass → Goat → Lion is an example of?

Grazing food chain

Conclusion

Mastering Food Chain & Food Web is crucial for SSC biology preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Food chain: Linear feeding sequence
Trophic levels: Producer → Consumer → Decomposer
GFC: Starts with green plants
DFC: Starts with dead matter
Food web: Network of food chains
10% Law: Energy transfer efficiency
Pyramid of energy: Always upright
Decomposers: Nature's recyclers
Primary consumer: Herbivores
Secondary consumer: Carnivores
