Greenhouse Effect

Environment - Lesson 6: Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is the Greenhouse Effect?

Greenhouse Effect = Process by which Earth's atmosphere traps heat, keeping the planet warm enough for life

Key Point

Natural Phenomenon

Essential for life on Earth

Without it:

Earth would be freezing (-18°C)

How it Works

1. Sunlight reaches Earth

2. Earth absorbs heat → radiates IR radiation

3. Greenhouse gases trap some heat

4. Earth stays warm (15°C average)

Greenhouse Gases (GHGs) - VERY IMPORTANT

Greenhouse Gas Symbol Main Source Importance
Carbon Dioxide CO₂ Fossil fuels, deforestation Major contributor
Methane CH₄ Cattle, rice fields, landfills More potent than CO₂
Nitrous Oxide N₂O Fertilizers, industrial processes Long-lasting effect
Chlorofluorocarbons CFCs Refrigerators, ACs, aerosols Ozone depletion + greenhouse
Water Vapour H₂O Natural evaporation Natural greenhouse gas

Memory Trick: C-M-N-C = CO₂, Methane, N₂O, CFCs (Main greenhouse gases)

Enhanced Greenhouse Effect = Global Warming

Enhanced Greenhouse Effect = Human activities increase GHGs → Extra heat trapped → Temperature rise

Causes

Burning fossil fuels

Coal, petrol, diesel

Industrial pollution

Factory emissions

Deforestation

Reduces CO₂ absorption

Cattle farming

Methane production

Global Warming Definition

Global Warming

Increase in Earth's average temperature due to extra greenhouse gases

Key Point: Natural GHE = Good, Enhanced GHE = Bad

Effects of Global Warming (SSC Favourites)

Rising Sea Levels

Melting ice caps

Melting Glaciers

Polar ice reduction

Extreme Weather

Heatwaves, floods

Coral Bleaching

Ocean temperature rise

Species Extinction

Habitat loss

Crop Yield Drop

Agriculture impact

Droughts & Fires

Forest destruction

Disease Spread

Malaria, dengue

Memory Trick: M-WED-FDS = Melting – Weather – Extinction – Drought – Fires – Disease – Sea level

Natural vs Enhanced Greenhouse Effect

Aspect Natural Greenhouse Effect Enhanced Greenhouse Effect
Nature Good, essential for life Bad, harmful
Function Keeps Earth warm (15°C) Causes global warming
Impact Makes life possible Extreme climate change
Gases Natural balance Excess CO₂, CH₄, CFCs
Result Habitable planet Environmental damage

Ozone Layer vs Greenhouse Effect

Ozone Layer

Function:

Protects from UV radiation

Location:

Stratosphere (upper atmosphere)

Damage:

CFCs cause ozone depletion

Greenhouse Effect

Function:

Traps heat in atmosphere

Location:

Troposphere (lower atmosphere)

Damage:

GHGs cause global warming

Important: CFCs cause BOTH ozone depletion AND act as greenhouse gases

Solutions to Reduce Global Warming

Individual Actions

• Plant more trees
• Use public transport
• Reduce electricity use
• Avoid burning waste
• Conserve water

Technological Solutions

• Renewable energy (solar, wind)
• Electric vehicles
• Energy-efficient appliances
• Reduce CFC usage
• Carbon capture technology

Government Actions

• Paris Climate Agreement
• Afforestation programs
• Pollution control laws
• Carbon tax policies
• International cooperation

SSC Practice Questions

1. Greenhouse effect is caused by?

Greenhouse gases in the atmosphere

2. Which gas is the main cause of global warming?

Carbon dioxide (CO₂)

3. Methane is produced from?

Rice fields, cattle, garbage dumps

4. Enhanced greenhouse effect leads to?

Global warming

5. Ozone depletion is caused by?

CFCs

6. Which gas traps heat in the atmosphere?

Greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄, etc.)

7. Effect of global warming?

Melting of glaciers / Rise in sea level

8. Which does NOT cause greenhouse effect?

Oxygen

9. CFCs are used in?

Refrigerators & air conditioners

10. Paris Agreement relates to?

Climate change control

Conclusion

Mastering Greenhouse Effect is crucial for SSC environment preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Greenhouse Effect: Natural heat trapping process
Main GHGs: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, CFCs, Water vapour
Enhanced GHE: Human-caused increase in GHGs
Global Warming: Rise in Earth's average temperature
Effects: Sea level rise, melting glaciers, extreme weather
CFCs: Cause ozone depletion AND greenhouse effect
Methane Sources: Cattle, rice fields, landfills
Natural GHE: Good (makes life possible)
Enhanced GHE: Bad (causes climate change)
Solutions: Renewable energy, afforestation, reduce emissions
