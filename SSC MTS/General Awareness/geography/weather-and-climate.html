Weather & Climate

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Geography

Introduction

Understanding the difference between weather and climate is fundamental to geography. This topic explains how atmospheric conditions vary from daily changes to long-term patterns.

This topic is VERY important for SSC MTS. Questions frequently come from weather vs climate differences, elements, and climate factors.

Weather vs Climate: Key Difference

  • Day-to-day condition of the atmosphere
  • Changes frequently (hourly/daily)
  • Measured for small areas
  • Includes temperature, rainfall, wind, humidity

Memory Trick: Weather = "Today"

Examples: "Today it's raining", "This morning is cold"

üå¶Ô∏è What is Climate?

  • Average weather pattern over 30‚Äì35 years
  • Relatively stable and predictable
  • Describes large regions
  • Determines vegetation, agriculture, lifestyle

Memory Trick: Climate = "Everyday" pattern

Examples: "Rajasthan is hot & dry", "Kerala is rainy"

Elements of Weather & Climate

These are the measurable factors that define weather and climate conditions:

1. Temperature

Degree of hotness or coldness

Measured in ¬∞C or ¬∞F

2. Atmospheric Pressure

Weight of air above a point

Low pressure ‚Üí rain, High pressure ‚Üí clear sky

3. Wind

Horizontal movement of air

From high to low pressure areas

4. Humidity

Amount of water vapor in air

High humidity ‚Üí feels hotter

5. Rainfall

Precipitation in liquid form

Measured in mm or cm

6. Cloud Cover

Extent of sky covered by clouds

Affects temperature & rainfall

Important Wind Types

Sea Breeze

Daytime: Cool breeze from sea to land

Land Breeze

Nighttime: Cool breeze from land to sea

Monsoon Winds

Seasonal winds bringing rain to India

Factors Affecting Climate

Memory Trick: "L A D O W R"

Latitude ‚Äì Altitude ‚Äì Distance from sea ‚Äì Ocean currents ‚Äì Winds ‚Äì Relief

1. Latitude

Distance from Equator determines temperature:

  • Near Equator ‚Üí Hot climate
  • Near Poles ‚Üí Cold climate
  • Temperate zones ‚Üí Moderate climate

Most important factor affecting climate globally.

2. Altitude (Height)

Temperature decreases with height:

Normal Lapse Rate: 6.5¬∞C decrease per 1000m ascent

Example: Shimla is cooler than Delhi

Why mountains are cooler despite being closer to sun? ‚Üí Thin air holds less heat.

3. Distance from Sea (Continentality)

Sea influences temperature moderation:

  • Coastal areas ‚Üí Moderate climate
  • Inland areas ‚Üí Extreme temperatures
  • Sea heats & cools slower than land

Example: Mumbai (coastal) has milder climate than Delhi (inland).

4. Ocean Currents

Warm currents warm coastal areas, cold currents cool them.

Example: Gulf Stream warms Western Europe.

5. Winds

Direction and nature of winds affect climate.

Monsoon winds bring rain to India.

6. Relief Features

Mountains affect rainfall distribution.

Rain shadow effect: Western Ghats cause heavy rain in Kerala, dry in Deccan.

Major Climate Zones of the World

1. Tropical Climate

  • Located near Equator (0¬∞‚Äì23.5¬∞)
  • Hot temperature year-round
  • High rainfall (tropical rainforests)
  • Examples: Southern India, Brazil, Indonesia

2. Temperate Climate

  • Located between tropics and polar circles
  • Moderate temperatures
  • Four distinct seasons
  • Examples: USA, Europe, China

3. Polar Climate

  • Located near poles (>66.5¬∞)
  • Extremely cold temperatures
  • Ice and snow cover year-round
  • Examples: Antarctica, Greenland, Arctic regions

Climate of India (VERY IMPORTANT)

India has a Tropical Monsoon Climate

Features of Indian Climate

  • Hot summers (March‚ÄìJune)
  • Cool winters (November‚ÄìFebruary)
  • Seasonal rainfall (June‚ÄìSeptember)
  • Influenced by monsoon winds
  • Regional variations due to relief features

Types of Rainfall in India

Convectional Rainfall

Due to strong heating, common in summer afternoons

Orographic (Relief) Rainfall

Mountains force air to rise ‚Üí Western Ghats, Himalayas

Cyclonic Rainfall

Due to cyclones, common in coastal areas

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. What is weather?

Day-to-day condition of the atmosphere

2. Climate is the average weather over how many years?

30‚Äì35 years

3. What type of climate does India have?

Tropical Monsoon Climate

4. Which factor MOST affects climate?

Latitude (distance from Equator)

5. Why is Shimla cooler than Delhi?

Higher altitude (temperature decreases with height)

6. What is orographic rainfall?

Rainfall caused when mountains force air to rise and cool

7. Areas near the sea generally have what type of climate?

Moderate climate (less extreme temperatures)

8. What is the rain shadow effect?

Dry area on leeward side of mountains where rainfall is reduced

Conclusion

Weather and climate are interconnected but distinct concepts. Weather describes short-term atmospheric conditions, while climate represents long-term patterns. Understanding the factors affecting climate ‚Äì latitude, altitude, distance from sea, ocean currents, winds, and relief ‚Äì is key to explaining regional climate variations.

Key Takeaways for SSC Exams

India = Tropical Monsoon Climate
Factors: L A D O W R (Memory trick)

Coastal = Moderate, Inland = Extreme climate
Rain shadow = Dry area behind mountains
