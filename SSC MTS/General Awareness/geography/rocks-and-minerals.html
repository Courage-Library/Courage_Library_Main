Rocks & Minerals

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Physical Geography

Introduction

Rocks are solid materials that make up the Earth's crust, composed of various minerals. Understanding rock types and mineral properties is essential for physical geography questions in SSC exams.

This is a high-scoring topic in SSC MTS. Questions usually come from rock types, examples, transformations, and mineral uses.

Rocks

Solid materials forming Earth's crust, composed of minerals.

Minerals

Naturally occurring chemical substances with definite composition.

Examples: Quartz, Mica, Feldspar, Diamond

Three Major Types of Rocks

Memory Trick: "I → S → M" – Igneous → Sedimentary → Metamorphic

1. Igneous Rocks

Formed by cooling & solidification of molten magma/lava.

Extrusive Igneous Rocks

  • Formed when lava cools outside Earth's surface
  • Cooling is rapid → small crystals
  • Examples: Basalt, Pumice, Obsidian

Intrusive Igneous Rocks

  • Formed when magma cools inside Earth's crust
  • Cooling is slow → large crystals
  • Examples: Granite, Diorite, Gabbro

Important Points for SSC

  • Basalt – Forms the Deccan Plateau
  • Pumice – Very light, floats on water
  • Granite – Common building stone
  • Igneous rocks contain no fossils

Memory Tricks

"BaPu Obey"

Basalt, Pumice, Obsidian (Extrusive)

"G–D–G"

Granite, Diorite, Gabbro (Intrusive)

2. Sedimentary Rocks

Formed by deposition & compaction of sediments carried by rivers, wind, seas, or glaciers.

Sandstone

From sand

Shale

From clay

Limestone

From shells

Conglomerate

Mixed pebbles

Coal

From plants

Rock Salt

Evaporated salt

Special Features

  • Contain fossils (remains of plants/animals)
  • Distinct layers (strata)
  • Softest rock type
  • Form over long periods

Memory Trick

"SShLCC"

Sandstone, Shale, Limestone, Conglomerate, Coal

3. Metamorphic Rocks

Formed when existing rocks transform due to heat, pressure, or chemical changes deep within Earth's crust.

Original Rock → Metamorphic Rock Key Feature
Limestone → Marble Used in sculpture & buildings
Shale → Slate Used for roofing & blackboards
Sandstone → Quartzite Very hard, glassy appearance
Granite → Gneiss Banded appearance

Super Memory Trick

"L–M, S–S, Sa–Q, G–G"

Limestone →

Marble

Shale →

Slate

Sandstone →

Quartzite

Granite →

Gneiss

The Rock Cycle

The continuous process of transformation: Igneous → Sedimentary → Metamorphic → Igneous

Key Concept

Any rock can change into any other type through geological processes:

  • Weathering & Erosion → Breaks down rocks
  • Deposition & Compaction → Forms sedimentary rocks
  • Heat & Pressure → Forms metamorphic rocks
  • Melting & Cooling → Forms igneous rocks

Very Important for SSC

Any rock can change into any other type through the rock cycle.

The rock cycle illustrates how Earth's materials are continuously recycled over millions of years.

Comparison Table (Most Important for SSC)

Type How it Forms Examples Features
Igneous Cooling & solidification of magma/lava Granite, Basalt, Pumice No fossils, crystalline structure
Sedimentary Deposition & compaction of sediments Sandstone, Shale, Coal, Limestone Contain fossils, layered structure
Metamorphic Transformation by heat & pressure Marble, Slate, Quartzite, Gneiss Strongest, foliated/non-foliated

Mineral Characteristics

  • Naturally occurring chemical substances
  • Definite chemical composition
  • Crystalline structure
  • Inorganic origin (usually)
  • Building blocks of rocks

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which type of rock contains fossils?

Sedimentary Rocks (e.g., Sandstone, Shale, Limestone)

2. Marble is formed from which rock?

Limestone (through metamorphism)

3. Which rock forms the Deccan Plateau?

Basalt (extrusive igneous rock)

4. Granite is which type of igneous rock?

Intrusive Igneous (cools slowly inside Earth)

5. Which rock floats on water?

Pumice (due to air bubbles)

6. Sandstone changes into which metamorphic rock?

Quartzite (under heat & pressure)

7. Which rocks show distinct layering?

Sedimentary Rocks (strata)

8. Give examples of metallic minerals.

Iron ore, Copper, Gold, Silver, Bauxite

Conclusion

Rocks & Minerals form the foundation of physical geography. Remember the simple associations: Igneous from magma, Sedimentary from sediments, Metamorphic from transformation. These concepts guarantee high scores in SSC geography questions.

Final Revision Points

Igneous = First rocks (Granite, Basalt)
Sedimentary = Layers & fossils (Sandstone, Coal)
Metamorphic = Changed rocks (Marble, Slate)
Minerals = Building blocks (Quartz, Mica, Feldspar)
Rock Cycle = Continuous transformation
