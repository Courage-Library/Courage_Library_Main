Countries & Capitals - World Geography

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Static GK

Introduction

Mastering countries and their capitals is a crucial static GK topic for SSC exams. This chapter provides continent-wise lists, memory tricks, and high-frequency questions.

SSC frequently asks about capitals of Asian countries, unique capital cities, and continent-wise distributions.

5. Australia & Oceania

Australia: Canberra
New Zealand: Wellington

Key Points
Canberra was purpose-built as capital (not Sydney/Melbourne)
Antarctica has no permanent population or capital
Memory Trick: "A–C, N–W" → Australia/Canberra, New Zealand/Wellington

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. What is the capital of Japan?

Tokyo

2. Canberra is the capital of which country?

Australia

3. What is the administrative capital of Sri Lanka?

Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte

4. Which country has three capital cities?

South Africa (Pretoria, Cape Town, Bloemfontein)

5. Ottawa is the capital of which country?

Canada

6. What is the capital of Afghanistan?

Kabul

7. Berlin is the capital of which European country?

Germany

8. What is the capital of Egypt?

Cairo

9. Brasília is the capital of which South American country?

Brazil

10. Which continent has no permanent capital cities?

Antarctica

Conclusion

Mastering countries and capitals is essential for scoring in SSC's static GK section. Focus on high-frequency countries, unique capital arrangements, and continent-wise distributions.

Key Takeaways

Asia: Largest continent, focus on Japan, China, India, Middle East
Africa: Note South Africa's three capitals, Egypt's Cairo
Europe: Major powers (UK, Germany, France, Russia)
Americas: USA, Canada, Brazil, Argentina
Oceania: Australia/Canberra, New Zealand/Wellington
Memory Tricks: Use patterns ("ton" endings, "stan" countries)
Unique Cases: Sri Lanka, South Africa, Bolivia, Netherlands
