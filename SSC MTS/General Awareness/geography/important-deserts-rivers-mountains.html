Important Deserts, Rivers & Mountains

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - World Geography

Introduction

This chapter covers the world's most significant deserts, rivers, and mountain ranges. These are high-scoring topics in SSC exams, with frequent questions on locations, sizes, and key characteristics.

SSC frequently asks about the largest/smallest, longest/highest, and location-based questions about deserts, rivers, and mountains.

PART 1: Important Deserts of the World

Types of Deserts

Hot Deserts
High temperatures year-round
Little rainfall
Examples: Sahara, Arabian, Kalahari
Cold Deserts
Low temperatures, ice/snow
Little precipitation
Examples: Antarctic, Arctic, Gobi

A. Hot Deserts (Major)

1. Sahara Desert

Location: North Africa
Countries: Algeria, Egypt, Libya, Sudan, Morocco
Size: Largest hot desert in the world
Features: Sand dunes, rocky plateaus, oasis
Key Facts

• Area: ~9.2 million sq km (size of China or USA)

• Temperatures can exceed 50°C (122°F)

• Home to nomadic tribes like Tuareg

2. Arabian Desert

Location: Arabian Peninsula
Countries: Saudi Arabia, Oman, UAE, Yemen
Size: Largest desert in Asia
Features: Rub' al Khali (Empty Quarter) is largest sand desert
Key Facts

• Rich in oil reserves

• Bedouin tribes traditionally inhabited

• Extreme temperatures with little vegetation

3. Gobi Desert

Location: China & Mongolia
Type: Cold desert
Features: Rocky, windy, temperature extremes
Significance: Part of Silk Road route
Key Facts

• Name means "waterless place" in Mongolian

• Temperature range: -40°C to 45°C (-40°F to 113°F)

• Known for dinosaur fossil discoveries

Kalahari Desert
Location: Southern Africa
Countries: Botswana, Namibia, South Africa
Feature: Red sand, semi-arid
Atacama Desert
Location: Chile, South America
Feature: Driest non-polar desert
Unique: Some areas haven't seen rain for centuries

B. Cold Deserts

Antarctic Desert
Location: Antarctica
Feature: Largest desert in the world
Type: Polar ice desert

Desert Memory Trick: "S A G K A G" → Sahara – Arabian – Gobi – Kalahari – Atacama – Great Victoria

Arctic Desert
Location: Arctic region around North Pole
Countries: Parts of Canada, Greenland, Russia, Alaska
Feature: Tundra vegetation, permafrost

PART 2: Important Rivers of the World

1. Nile River – Longest River in the World

Length: ~6,650 km (longest in the world)
Source: Lake Victoria (White Nile) & Lake Tana (Blue Nile)
Countries: Uganda, Sudan, Egypt
Mouth: Mediterranean Sea
Significance: Cradle of ancient Egyptian civilization
Key Facts

• Annual flooding deposited fertile silt for agriculture

• Aswan High Dam controls flooding and provides hydroelectric power

• Major cities: Cairo, Khartoum, Luxor

2. Amazon River – Largest River by Water Volume

Length: ~6,400 km (2nd longest)
Source: Andes Mountains, Peru
Countries: Brazil, Peru, Colombia
Mouth: Atlantic Ocean
Basin: Amazon Rainforest (world's largest rainforest)
Key Facts

• Carries about 20% of world's river water to ocean

• Home to pink river dolphins, piranhas, anacondas

• Contains thousands of tributaries

Other Major Rivers

Yangtze River (Chang Jiang)
Location: China
Feature: Longest river in Asia
Significance: Three Gorges Dam (world's largest power station)
Mississippi-Missouri River System
Location: United States
Feature: Longest river system in North America
Mouth: Gulf of Mexico
Danube River
Location: Europe
Feature: Passes through most European countries (10)
Mouth: Black Sea
Volga River
Location: Russia
Feature: Longest river in Europe
Mouth: Caspian Sea

River Memory Trick

"N A Y M D V M T R"

Nile – Amazon – Yangtze – Mississippi – Danube – Volga – Mekong – Thames – Rhine

Remember: "NAY MD V MTR" for major world rivers

PART 3: Important Mountain Ranges of the World

1. Himalayas – Highest Mountain Range

Location: Asia (Indian subcontinent)
Countries: India, Nepal, China, Bhutan, Pakistan
Highest Peak: Mount Everest (8,848 m)
Other Peaks: K2, Kanchenjunga, Lhotse, Makalu
Significance: Source of major Asian rivers (Ganga, Indus, Brahmaputra)
Key Facts

• Young fold mountains (formed 40-50 million years ago)

• Acts as climate barrier between Indian subcontinent and Tibetan plateau

• Home to Sherpa people and unique mountain ecosystems

2. Andes Mountains – Longest Mountain Range

Location: South America (western coast)
Length: ~7,000 km (longest continental mountain range)
Countries: Chile, Peru, Argentina, Colombia, Ecuador
Highest Peak: Aconcagua (6,961 m) in Argentina
Features: Active volcanoes, altiplano (high plateau)
Key Facts

• Source of Amazon River

• Home to ancient Inca civilization (Machu Picchu)

• Rich in minerals (copper, silver, tin)

Other Important Mountain Ranges

Rocky Mountains
Location: North America
Countries: USA, Canada
Highest Peak: Mount Elbert (4,401 m)
Feature: Continental Divide follows Rockies
Alps
Location: Europe
Countries: Switzerland, Italy, France, Austria
Highest Peak: Mont Blanc (4,809 m)
Feature: Popular for skiing and mountaineering
Ural Mountains
Location: Russia
Significance: Traditional boundary between Europe and Asia
Feature: Rich in mineral resources

Mountain Memory Trick

"H A R A U A"

Himalayas – Andes – Rockies – Alps – Urals – Atlas

Remember: "HAR-AUA" for major world mountain ranges

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which is the largest hot desert in the world?

Sahara Desert (North Africa)

2. Which desert is the driest in the world?

Atacama Desert (Chile, South America)

3. Which is the longest river in the world?

Nile River (Africa)

4. Which river has the largest water volume?

Amazon River (South America)

5. Which is the longest mountain range in the world?

Andes Mountains (South America)

6. Which mountains form the traditional boundary between Europe and Asia?

Ural Mountains (Russia)

7. What is the highest mountain peak in the world?

Mount Everest (Himalayas, Nepal/China)

8. Where is the Gobi Desert located?

China and Mongolia (Asia)

9. Which river flows through London?

Thames River (United Kingdom)

10. The Alps mountain range is located in which continent?

Europe

Conclusion

Mastering the world's major deserts, rivers, and mountain ranges is crucial for world geography questions in SSC exams. These physical features shape climate, human settlement, and economic activities across continents.

Final Summary

Largest hot desert: Sahara (Africa)
Driest desert: Atacama (South America)
Longest river: Nile (Africa)
Largest river by volume: Amazon (South America)
Highest mountains: Himalayas (Asia)
Longest mountain range: Andes (South America)
Europe-Asia boundary: Ural Mountains
Memory tricks: SAGKAG (deserts), NAY MD V MTR (rivers), HAR-AUA (mountains)
