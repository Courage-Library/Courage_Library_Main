Latitude, Longitude & Time Zones

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Geography

Earth's Grid System

To locate any place on Earth precisely, we use an imaginary grid system consisting of latitudes (horizontal lines) and longitudes (vertical lines).

SSC MTS frequently asks about latitudes, longitudes, time zones, and time difference calculations. This is a scoring topic!

Latitudes

Horizontal lines measuring distance North/South of Equator

Parallel lines, never meet

Longitudes

Vertical lines measuring distance East/West of Prime Meridian

Meet at poles, determine time zones

1. Latitudes

  • Horizontal lines running East-West
  • Measure distance North or South of Equator
  • Range: 0° to 90° N and 0° to 90° S
  • All latitudes are parallel → never meet
  • Also called parallels

Think of latitudes as layers on a cake – equally spaced horizontal circles around Earth.

Important Latitudinal Lines

Latitude Name Significance
0° Equator Divides Earth into Northern & Southern Hemispheres
23½° N Tropic of Cancer Passes through India, northernmost point where Sun is directly overhead
23½° S Tropic of Capricorn Southernmost point where Sun is directly overhead
66½° N Arctic Circle Boundary of Arctic region, land of midnight sun
66½° S Antarctic Circle Boundary of Antarctic region
90° N North Pole Northernmost point on Earth
90° S South Pole Southernmost point on Earth

VERY IMPORTANT for SSC: Tropic of Cancer (23½° N) passes through India.

2. Longitudes

  • Vertical lines running North-South
  • Measure distance East or West of Prime Meridian
  • Range: 0° to 180° E and 0° to 180° W
  • All longitudes meet at the poles
  • Also called meridians

Longitude determines time zones – the key to understanding global time differences.

Prime Meridian (0° Longitude)

  • Passes through Greenwich, London (UK)
  • International reference line for longitude
  • World time (GMT/UTC) starts here
  • Divides Earth into Eastern & Western Hemispheres

Memory Trick

"Latitudes are FLAT (horizontal)"

"Longitudes are LONG (vertical)"

Easy way to remember: Latitudes = parallel, Longitudes = converge at poles.

3. Time Zones & Longitude Relationship

Earth Rotation Calculations

Earth rotates 360° in

24 hours

Therefore, 1 hour =

360° ÷ 24 = 15°

Therefore, 1° longitude =

4 minutes

CRUCIAL FOR SSC: 1° longitude = 4 minutes time difference

Time Difference Formula (Important for SSC)

Time difference = Difference in longitude × 4 minutes

Key Rule:

  • Earth rotates West → East
  • If you go East, time increases (ahead)
  • If you go West, time decreases (behind)

Example Calculation

Between 45°E and 15°E:

  • Longitude difference = 45° - 15° = 30°
  • Time difference = 30 × 4 = 120 minutes
  • 120 minutes = 2 hours
  • 45°E is 2 hours ahead of 15°E

4. Indian Standard Time (IST)

Key Facts about IST

  • India has only one time zone
  • Based on 82.5° East longitude (Standard Meridian of India)
  • IST = UTC + 5 hours 30 minutes
  • No daylight saving time in India
  • Covers entire country despite large east-west extent

Why 82.5°E was chosen?

  • Almost middle of India (west to east)
  • Minimizes time difference across states
  • Passes near Allahabad (Prayagraj), Uttar Pradesh
  • Reduces confusion in railway and communication systems

5. Multiple Time Zones & International Date Line

Why Some Countries Have Multiple Time Zones?

Large countries with significant east-west spread need multiple time zones to keep local time aligned with solar time.

USA

6 time zones

Russia

11 time zones

Canada

6 time zones

Australia

3 time zones

International Date Line (IDL)

  • Located at approximately 180° longitude
  • Opposite side of Earth from Prime Meridian
  • Where date changes occur
  • Not a straight line – zigzags to avoid land

Crossing the Date Line

East → West

Add 1 day

West → East

Subtract 1 day

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. The Earth is divided into time zones based on?

Longitude (1° = 4 minutes time difference)

2. 1° longitude equals how many minutes of time difference?

4 minutes

3. Which longitude is taken as Indian Standard Time (IST)?

82.5° East longitude

4. Tropic of Cancer is located at which latitude?

23½° North latitude (passes through India)

5. The Prime Meridian passes through which location?

Greenwich, London (United Kingdom)

6. Lines parallel to the Equator are called?

Latitudes or Parallels

7. Which line divides Earth into two equal hemispheres?

Equator (0° latitude)

8. If you travel east, what happens to local time?

Time increases (you gain time, ahead of starting point)

Conclusion

You now understand the essential concepts of latitudes, longitudes, and time zones: Latitudes determine climate, longitudes determine time, 1° = 4 minutes, IST = 82.5°E, and time increases to the east. These fundamentals are crucial for SSC geography questions.

Key Formulas & Rules to Remember

1° longitude = 4 minutes time difference
Time difference = Longitude difference × 4 minutes
East → Time increases (ahead)
West → Time decreases (behind)
IST = 82.5°E = UTC + 5:30
Tropic of Cancer = 23½°N (through India)
