Rivers (World & India)

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Geography

Introduction

Understanding major rivers of the world and India is crucial for geography questions in SSC exams. This chapter covers river lengths, origins, tributaries, and classifications.

SSC frequently asks about longest rivers, river sources, tributaries, and classification of Indian rivers into Himalayan and Peninsular systems.

PART 2: Rivers of India

India has two major river systems: Himalayan Rivers (snow-fed, perennial) and Peninsular Rivers (rain-fed, seasonal).

Himalayan Rivers

  • Originate from glaciers in Himalayas
  • Perennial flow (year-round water)
  • Major systems: Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra
  • Long courses with large basins

Peninsular Rivers

  • Originate from plateaus and hills
  • Seasonal flow (mainly monsoon)
  • Shorter courses compared to Himalayan rivers
  • Classified as East-flowing and West-flowing

A. Himalayan Rivers of India

1. Indus River System

  • Origin: Tibet (Lake Mansarovar)
  • Flow: Ladakh → Pakistan
  • Ends in: Arabian Sea
  • Length: 3,180 km (mostly in Pakistan)

Major Tributaries (VERY IMPORTANT):

Jhelum
Chenab
Ravi
Beas
Sutlej

Memory Trick for Indus Tributaries

"J C R B S"

"Just Check River By Size"

Jhelum → Chenab → Ravi → Beas → Sutlej

2. Ganga River System

  • Origin: Gangotri Glacier (Bhagirathi)
  • Confluence: Meets Alaknanda at Devprayag → Forms Ganga
  • Flow: UP → Bihar → West Bengal
  • Ends in: Bay of Bengal (as Hooghly + Padma)
  • Length: 2,525 km (Longest river in India)

Left-bank Tributaries

Gandak, Kosi, Ghaghara, Ramganga

Right-bank Tributaries

Yamuna, Son, Tons

Memory Trick: Left = "GKGK", Right = "YST"

3. Brahmaputra River System

  • Origin: Tibet (as Tsangpo)
  • Enters India: Arunachal (as Siang/Dihang)
  • Flow: Assam → Bangladesh
  • Ends in: Bay of Bengal
  • Length: 2,900 km

Known as "Sorrow of Assam" due to frequent devastating floods.

Forms the world's largest delta (Sunderbans) along with Ganga.

Carries large sediment load, making its water muddy.

B. Peninsular Rivers of India

Rivers Flowing into Bay of Bengal (East-flowing)

Godavari

  • Longest Peninsular river (1,465 km)
  • Called "Dakshin Ganga"
  • Origin: Maharashtra (Trimbakeshwar)
  • Ends in: Bay of Bengal
  • Drains parts of Maharashtra, Telangana, AP

Krishna

  • Origin: Mahabaleshwar (Maharashtra)
  • Ends in: Bay of Bengal
  • Major tributaries: Bhima, Tungabhadra
  • Second longest Peninsular river

Kaveri

  • Origin: Brahmagiri Hills (Karnataka)
  • Ends in: Bay of Bengal
  • Famous waterfalls: Shivasamudram
  • Known as "Ganga of the South"

Mahanadi

  • Origin: Chhattisgarh
  • Ends in: Bay of Bengal
  • Major river of Odisha and Chhattisgarh
  • Forms Hirakud Dam reservoir

Memory Trick for East-flowing Rivers

"GKM M"

Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri, Mahanadi

Rivers Flowing into Arabian Sea (West-flowing)

Narmada

  • Origin: Amarkantak (Madhya Pradesh)
  • Flows through Rift Valley (very important)
  • Ends in: Arabian Sea
  • Also called "Reva" and "Life Line of Madhya Pradesh"

Tapi (Tapti)

  • Origin: Satpura Range (Madhya Pradesh)
  • Flows through: Maharashtra, Gujarat
  • Ends in: Arabian Sea
  • Parallel to Narmada but south of it

Memory Trick for West-flowing Rivers

"NT"

Narmada, Tapi

Inland Rivers (do not reach sea)

Luni: Rajasthan → Rann of Kutch

Ghaggar: Seasonal river flowing through Haryana, Rajasthan

These rivers disappear into desert or inland basins without reaching any sea.

Major Indian River Classification

Category Rivers Sea/Destination
Himalayan Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra Arabian Sea / Bay of Bengal
Peninsular (East-flowing) Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri, Mahanadi Bay of Bengal
Peninsular (West-flowing) Narmada, Tapi Arabian Sea
Inland Luni, Ghaggar No sea (disappear inland)

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which is the longest river in the world?

Nile River (6,650 km) in Africa

2. Which is the longest river in India?

Ganga (2,525 km)

3. Which is the longest Peninsular river of India?

Godavari (1,465 km)

4. Where does the Indus River originate?

Tibet (Lake Mansarovar)

5. Where does the Ganga originate?

Gangotri Glacier (Bhagirathi)

6. Name the major west-flowing rivers of India.

Narmada and Tapi (Tapti)

7. What is Brahmaputra called in Tibet?

Tsangpo

8. Which rivers flow through Rift Valley in India?

Narmada and Tapi

9. Which river is called "Dakshin Ganga"?

Godavari

10. Which river forms the Sunderbans delta?

Ganga–Brahmaputra system

Conclusion

This comprehensive guide covers major world rivers and detailed classification of Indian rivers. Remember key distinctions between Himalayan (perennial, long) and Peninsular (seasonal, shorter) rivers, along with their tributaries and destinations.
