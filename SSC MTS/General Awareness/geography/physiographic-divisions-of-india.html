Physiographic Divisions of India

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Geography

Introduction

India is divided into six major physiographic divisions based on physical features. Understanding these divisions is crucial for SSC geography questions as they form the foundation of India's physical geography.

SSC MTS frequently asks about Himalayan ranges, plateaus, coastal plains, deserts, and island groups.

1. The Himalayan Mountains

Young Fold Mountains

The Himalayas are young fold mountains formed about 40 million years ago.

They stretch across northern India, acting as a climate barrier and source of major rivers.

Three parallel ranges from north to south: Himadri → Himachal → Shiwalik

Three Parallel Ranges (North to South)

A. Himadri (Greater Himalayas)

The highest and northernmost range with permanent snow cover.

Average height: 6000 meters
Contains Mount Everest, Kanchenjunga
Source of Ganga, Yamuna, Brahmaputra
Major glaciers: Gangotri, Siachen

Exam Tip

Himadri = Highest = Contains highest peaks

Remember: "Himadri has the highest altitude"

B. Himachal (Lesser Himalayas)

The middle range known for hill stations and valleys.

Famous Hill Stations:

Shimla
Mussoorie
Nainital
Darjeeling

Important valleys: Kashmir, Kullu, Kangra

Memory Trick

"H for Hill Stations"

Himachal = Hill Stations

Most famous tourist destinations are here

C. Shiwaliks (Outer Himalayas)

The lowest and southernmost range, consisting of foothills.

Height: 900-1100 meters
Made of loose sediments and alluvium
Prone to landslides and soil erosion
Forms the transition to plains

Range Trick

"H-H-S"

Himadri → Himachal → Shiwalik

North to South: High → Middle → Small

2. Northern Plains

Fertile Alluvial Plains

Formed by river deposits (alluvium) of the Indus, Ganga, and Brahmaputra systems.

One of the world's most fertile regions and most densely populated areas.

Characteristics: Flat terrain, fertile soil, extensive agriculture, high population density.

Regions of Northern Plains

Punjab Plains

Formed by Indus river system
States: Punjab, Haryana
Known as "Granary of India"
Main crops: Wheat, rice

Ganga Plains

Largest part of northern plains
States: UP, Bihar, West Bengal
Most densely populated
River: Ganga and tributaries

Brahmaputra Plains

Easternmost part
State: Assam
Heavy rainfall, tea plantations
River: Brahmaputra

Memory Trick

"P-G-B"

Punjab → Ganga → Brahmaputra

West to East sequence of northern plains

3. Peninsular Plateau

Oldest Landmass of India

Formed from the ancient Gondwana land, composed of igneous and metamorphic rocks.

Triangular in shape, bordered by mountain ranges on three sides.

Characteristics: Oldest geological structure, rich in minerals, varied topography.

Major Plateaus

Deccan Plateau
Largest in India
Volcanic basalt rocks
Covered by black soil
Chota Nagpur Plateau
Rich in minerals
Called "Mineral Heartland"
States: Jharkhand, Bihar
Malwa Plateau
Central India
Drained by Chambal river
States: MP, Rajasthan

Western & Eastern Ghats

Western Ghats
Higher and continuous
Cause heavy rainfall (windward side)
Biodiversity hotspot
Highest peak: Anamudi
Eastern Ghats
Lower and broken
Receive less rainfall
Discontinuous hills
Rivers cut through them

Memory Trick: "West = Higher, East = Lower"

4. Indian Desert (Thar Desert)

The Great Indian Desert

Located in western Rajasthan, extending into Pakistan.

Characterized by sand dunes, extreme temperatures, and low rainfall.

Features: Sandy soil, arid climate, sparse vegetation, unique desert ecosystem.

Characteristics
Area: ~200,000 sq km
Rainfall: Less than 150 mm annually
Temperature: Up to 50°C in summer
Vegetation: Thorny bushes, cacti
Important Facts
Only river: Luni (inland drainage)
Major cities: Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, Bikaner
Indira Gandhi Canal: Provides irrigation
Desert National Park: Wildlife sanctuary

Memory Trick

"L + U + N + I"

Least Useful for farming → Luni river flows here

The only significant river in the Thar Desert

5. Coastal Plains

India's Coastal Regions

India has approximately 7,500 km of coastline along the Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal.

Divided into western and eastern coastal plains with distinct characteristics.

Major ports, fishing harbors, and tourist beaches are located along these coasts.

Western vs Eastern Coastal Plains

Western Coastal Plains

Narrow (50-80 km wide)
Steep slope into Arabian Sea
Subdivisions: Konkan, Kannada, Malabar
Major ports: Mumbai, Kochi, Goa
Backwaters and lagoons

Eastern Coastal Plains

Wide (100-130 km wide)
Gentle slope into Bay of Bengal
Subdivisions: Coromandel, Northern Circar
Major ports: Chennai, Visakhapatnam, Kolkata
Large deltas: Ganga, Godavari, Krishna

Sunderban Delta (World's Largest)

Formed by the Ganga and Brahmaputra rivers in West Bengal and Bangladesh.

Area: ~10,000 sq km
World's largest mangrove forest
Home to Royal Bengal Tiger
UNESCO World Heritage Site
Memory Trick

"East = Wide, West = Narrow"

Eastern plains are wider than western plains

6. Islands

India's Island Groups

India has two major island groups: Andaman & Nicobar in Bay of Bengal and Lakshadweep in Arabian Sea.

These islands have strategic importance and unique ecosystems.

Andaman & Nicobar: Volcanic origin. Lakshadweep: Coral origin.

Major Island Groups

A. Andaman & Nicobar Islands

Location: Bay of Bengal
Origin: Volcanic
Capital: Port Blair
Barren Island: India's only active volcano
Indira Point: Southernmost point of India
Tribal groups: Jarawa, Sentinelese

Memory Trick

"A for Active Volcano"

Andaman = Active volcano (Barren Island)

Volcanic origin, strategic location

B. Lakshadweep Islands

Location: Arabian Sea
Origin: Coral
Capital: Kavaratti
Smallest Union Territory
No hill ranges, all low-lying
Coral reefs and lagoons

Memory Trick

"L for Low Coral"

Lakshadweep = Low coral islands

Coral origin, smallest UT

Quick Revision Table

Division Key Features Examples/Importance
Himalayas Young fold mountains, 3 parallel ranges Himadri, Himachal, Shiwalik
Northern Plains Alluvial, fertile, densely populated Ganga, Indus, Brahmaputra plains
Peninsular Plateau Oldest landmass, mineral-rich Deccan, Chota Nagpur, Malwa
Indian Desert Arid, sandy, low rainfall Thar Desert, Luni river
Coastal Plains Western (narrow), Eastern (wide) Konkan, Coromandel, Sunderbans

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which are the youngest mountains in India?

Himalayas (fold mountains formed 40 million years ago)

2. Which plateau is known as the mineral heartland of India?

Chota Nagpur Plateau

3. Which is the only active volcano in India?

Barren Island (Andaman & Nicobar Islands)

4. Which coastal plain is wider?

Eastern Coastal Plain (100-130 km wide)

5. Which river flows through the Thar Desert?

Luni River (inland drainage)

6. What is the origin of Lakshadweep Islands?

Coral origin

7. Which is the highest range of the Himalayas?

Himadri (Greater Himalayas)

8. Which plains are formed by river deposits?

Northern Plains (alluvial plains)

Himadri → Himachal → Shiwalik

North to South sequence

Northern Plains

Punjab → Ganga → Brahmaputra

West to East sequence

Islands

"A-L"

Andaman = Active volcano

Lakshadweep = Low coral

Conclusion

Mastering India's six physiographic divisions provides a solid foundation for understanding the country's geography. Each division has unique characteristics, formation processes, and significance for human activities and economic development.

Final Summary

Mountains → Himalayas (young fold)
Plains → Northern (fertile alluvial)
Plateau → Peninsular (oldest, mineral-rich)
Desert → Thar (arid, Luni river)
Coasts → Western (narrow), Eastern (wide)
Islands → Andaman (volcanic), Lakshadweep (coral)
