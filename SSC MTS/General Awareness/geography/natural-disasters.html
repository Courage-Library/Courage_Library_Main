Natural Disasters - Earthquakes & Volcanoes

World Geography - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction

Understanding earthquakes and volcanoes is crucial for SSC's geography section. This chapter covers tectonic movements, disaster zones, measurement scales, and high-frequency facts.

SSC frequently asks about earthquake zones, volcano types, measurement instruments, and geographical locations of major disasters.

PART 1: Earthquakes

Sudden Shaking of Earth's Crust

Earthquakes occur due to the movement of tectonic plates. When plates collide, move apart, or slide past each other, stored energy releases suddenly.

Collision

Plates pushing together

Divergence

Plates moving apart

Transform

Plates sliding past

Key Earthquake Terms (Important for SSC)

Focus (Hypocenter):

Point inside Earth where earthquake originates.

Epicenter:

Point on Earth's surface directly above the focus.

Seismograph:

Instrument that records earthquake waves.

Richter Scale:

Measures earthquake magnitude (logarithmic scale).

P-waves (Primary):

Fastest seismic waves, travel through solids & liquids.

S-waves (Secondary):

Slower waves, travel only through solids.

Types of Earthquakes

Tectonic Earthquakes:

Most common type, caused by plate movement.

Volcanic Earthquakes:

Occur in association with volcanic eruptions.

Collapse Earthquakes:

Caused by mining activities or cave collapses.

Human-Induced Earthquakes:

Result from dams, oil extraction, or nuclear tests.

PART 2: Volcanoes

Opening in Earth's Crust

A volcano is an opening where molten rock (magma), ash, and gases erupt from below Earth's surface. Formed at tectonic plate boundaries or hotspots.

Types of Volcanoes

1. Shield Volcano
Shape: Broad, gentle slopes
Eruption: Low viscosity lava flows
Example: Mauna Loa (Hawaii)
Characteristic: Largest volcanoes by area
2. Composite Volcano (Stratovolcano)
Shape: Steep, cone-shaped
Eruption: Violent, explosive
Example: Mount Fuji (Japan)
Characteristic: Most dangerous type
3. Cinder Cone Volcano
Shape: Small, steep-sided
Eruption: Ash and cinders
Example: Paricutin (Mexico)
Characteristic: Short-lived eruptions
4. Caldera
Shape: Large crater/depression
Formation: Collapse after massive eruption
Example: Yellowstone (USA)
Characteristic: Supervolcano potential

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. What is the point inside Earth where an earthquake originates called?

Focus (Hypocenter)

2. Which instrument records earthquake waves?

Seismograph

3. Which region has the world's most active earthquake zone?

Ring of Fire (Circum-Pacific Belt)

4. What is India's only active volcano?

Barren Island (Andaman & Nicobar Islands)

5. Mount Fuji in Japan is which type of volcano?

Composite Volcano (Stratovolcano)

6. Which earthquake zone in India has the highest risk?

Zone V (Very High Risk)

7. Which scale measures earthquake magnitude?

Richter Scale

8. Which volcano destroyed Pompeii in 79 AD?

Mount Vesuvius (Italy)

9. Which seismic waves travel fastest?

P-waves (Primary waves)

10. The Himalayan region belongs to which earthquake belt?

Alpine-Himalayan Belt

Conclusion

Mastering earthquakes and volcanoes is essential for SSC's geography section. Focus on key terms, geographical zones, measurement methods, and famous examples.

Key Takeaways

Earthquake Focus vs Epicenter: Focus is inside, epicenter is on surface
Ring of Fire: Contains 75% of world's volcanoes and most earthquakes
India Zones: Zone V (highest risk) includes NE states and Himalayas
Volcano Types: Shield (gentle), Composite (violent), Cinder Cone (small)
Instruments: Seismograph for earthquakes, VEI scale for volcanoes
Indian Volcano: Barren Island is India's only active volcano
Memory Tricks: "F–S–C–V" for volcanoes, "RING → FIRE → QUAKE" for zones
