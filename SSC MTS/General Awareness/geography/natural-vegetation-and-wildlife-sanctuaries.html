Natural Vegetation & Wildlife Sanctuaries of India

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Geography

Introduction

India's diverse climate and topography support a wide variety of natural vegetation types and rich wildlife. Understanding these ecosystems, along with major national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, is essential for SSC environment and geography sections.

SSC frequently asks about forest types, characteristic species, important wildlife sanctuaries, and their locations.

PART 1: Natural Vegetation of India

Factors Influencing Vegetation

Natural vegetation depends on rainfall, temperature, soil type, and relief features.

Rainfall: Most critical factor
Temperature: Affects plant growth patterns
Soil: Determines nutrient availability
Relief: Altitude and slope affect vegetation

India has five major vegetation types, each adapted to specific climatic conditions.

1. Tropical Evergreen Forests (Rainforests)

"Evergreen = Always Green = Always Rain" - Receive rainfall throughout the year.

Rainfall: >200 cm annually
Features: Dense, multi-layered, no definite leaf shedding season
Major Trees: Ebony, Mahogany, Rosewood, Rubber
Common Animals: Elephant, Lion-tailed macaque, Malabar squirrel
Areas Found
Western Ghats (Kerala, Karnataka)
Andaman & Nicobar Islands
North-Eastern states (Assam, Nagaland, Mizoram)
Coastal regions of Odisha, Tamil Nadu

Memory Trick: "EVERGREEN = Western Ghats + North-East"

2. Tropical Deciduous Forests (Monsoon Forests)

"Deciduous = Drop Leaves" - Most widespread forest type in India.

Rainfall: 100–200 cm annually
Features: Trees shed leaves in dry season, open canopy
Major Trees: Teak, Sal, Shisham, Neem, Bamboo
Common Animals: Tiger, Deer, Leopard, Elephants, Birds
Areas Found
Madhya Pradesh
Jharkhand & Chhattisgarh
Uttar Pradesh & Bihar
Odisha & parts of Maharashtra

Memory Trick: "Deciduous = Maximum area = Central India"

3. Thorn Forests & Scrubs

"Thorn = Desert Climate" - Adapted to arid conditions.

Rainfall: <70 cm annually
Features: Thorny bushes, deep roots, small leaves
Major Plants: Babool, Kikar, Cactus, Date palm, Wild palms
Common Animals: Camel, Blackbuck, Desert fox, Rabbits
Areas Found
Rajasthan (Thar Desert)
Gujarat
Haryana & Punjab (dry areas)
Deccan plateau (rain shadow areas)

4. Mountain Forests (Himalayan Forests)

"Height decides type" - Vegetation changes with altitude.

1000-2000m: Temperate broadleaf (Oak, Chestnut)
1500-3000m: Temperate coniferous (Pine, Deodar, Fir)
3000-4000m: Alpine shrubs (Rhododendron, Juniper)
>4000m: Alpine meadows, mosses, lichens
Common Animals: Snow leopard, Yak, Musk deer, Red panda
Key Features
Found in Himalayas, Western Ghats, Nilgiris
Temperature decreases with altitude
Important for watershed protection
Rich in medicinal plants

5. Mangrove Forests (Tidal Forests)

"Mangrove = Marshy = Coast + Delta" - Grow in saline coastal waters.

Features: Breathing roots (pneumatophores), salt tolerance
Major Trees: Sundari (hence Sundarbans), Rhizophora
Ecosystem Services: Coastal protection, fish breeding grounds
Common Animals: Bengal tiger, Crocodile, Fishing cat, Birds
Areas Found
Sundarbans (Ganga-Brahmaputra delta)
Godavari & Krishna deltas
Andaman & Nicobar Islands
Gulf of Kutch, Gulf of Khambhat

Sundarbans: World's largest mangrove forest, UNESCO World Heritage Site

Super Memory Trick

"E–D–T–M–M"

Evergreen → Deciduous → Thorn → Mountain → Mangrove

Sequence of vegetation types from highest to lowest rainfall areas

Major Wildlife Sanctuaries (SSC Important)

1. Jim Corbett National Park

State: Uttarakhand
Established: 1936 (First national park of India)
Famous for: Bengal Tiger
River: Ramganga flows through park

2. Kaziranga National Park

State: Assam
UNESCO: World Heritage Site
Famous for: One-horned Rhinoceros
Also has: Tigers, Elephants, Wild water buffalo

3. Gir National Park

State: Gujarat
Unique: Only home of Asiatic Lions
Also has: Leopards, Antelopes
Conservation: Successful lion conservation program

4. Sundarbans National Park

State: West Bengal
UNESCO: World Heritage Site
Famous for: Royal Bengal Tiger
Feature: World's largest mangrove forest

Other Important Protected Areas

Ranthambore NP

State: Rajasthan

Famous: Tiger Reserve

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

State: Kerala

Famous: Elephants & Tigers

Keoladeo Ghana NP

State: Rajasthan

Famous: Migratory birds (UNESCO)

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which is India's first national park?

Jim Corbett National Park, Uttarakhand (established 1936)

2. The one-horned rhinoceros is found in which national park?

Kaziranga National Park, Assam

3. Where are Asiatic lions found in India?

Gir National Park, Gujarat

4. What is Sundarbans National Park famous for?

Royal Bengal Tiger and mangrove forests

5. Thorn forests in India are mainly found in which region?

Rajasthan and Gujarat (arid regions)

6. Where are tropical evergreen forests found in India?

Western Ghats and North-Eastern states

7. Which wildlife sanctuary in Kerala is famous for elephants?

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

8. Alpine vegetation in India is found at what altitude?

Above 3500 meters in the Himalayas

Conclusion

India's rich biodiversity is protected through various natural vegetation zones and wildlife sanctuaries. Understanding these ecosystems, their characteristic species, and important protected areas is crucial for environment-related questions in SSC exams.

Final Summary

5 Vegetation Types: Evergreen, Deciduous, Thorn, Mountain, Mangrove
First NP: Jim Corbett (1936, Uttarakhand)
Only Asiatic Lions: Gir National Park, Gujarat
One-horned Rhino: Kaziranga, Assam
Mangrove Tigers: Sundarbans, West Bengal
Migratory Birds: Keoladeo, Rajasthan
Tiger Reserves: 54 across India, "Project Tiger" conservation
