Major Crops of India – Kharif, Rabi & Zaid

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Geography

Introduction

India's agriculture is characterized by three distinct cropping seasons based on monsoon patterns. Understanding Kharif, Rabi, and Zaid crops, along with their climatic requirements and major producing states, is essential for SSC geography preparation.

SSC frequently asks about crop seasons, climatic conditions, leading producing states, and soil requirements for major crops.

PART 1: Types of Cropping Seasons in India

1. Kharif Crops (Monsoon Crops)

Sown with the onset of monsoon (June-July) and harvested in October-November.

Climate: Hot and wet, rain-fed cultivation
Sowing: June-July
Harvest: October-November
Dependence: Mainly dependent on monsoon rains
Major Kharif Crops
Rice
Maize
Cotton
Groundnut
Jowar
Bajra
Soybean
Sugarcane*

*Sugarcane: Annual crop but mostly sown in Kharif season

Memory Trick for Kharif Crops

"R M J B C G S"

Rice – Maize – Jowar – Bajra – Cotton – Groundnut – Soybean

2. Rabi Crops (Winter Crops)

Sown in winter (October-November) and harvested in March-April.

Climate: Cool and dry, requires irrigation
Sowing: October-November
Harvest: March-April
Dependence: Irrigation or winter rainfall
Major Rabi Crops
Wheat
Barley
Gram (Chana)
Mustard
Peas
Masoor
Oats
Linseed
Memory Trick for Rabi Crops

"W B C M P M"

Wheat – Barley – Chana – Mustard – Peas – Masoor

3. Zaid Crops (Summer Crops)

Short season crops grown between Rabi and Kharif seasons (March-June).

Climate: Hot summer, requires irrigation
Sowing: March-June
Harvest: Before Kharif season
Duration: Short duration crops
Major Zaid Crops
Watermelon
Muskmelon
Cucumber
Vegetables
Fodder crops
Pumpkin

Memory Trick: "Zaid = Juicy Fruits Season"

PART 2: Major Crops of India – Full Details

1. Rice (Kharif Crop)

Temperature: 25–35°C
Rainfall: 100–200 cm (needs standing water)
Soil: Alluvial, clayey soil with good water retention
Special Requirement: Requires flooded fields during growth
Top Producing States
1. West Bengal (Largest producer)
2. Uttar Pradesh
3. Punjab
4. Andhra Pradesh
5. Odisha

2. Wheat (Rabi Crop)

Temperature: 10–15°C (sowing), 21–26°C (harvest)
Rainfall: 50–75 cm (low rainfall suitable)
Soil: Alluvial, black soil (well-drained)
Special Requirement: Cool, dry winter with irrigation
Top Producing States
1. Uttar Pradesh (Largest producer)
2. Punjab
3. Haryana
4. Madhya Pradesh
5. Rajasthan

3. Pulses (Gram, Arhar, Masoor, etc.)

Types: Gram (Rabi), Arhar/Tur (Kharif), Masoor (Rabi)
Climate: Low to moderate rainfall, dry conditions
Soil: Well-drained, sandy loam to clay loam
Special Feature: Nitrogen-fixing, improve soil fertility
Top Producing States
1. Madhya Pradesh (Largest pulses producer)
2. Rajasthan
3. Maharashtra
4. Uttar Pradesh
5. Karnataka

Cash Crops of India

A. Cotton (Kharif Crop)

Temperature: 21–30°C
Rainfall: 50–100 cm (moderate)
Soil: Black soil (Regur soil) ideal
Special Requirement: Clear sunny days during flowering
Top Producing States
1. Maharashtra
2. Gujarat
3. Telangana
4. Andhra Pradesh
5. Rajasthan

B. Sugarcane (Annual/Kharif Crop)

Temperature: 21–27°C
Rainfall: 75–150 cm
Soil: Deep, fertile, well-drained alluvial soil
Special Feature: Annual crop, takes 10-12 months to mature
Top Producing States
1. Uttar Pradesh (Largest producer)
2. Maharashtra
3. Karnataka
4. Tamil Nadu
5. Bihar

C. Tea (Perennial Plantation Crop)

Temperature: 20–30°C
Rainfall: 150–300 cm (heavy, well-distributed)
Soil: Deep, fertile, well-drained acidic soil
Special Requirement: Grown on hill slopes for drainage
Top Producing States
1. Assam (Largest producer)
2. West Bengal (Darjeeling)
3. Tamil Nadu (Nilgiris)
4. Kerala
5. Himachal Pradesh

D. Coffee (Perennial Plantation Crop)

Temperature: 15–28°C
Rainfall: 150–250 cm
Soil: Rich, well-drained, volcanic soil ideal
Special Requirement: Shade-grown, protection from direct sun
Top Producing States
1. Karnataka (Largest producer)
2. Kerala
3. Tamil Nadu

Difference Between Kharif, Rabi & Zaid Crops

Type Sowing Season Harvest Season Climate Examples
Kharif June-July October-November Hot & wet, monsoon dependent Rice, Maize, Cotton, Groundnut
Rabi October-November March-April Cool & dry, irrigation needed Wheat, Barley, Mustard, Gram
Zaid March-June Before Kharif season Hot summer, irrigated Watermelon, Muskmelon, Cucumber

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which of these is a Kharif crop?

Rice, Maize, Cotton, Groundnut, Soybean

2. Which of these is a Rabi crop?

Wheat, Barley, Gram (Chana), Mustard, Peas

3. What are Zaid crops?

Short season crops grown between Rabi and Kharif (Watermelon, Muskmelon, Cucumber)

4. What climate is best for wheat cultivation?

Cool and dry climate with irrigation (Rabi season)

5. What are the requirements for rice cultivation?

High rainfall (100-200 cm), standing water, alluvial soil, hot climate

6. Which is India's largest wheat-producing state?

Uttar Pradesh

7. Which is India's largest rice-producing state?

West Bengal

8. What are the ideal conditions for tea cultivation?

Heavy rainfall, humid climate, hill slopes, well-drained acidic soil

9. Which soil is best for cotton cultivation?

Black soil (Regur soil)

10. Which state is the largest producer of pulses in India?

Madhya Pradesh

Conclusion

Understanding India's agricultural seasons and major crops is fundamental to grasping the country's economy and geography. The Kharif-Rabi-Zaid cycle, along with crop-specific requirements and producing states, forms a crucial part of SSC geography syllabus.

Final Summary

Kharif: Monsoon crops (Rice, Cotton, Maize)
Rabi: Winter crops (Wheat, Barley, Mustard)
Zaid: Summer crops (Watermelon, Cucumber)
Largest Wheat: Uttar Pradesh
Largest Rice: West Bengal
Largest Pulses: Madhya Pradesh
Cotton Soil: Black soil
Tea Climate: Hill slopes, heavy rain
Sugarcane: Annual crop, UP largest producer
