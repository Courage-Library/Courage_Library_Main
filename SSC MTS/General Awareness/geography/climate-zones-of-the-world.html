Climate Zones of the World

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - World Geography

Introduction

Understanding world climate zones is essential for grasping global weather patterns, vegetation, and human adaptations. This chapter covers the three major climate zones and their sub-types, with key regions and characteristics.

SSC frequently asks about climate zone locations, characteristics, vegetation types, and regional examples.

Three Major Climate Zones (Based on Latitude)

World Climate Zones

T
Tropical Zone

0°–23.5° Latitude

Hot climate near Equator

T
Temperate Zone

23.5°–66.5° Latitude

Moderate climate

P
Polar Zone

66.5°–90° Latitude

Cold climate near poles

PART 1: Tropical Climate Zone (0°–23.5°)

Hot Zone near the Equator

Also called the Torrid Zone, characterized by high temperatures throughout the year.

Located between Tropic of Cancer and Tropic of Capricorn
Sun overhead at least once a year
High solar radiation, minimal seasonal variation
Three Main Types
Rainforest
Savanna
Desert

Tropical Zone Trick: "R–G–D" → Rainforest – Grassland (Savanna) – Desert

1. Equatorial Climate (Tropical Rainforest)

Location: 0°–10° latitude, near Equator
Temperature: Hot year-round (25°–27°C average)
Rainfall: Heavy, daily convectional rainfall (>2000 mm annually)
Vegetation: Dense evergreen rainforests (Amazon, Congo)
Regions & Examples
Amazon Basin (Brazil, Peru, Colombia)
Congo Basin (Central Africa)
Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines
Parts of Central America

2. Savanna Climate (Tropical Grasslands)

Location: 10°–23.5° latitude, edges of tropical zone
Temperature: Hot with distinct seasons
Rainfall: Seasonal, wet & dry seasons (500–1500 mm annually)
Vegetation: Tall grasses with scattered trees
Regions & Examples
African Savanna (Kenya, Tanzania, Sudan)
Brazilian Highlands (Cerrado)
Northern Australia
Venezuela, Colombia (Llanos)

Known as: Savanna (Africa), Cerrado (Brazil), Llanos (Venezuela)

3. Tropical Desert Climate

Location: 15°–30° latitude, western sides of continents
Temperature: Extreme, hot days, cold nights
Rainfall: Very low (<250 mm annually), irregular
Vegetation: Sparse, xerophytic plants (cacti, thorny bushes)
Regions & Examples
Sahara Desert (North Africa)
Arabian Desert (Middle East)
Thar Desert (India/Pakistan)
Atacama Desert (Chile - driest)

PART 2: Temperate Climate Zone (23.5°–66.5°)

Moderate Zone with Four Seasons

Characterized by distinct seasons: spring, summer, autumn, winter.

Located between tropics and polar circles
Moderate temperatures, seasonal variations
Most densely populated zone
Main Types
Mediterranean
Grassland
Oceanic

Temperate Zone Trick: "M–G–O" → Mediterranean – Grassland – Oceanic

4. Mediterranean Climate

Location: 30°–45° latitude, western sides of continents
Temperature: Hot dry summers, mild wet winters
Rainfall: Winter rainfall, summer drought
Vegetation: Chaparral, maquis, sclerophyllous plants
Regions & Examples
Mediterranean Basin (Spain, Italy, Greece)
California (USA)
Central Chile
Southwestern Australia
Cape Town region (South Africa)

5. Temperate Grassland (Steppe/Prairie)

Location: Interior of continents, 30°–55° latitude
Temperature: Hot summers, cold winters, extreme range
Rainfall: Moderate (250–500 mm), insufficient for forests
Vegetation: Tall and short grasses, no trees
Regions & Examples
Prairies (North America - USA/Canada)
Steppes (Eurasia - Russia, Kazakhstan)
Pampas (South America - Argentina)
Velds (South Africa)
Downs (Australia)

6. Temperate Oceanic (Marine West Coast)

Location: 40°–60° latitude, western coasts
Temperature: Cool summers, mild winters, small range
Rainfall: Year-round, cyclonic (750–1500 mm)
Vegetation: Deciduous forests, mixed forests
Regions & Examples
Northwest Europe (UK, Ireland, France, Germany)
Pacific Northwest (USA/Canada)
Southern Chile
New Zealand
Tasmania (Australia)

PART 3: Polar Climate Zone (66.5°–90°)

Cold Zone near the Poles

Characterized by extremely low temperatures, ice, and snow cover for most of the year.

Located within Arctic and Antarctic Circles
Midnight sun in summer, polar night in winter
Sparse human population
Two Main Types
Tundra
Icecap

Polar Zone Trick: "T–I" → Tundra – Icecap

7. Tundra Climate

Location: 60°–75° latitude, Arctic fringes
Temperature: Very cold, short cool summers (-30°C to 10°C)
Rainfall: Low (150–250 mm), mostly as snow
Vegetation: Mosses, lichens, dwarf shrubs, no trees
Regions & Examples
Northern Canada, Alaska
Northern Russia, Siberia
Greenland coastal areas
Northern Scandinavia
Antarctic Peninsula (mildest parts)

8. Icecap Climate (Polar Climate)

Location: >75° latitude, polar regions
Temperature: Permanently below freezing (-40°C to 0°C)
Rainfall: Very low (<100 mm), all as snow
Surface: Permanent ice and snow cover
Vegetation: None, except algae/lichen on rocks
Regions & Examples
Antarctica interior
Greenland interior
Arctic Ocean (sea ice)
High altitude ice caps

Vostok Station, Antarctica: Coldest temperature recorded: -89.2°C (-128.6°F)

Full Climate Zone Summary

Zone Climate Types Key Areas Characteristics
Tropical Rainforest, Savanna, Desert Amazon, Congo, Sahara Hot year-round, high solar radiation
Temperate Mediterranean, Grassland, Oceanic Europe, USA, S. Africa Four seasons, moderate temperatures
Polar Tundra, Icecap Arctic, Antarctica Extremely cold, ice/snow cover

Super Memory Trick (All Zones)

"R-G-D / M-G-O / T-I"

Tropical Zone

R = Rainforest

G = Grassland (Savanna)

D = Desert

Temperate Zone

M = Mediterranean

G = Grassland (Steppe)

O = Oceanic

Polar Zone

T = Tundra

I = Icecap

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. The Equatorial climate is found near which geographical line?

Equator (0° latitude)

2. What are the tropical grasslands in Africa called?

Savanna

3. Which climate has hot dry summers and mild rainy winters?

Mediterranean Climate

4. What type of vegetation is found in Tundra climate?

No trees, only mosses, lichens, and dwarf shrubs

5. Which climate zone is the coldest?

Polar Zone

6. The Prairies of North America are found in which climate zone?

Temperate Grassland (Temperate Zone)

7. The Atacama Desert (driest desert) lies in which climate zone?

Tropical Desert (Tropical Zone)

8. Which climate type supports tropical rainforests?

Equatorial Climate (Tropical Rainforest)

9. The UK experiences which type of temperate climate?

Temperate Oceanic (Marine West Coast)

10. What are the grasslands in Argentina called?

Pampas (Temperate Grassland)

Conclusion

Understanding the world's climate zones provides insight into global weather patterns, vegetation types, and human settlement patterns. The three major zones—Tropical, Temperate, and Polar—each have distinct characteristics that influence life on Earth.

Final Summary

Tropical Zone: 0°–23.5°, hot year-round (Rainforest, Savanna, Desert)
Temperate Zone: 23.5°–66.5°, four seasons (Mediterranean, Grassland, Oceanic)
Polar Zone: 66.5°–90°, extremely cold (Tundra, Icecap)
Equatorial Climate: Amazon, Congo basins, heavy rainfall
Mediterranean Climate: Hot dry summers, mild wet winters
Tundra Climate: No trees, permafrost, Arctic regions
Memory Trick: "R-G-D / M-G-O / T-I" for all climate types
Most populated: Temperate Zone; Least populated: Polar Zone
