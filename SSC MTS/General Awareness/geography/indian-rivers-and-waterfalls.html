Indian Rivers & Waterfalls

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Geography

Introduction

Understanding Indian rivers and waterfalls is crucial for SSC geography. This chapter covers river origins, tributaries, flow directions, and major waterfalls with memory tricks for quick revision.

SSC frequently asks about river origins, Himalayan vs Peninsular rivers, major waterfalls, and river systems.

PART 1: Indian Rivers

Two Major River Systems

Himalayan Rivers
Snow-fed, perennial flow
Longer courses
Examples: Ganga, Indus, Brahmaputra
Form large basins and deltas
Peninsular Rivers
Rain-fed, seasonal flow
Shorter courses
Examples: Godavari, Krishna, Narmada
Mostly flow eastward

A. Himalayan River System

1. Indus River System

Origin: Lake Mansarovar, Tibet

Flow: Ladakh → Pakistan → Arabian Sea

Major Tributaries:

Jhelum
Chenab
Ravi
Beas
Sutlej

Memory Trick

"JCRBS"

Just Check Rivers Before Sleeping

Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Beas, Sutlej

2. Ganga River System

Origin: Gangotri Glacier (Bhagirathi)

Confluence: Bhagirathi + Alaknanda at Devprayag

Ends in: Bay of Bengal (as Hooghly & Padma)

Left Bank:

Gandak
Ghaghara
Kosi
Ramganga

Right Bank:

Yamuna
Son
Tons

Memory Tricks

"G-G-K-R"

Left: Gandak, Ghaghara, Kosi, Ramganga

"Y-S-T"

Right: Yamuna, Son, Tons

3. Brahmaputra River System

Origin: Tibet (as Tsangpo)

Tibet → Tsangpo
Arunachal → Dihang
Assam → Brahmaputra
Bangladesh → Jamuna
Ends in Bay of Bengal

Memory Trick

"T→D→B→J"

Tsangpo → Dihang → Brahmaputra → Jamuna

Sequence of names along its course

B. Peninsular River System

East-Flowing Rivers (Bay of Bengal)

Godavari

Origin: Nasik, Maharashtra | Longest Peninsular | Called "Dakshin Ganga"

Krishna

Origin: Mahabaleshwar, Maharashtra | Tributaries: Bhima, Tungabhadra

Kaveri

Origin: Brahmagiri Hills, Karnataka | Forms Shivasamudram Falls

Mahanadi

Origin: Chhattisgarh | Major river of Odisha

Memory Trick

"G-K-K-M"

Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri, Mahanadi

All flow east into Bay of Bengal

West-Flowing Rivers (Arabian Sea)

Narmada

Origin: Amarkantak | Flows through rift valley | Marble Rocks of Jabalpur

Tapi

Origin: Satpura Range | Also flows in rift valley

Inland Rivers (do not reach sea):

Luni (Rajasthan → Rann of Kutch), Ghaggar

Memory Trick

"NT"

Narmada & Tapi = North-to-West Flow

Only major west-flowing rivers in India

PART 2: Major Waterfalls of India

1. Jog Falls

River: Sharavathi
State: Karnataka
Type: Segmented waterfall
One of India's highest waterfalls

2. Shivasamudram Falls

River: Kaveri
State: Karnataka
Asia's first hydroelectric plant (1902)

3. Dhuandhar Falls

River: Narmada
State: Madhya Pradesh
Located near Marble Rocks, Jabalpur

4. Hogenakkal Falls

River: Kaveri
State: Tamil Nadu
Known as "Niagara of India"

5. Athirappilly Falls

River: Chalakudy
State: Kerala
Largest waterfall in Kerala

6. Hundru Falls

River: Subarnarekha
State: Jharkhand
On Chota Nagpur Plateau

Memory Trick for Waterfalls

"S–N–K–C–S–H"

Shivasamudram → Kaveri, Dhuandhar → Narmada, Hogenakkal → Kaveri

Chitrakoot → Indravati, Jog → Sharavathi, Hundru → Subarnarekha

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which is the longest peninsular river of India?

Godavari (also called Dakshin Ganga)

2. Name the major west-flowing rivers of India.

Narmada and Tapi (flow into Arabian Sea)

3. The Luni river ends in which geographical feature?

Rann of Kutch (inland drainage)

4. Jog Falls is located on which river?

Sharavathi River (Karnataka)

5. Dhuandhar Falls is associated with which river?

Narmada River (Madhya Pradesh)

6. Which river forms Shivasamudram and Hogenakkal waterfalls?

Kaveri River

7. What is Brahmaputra called in Tibet?

Tsangpo

8. Where does the Indus river originate?

Lake Mansarovar, Tibet

Conclusion

Mastering Indian rivers and waterfalls is essential for scoring well in SSC geography. Focus on differentiating Himalayan and Peninsular rivers, remembering their origins and flow directions, and associating waterfalls with their respective rivers.

Final Summary

Himalayan rivers → Snow-fed, perennial (Ganga, Indus, Brahmaputra)
Peninsular rivers → Rain-fed, seasonal (Godavari, Krishna, Narmada)
East-flowing → Bay of Bengal (G-K-K-M)
West-flowing → Arabian Sea (Narmada, Tapi)
Waterfalls: Jog (Sharavathi), Dhuandhar (Narmada), Shivasamudram (Kaveri)
