Continents & Oceans

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - World Geography

Introduction

Understanding continents and oceans is the foundation of world geography. This chapter covers the seven continents and five oceans, their key features, and important geographical facts essential for SSC exams.

SSC frequently asks about the number of continents/oceans, largest and smallest, and location-based questions.

Memory Trick for Continents

"A A N S A E A"

Asia – Africa – North America – South America – Antarctica – Europe – Australia

Remember: "EE NAASA" (EEnAASA) for sequence

1. Asia – Largest Continent

Size: Largest continent (covers 1/3rd of world's land area)
Population: Highest population (over 4.7 billion)
Key Features: Himalayas, Gobi Desert, Yangtze River
Borders: Pacific, Indian, and Arctic Oceans
Major Countries: China, India, Japan, Russia
Important Facts

• Connected to Europe through Ural Mountains

• Contains world's highest peak: Mount Everest

• Birthplace of major civilizations and religions

2. Africa – 2nd Largest Continent

Unique: Only continent through which Equator, Tropic of Cancer & Capricorn pass
Longest River: Nile River (world's longest)
Largest Desert: Sahara Desert (world's largest hot desert)
Key Features: Congo Basin, Kilimanjaro, Victoria Falls
Important Facts

• Rich in mineral resources (gold, diamonds, oil)

• Great Rift Valley runs through eastern Africa

• Continent with most countries (54 countries)

3. North America – 3rd Largest Continent

Location: Northern and Western Hemispheres
Major Mountains: Rocky Mountains, Appalachian Mountains
Major Rivers: Mississippi, Missouri, Mackenzie
Great Lakes: Largest freshwater system (Superior, Michigan, Huron, Erie, Ontario)
Important Facts

• Connected to South America by Isthmus of Panama

• Contains world's largest island: Greenland (politically part of Denmark)

• Major countries: USA, Canada, Mexico

4. South America – 4th Largest Continent

Largest River: Amazon River (by water volume)
Longest Mountain Range: Andes Mountains
Key Features: Amazon Rainforest, Atacama Desert, Patagonia
Major Countries: Brazil, Argentina, Chile, Peru
Important Facts

• Amazon Rainforest is world's largest tropical rainforest

• Atacama Desert is world's driest desert

• Angel Falls (Venezuela) is world's highest waterfall

5. Antarctica – 5th Largest Continent

Climate: Coldest continent on Earth
Ice Cover: 98% covered by ice
Population: No permanent human population
Key Feature: South Pole located here
Important Facts

• Contains about 70% of world's freshwater (as ice)

• Surrounded by Southern Ocean

• Research stations from multiple countries for scientific study

6. Europe – 6th Largest Continent

Size: Second smallest continent (after Australia)
Major Mountains: Alps, Pyrenees, Carpathians, Ural
Major Rivers: Danube, Rhine, Volga, Seine
Development: High standard of living in many countries
Important Facts

• Connected to Asia (forms Eurasia landmass)

• Birthplace of Western civilization, Renaissance, Industrial Revolution

• European Union promotes economic and political cooperation

7. Australia – Smallest Continent

Size: Smallest continent
Type: Island continent (country and continent)
Key Features: Great Barrier Reef, Outback, Great Dividing Range
Oceania: Part of Oceania region with Pacific islands
Important Facts

• Great Barrier Reef is world's largest coral reef system

• Unique wildlife (kangaroos, koalas, platypus)

• Most urbanized population (majority lives in coastal cities)

Memory Trick for Oceans

"P A I S A"

Pacific – Atlantic – Indian – Southern – Arctic

Remember: "PAISA" (like the currency)

1. Pacific Ocean – Largest Ocean

Size: Largest and deepest ocean
Deepest Point: Mariana Trench (Challenger Deep)
Ring of Fire: Surrounds Pacific with active volcanoes & earthquakes
Location: Separates Asia/Australia from Americas
Important Facts

• Covers about 46% of Earth's water surface

• Contains about 25,000 islands (most of any ocean)

• Named by Ferdinand Magellan ("Pacific" = peaceful)

2. Atlantic Ocean – 2nd Largest Ocean

Shape: S-shaped
Location: Between Americas (west) and Europe/Africa (east)
Importance: Major trade routes (historically and currently)
Mid-Atlantic Ridge: Longest mountain range (underwater)
Important Facts

• Bermuda Triangle located here (mysterious disappearances)

• Gulf Stream current warms Western Europe

• Name derived from Greek mythology (Atlas)

3. Indian Ocean – Only Ocean Named After a Country

Unique: Named after India (Indian subcontinent)
Location: Bounded by Asia (north), Africa (west), Australia (east)
Climate: Warmest ocean
Importance: Major shipping routes for oil from Middle East
Important Facts

• Monsoon winds strongly influence climate

• Contains island nations: Sri Lanka, Maldives, Mauritius

• Strategic importance for international trade

4. Southern Ocean – Newest Recognized Ocean

Recognition: Officially recognized in 2000
Location: Surrounds Antarctica
Current: Antarctic Circumpolar Current (world's strongest)
Climate: Cold, stormy waters
Important Facts

• Also called Antarctic Ocean

• Important for global ocean circulation and climate

• Rich in marine life (krill, whales, seals)

5. Arctic Ocean – Smallest Ocean

Size: Smallest and shallowest ocean
Location: Surrounds North Pole, mostly within Arctic Circle
Ice Cover: Partially covered by sea ice throughout year
Bordering Continents: Asia, Europe, North America
Important Facts

• Connected to Pacific via Bering Strait

• Northwest Passage (shipping route) becoming more accessible due to melting ice

• Home to polar bears, Arctic foxes, walruses

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. How many continents are there in the world?

7 Continents

2. Which is the largest continent?

Asia

3. Which is the smallest continent?

Australia

4. How many oceans are there?

5 Oceans

5. Which is the largest ocean?

Pacific Ocean

6. Which ocean is named after a country?

Indian Ocean (named after India)

7. Which continent contains the Sahara Desert?

Africa

8. Which continent is also an island?

Australia (island continent)

9. Amazon River flows through which continent?

South America

10. Which ocean surrounds Antarctica?

Southern Ocean (Antarctic Ocean)

Conclusion

Mastering continents and oceans provides the foundation for understanding world geography. These basic facts about Earth's major landmasses and water bodies are frequently tested in SSC exams and form the basis for more advanced geographical concepts.

Final Summary

Continents: 7 total (A A N S A E A)
Oceans: 5 total (P A I S A)
Largest Continent: Asia
Smallest Continent: Australia
Largest Ocean: Pacific
Smallest Ocean: Arctic
Indian Ocean: Named after India
Southern Ocean: Newest recognized (2000), surrounds Antarctica
