Financial Inclusion (UPI, Jan Dhan, etc.)

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Financial Inclusion?

Providing affordable financial services to every citizen, especially the poor

Services Included

Bank accounts
Digital payments
Credit facilities
Insurance
Pension & Savings

Simple Definition

"Banking for Everyone"

Making financial services accessible to all sections of society

PM Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

Key Information

Launched:

28 August 2014

Aim:

Provide basic bank account to every household

Account Type:

Zero-balance account

Debit Card:

RuPay debit card provided

Key Features & Benefits

Overdraft Facility:

Up to ₹10,000

Accidental Insurance:

₹2 lakh

Life Insurance:

₹30,000 (for early members)

Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT):

Government subsidies directly to account

Benefits of Jan Dhan

Money transfers become easy
Government subsidies reach directly (DBT)
Women, farmers, poor get bank access
Reduces corruption and leakage

JAM Trinity (SSC LOVES THIS)

J A M

J

Jan Dhan

A

Aadhaar

M

Mobile

Purpose of JAM

  • •Ensure direct transfer of government benefits
  • •Reduce middlemen
  • •Increase transparency
  • •Prevent leakages in subsidy distribution

How JAM Works

Jan Dhan provides bank account

Aadhaar provides identity verification

Mobile enables communication & alerts

Memory Trick: "Money Jam karo seedha bank mein!"

UPI (Unified Payments Interface)

Key Information

Launched by:

NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India)

Year:

2016

What is UPI?

Real-time digital payment system for bank-to-bank instant transfer

Availability:

24×7, 365 days

Features & Benefits

Instant fund transfer

QR code payments

No transaction charges

Works with Aadhaar-linked mobile

Secure with UPI PIN

Memory Trick: UPI = "Universal Payment Instant"

Popular UPI Apps

BHIM
Google Pay
PhonePe
Paytm

Other Key Components

BHIM App

Full Form:

Bharat Interface for Money

Launched:

2016

Based on:

UPI platform

Special:

Government's official UPI app

RuPay Card

Type:

India's domestic card network

Launched by:

NPCI

Works like:

Visa/Mastercard

Linked with:

Jan Dhan accounts

Debit Card
Credit Card
Prepaid Card

AEPS

Full Form:

Aadhaar Enabled Payment System

Technology:

Biometric (fingerprint/iris)

Requirements:

Aadhaar number, No card needed

Useful for:

Villages & rural areas

Services: Cash withdrawal, Balance inquiry, Fund transfer

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. PMJDY was launched in which year?

2014

2. Which scheme provides zero-balance accounts?

Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

3. What insurance is provided under PMJDY?

₹2 lakh accidental insurance

4. JAM stands for?

Jan Dhan, Aadhaar, Mobile

5. UPI was developed by?

NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India)

6. Which app is based on UPI and is government's official app?

BHIM App

7. RuPay card is issued under which scheme?

Jan Dhan Yojana

8. AEPS allows banking using?

Aadhaar number with biometric authentication

9. Maximum overdraft in Jan Dhan accounts?

₹10,000

10. Which of these can Payments Banks NOT issue?

Credit cards (they can only issue debit cards)

Conclusion

Financial inclusion initiatives have transformed India's economic landscape, bringing banking services to millions. Understanding these schemes and digital payment systems is crucial for SSC exams.

Key Takeaways

Financial Inclusion: Banking for everyone
PMJDY: 2014, zero-balance account, insurance, overdraft
JAM Trinity: Jan Dhan + Aadhaar + Mobile
UPI: 2016, NPCI, instant bank transfers
BHIM: Government's UPI app
RuPay: India's domestic card network
AEPS: Aadhaar-based biometric banking
Impact: India = world's largest digital payments market
