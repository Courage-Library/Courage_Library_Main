Economic Sectors — Primary, Secondary & Tertiary

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Three Sectors of the Economy

The Indian economy is divided into 3 sectors based on nature of economic activity:

1. Primary Sector

Extracting resources from nature

2. Secondary Sector

Manufacturing and construction

3. Tertiary Sector

Providing services

Primary Sector (Agriculture/Extractive Sector)

What It Includes

  • •Agriculture
  • •Fishing
  • •Forestry
  • •Animal Husbandry
  • •Mining & Quarrying

Key Features

Depends on Nature: Highly dependent on monsoon and natural conditions

Employment: Largest employer in India

Productivity: Low productivity but high employment

Example: Farmer growing wheat → Primary activity.

Secondary Sector (Industrial/Manufacturing Sector)

What It Includes

  • •Factories & Industries
  • •Construction
  • •Electricity Generation
  • •Food Processing
  • •Textiles, Cement, Steel
  • •Automobiles

Key Concept

"Primary gives raw materials, Secondary makes products"

Value Addition: Raw materials are converted into finished goods

Example: Wheat → Flour mill → Bread

Tertiary Sector (Service Sector)

Fastest Growing Sector in India - Highest Contributor to GDP

What It Includes

Banking
Insurance
Transport
IT & Software
Education
Healthcare
Tourism
E-commerce

Key Features

GDP Contribution: Highest contributor to India's GDP

Employment: Employs skilled workers

Role: Backbone of modern economy

Share of Sectors in India's Economy (VERY IMPORTANT)

Contribution to GDP

1. Tertiary Sector: Largest contributor
2. Secondary Sector: Second largest
3. Primary Sector: Smallest contributor

Contribution to Employment

1. Primary Sector: Largest employer
2. Secondary Sector: Second largest
3. Tertiary Sector: Third largest

Memory Trick

GDP =

T S P

(Tertiary > Secondary > Primary)

Jobs =

P S T

(Primary > Secondary > Tertiary)

Sector Names

P S T

(Primary, Secondary, Tertiary)

Other Sector Classifications (SSC Frequently Asks)

Organized Sector

• Fixed working hours

• Job security

• Government rules followed

Examples: Government offices, Banks, Registered companies

Unorganized Sector

• No job security

• Irregular wages

• No paid leaves

Examples: Small shops, Daily labor, Rickshaw pullers

Public Sector

Owned by Government

Railways
ONGC
BSNL
LIC
ISRO

Private Sector

Owned by Individuals/Companies

Reliance
TCS
Infosys
Tata Motors

Sectoral Shifts in Indian Economy

Before Independence

Primary sector dominated the economy

After Independence (1947-1990)

Secondary sector grew (emphasis on industries)

After 1991 Economic Reforms

Tertiary sector became dominant (IT & service exports increased)

Differences Between Sectors (SSC Table)

Sector Main Activity Examples
Primary Extracts raw materials from nature Farming, mining, fishing
Secondary Converts raw materials to finished goods Factory, cement, steel, automobiles
Tertiary Provides services Banking, transport, IT, education

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Primary sector includes which activities?

Agriculture, mining, fishing, forestry

2. Secondary sector is also called?

Industrial sector / Manufacturing sector

3. Tertiary sector is also called?

Service sector

4. Largest contributor to India's GDP?

Tertiary sector (Service sector)

5. Largest employer in India?

Primary sector (Agriculture sector)

6. IT and banking belong to which sector?

Tertiary sector

7. Construction belongs to which sector?

Secondary sector

8. Mining belongs to which sector?

Primary sector

9. Public sector includes?

Government-owned undertakings (Railways, ONGC, BSNL, LIC)

10. Organized sector includes?

Registered companies & government offices with fixed rules

Conclusion

Understanding the three economic sectors is fundamental for economics questions in SSC exams. This topic provides a clear framework for analyzing India's economic structure and development.

Key Takeaways

Primary: Extraction, largest employment provider
Secondary: Manufacturing, value addition
Tertiary: Services, largest GDP contributor
GDP Order: Tertiary > Secondary > Primary
Employment Order: Primary > Secondary > Tertiary
Organized vs Unorganized: Job security difference
Public vs Private: Ownership difference
Memory Tricks: "P S T", "GDP = T S P", "Jobs = P S T"

This topic typically yields 2-3 easy marks in SSC MTS/GD/CHSL exams.
