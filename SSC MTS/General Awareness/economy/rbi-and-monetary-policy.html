RBI & Monetary Policy

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is RBI?

Reserve Bank of India - Central Bank of India

Key Information

Established:

1 April 1935

Nationalized:

1949

Headquarters:

Mumbai

Role:

Central bank of India

Currency Note

Important SSC Fact:

₹2 to ₹2000 notes issued by RBI

Exception: ₹1 note & coins issued by Ministry of Finance

Memory Trick: "₹1 = Government, Rest = RBI"

Key Functions of RBI (SSC's Most Important)

1. Bank of Banks

Regulates and supervises all banks, keeps their reserves

2. Bank to Government

Manages govt accounts, issues government securities

3. Issuer of Currency

Issues currency notes (except ₹1 note)

4. Custodian of Forex

Manages foreign exchange reserves, controls forex (FEMA Act)

5. Controller of Credit

Controls money supply and inflation through Monetary Policy

6. Lender of Last Resort

Provides emergency funds to banks in crisis

What is Monetary Policy?

Steps taken by RBI to control money supply, inflation, credit availability, and interest rates

Implemented by

MPC

Monetary Policy Committee

Main Objectives

  • •Control inflation
  • •Regulate money supply
  • •Manage interest rates
  • •Ensure credit availability

Monetary Policy Tools (SSC ALWAYS Asks!)

1. Repo Rate (VERY IMPORTANT)

Rate at which RBI lends money to commercial banks

Repo ↑ → Loans expensive → Inflation ↓
Repo ↓ → Loans cheaper → Inflation ↑

Memory: "REPO = RBI → Bank"

2. Reverse Repo Rate

Rate at which RBI borrows money FROM banks

Memory: "Reverse Repo = Bank → RBI"

3. Bank Rate

Rate at which RBI lends money for LONG TERM loans to banks

Difference: Repo = Short term, Bank Rate = Long term

4. CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio)

% of deposits banks must keep with RBI in CASH (No interest)

Memory: "CRR = Cash with RBI"

5. SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio)

% of deposits banks must keep in Gold/Government securities (held by banks)

Memory: "SLR = Securities with bank"

6. OMO (Open Market Operations)

RBI buys/sells government bonds in market to control money supply

RBI Buys → Money enters → Inflation ↑
RBI Sells → Money removed → Inflation ↓
7. MSF (Marginal Standing Facility)

Banks borrow from RBI overnight at rate higher than Repo (emergency use)

8. LAF (Liquidity Adjustment Facility)

Includes Repo + Reverse Repo operations

Monetary Policy Committee (MPC)

Key Information

Formed under:

RBI Act, 1934 (Amended in 2016)

Total Members:

6 members

Meetings:

Every 2 months

Main Decision:

Sets the Repo Rate

Composition

3 from RBI

  • • Governor (Chairperson)
  • • Deputy Governor
  • • One RBI official

3 appointed by Government of India

Inflation Target:

4% ± 2%

Types of Monetary Policy

1. Tight / Contractionary Policy

Repo Rate ↑
CRR ↑
SLR ↑

Effect: Money supply ↓, Inflation ↓

2. Easy / Expansionary Policy

Repo Rate ↓
CRR ↓
SLR ↓

Effect: Money supply ↑, Growth ↑

Policy Responsibility

Monetary Policy

RBI

Fiscal Policy

Government (Ministry of Finance)

Super Memory Tricks

Repo Rate: "RBI → Bank"
Reverse Repo: "Bank → RBI"
CRR: "Cash with RBI"
SLR: "Securities with bank"
Bank Rate: Long-term loan rate
High Rate: Inflation goes DOWN
Low Rate: Inflation goes UP
₹1 Note: Issued by Government, not RBI

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which institution controls monetary policy in India?

Reserve Bank of India (RBI)

2. Repo Rate means?

RBI lends money to commercial banks

3. Reverse Repo Rate means?

RBI borrows money from banks

4. CRR is kept in the form of?

Cash with RBI

5. SLR is kept in form of?

Gold / Government securities (held by banks)

6. MPC has how many members?

6 members (3 from RBI, 3 appointed by Government)

7. What is India's inflation target?

4% ± 2%

8. OMO means?

Open Market Operations (RBI buys/sells government bonds)

9. Which rate is for long-term loans to banks?

Bank Rate

10. MSF is used for?

Emergency overnight borrowing by banks from RBI

Conclusion

Understanding RBI and Monetary Policy is crucial for SSC economics preparation. These concepts frequently appear in exams and form the foundation of India's financial system.

Key Takeaways

RBI: Central bank, established 1935, HQ Mumbai
Functions: Bank of banks, currency issuer, credit controller
Repo Rate: RBI lends to banks (short-term)
Reverse Repo: RBI borrows from banks
CRR: Cash reserve with RBI (no interest)
SLR: Securities reserve with banks
MPC: 6 members, sets Repo Rate, inflation target 4% ± 2%
Memory Tricks: "Repo = RBI→Bank", "CRR = Cash with RBI", "High Rate → Inflation ↓"
