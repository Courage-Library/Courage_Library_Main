Budget Deficit

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is a Budget Deficit?

Budget Deficit = When Government's expenses > Government's earnings

Simple Explanation

"Kharcha zyada, kamaai kam"

(Spending more, earning less)

Government finances become weak when deficit increases

Key Point

Expenses

>

Earnings

Results in Budget Deficit

Types of Budget Deficits (SSC's FAVORITE)

1. Revenue Deficit

Daily expenses > Daily income

2. Fiscal Deficit

MOST IMPORTANT - Borrowings needed

3. Primary Deficit

Pure deficit excluding interest

4. Effective Revenue Deficit

Wasteful revenue spending

Revenue Deficit

Revenue Deficit = Revenue Expenditure – Revenue Receipts

Revenue Expenditure

Daily/Regular Expenses

Salaries
Subsidies
Pensions
Interest Payments
Defence Maintenance

Revenue Receipts

Daily/Regular Income

GST
Income Tax
Customs Duty
Fees & Fines
Dividends from PSUs

Memory Trick: "Daily expenses > Daily income"

Fiscal Deficit (MOST IMPORTANT — 100% Asked)

Fiscal Deficit = Total Expenditure – Total Receipts (excluding borrowings)

What It Shows

Borrowing Need:

Shows how much money government needs to borrow

Implication:

Government spending more than its income

Consequence:

Borrowing increases national debt

FRBM Act Target

3%

of GDP

Long-term fiscal deficit target under FRBM Act

Memory Trick: "FD = Financial Demand (Borrowing Needed)"

Primary Deficit

Primary Deficit = Fiscal Deficit – Interest Payments

What It Shows

Real Financial Condition:

Shows deficit excluding old loan interest burden

Key Point:

Indicates government's current financial health, ignoring past debt interest

Significance

Measures:

Current fiscal position excluding interest commitments

Zero Primary Deficit:

Government can pay interest from its revenue

Negative Primary Deficit:

Government has surplus after paying interest

Memory Trick: "Primary = Pure deficit (after removing interest burden)"

Effective Revenue Deficit

Effective Revenue Deficit = Revenue Deficit – Grants for Capital Creation

Why This Concept?

Key Reason:

Some revenue expenses actually create assets (like grants to states for roads)

What It Shows:

Real wasteful revenue spending after excluding productive grants

Example

Scenario:

Government gives ₹100 crore grant to states for building schools

• This is revenue expenditure (grant)

• But it creates capital assets (schools)

• So it's subtracted from Revenue Deficit to get Effective RD

Super Quick Summary Table

Deficit Type Formula Meaning
Revenue Deficit RE – RR Routine expenses > income
Fiscal Deficit TE – TR (excluding borrowings) Borrowings needed
Primary Deficit FD – Interest Deficit excluding interest
Effective Revenue Deficit RD – Grants for capital Wasteful revenue gap

Government Funding & Impacts

How Government Funds Deficit?

Borrowing from RBI
Borrowing from banks
External loans
Selling government bonds
Disinvestment of PSUs

Why High Deficit is Dangerous?

Leads to inflation

Increases national debt

Weakens the currency

Reduces future growth

When Some Deficit is Good?

Infrastructure projects
Employment schemes
Productive investments

When used for productive purposes, it boosts economic growth

Memory Tricks

Fiscal Deficit

"Financial Borrowing"

Revenue Deficit

"Regular Expenses – Regular Income"

Primary Deficit

"Fiscal – Interest"

Effective RD

"Real Revenue Deficit"

Ultra Trick

"Father Rides Pure Electric"

F → Fiscal
R → Revenue
P → Primary
E → Effective Revenue

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Fiscal Deficit is?

Difference between total expenditure & total receipts (excluding borrowings)

2. Revenue deficit occurs when?

Revenue expenditure > Revenue receipts

3. Primary Deficit formula?

Fiscal Deficit – Interest Payments

4. Effective Revenue Deficit formula?

Revenue Deficit – Grants for capital creation

5. What does fiscal deficit show?

Total borrowing requirement of the government

6. Which deficit indicates overall financial health?

Fiscal Deficit

7. Examples of revenue expenditure?

Salaries, pensions, subsidies, interest payments

8. Examples of capital expenditure?

Roads, railways, bridges, infrastructure projects

9. High fiscal deficit may lead to?

Inflation

10. Fiscal deficit target under FRBM Act?

3% of GDP

Conclusion

Mastering the different budget deficit concepts is crucial for understanding government finances and scoring well in SSC economics questions.

Key Takeaways

Budget Deficit: Expenses > Earnings
Revenue Deficit: RE – RR (daily gap)
Fiscal Deficit: TE – TR (borrowing need)
Primary Deficit: FD – Interest (pure deficit)
Effective RD: RD – Capital grants (wasteful spending)
FRBM Target: 3% of GDP for fiscal deficit
High Deficit Risks: Inflation, debt, weak currency
Memory: "F R P E → Father Rides Pure Electric"
