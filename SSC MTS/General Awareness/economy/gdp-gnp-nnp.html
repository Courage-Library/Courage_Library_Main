GDP, GNP, NNP — Explained for SSC MTS

Economics - National Income Concepts - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

GDP (Gross Domestic Product)

Definition

GDP is the total value of all final goods & services produced inside a country in one year.

Key Concept

"Inside the country" (Domestic) — Location based, not citizenship based.

Includes: Foreign companies operating in India
Excludes: Indians working abroad

GDP Formula

GDP = C + I + G + (X – M)

C: Consumption (Household spending)
I: Investment (Business spending)
G: Government Spending
X – M: Net Exports (Exports – Imports)

Types of GDP

Nominal GDP

Calculated at current prices. Affected by inflation.

Shows present market value with inflation effect

Real GDP

Calculated at constant/base year prices. Removes inflation effect.

Shows real economic growth, adjusted for inflation

GDP Memory Tricks

"Inside India"

GDP measures domestic production

"CIG-XM" (SIG EXAM)

Formula: C + I + G + (X – M)

"Nominal = Now"

Current prices calculation

"Real = Reality"

Inflation-adjusted calculation

GDP at Factor Cost vs Market Price

GDP at Factor Cost (FC)

Cost of production including wages, rent, interest, profit.

Represents income earned by factors of production

GDP at Market Price (MP)

Price paid by consumers in the market.

Includes indirect taxes, excludes subsidies

Conversion Formula

GDP at MP = GDP at FC + Indirect Taxes – Subsidies

GDP at FC

Cost of production

+

Add Indirect Taxes

–

Subtract Subsidies

Memory Trick

"Market Price = Factory + Tax – Subsidy"

GDP at Market Price equals Factor Cost plus taxes minus subsidies.

GNP (Gross National Product)

Definition

GNP is the total income earned by the residents (nationals) of a country, whether they live inside or outside the country.

Key Concept

"By Indians" (National) — Citizenship based, not location based.

Includes: Indians working abroad
Excludes: Foreigners working in India

GNP Formula

GNP = GDP + NFIA

NFIA = Net Factor Income from Abroad

NFIA Formula: Income earned by Indians abroad – Income earned by foreigners in India

If NFIA is positive → GNP > GDP

If NFIA is negative → GNP < GDP

GNP Memory Tricks

"Global National People"

GNP measures income by nationals globally

"GNP = GDP + Indians Abroad – Foreigners in India"

Simplified formula explanation

NNP (Net National Product)

Definition

NNP is GNP minus depreciation (consumption of fixed capital).

Depreciation Explained

Machines, buildings, vehicles lose value every year due to wear and tear. This loss is called depreciation.

Also called: Consumption of Fixed Capital

NNP Formula

NNP = GNP – Depreciation

Depreciation: Loss in value of capital assets
Purpose: To get net value after accounting for wear and tear
Result: More accurate measure of sustainable income

NNP at Factor Cost (National Income)

NNP at Factor Cost = National Income — This is the most accurate measure of a country's income.

National Income = NNP at Factor Cost

Most comprehensive measure
Accounts for depreciation
At factor cost (production cost)

NNP Memory Tricks

"Net after subtracting depreciation"

NNP is GNP minus wear and tear

"National Income = NNP at FC"

Most accurate income measure

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. GDP measures income based on?

Location (Domestic production within country)

2. GNP measures income based on?

Nationality (Income earned by nationals anywhere)

3. What is the formula for GNP?

GNP = GDP + NFIA (Net Factor Income from Abroad)

4. What does NFIA stand for?

Net Factor Income from Abroad

5. National Income is equal to?

NNP at Factor Cost

6. What is deducted from GNP to get NNP?

Depreciation (Consumption of Fixed Capital)

7. Real GDP is measured at?

Constant prices (Base year prices)

8. Nominal GDP is measured at?

Current prices (Present market prices)

9. A foreign company producing in India is counted in?

GDP of India (but not GNP of India)

10. An Indian working abroad contributes to?

GNP of India (but not GDP of India)

Conclusion

Understanding GDP, GNP, and NNP is fundamental for economics questions in SSC exams. These concepts form the basis for measuring a country's economic performance and are frequently tested in various competitive examinations.

Key Takeaways

GDP: Domestic production, location based, formula C + I + G + (X – M)
GNP: National income, citizenship based, formula GDP + NFIA
NNP: GNP minus depreciation, NNP at FC = National Income
NFIA: Net Factor Income from Abroad (Indians abroad – foreigners in India)
Real vs Nominal: Real adjusts for inflation, Nominal uses current prices
GDP at MP = GDP at FC + Indirect Taxes – Subsidies
Memory Tricks: "Inside India" for GDP, "Global National People" for GNP, "G → Ground, N → Nation, N → Net"
