Five-Year Plans of India (1st to 12th)

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What are Five-Year Plans?

India followed planned economic development from 1951 to 2017

12

Total Five-Year Plans

Planning Commission

1950–2014

NITI Aayog

From 2015 onwards

After 12th Plan → 3-Year Action Agenda (NITI Aayog)

Early Five-Year Plans

1st Plan (1951–56)

Focus:

Agriculture

Reason:

Food shortage after independence

Growth:

Target: 2.1% | Actual: 3.6% (Success)

Memory: "1st = Farming First"

2nd Plan (1956–61)

Focus:

Industry (Heavy industries)

Based on:

Mahalanobis Model

Achievements:

Steel plants (Bhilai, Rourkela, Durgapur)

Memory: "2nd = Steel & Industry"

3rd Plan (1961–66)

Focus:

Self-sufficiency in food grains

Failed due to:

Indo-China War (1962), Indo-Pak War (1965), Drought

Outcome:

Led to Green Revolution

Middle Period Plans

Plan Holidays (1966–69)

3 annual plans due to economic crisis

4th Plan (1969–74)

Focus:

Growth + Stability

Major Move:

Nationalization of 14 banks (1969)

Memory: "4th = Banks Nationalized"

5th Plan (1974–79)

Focus:

Garibi Hatao (Remove Poverty)

Launched by:

Indira Gandhi

Outcome:

Ended early in 1978

Memory: "5th = Garibi Hatao"

Rolling Plan (1978–80)

No fixed duration

Introduced by Janata Party, later abandoned

Reform Era Plans

6th Plan (1980–85)

Focus:

Poverty reduction, Technology, Infrastructure

Outcome:

Successful plan

Memory: "6th = Tech + Poverty reduction"

7th Plan (1985–90)

Focus:

Employment generation, Food, work, productivity

Emphasis by:

Rajiv Gandhi (Modernization, IT, Telecom)

8th Plan (1992–97)

Focus:

Liberalization, Privatization, Globalization (LPG)

Context:

After 1991 economic reforms

Key Figures:

PM: P.V. Narasimha Rao, FM: Dr. Manmohan Singh

Memory: "8th = LPG Reforms"

Annual Plans (1990–92)

Due to economic crisis, Gulf War, political instability

Modern Five-Year Plans

9th Plan (1997–2002)

Focus: Growth with social justice, Employment

10th Plan (2002–07)

Aim: 8% GDP growth, Poverty reduction

Growth achieved: 7.7%

11th Plan (2007–12)

Theme: Inclusive Growth

Focus: Reduce inequality, Education, Health

Memory: "11th = Inclusive India"

12th Plan (2012–17)

Theme: Faster, More Inclusive, Sustainable Growth

India's last Five-Year Plan

Replaced by NITI Aayog's 3-year Action Plan

Memory: "12th = 3 words (Faster+Inclusive+Sustainable)"

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which was India's first Five-Year Plan focused on?

Agriculture

2. Second Five-Year Plan was based on which model?

Mahalanobis Model

3. Green Revolution started after failure of which Plan?

3rd Five-Year Plan

4. Nationalization of banks happened in which plan?

4th Plan

5. "Garibi Hatao" was the slogan of which Plan?

5th Plan

6. LPG reforms were introduced in which Plan?

8th Plan

7. Inclusive Growth is theme of which Plan?

11th Plan

8. Last Five-Year Plan of India?

12th Plan (2012–17)

9. Rolling Plan was introduced by?

Janata Government

10. Which Plan ended early?

5th Plan

Conclusion

The Five-Year Plans shaped India's economic development from 1951 to 2017. Understanding their chronology, focus areas, and key events is essential for SSC economics questions.

Key Takeaways

Total: 12 Five-Year Plans (1951-2017)
1st Plan: Agriculture focus
2nd Plan: Heavy industries, Mahalanobis Model
4th Plan: Banks nationalized
5th Plan: Garibi Hatao slogan
8th Plan: LPG reforms (1991 reforms)
11th Plan: Inclusive Growth theme
12th Plan: Last plan, 3-word theme
Memory: "A I F B G" for first 5 plans, "8th=LPG", "11th=Inclusive"
