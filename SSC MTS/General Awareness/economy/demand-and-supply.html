Demand & Supply

Economics - Market Forces - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Demand

Definition

Demand is the quantity of a product that consumers are willing and able to buy at a given price and time.

Key Concept

"Want + Ability = Demand"

If you want something but cannot pay for it → NOT demand

Law of Demand (VERY IMPORTANT)

"When price increases → demand decreases
When price decreases → demand increases"

Curve: Demand curve slopes downward from left to right
Reason: Income effect, substitution effect, diminishing marginal utility

Determinants of Demand (SSC Frequently Asks)

Demand increases when:

Price Falls

Lower price makes goods more affordable.

Income Increases

Higher disposable income increases purchasing power.

Population Increases

More consumers in the market.

Tastes/Preferences Improve

Favorable changes in consumer preferences.

Substitute Becomes Costly

If substitute's price rises, demand for this product increases.

Complement Becomes Cheaper

If complementary good's price falls, demand for this product increases.

Memory Trick for Demand Determinants

"P I T S C"

Price
Income
Taste
Substitute
Complement

Exceptions to Law of Demand (VERY IMPORTANT for SSC)

1. Giffen Goods

Poor-quality inferior goods where demand increases when price rises.

Example: Bajra, inferior wheat for low-income groups

Memory Trick: "Giffen = Gareeb Goods"

2. Veblen Goods (Luxury Goods)

High price increases demand as status symbol.

Example: Rolex watches, Ferrari cars, expensive jewellery

Memory Trick: "Veblen = VIP Goods"

3. Expectations of Future Price Rise

If people expect prices to increase tomorrow, demand increases today.

Example: Fuel price hike announcement

4. Necessities

Demand does not change significantly with price changes.

Example: Salt, medicines, basic food items

Types of Demand (SSC Likes These)

Joint Demand

Two goods used together, demand for one affects demand for the other.

Example: Car + Petrol, Printer + Ink

Composite Demand

One product used for multiple purposes, demand comes from various uses.

Example: Electricity (lighting, cooling, heating)

Derived Demand

Demand for input depends on demand for final product.

Example: Steel demand depends on car/construction demand

Supply

Definition

Supply is the quantity of a product that producers are willing to sell at a given price and time.

Law of Supply

"When price increases → supply increases
When price decreases → supply decreases"

Curve: Supply curve slopes upward from left to right
Reason: Higher profit motivation, increased production capacity

Determinants of Supply

Supply Increases When:
Price of product rises
Production cost falls
Technology improves efficiency
Government provides subsidy
Number of sellers increases
Supply Decreases When:
Taxes increase
Input costs rise
Natural disasters occur
Government regulations tighten
Future price fall expected

Memory Trick for Supply Determinants

"P C T S S"

Price
Cost
Technology
Subsidy
Sellers

Market Equilibrium (SSC Sometimes Asks)

Equilibrium occurs where Demand = Supply

Equilibrium Price

Price at which quantity demanded equals quantity supplied.

Market clearing price

Above Equilibrium

When price is above equilibrium → Surplus (excess supply)

Supply > Demand

Below Equilibrium

When price is below equilibrium → Shortage (excess demand)

Demand > Supply

Memory Trick for Equilibrium

"E = Equal"

Equilibrium occurs when Demand equals Supply.

Price Elasticity of Demand (Basic for SSC)

Measures how sensitive demand is to price changes

PED > 1
Elastic Demand

Demand changes significantly with price change.

Examples: Luxury goods, non-essentials

PED < 1
Inelastic Demand

Demand changes little with price change.

Examples: Salt, medicines, necessities

PED = 1
Unit Elastic

Demand changes proportionally with price change.

Rare case: Perfect proportional response

Memory Trick for Elasticity

"NE (Necessities = InElastic)"

Necessity goods have inelastic demand.

Important Differences (VERY IMPORTANT TABLE)

Concept Meaning Caused By

Quantity DemandedSpecific point on demand curveChange in price of the good itself
SupplyEntire supply curve (relationship between price & quantity)Changes in non-price factors (technology, costs, taxes)

Memory Tricks Summary

Demand Curve: "DOWNWARD → D for Demand, D for Down"
Supply Curve: "UPWARD → S for Supply, S for Sky"
Giffen Goods: "Gareeb Goods" (demand ↑ when price ↑)
Veblen Goods: "VIP Goods" (luxury, demand ↑ when price ↑)
Equilibrium: "E = Equal" (Demand = Supply)
Elasticity: "NE (Necessities = InElastic)"

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Law of demand shows what type of relationship between price and demand?

Inverse relationship (price ↑ → demand ↓, price ↓ → demand ↑)

2. Which goods violate the law of demand?

Giffen goods and Veblen goods

3. Demand curve generally slopes in which direction?

Downward from left to right

4. Supply curve generally slopes in which direction?

Upward from left to right

5. Give an example of joint demand.

Car and Petrol, Printer and Ink

6. Demand is defined as quantity buyers are willing to buy at?

A given price, time, and with purchasing ability

7. If price increases and demand also increases, what is this called?

Veblen effect (for luxury goods) or Giffen effect (for inferior goods)

8. Market equilibrium occurs when?

Demand equals Supply

9. PED < 1 indicates what type of demand?

Inelastic demand (demand changes little with price change)

10. What do CPI and WPI measure in relation to demand-supply?

Inflation, which often results from demand-supply imbalances

Conclusion

Demand and Supply are fundamental concepts that explain how markets work. Understanding these principles is essential for economics questions in SSC exams and forms the foundation for more advanced economic concepts.

Key Takeaways

Demand: Willingness + ability to buy, law shows inverse price relationship
Supply: Willingness to sell, law shows direct price relationship
Exceptions: Giffen goods (inferior), Veblen goods (luxury)
Demand Types: Joint, Composite, Derived demand
Determinants: P I T S C for demand, P C T S S for supply
Equilibrium: Demand = Supply, surplus above, shortage below
Elasticity: PED > 1 (elastic), < 1 (inelastic), = 1 (unit elastic)
Memory Tricks: "Demand = Downward", "Supply = Upward", "Gareeb Goods", "VIP Goods", "E = Equal"
