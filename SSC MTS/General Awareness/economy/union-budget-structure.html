Union Budget Structure

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Union Budget?

The Union Budget is a government's annual financial statement showing total money coming in (Receipts) and going out (Expenditure)

Key Features

  • •Annual financial statement
  • •Shows government's income and expenditure
  • •Presented under Article 112

Important Date

1st February

Presented every year on this date

Structure of Union Budget (VERY IMPORTANT)

1. Revenue Budget

Deals with day-to-day operations

Revenue Receipts
Revenue Expenditure

2. Capital Budget

Deals with long-term assets

Capital Receipts
Capital Expenditure

Memory Trick

"R-C-R-C"

Revenue Receipts
Revenue Expenditure
Capital Receipts
Capital Expenditure

Revenue Budget

Revenue Budget = Revenue Receipts + Revenue Expenditure
No creation of assets, No reduction of liabilities

A. Revenue Receipts

1. Tax Revenue

Direct Taxes

Income Tax, Corporation Tax

Indirect Taxes

GST, Customs Duty, Excise Duty

2. Non-Tax Revenue
Fees
Fines
Dividends
Interest
Spectrum Auction

B. Revenue Expenditure

Money spent on routine/regular expenses

Salaries
Subsidies
Interest Payments
Pensions
Defense Maintenance
Day-to-day Expenses

Memory Trick: "Revenue Expenditure = Regular Expenses"

Capital Budget

Capital Budget = Capital Receipts + Capital Expenditure
Involves creation of assets or reduction of liabilities

A. Capital Receipts

These create liabilities or reduce assets

1. Loans raised by Government

Market borrowings, External loans, Ways and Means Advances

2. Disinvestment

Selling shares of government companies (PSUs)

3. Loan Recoveries

Recovery of loans given earlier

Memory Trick: "Capital Receipts = Borrowing + Selling + Recoveries"

B. Capital Expenditure

Money spent on creating long-term assets

Roads
Railways
Schools
Hospitals
Defense Equipment
Loans to States

Memory Trick: "Capital Expenditure = Constructing Assets"

Difference Table (SUPER IMPORTANT)

Category Revenue Budget Capital Budget
Purpose Day-to-day operations Long-term assets creation
Receipts Taxes & fees Loans & disinvestment
Expenditure Salaries, subsidies Roads, railways
Assets Created? No Yes
Liabilities Change? No Yes

Types of Deficits (SSC ALWAYS ASKS)

1. Revenue Deficit

Revenue Exp – Revenue Rec

Government spending more on routine expenses than income

2. Fiscal Deficit
(MOST IMPORTANT)

Total Exp – Total Rec
(excluding borrowings)

Indicates how much government needs to borrow

Memory: "Fiscal = Financial Gap"

3. Primary Deficit

Fiscal Deficit – Interest Payments

Financial condition excluding interest burden

Key Points About Fiscal Deficit

Most Important

Key indicator of government's borrowing needs

FRBM Target

Long-term target: Around 3% of GDP

GST & FRBM Act

GST in Budget

Major source of indirect tax revenue

Collected by Centre & States

Shared tax revenue

Part of Revenue Receipts

Under Indirect Taxes

FRBM Act
(Fiscal Responsibility & Budget Management)

Purpose

Reduce fiscal deficit, Maintain financial discipline

Key Features

Increase transparency, Set deficit targets

Fiscal Deficit Target

Long-term target: Around 3% of GDP

Memory Tricks Summary

Budget Structure

"R-C-R-C" (Revenue/Capital)

Capital Expenditure

"Construct Assets"

Fiscal Deficit

"FD = Financial Demand"

Revenue Expenditure

"Routine + Daily Expenses"

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Budget is presented under which Article of Constitution?

Article 112

2. Which part of budget deals with day-to-day expenses?

Revenue Budget

3. GST is part of which revenue category?

Indirect tax revenue

4. Road construction falls under which expenditure?

Capital expenditure

5. Revenue deficit formula?

Revenue Expenditure – Revenue Receipts

6. Fiscal deficit indicates what?

Total borrowing requirement of government

7. Disinvestment is a part of?

Capital Receipts

8. Salaries of government employees are?

Revenue Expenditure

9. Loans taken by government are under?

Capital Receipts

10. Primary deficit formula?

Fiscal Deficit – Interest Payments

Conclusion

The Union Budget is a crucial economic document that reveals the government's financial priorities and health. Understanding its structure is essential for SSC economics questions.

Key Takeaways

Two main parts: Revenue Budget & Capital Budget
Revenue: Day-to-day, no assets created
Capital: Long-term assets, loans, disinvestment
Three deficits: Revenue, Fiscal, Primary
Fiscal Deficit: Most important, shows borrowing need
FRBM Act: Targets fiscal deficit at ~3% of GDP
GST: Major indirect tax revenue source
Memory Tricks: "R-C-R-C", "Construct Assets", "FD = Financial Demand"
