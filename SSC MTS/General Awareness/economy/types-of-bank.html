Types of Banks in India

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Broad Classification of Banks in India

1. Commercial Banks

Most common, public/private/foreign

2. Regional Rural Banks

Rural banking focus

3. Cooperative Banks

Member-owned, rural credit

4. Small Finance Banks

Financial inclusion focus

5. Payments Banks

Digital banking, limited services

6. Development Banks

NABARD, EXIM, SIDBI

Commercial Banks (SSC's MOST Asked)

1. Public Sector Banks (PSBs)

Majority-owned by Government of India

SBI (State Bank of India)
Punjab National Bank
Bank of Baroda
Canara Bank

Hold 70% of deposits in India

2. Private Sector Banks

Owned by private individuals/companies

HDFC Bank
ICICI Bank
Axis Bank
Kotak Mahindra Bank
Yes Bank

3. Foreign Banks

Headquartered in foreign countries

Citi Bank
HSBC
Standard Chartered
Deutsche Bank

Regional Rural Banks (RRBs)

Key Information

Established:

1975

Purpose:

Provide banking services in rural areas

Joint Ownership:

Central Govt

50%

State Govt

15%

Sponsor Bank

35%

Memory Trick: "50-15-35 Rule"

Examples

Andhra Pradesh Gramin Bank
Baroda UP Bank
Kerala Gramin Bank
Prathama UP Gramin Bank

Cooperative & Small Finance Banks

Cooperative Banks

Ownership:

Members / Cooperative societies

Types:

State, District, Primary Agricultural Credit Societies

Role:

Rural credit, Agricultural loans, Small borrowers

Based on cooperation + mutual help

Small Finance Banks (SFBs)

Purpose:

Financial inclusion for underserved sections

Serve:

Small businesses, Farmers, Unorganized workers, Micro industries

Powers:

Can accept deposits, Can give loans

AU SFB
Ujjivan SFB
Equitas SFB
Fincare SFB

Payments Banks (VERY IMPORTANT for SSC)

Key Features

Purpose:

Promote digital banking and financial inclusion

Deposit Limit:

₹2 lakh per customer

Cannot:

Give loans, Issue credit cards

Can:

Accept deposits, Provide payment services, UPI, Debit cards

Memory Trick: "Payments Bank = No Loans Bank"

Examples

Paytm Payments Bank

Airtel Payments Bank

India Post Payments Bank

Jio Payments Bank

Development Banks (Financial Institutions)

NABARD

National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development

Established:

1982

Focus:

Agriculture & rural development

SIDBI

Small Industries Development Bank of India

Established:

1990

Focus:

Small industries & MSME support

EXIM Bank

Export-Import Bank of India

Established:

1982

Focus:

International trade support

Scheduled vs Non-Scheduled Banks (SSC LOVES THIS!)

Scheduled Banks

Definition:

Listed in Second Schedule of RBI Act, 1934

Requirements:

Maintain CRR with RBI, Eligible for RBI loans

Examples:

All PSU banks, Private banks, Foreign banks, Most RRBs, SFBs

Non-Scheduled Banks

Definition:

NOT in Second Schedule of RBI Act

Limitations:

Cannot borrow from RBI, Smaller operations

Examples:

Few small cooperative banks

Memory Trick: "Scheduled Banks = In Second Schedule"

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Can Payments Banks give loans?

No

2. Maximum deposit limit in Payments Banks?

₹2 lakh per customer

3. RRB ownership composition?

50% Centre, 15% State, 35% Sponsor Bank

4. Which bank supports agriculture and rural development?

NABARD

5. EXIM Bank deals with?

Foreign trade (Export-Import)

6. SIDBI supports which sector?

Small industries / MSMEs

7. Which banks are listed in Second Schedule of RBI Act?

Scheduled Banks

8. Example of foreign bank in India?

HSBC, Citi Bank, Standard Chartered

9. Cooperative banks belong to which sector?

Rural / agriculture sector

10. Which bank category focuses on small borrowers?

Small Finance Banks

Conclusion

Understanding the different types of banks in India is essential for SSC economics preparation. Each bank type serves specific purposes and target audiences in India's financial system.

Key Takeaways

Commercial Banks: Public, Private, Foreign
RRBs: 50-15-35 ownership, rural focus
Cooperative Banks: Member-owned, agriculture focus
Payments Banks: ₹2 lakh limit, no loans
Small Finance Banks: Financial inclusion focus
Development Banks: NABARD, SIDBI, EXIM
Scheduled Banks: In RBI's Second Schedule
Memory: "Pay, Not Loan", "50-15-35", "Small Loans for Small People"
