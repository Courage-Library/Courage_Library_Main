Repo Rate & Reverse Repo Rate

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Repo Rate?

Repo Rate = Rate at which RBI LENDS money to banks

How It Works

Transaction:

Banks borrow from RBI when they need money

Collateral:

Loan is given against government securities

Purpose:

Control inflation and money supply

Memory Trick: "REPO = RBI → Banks (R → B)"

Repo = RBI gives money to Banks

Effect on Economy

If Repo Rate ↑ (Increases)
  • • Loans become expensive
  • • People borrow less
  • • Money supply ↓
  • • Inflation ↓
If Repo Rate ↓ (Decreases)
  • • Loans become cheaper
  • • People borrow more
  • • Money supply ↑
  • • Economy grows

What is Reverse Repo Rate?

Reverse Repo Rate = Rate at which RBI BORROWS money FROM banks

How It Works

Transaction:

Banks deposit surplus money with RBI

Interest:

Banks earn interest on deposited money

Purpose:

Absorb excess money from the system

Memory Trick: "Reverse Repo = Bank → RBI (B → R)"

Reverse Repo = Banks give money to RBI

Effect on Economy

If Reverse Repo ↑ (Increases)
  • • Banks keep more money with RBI
  • • Less money in market
  • • Money supply ↓
  • • Inflation ↓
If Reverse Repo ↓ (Decreases)
  • • Banks keep less money with RBI
  • • More money flows into market
  • • Money supply ↑
  • • Growth ↑

Difference Between Repo & Reverse Repo (SSC's Favourite!)

Feature Repo Rate Reverse Repo Rate
Who gives money? RBI → Banks Banks → RBI
Purpose Control inflation by reducing borrowing Absorb excess money from system
Interest Rate Always higher Always lower
Effect of Increase Money supply ↓ Money supply ↓
Used for Lending (banks borrow) Deposits (banks lend to RBI)

Memory Trick: "REPO = R Elease money"

Memory Trick: "REVERSE = R Eceive money back"

Why Repo & Reverse Repo Are Important?

Affects Loan EMIs

Changes in repo rate directly impact home, car, and personal loan EMIs

Controls Inflation

Primary tool to control rising prices in the economy

Monetary Policy Basis

Key instruments of RBI's monetary policy

MPC Decisions

Monetary Policy Committee decides repo rate every 2 months

Banking System Impact

Affects entire banking system's lending and deposit rates

Economic Growth

Influences investment, consumption, and overall economic growth

Related Terms (Frequently Asked)

MSF

Marginal Standing Facility

Banks borrow from RBI overnight at rate higher than Repo Rate

Used for emergency cash shortages

Bank Rate

Long-term lending rate

Rate at which RBI lends money for LONG TERM loans to banks

Higher than Repo Rate

LAF

Liquidity Adjustment Facility

Includes Repo + Reverse Repo operations

Main liquidity management tool of RBI

Memory Tricks to Remember Easily

Trick 1:

"Repo = RBI Repaise deta hai" (RBI gives money)

Trick 2:

"Reverse Repo = RBI Reverse money leta hai" (RBI takes money)

Trick 3:

"Repo = Reduce inflation | Reverse Repo = Remove money"

Trick 4:

Think: "RBI to Banks = Repo | Banks to RBI = Reverse Repo"

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Repo Rate is:

Rate at which RBI lends money to banks

2. Reverse Repo Rate is:

Rate at which RBI borrows money from banks

3. Repo Rate is primarily used to:

Control inflation

4. Reverse Repo Rate is used to:

Absorb excess liquidity from the system

5. What happens when Repo Rate increases?

Loans become costly → inflation falls

6. What happens when Reverse Repo Rate increases?

Banks deposit more money with RBI → money supply decreases

7. Reverse Repo Rate is always:

Lower than Repo Rate

8. Repo Rate is decided by?

Monetary Policy Committee (MPC)

9. LAF includes which two operations?

Repo + Reverse Repo

10. MSF is used for?

Overnight emergency borrowing by banks from RBI

Conclusion

Understanding Repo Rate and Reverse Repo Rate is fundamental for grasping how RBI controls the economy. These rates are the most important monetary policy tools in India.

Key Takeaways

Repo Rate: RBI lends to banks, Controls inflation
Reverse Repo: RBI borrows from banks, Absorbs excess money
Repo ↑ → Loans expensive → Inflation ↓
Reverse Repo ↑ → Banks deposit more → Money supply ↓
Repo always higher than Reverse Repo
MPC decides Repo Rate every 2 months
MSF: Emergency overnight borrowing
Memory: "Repo = RBI→Banks", "Reverse Repo = Banks→RBI"
