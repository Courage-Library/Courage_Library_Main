Revenue vs. Capital Expenditure

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Expenditure?

Money spent by the government, classified into two types:

1. Revenue Expenditure

Day-to-day operations

2. Capital Expenditure

Long-term asset creation

Revenue Expenditure (IMPORTANT for SSC)

Money spent on day-to-day operations that does NOT create assets or reduce liabilities

Key Characteristics

Does NOT create assets

Does NOT reduce liabilities

Recurring/regular in nature

Short-term impact

Examples (Learn These for SSC!)

Salaries
Pensions
Subsidies
Interest Payments
Defense Maintenance
Grants to States
Police Administration
Teachers' Salaries

Memory Trick: "Revenue = Regular"

Capital Expenditure (VERY IMPORTANT for SSC)

Money spent to create assets or reduce liabilities - Long-term investment in economy

Key Characteristics

Creates assets

Reduces liabilities

Non-recurring in nature

Long-term impact

Examples (SSC Favorites!)

Road Construction
Highways
Schools
Hospitals
Railways
Defense Equipment
Loans to States
Metro Projects

Memory Trick: "Capital = Construct" (creates long-term assets)

Difference Table (SSC's Favourite Question)

Feature Revenue Expenditure Capital Expenditure
Purpose Day-to-day functions Creating long-term assets
Creates Assets? No Yes
Reduces Liabilities? No Yes
Nature Recurring Non-recurring
Examples Salaries, subsidies Roads, railways
Economic Impact Short-term Long-term growth

Memory Trick: Revenue → Routine | Capital → Construction

Why Does Revenue vs Capital Matter?

High Revenue Expenditure

Indicates too much spending on routine tasks

Less funds available for development

May lead to higher deficits

High Capital Expenditure

Good for long-term economic growth

Creates infrastructure for future

Boosts employment and productivity

Capital expenditure boosts: Infrastructure, Employment, Productivity

What SSC MTS LOVES to Ask

Revenue Expenditure Examples

Salaries → Revenue
Subsidies → Revenue
Interest Payments → Revenue
Pensions → Revenue

Capital Expenditure Examples

Road Construction → Capital
Buying Fighter Jets → Capital
Loans to States → Capital
Setting up Railways → Capital

Memorize These Examples for SSC!

Super Memory Tricks

Revenue

"REGULAR"

R = Revenue
R = Regular daily expenses

Capital

"CONSTRUCT"

C = Capital
C = Construct assets

Simple Line

"Revenue Expenditure runs the government,
Capital Expenditure builds the nation."

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which of the following is Revenue Expenditure?

Salary of government employees, Subsidies, Interest payments

2. Which of the following is Capital Expenditure?

Construction of bridges, Investment in PSUs, Loans to state governments

3. Does revenue expenditure create assets?

No

4. Does capital expenditure reduce liabilities?

Yes

5. Buying fighter aircraft is:

Capital Expenditure

6. Paying pensions is:

Revenue Expenditure

7. Road construction comes under:

Capital Expenditure

8. Which improves long-term economic growth?

Capital Expenditure

Conclusion

Understanding the difference between Revenue and Capital Expenditure is crucial for government finance questions in SSC exams. This distinction helps analyze government spending priorities.

Key Takeaways

Revenue: Day-to-day, no assets created
Capital: Long-term, creates assets
Revenue Examples: Salaries, subsidies, pensions
Capital Examples: Roads, railways, defense equipment
Revenue: Recurring, short-term impact
Capital: Non-recurring, long-term growth
Memory: "Revenue = Regular", "Capital = Construct"
SSC Focus: Memorize specific examples from both categories

This topic guarantees 1-2 easy marks in SSC MTS/GD exams.
