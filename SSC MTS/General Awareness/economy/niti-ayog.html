NITI Aayog

Economics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is NITI Aayog?

National Institution for Transforming India

Key Information

Formed:

1 January 2015

Replaced:

Planning Commission (1950â€“2014)

Nature:

Policy think tank of Government of India

Key Role:

Gives recommendations, does NOT allocate funds

Why Created?

Planning Commission (Old)

Centralized planning, One-way instructions, Fund allocation

NITI Aayog (New)

Bottom-up planning, Cooperative federalism, Flexibility for states

Structure of NITI Aayog (SSC LOVES THIS)

1. Chairperson

Prime Minister of India

2. Vice-Chairperson

Appointed by Prime Minister

3. Governing Council

Chief Ministers of all states + Lieutenant Governors of UTs

Most important body

4. CEO

Chief Executive Officer, Appointed by Prime Minister

Regional Councils

Resolve regional issues

Full-time Members

Experts in various fields

Special Invitees

Experts from different sectors

Functions of NITI Aayog (VERY IMPORTANT)

1. Policy Think Tank

Provides ideas & strategies to government

2. Cooperative Federalism

Works with state governments as partners

3. Competitive Federalism

Encourages states to compete in development

4. Long-term Vision

15-year vision, 7-year strategy, 3-year action agenda

5. Monitoring Schemes

PM Gati Shakti, Aspirational Districts Programme

6. Innovation Promotion

Promotes innovation & entrepreneurship

7. SDG Implementation

Encourages Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

8. Performance Ranking

Health Index, School Education Index for states

Important Initiatives of NITI Aayog

1. Aspirational Districts Programme

Develop backward districts, Focus on health, education, agriculture

2. Atal Innovation Mission (AIM)

Atal Tinkering Labs in schools, Promotes innovation, startups, STEM

3. SDG India Index

Tracks progress on Sustainable Development Goals

4. Health & Education Indices

Ranks states on health and school education performance

5. National Nutrition Mission

Improves nutrition among women & children

6. Policy Support

Digital India, Make in India policy inputs

NITI Aayog vs Planning Commission (SSC's Favourite)

Feature NITI Aayog Planning Commission

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. NITI Aayog was formed in which year?

2015

2. NITI Aayog replaced which organization?

Planning Commission

3. Who is the Chairperson of NITI Aayog?

Prime Minister of India

4. NITI Aayog is primarily a _____ body.

Policy think tank

5. Does NITI Aayog allocate funds to states?

No

6. The Governing Council includes?

Chief Ministers of all states & Lieutenant Governors of UTs

7. Atal Innovation Mission is under?

NITI Aayog

8. Which programme aims to improve backward districts?

Aspirational Districts Programme

9. NITI Aayog promotes which type of federalism?

Cooperative & Competitive federalism

10. CEO of NITI Aayog is appointed by?

Prime Minister

Conclusion

NITI Aayog represents a fundamental shift in India's planning approach from centralized control to cooperative federalism. Understanding its structure and functions is crucial for SSC economics questions.

Key Takeaways

Full Form: National Institution for Transforming India
Formed: 1 January 2015, replaced Planning Commission
Nature: Policy think tank, not fund allocator
Chairperson: Prime Minister of India
Governing Council: CMs of all states + LGs of UTs
Key Initiatives: Aspirational Districts, Atal Innovation Mission
Federalism: Cooperative & Competitive approach
Memory: "NITI = New India Think Institution", "No funds, Only ideas"
