Acids, Bases & Salts

Chemistry - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What Are Acids, Bases & Salts?

Acids

Ion Released:

H⁺ (Hydrogen ions)

Examples:

• HCl (Hydrochloric acid)

• H₂SO₄ (Sulphuric acid)

• Vinegar (Acetic acid)

Memory Trick: Acids → A for "Attack" (corrosive)

Bases

Ion Released:

OH⁻ (Hydroxide ions)

Examples:

• NaOH (Sodium hydroxide)

• KOH (Potassium hydroxide)

• Baking soda solution

Memory Trick: Base → Bitter + Slippery

Salts

Formation:

Acid + Base → Salt + Water

Examples:

• NaCl (Table salt)

• NaHCO₃ (Baking soda)

• CaSO₄·2H₂O (Gypsum)

Properties of Acids & Bases

Property Acids Bases
Taste Sour Bitter
Touch Not safe Slippery
Nature Corrosive Corrosive
Ion Released H⁺ OH⁻
Metal Reaction Produces H₂ gas No H₂ gas
Conductivity Conducts electricity Conducts electricity

Chemical Reactions (IMPORTANT)

Key Reactions

1. Neutralization

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

Acid + Base → Salt + Water

2. Acid + Metal

Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂↑

Memory Trick: Acid with Metal → H₂ gas

Other Reactions

3. Acid + Carbonate

2HCl + Na₂CO₃ → 2NaCl + CO₂ + H₂O

Produces CO₂ gas

4. Acid + Metal Oxide

CuO + 2HCl → CuCl₂ + H₂O

Metal oxide + Acid → Salt + Water

Indicators

Litmus

Acid:

Blue → Red

Base:

Red → Blue

Memory Trick: Acid attacks Blue → turns Red

Phenolphthalein

Acid:

Colorless

Base:

Pink

Turmeric

Acid:

No change

Base:

Turns red

Methyl Orange

Acid:

Red

Base:

Yellow

pH Scale

0 ← Acidic | 7 → Neutral | 14 → Basic

Acidic (pH < 7)

Stomach acid:

pH 1–2

Vinegar:

pH 3

Lemon juice:

pH 2–3

Neutral (pH = 7)

Pure Water

Basic (pH > 7)

Soap:

pH 10

Bleach:

pH 13

Baking soda solution:

pH 8–9

Memory Trick: Acids = A (Aage numbers chhote), Bases = B (Bade numbers)

Important Salts (SSC High Priority)

Baking Soda

NaHCO₃

Uses:

  • • Baking (makes dough rise)
  • • Antacid (neutralizes stomach acid)
  • • Fire extinguisher

Washing Soda

Na₂CO₃·10H₂O

Uses:

  • • Removes hardness of water
  • • Cleaning agent
  • • Water softening

Bleaching Powder

CaOCl₂

Uses:

  • • Disinfection
  • • Cleaning water
  • • Bleaching paper

Plaster of Paris

CaSO₄·½H₂O

Uses:

  • • Casts for fractures
  • • Statues
  • • Decorative items

Gypsum

CaSO₄·2H₂O

Uses:

  • • Making cement
  • • Fertilizer
  • • Making POP

Strong vs Weak Acids/Bases

Acids

Strong Acids:

• HCl (Hydrochloric acid)

• H₂SO₄ (Sulphuric acid)

• HNO₃ (Nitric acid)

Weak Acids:

• CH₃COOH (Acetic acid)

• Citric acid

• Lactic acid

Bases

Strong Bases:

• NaOH (Sodium hydroxide)

• KOH (Potassium hydroxide)

• Ca(OH)₂ (Calcium hydroxide)

Weak Bases:

• NH₄OH (Ammonium hydroxide)

• Baking soda solution

3. What color does litmus turn in acids?

Red

4. What color is phenolphthalein in bases?

Pink

5. What is the chemical formula of baking soda?

NaHCO₃

6. What is the formula of Plaster of Paris?

CaSO₄·½H₂O

7. What is bleaching powder formula?

CaOCl₂

8. What is neutralization reaction?

Acid + Base → Salt + Water

9. Which acid is present in vinegar?

10. What does washing soda remove from water?

Hardness

Conclusion

Mastering Acids, Bases & Salts is crucial for SSC chemistry preparation. These concepts form the foundation of chemical reactions and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Acid: Releases H⁺ ions (sour taste)
Base: Releases OH⁻ ions (bitter taste)
Neutralization: Acid + Base → Salt + Water
Litmus: Acid→Red, Base→Blue
pH scale: Acidic < 7, Neutral=7, Basic> 7
Baking soda: NaHCO₃ (antacid, baking)
Washing soda: Na₂CO₃·10H₂O (water softener)
Bleaching powder: CaOCl₂ (disinfectant)
Plaster of Paris: CaSO₄·½H₂O (casts)
Gypsum: CaSO₄·2H₂O (cement, fertilizer)
