Human Body Systems

Biology - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Overview of Human Body Systems

The human body has 11 major systems working together

1. Digestive System
2. Respiratory System
3. Circulatory System
4. Excretory System
5. Nervous System
6. Muscular System
7. Skeletal System
8. Endocrine System
9. Reproductive System
10. Immune System
11. Integumentary System

Digestive System (SSC Favorite)

Breaks down food into nutrients for energy

Major Organs & Functions

Mouth:

Chewing + salivary amylase (digests starch)

Stomach:

HCl acid + pepsin (digests protein)

Small Intestine:

Main digestion + absorption

Liver:

Produces bile (digests fat)

Pancreas:

Produces enzymes (trypsin, lipase)

Key Enzymes

A

Amylase

Digests starch (carbohydrates)

P

Pepsin

Digests proteins

L

Lipase

Digests fats

Memory Trick: APL = Amylase (carbs), Pepsin (protein), Lipase (fat)

Respiratory System (VERY IMPORTANT)

Exchange of gases (O₂ in, CO₂ out)

Organs

Nose:

Filters and warms air

Trachea:

Windpipe

Bronchi:

Branches to lungs

Alveoli:

Site of gas exchange

Diaphragm:

Muscle that helps breathing

Breathing Process

⬇️

Inhalation

Diaphragm contracts

(Air enters lungs)

⬆️

Exhalation

Diaphragm relaxes

(Air leaves lungs)

Key Fact:

Gas exchange occurs in alveoli through diffusion

Circulatory System (Blood & Heart)

Heart Structure

Right Atrium

Receives deoxygenated blood

Right Ventricle

Pumps to lungs

Left Atrium

Receives oxygenated blood

Left Ventricle

Strongest chamber

Blood Vessels

Arteries:

Carry blood AWAY from heart

Memory Trick: A = Away (Artery)

Veins:

Carry blood TOWARD heart

Capillaries:

Site of gas & nutrient exchange

Blood Components

RBC (Red Blood Cells)

Carries oxygen (contains haemoglobin)

WBC (White Blood Cells)

Fights infection

Platelets

Clotting of blood

Plasma

Liquid part of blood

Excretory System (Urinary)

Removes waste (urea) from the body

Organs

Kidneys:

Filter blood, produce urine

Ureters:

Tubes carrying urine to bladder

Urinary Bladder:

Stores urine

Urethra:

Expels urine from body

Nephron (Functional Unit)

Each kidney has ~1 million nephrons

Functions:

  • • Filtration of blood
  • • Reabsorption of nutrients
  • • Urine formation

Nervous System

Two Main Parts

Central Nervous System (CNS):

Brain + Spinal Cord

Peripheral Nervous System (PNS):

All nerves connecting CNS to body

Brain Parts

Cerebrum:

Thinking, learning, memory

Cerebellum:

Controls balance and posture

Medulla (Brainstem):

Controls heartbeat, breathing

Memory Trick: CeREBRum = Remembering, CeREBELLum = Balance

Skeletal & Muscular Systems

Skeletal System

Functions:

  • • Support body
  • • Protect organs
  • • Help movement
  • • Produce blood cells

Important Bones:

Skull (protects brain)
Ribs (12 pairs)
Femur (longest)
Stapes (smallest)

Muscular System

3 Types of Muscles:

1. Skeletal Muscles

Voluntary movement (attached to bones)

2. Smooth Muscles

Involuntary (organs, blood vessels)

3. Cardiac Muscles

Involuntary (heart only)

Endocrine System (Hormones)

Pituitary Gland:

"Master gland" controls other glands

Thyroid Gland:

Produces thyroxine (controls metabolism)

Pancreas:

Produces insulin (controls blood sugar)

Adrenal Glands:

Produce adrenaline (fight or flight)

Testes (Males):

Produce testosterone

Ovaries (Females):

Produce estrogen, progesterone

Memory Trick: TIPAT-O → Thyroid, Insulin, Pituitary, Adrenal, Testes, Ovary

Reproductive System

Male

Testes:

Produce sperm

Hormone:

Testosterone

Female

Ovaries:

Produce ova (eggs)

Fallopian Tubes:

Site of fertilization

Uterus:

Where pregnancy develops

Memory Tricks

Brain Parts:

Cerebrum = THINK, Cerebellum = BALANCE, Medulla = LIFE functions

Arteries vs Veins:

Artery = Away, Vein = In

RBC vs WBC:

RBC = Oxygen, WBC = Defense

Digestive Enzymes:

APL = Amylase (carbs), Pepsin (protein), Lipase (fat)

Hormones:

INSulin = controls INSide sugar, Adrenaline = emergency

Respiratory:

Gas exchange in Alveoli

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Functional unit of kidney?

Nephron

2. Which part of brain controls balance?

Cerebellum

3. RBC contains which pigment?

Haemoglobin

4. Arteries carry blood?

Away from heart

5. Smallest bone in human body?

Stapes (in ear)

6. Which gland produces insulin?

Pancreas

7. Site of fertilization in females?

Fallopian tube

8. Where does gas exchange occur?

Alveoli (in lungs)

9. Largest organ in human body?

Skin

10. Hormone for metabolism?

Thyroxine

Conclusion

Mastering Human Body Systems is crucial for SSC biology preparation. These concepts are frequently tested and form the foundation of biology questions.

Key Takeaways

Digestive: APL enzymes (Amylase, Pepsin, Lipase)
Respiratory: Gas exchange in alveoli
Circulatory: Artery = Away, Vein = Toward heart
Excretory: Nephron = functional unit of kidney
Nervous: Cerebellum = balance, Cerebrum = thinking
Skeletal: Femur = longest, Stapes = smallest bone
Endocrine: Pancreas → Insulin, Thyroid → Thyroxine
Reproductive: Fertilization in fallopian tube
Blood: RBC = oxygen, WBC = defense, Platelets = clotting
Brain: Medulla controls heartbeat & breathing
