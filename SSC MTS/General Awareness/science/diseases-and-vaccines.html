Diseases & Vaccines

Biology - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is a Disease?

Disease = Any condition that disturbs normal functioning of the body

Communicable Diseases

Infectious

Spread from person to person

Examples: TB, Cholera, COVID-19, Malaria

Non-Communicable Diseases

Non-infectious

Do NOT spread (lifestyle/hereditary)

Examples: Diabetes, Heart disease, Cancer

Communicable Diseases

Bacterial Diseases

• Tuberculosis (TB)
• Cholera
• Typhoid
• Pneumonia
• Diphtheria

Treatment: Antibiotics

Memory Trick: T C T P D (TB, Cholera, Typhoid, Pneumonia, Diphtheria)

Viral Diseases

• Common cold
• Influenza (Flu)
• Measles
• Mumps
• Chickenpox
• Dengue
• Polio
• COVID-19

Treatment: Vaccines & antiviral drugs

Protozoan Diseases

• Malaria (Plasmodium)
• Amoebiasis

Fungal Diseases

• Ringworm
• Athlete's foot

Helminthic (Worms)

• Tapeworm
• Roundworm

Non-Communicable Diseases

Diabetes

High blood sugar levels

Hypertension

High blood pressure

Cancer

Uncontrolled cell growth

Heart Disease

Cardiovascular disorders

Asthma

Respiratory condition

Obesity

Excessive body fat

Causes:

Lifestyle factors, genetics, poor diet, lack of exercise, smoking, alcohol

Mode of Transmission

Disease Spread Through
Tuberculosis (TB) Air (droplets)
Cholera Contaminated water
Typhoid Contaminated food/water
Malaria Female Anopheles mosquito
Dengue Aedes mosquito (Day-biter)
Rabies Dog bite
AIDS Blood, sexual contact
Polio Contaminated food/water

Memory Trick: Malaria = Anopheles, Dengue = Aedes (Day-biter)

Immunity

Natural (Innate) Immunity

Present from birth

  • • Skin barrier
  • • Mucous membranes
  • • White blood cells
  • • Inflammatory response

Acquired Immunity

Develops after exposure

Active Immunity:

From infection or vaccination

Passive Immunity:

From mother (breastfeeding) or antibodies

Vaccines (SSC MOST IMPORTANT)

Vaccines contain weakened or dead microbes that trigger immunity

Disease Vaccine
Polio OPV / IPV
Tuberculosis (TB) BCG
Measles, Mumps, Rubella MMR
COVID-19 Covaxin / Covishield
Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus DPT
Hepatitis B Hep-B vaccine
Chickenpox Varicella vaccine
Rabies Anti-rabies vaccine
Influenza Flu vaccine
HPV (Cervical cancer) HPV vaccine

Memory Trick: BCG for TB, MMR for 3 viral diseases (Measles, Mumps, Rubella)

Symptoms of Common Diseases

Malaria

  • • High fever with chills
  • • Caused by Plasmodium
  • • Spread by Anopheles

Dengue

  • • Severe joint pains
  • • "Break-bone fever"
  • • Low platelet count
  • • Spread by Aedes

Tuberculosis (TB)

  • • Continuous cough
  • • Weight loss
  • • Blood in sputum

Cholera

  • • Severe diarrhea
  • • Dehydration

Typhoid

  • • Prolonged high fever
  • • Abdominal pain

Measles

  • • Red rash
  • • High fever

Use nets, repellents

Proper cooking & storage

Proper waste disposal

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Malaria is caused by which organism?

Plasmodium

2. Which mosquito spreads dengue?

Aedes mosquito

3. BCG vaccine is for which disease?

Tuberculosis (TB)

4. What is the polio vaccine called?

OPV / IPV

5. Which vaccine is MMR for?

Measles, Mumps, Rubella

6. Example of a viral disease?

Influenza / Dengue / COVID-19

7. Example of a bacterial disease?

Cholera / TB / Typhoid

8. How is rabies transmitted?

Dog bite

9. Which is a non-communicable disease?

Diabetes

10. Vector for malaria transmission?

Female Anopheles mosquito

Conclusion

Mastering Diseases & Vaccines is crucial for SSC biology preparation. These concepts are frequently tested and form a significant portion of biology questions.

Key Takeaways

Communicable: Spreads (TB, Cholera, Malaria)
Non-communicable: Doesn't spread (Diabetes, Cancer)
Malaria: Plasmodium, Anopheles mosquito
Dengue: Aedes mosquito (day biter)
TB vaccine: BCG
Polio vaccine: OPV/IPV
MMR: Measles, Mumps, Rubella
DPT: Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus
Viral diseases: No antibiotics, only vaccines
Bacterial diseases: Treated with antibiotics
