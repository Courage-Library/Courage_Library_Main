Work, Energy & Power

Physics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Work

Work is done when a force produces displacement in the direction of force

W = F × d

Key Information

Unit:

Joule (J)

Where:

F = Force (N), d = Displacement (m)

Condition:

Displacement must be in direction of force

When Work is NOT Done

No Displacement:

Pushing a wall (force but no movement)

Perpendicular Force:

Carrying a bag while walking (force ⟂ displacement)

Zero Force:

Object moving by inertia (no force applied)

Memory Trick: "No displacement = No work"

Types of Work

Positive Work

Force & displacement in same direction

Example: Pulling a trolley forward

Negative Work

Force & displacement in opposite direction

Example: Friction slowing down a car

Zero Work

Force perpendicular to displacement

Example: Walking with bag on head

Energy

Energy = Capacity to do work

Unit: Joule (J)

Kinetic Energy (KE)

KE = ½ m v²

Energy due to motion

Where:

m = mass, v = velocity

Key Point:

KE ∝ v² (if velocity doubles, KE becomes 4 times)

Memory Trick: "Moving energy = KE"

Potential Energy (PE)

PE = m g h

Energy due to position/height

Where:

m = mass, g = gravity (9.8 ≈ 10), h = height

Key Point:

PE ∝ h (if height doubles, PE doubles)

Memory Trick: "Height energy = PE"

Mechanical Energy

Mechanical Energy = KE + PE

Total energy of a body (sum of kinetic and potential energy)

Example: A moving ball at height has both KE & PE

Law of Conservation of Energy

"Energy can neither be created nor destroyed, only transformed from one form to another."

Key Concept

Total Energy = Constant

In any process, total energy remains the same

Memory Trick: "Total energy always constant"

Examples

Hydroelectricity:

PE → KE → Electrical energy

Pendulum:

PE ↔ KE continuously during swing

Falling Object:

PE decreases, KE increases, total energy constant

Power

Power = Rate at which work is done

P = W / t

Key Information

Unit:

Watt (W)

Definition:

1 Watt = 1 Joule/sec

Larger Unit:

Horsepower (HP)

1 HP = 746 W

Understanding Power

Where:

P = Power, W = Work, t = Time

Relation:

P ∝ 1/t (Power increases if work done faster)

Example:

Lifting same weight in half time → Power doubles

Memory Trick: Power = "Work fast"

Work Done Against Gravity

Work done to lift an object against gravity

W = m g h

Same as Potential Energy formula

Example:

Lifting a 2 kg object to height of 5 m

W = m × g × h = 2 × 10 × 5 = 100 J

SSC Tip:

This is frequently asked in numerical problems

Units & Numerical Shortcuts

Important Units (SSC FAVORITE)

Quantity Unit Symbol
WorkJouleJ
EnergyJouleJ
PowerWattW
ForceNewtonN

Numerical Shortcuts

Velocity doubles → KE becomes 4 times

(KE ∝ v²)

Height doubles → PE doubles

(PE ∝ h)

Work done in half time → Power doubles

(P ∝ 1/t)

Mass doubles → KE & PE double

(KE ∝ m, PE ∝ m)

Memory Tricks

KE

"K for Kicking ball (motion)"

PE

"P for Peak (height)"

Power

"Speed of doing work"

Work

"Force × Distance"

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Unit of work?

Joule (J)

2. Work is done only when there is?

Displacement in direction of force

3. Formula for kinetic energy?

KE = ½ m v²

4. Formula for potential energy?

PE = m g h

5. Unit of power?

Watt (W)

6. 1 Horsepower (HP) = ?

746 Watt

7. Potential energy depends on?

Mass, gravity, and height

8. Kinetic energy depends on?

Mass and velocity

9. If velocity becomes 3 times, KE becomes?

9 times (KE ∝ v²)

10. Which law states energy cannot be created or destroyed?

Law of conservation of energy

Conclusion

Mastering Work, Energy, and Power concepts is essential for physics questions in SSC exams. These topics consistently appear and form the foundation of mechanics.

Key Takeaways

Work: W = F × d, needs displacement
KE = ½ m v² (motion energy)
PE = m g h (height energy)
Power: P = W/t (rate of work)
Energy Conservation: Total energy constant
Units: Joule (J) for work/energy, Watt (W) for power
1 HP = 746 W
Shortcuts: KE ∝ v², PE ∝ h, P ∝ 1/t
