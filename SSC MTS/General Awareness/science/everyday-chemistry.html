Everyday Chemistry

Soaps, Detergents & Plastics - SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Soaps

Soap = Sodium or Potassium salt of fatty acids

How Soap Cleans (VERY IMPORTANT)

Micelle Formation:

Soap molecules form micelles in water

Hydrophobic Tail

Attracts dirt/oil (repels water)

Hydrophilic Head

Attracts water (repels oil)

Cleaning Process:

1. Tail attaches to dirt → 2. Forms micelle → 3. Water washes away

Memory Trick:

Tail loves oil, Head loves water

Soap + Hard Water

Hard Water Contains:

Ca²⁺

Calcium ions

Mg²⁺

Magnesium ions

Problem:

Soap reacts with Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ → forms insoluble scum

Scum = reduces cleaning ability

Result:

Soaps do NOT work in hard water

Memory Trick:

"Soap hates HARD people (Ca, Mg)"

Detergents

Detergent = Sodium salts of long-chain sulphonic acids

Advantages Over Soap

✅ Works in Hard Water

No reaction with Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ ions

✅ More Effective

Better cleaning action

✅ More Foam

Produces abundant foam

⚠️ Disadvantage:

Some are non-biodegradable

Why Works in Hard Water?

Key Reason:

Detergents do NOT form insoluble compounds with Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺

No Scum Formation

Unlike soaps, detergents don't create scum

Example:

Sodium lauryl sulphate (common detergent)

Soaps vs Detergents (SSC Favorite)

Feature Soap Detergent
Made of Fatty acids + Na/K Sulphonic acids + Na
Hard Water ❌ No ✅ Yes
Scum Formation ✅ Yes ❌ No
Foam Production Less More
Biodegradable Mostly yes Some are not

Hard Water vs Soft Water

Hard Water

Contains:

Calcium (Ca²⁺) and Magnesium (Mg²⁺) ions

Effect on Soap:

Forms insoluble scum, reduces cleaning

Sources:

  • • Underground water
  • • Well water
  • • Borewell water

Soft Water

Contains:

Minimal Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ ions

Effect on Soap:

Works efficiently, produces good lather

Sources:

  • • Rainwater
  • • Distilled water
  • • Softened water

Plastics (VERY IMPORTANT)

Plastics are long-chain molecules (polymers) - Two main types

Thermoplastics

Property:

Soften on heating, can be reshaped

Examples:

PVC
PET
Polythene
Nylon
Acrylic

Memory Trick:

Thermo = Heat, Plastic = Shape again

Thermosetting Plastics

Property:

Cannot be reshaped once molded

Examples:

Bakelite
Melamine
Teflon
Rubber

Memory Trick:

Thermosetting = Set forever

Important Plastics & Their Uses

Plastic Type Uses
PVC Thermoplastic Pipes, credit cards, floor tiles
PET Thermoplastic Cold drink bottles, food containers
Nylon Thermoplastic Clothes, ropes, toothbrush bristles
Bakelite Thermosetting Electrical switches, handles
Teflon Thermosetting Non-stick cookware, heat-resistant
Melamine Thermosetting Plates, bowls, kitchenware

Environmental Impact

Why Are Plastics Harmful?

Non-biodegradable

Take centuries to decompose

Pollution

Cause soil & water pollution

Toxic Fumes

Release harmful gases when burned

Solution: 3R Principle

Reduce

Use less plastic

Reuse

Use plastic items multiple times

Recycle

Convert used plastic into new products

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Soaps are made from?

Sodium/potassium salts of fatty acids

2. Why do detergents work in hard water?

They do not react with Ca²⁺ and Mg²⁺ ions

3. What are micelles formed by?

Soaps and detergents

4. Example of thermoplastic?

PVC

5. Example of thermosetting plastic?

Bakelite

6. What is Teflon used for?

Non-stick cookware

7. Why do soaps fail in hard water?

Form insoluble scum with Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺

8. What is PET used for?

Bottles and food containers

9. Hard water contains which ions?

Calcium (Ca²⁺) and Magnesium (Mg²⁺) ions

10. What is Bakelite used in?

Electrical switches and handles

Conclusion

Mastering Everyday Chemistry (Soaps, Detergents & Plastics) is essential for SSC preparation. These practical concepts frequently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Soap: Fatty acid salt, fails in hard water
Detergent: Sulphonic acid salt, works in hard water
Micelle: Hydrophobic tail + hydrophilic head
Hard water: Contains Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺ ions
Thermoplastic: Re-shapeable (PVC, PET, Nylon)
Thermosetting: Permanent shape (Bakelite, Teflon)
PVC: Pipes, credit cards
PET: Bottles, containers
Bakelite: Electrical switches
Teflon: Non-stick cookware
