Nutrition in Plants & Animals

Biology - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Nutrition – What & Why?

Nutrition = Process by which organisms obtain food and utilise it for energy, growth, repair

Autotrophic Nutrition

Plants

Organisms make their own food

Heterotrophic Nutrition

Animals

Organisms depend on others for food

Nutrition in Plants

CO₂ + H₂O → Glucose + O₂

Photosynthesis Equation

Raw Materials for Photosynthesis

CO₂

Carbon dioxide (from air)

H₂O

Water (from roots)

Sunlight

Energy source

Chlorophyll

Green pigment in leaves

Memory Trick: C-HO-SC (CO₂, H₂O, Sunlight, Chlorophyll)

Plant Structures

Stomata:

Tiny pores for gas exchange (CO₂ in, O₂ out)

Controlled by guard cells

Transportation:

Xylem

Water + minerals

Phloem

Food (glucose)

Memory Trick: Xylem = X for H₂O, Phloem = Food

Heterotrophic Plants (Exceptions)

Cuscuta (Amarbel)

Parasite (depends on host)

Pitcher Plant

Insectivorous

Venus Flytrap

Insectivorous

Human Digestive System (SUPER IMPORTANT)

1. Mouth

  • • Teeth chew food
  • • Saliva contains amylase
  • • Digests starch

2. Stomach

  • • Contains HCl (kills germs)
  • • Pepsin digests proteins
  • • Churning action

3. Small Intestine

Most important part

  • • Complete digestion
  • • Absorption via villi
  • • Bile digests fat

4. Large Intestine

  • • Absorbs water
  • • Forms feces

5. Anus

  • • Excretion of waste

Digestive Enzymes (Key to Remember)

Enzyme Works On Found In
Amylase Starch Mouth (Saliva)
Pepsin Proteins Stomach
Lipase Fats Pancreas/Small Intestine
Trypsin Proteins Pancreas
Maltase Maltose → Glucose Intestine

Memory Trick: APL = Amylase, Pepsin, Lipase

Nutrition in Amoeba (SSC Loves This!)

How Amoeba Feeds

Uses:

Pseudopodia

"False feet" for movement & feeding

Process:

IDAAE

Steps (IDAAE)

I → Ingestion

Taking in food using pseudopodia

D → Digestion

Food vacuole + enzymes break down food

A → Absorption

Nutrients absorbed into cytoplasm

A → Assimilation

Using nutrients for energy & growth

E → Egestion

Expelling undigested waste

Nutrition in Ruminants (Cow, Buffalo)

Ruminants have a four-chambered stomach

Four Chambers (RROA)

Rumen

Storage and fermentation

Reticulum

Honeycomb structure

Omasum

Absorbs water

Abomasum

"True stomach" with enzymes

Memory Trick: RROA → Rumen, Reticulum, Omasum, Abomasum

Rumination Process

Step 1:

Swallow grass → stored in rumen

Step 2:

Rumination (chewing cud)

Bring food back to mouth for re-chewing

Step 3:

Microbes in rumen digest cellulose

Types of Heterotrophic Nutrition

Herbivores

Eat plants only

Examples: Cow, Goat, Deer

Carnivores

Eat meat only

Examples: Lion, Tiger

Omnivores

Eat plants + meat

Examples: Human, Bear

Parasites

Depend on host

Examples: Louse, Tapeworm

Saprophytes

Feed on dead matter

Examples: Fungi, Bacteria

Memory Tricks

Photosynthesis:

C-HO-SC (CO₂, H₂O, Sunlight, Chlorophyll)

Plant Transport:

Xylem = Water, Phloem = Food

Digestive Enzymes:

APL = Amylase, Pepsin, Lipase

Amoeba:

Pseudo = Fake feet, IDAAE process

Ruminants:

RROA chambers (Rumen, Reticulum, Omasum, Abomasum)

Villi:

Absorption in small intestine

Complete Digestion:

Small intestine (main site)

Bile:

Digests fats (from liver)

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Where does photosynthesis occur in plant cells?

Chloroplast

2. What do guard cells control?

Stomata

3. What does xylem transport?

Water and minerals

4. Main site of digestion in humans?

Small intestine

5. Which enzyme digests protein in stomach?

Pepsin

6. What does Amoeba use for movement & feeding?

Pseudopodia

7. What does rumination mean in ruminants?

Chewing cud

8. What does bile help digest?

Fats

9. What is the function of villi?

Absorption of nutrients

10. Where is water absorbed in digestive system?

Large intestine

Conclusion

Mastering Nutrition in Plants & Animals is essential for SSC biology preparation. These concepts are fundamental and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Photosynthesis: CO₂ + H₂O → Glucose + O₂
Autotrophic: Plants make own food
Heterotrophic: Animals depend on others
Xylem: Water transport, Phloem: Food transport
Stomata: Gas exchange (guard cells control)
APL enzymes: Amylase, Pepsin, Lipase
Small intestine: Main digestion site
Villi: Increase absorption surface
Amoeba: Uses pseudopodia, IDAAE process
Ruminants: Four-chambered stomach (RROA)
