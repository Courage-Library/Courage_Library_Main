Genetics Basics

Biology - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Genetics?

Genetics = Study of heredity (passing of traits from parents to offspring)

DNA, Genes & Chromosomes

DNA

Deoxyribonucleic Acid

  • • Blueprint of life
  • • Double helix structure
  • • Contains genetic information

Gene

Segment of DNA

  • • Controls specific traits
  • • Example: Eye color, height
  • • Basic unit of heredity

Chromosome

46

Chromosomes in humans

  • • Thread-like structure
  • • Inside nucleus
  • • 23 pairs = 46 total

Memory Trick: DNA → Genes → Chromosomes (small to big)

Mendel – Father of Genetics

Gregor Mendel studied pea plants & discovered inheritance laws

1. Law of Dominance

Principle:

Some traits are dominant, some recessive

Dominant

Always expresses (T)

Recessive

Hidden when dominant present (t)

Example:

Tall (T) is dominant over short (t)

2. Law of Segregation

Principle:

During gamete formation, gene pairs separate

Example:

Parent Tt → produces gametes T & t

Result:

Each gamete carries only one allele

Dominant & Recessive Traits

Dominant Trait

Capital Letter (T)

Visible when:

  • • One allele present (Tt)
  • • Both alleles present (TT)

Example: Tallness, Brown eyes

Recessive Trait

Small Letter (t)

Visible only when:

Both alleles are recessive (tt)

Example: Shortness, Blue eyes

Mutation = Sudden change in gene or DNA structure

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who is the father of genetics?

Gregor Mendel

2. What does DNA stand for?

Deoxyribonucleic Acid

3. How many chromosomes do humans have?

46 (23 pairs)

4. Cross between TT (tall) and tt (short) gives?

All tall (Tt)

5. What determines the sex of a baby?

Father's sperm (X or Y chromosome)

6. Which is dominant - T or t?

T (capital letter)

7. What does genotype refer to?

Genetic makeup (e.g., TT, Tt, tt)

8. What does phenotype refer to?

Physical appearance (e.g., Tall, Short)

9. What do TT and tt represent?

Homozygous condition

10. What is a mutation?

Sudden change in DNA

Conclusion

Mastering Genetics Basics is essential for SSC biology preparation. These concepts form the foundation of heredity and are frequently tested in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

DNA: Deoxyribonucleic acid, blueprint of life
Genes: Control specific traits
Chromosomes: Humans have 46 (23 pairs)
Mendel: Father of genetics, pea plant experiments
Dominant: Capital letter (T), always expresses
Recessive: Small letter (t), hidden with dominant
Homozygous: Same alleles (TT, tt)
Heterozygous: Different alleles (Tt)
Sex determination: XX = female, XY = male
Mutation: Sudden change in DNA structure
