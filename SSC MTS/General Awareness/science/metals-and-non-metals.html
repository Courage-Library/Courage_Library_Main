Metals & Non-Metals

Chemistry - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What Are Metals & Non-Metals?

Metals

Electron Behavior:

Lose electrons (form positive ions)

Examples:

Iron (Fe)
Copper (Cu)
Silver (Ag)
Gold (Au)

State:

Mostly solid at room temperature

Non-Metals

Electron Behavior:

Gain electrons (form negative ions)

Examples:

Oxygen (O)
Nitrogen (N)
Carbon (C)
Sulfur (S)

State:

Solid, liquid, or gas at room temperature

Physical Properties (VERY IMPORTANT)

Metals

Appearance:

Shiny (lustrous)

Conductivity:

Good conductors of heat & electricity

Malleability:

Can be hammered into sheets

Ductility:

Can be drawn into wires

Sonority:

Produce ringing sound

Exception:

Mercury - liquid metal

Memory Trick: "Shiny MDH SSS" (Shiny, Malleable, Ductile, Hard, Strong, Sonorous, Solid)

Non-Metals

Appearance:

Dull

Conductivity:

Poor conductors (good insulators)

Malleability:

Not malleable

Ductility:

Not ductile

Brittleness:

Break easily (brittle)

Exceptions:

• Graphite - conducts electricity

• Bromine - liquid non-metal

Memory Trick: "BPD" (Brittle, Poor conductors, Dull)

Chemical Properties

Metals

1. Metals + Oxygen → Metal Oxide

4Na + O₂ → 2Na₂O

Metal oxides are basic in nature

2. Metals + Water → Metal Hydroxide + H₂

2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂↑

Sodium & Potassium react violently

3. Metals + Acid → Salt + H₂

Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂↑

Memory Trick: Metal + Acid → H₂ gas

Non-Metals

1. Non-metals + Oxygen → Non-metal Oxide

C + O₂ → CO₂

Non-metal oxides are acidic

2. Non-metals + Hydrogen → Covalent Compounds

H₂ + S → H₂S

3. Non-metals + Acids

Do NOT react with acids (don't release electrons)

Alloys (VERY IMPORTANT)

Alloy = Homogeneous mixture of metals

Brass

Copper + Zinc

Used in: Musical instruments, decoration

Bronze

Copper + Tin

Used in: Statues, medals, bearings

Steel

Iron + Carbon

Used in: Construction, machinery

Stainless Steel

Iron + Chromium + Nickel

Used in: Utensils, medical instruments

Solder

Lead + Tin

Used in: Electrical circuits, welding

Important Metals & Their Uses

Metal Symbol Uses
Iron Fe Construction, machinery, vehicles
Copper Cu Electrical wires, plumbing
Aluminium Al Aircraft, utensils (lightweight)
Silver Ag Jewellery, best conductor
Sodium & Potassium Na, K Highly reactive (stored in kerosene)
Calcium Ca Bone & teeth formation

Important Non-Metals & Their Uses

Non-Metal Symbol Uses
Oxygen O₂ Respiration, combustion
Nitrogen N₂ Fertilizers, inert atmosphere
Chlorine Cl₂ Water purification, disinfectants
Sulfur S Medicine, firecrackers, vulcanization
Carbon C Fuel, graphite (pencil), diamond

Important Exceptions (SSC Always Asks!)

Liquid Metal:

Mercury (Hg)

Metals cut with knife:

Sodium, Potassium

Stored in kerosene:

Sodium, Potassium

Most reactive metal:

Caesium (Cs), then Potassium (K)

Liquid Non-Metal:

Bromine (Br)

Conducting Non-Metal:

Graphite (form of carbon)

Lowest density metal:

Lithium (Li)

Most reactive non-metal:

Fluorine (F)

Difference Between Metals & Non-Metals

Property Metals Non-Metals
Conductivity High Low
Appearance Shiny (lustrous) Dull
State Mostly solid Solid, liquid, or gas
Malleability Yes No
Ductility Yes No
Oxide Nature Basic Acidic
Electron Behavior Lose electrons Gain electrons

Memory Tricks

Metals:

MD S3 (Malleable, Ductile, Shiny, Strong, Sonorous)

Metal + Acid:

→ H₂ gas released

Sodium & Potassium:

"Knife metals" (cut with knife)

Non-Metals:

BPD (Brittle, Poor conductors, Dull)

Non-metal + Oxygen:

→ Acidic oxide

Alloys:

Brass = Cu+Zn, Bronze = Cu+Sn

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which metal is liquid at room temperature?

Mercury

2. Which non-metal is a good conductor of electricity?

Graphite

3. Brass is an alloy of which metals?

Copper + Zinc

4. Which metal reacts violently with water?

Sodium

5. Which non-metal is used in water purification?

Chlorine

6. Which type of oxide is basic in nature?

Metal oxide

7. Bronze is a mixture of which metals?

Copper + Tin

8. Which non-metal is used in fertilizers?

Nitrogen

9. What is the most reactive non-metal?

Fluorine

10. What are metals usually?

Malleable & ductile

Conclusion

Mastering Metals & Non-Metals is crucial for SSC chemistry preparation. These concepts are foundational and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Metals: Shiny, conductive, malleable
Non-metals: Dull, brittle, insulators
Metal oxides: Basic
Non-metal oxides: Acidic
Brass: Copper + Zinc
Bronze: Copper + Tin
Exceptions: Mercury (liquid metal), Graphite (conducting non-metal)
Sodium & Potassium: Stored in kerosene
Metal + Acid → H₂ gas
Most reactive non-metal: Fluorine
