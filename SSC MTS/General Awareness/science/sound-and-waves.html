Sound & Waves

Physics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is a Wave?

Wave = Disturbance that travels through a medium, transferring energy without transferring matter

Types of Waves

Longitudinal Waves

Particle Vibration:

Parallel to wave direction

Features:

  • • Compressions and rarefactions
  • • Example: Sound waves in air

Memory Trick:

Long → Along (parallel)

Transverse Waves

Particle Vibration:

Perpendicular to wave direction

Examples:

  • • Light waves
  • • Water waves
  • • Waves on rope

Memory Trick:

Transverse = T (perpendicular like T)

Sound Waves

Sound is a mechanical, longitudinal wave that needs a medium to travel

Key Characteristics

Type:

Mechanical longitudinal wave

Medium Required:

Cannot travel in vacuum

Memory Trick:

Sound ≠ Space (no medium → no sound)

Speed in Different Media

Fastest in:

Solids (~5000 m/s in iron)

Medium in:

Liquids (~1500 m/s in water)

Slowest in:

Gases (~340 m/s in air at 25°C)

Memory Trick: Solid > Liquid > Gas

Properties of Sound Waves

Amplitude (A)

Determines:

Loudness of sound

More amplitude → Louder sound

Frequency (f)

Unit:

Hertz (Hz)

Determines pitch of sound

High frequency → High pitch

Low frequency → Low pitch

Wavelength (λ)

Definition:

Distance between two compressions or rarefactions

Unit: meter (m)

Time Period (T)

Formula:

T = 1 / f

Time for one complete vibration

Speed of Sound (v)

Formula:

v = f × λ

Depends on medium properties

Echo & Reverberation

Echo

Definition:

Repetition of sound due to reflection

Conditions:

  • • Time delay ≥ 0.1 sec
  • • Minimum distance = 17.2 meters

Memory Trick:

Echo = 17 meters rule

Reverberation

Definition:

Repeated reflections causing prolonged sound

Example:

Sound in empty hall

Solution:

  • • Carpets
  • • Curtains
  • • Sound absorbers

SONAR (Sound Navigation and Ranging)

SONAR uses ultrasonic waves to measure depth and detect objects underwater

Working Principle

Based on:

Reflection of sound waves

Wave Type:

Ultrasonic waves (above 20 kHz)

Applications

Depth Measurement:

Measuring sea/ocean depth

Detection:

Submarine detection

Navigation:

Underwater navigation

Human Hearing Range

Normal Hearing Range: 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz (20 kHz)

Infrasonic Waves

Frequency:

Below 20 Hz

Below human hearing range

Ultrasonic Waves

Frequency:

Above 20 kHz

Uses:

  • • Medical scanners
  • • SONAR
  • • Bat navigation

Important Formulas (Quick Revision)

Wave Speed

v = f × λ

v = speed, f = frequency, λ = wavelength

Time Period

T = 1 / f

T = time period, f = frequency

Speed Order

Solid > Liquid > Gas

Speed of sound in different media

Memory Tricks

Sound Wave Type:

Mechanical + Longitudinal

Pitch:

Depends on Frequency

Loudness:

Depends on Amplitude

Echo:

17 meters minimum distance

Human Hearing:

20 Hz – 20 kHz

SONAR:

Ultrasonic + Reflection

Speed Order:

Solid > Liquid > Gas

Sound in Space:

No medium → No sound

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Sound travels fastest in which medium?

Solids

2. Sound cannot travel through?

Vacuum

3. What is the unit of frequency?

Hertz (Hz)

4. High pitch means?

High frequency

5. What is the formula for speed of sound?

v = f × λ

6. Minimum distance for echo to be heard clearly?

17.2 meters

7. Normal hearing range of humans?

20 Hz – 20,000 Hz

8. SONAR works on which principle?

Reflection of sound

9. Frequency of ultrasonic waves?

Above 20 kHz

10. What type of wave is sound?

Mechanical & Longitudinal

Conclusion

Mastering Sound & Waves is essential for SSC physics preparation. These concepts consistently appear in competitive exams and form the foundation of acoustics.

Key Takeaways

Sound: Mechanical longitudinal wave
Needs medium: Cannot travel in vacuum
Speed: Solid > Liquid > Gas
Frequency: Determines pitch
Amplitude: Determines loudness
Echo: Minimum 17.2 meters distance
Human hearing: 20 Hz – 20 kHz
SONAR: Uses ultrasonic wave reflection
