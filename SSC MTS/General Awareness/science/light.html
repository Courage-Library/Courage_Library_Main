Light (Reflection & Refraction)

Physics - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

What is Light?

Light is a form of energy that travels in straight lines

Key Properties

Type:

Transverse wave

Travel Medium:

Can travel through vacuum

Speed in Air:

3 × 10⁸ m/s

Basic Nature

Important Concept:

Light exhibits both wave and particle properties (wave-particle duality)

Reflection of Light

Reflection = Light bouncing back from a surface (like a mirror)

Laws of Reflection (VERY IMPORTANT)

First Law:

Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection

I = R

Second Law:

Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in the same plane

Plane Mirror Properties

Memory Trick: VESLO

V → Virtual (cannot be obtained on screen)
E → Erect (upright)
S → Same size as object
L → Laterally inverted (LEFT ↔ RIGHT reversed)
O → Object distance = Image distance

Spherical Mirrors

Concave Mirror (Converging)

Produces:

Mostly real & inverted images

Uses:

  • • Shaving mirror
  • • Dentist mirror
  • • Torch/Headlight
  • • Solar cooker

Special Case:

When object is very close → virtual & erect image

Convex Mirror (Diverging)

ALWAYS Produces:

  • • Virtual
  • • Erect
  • • Diminished (smaller)

Uses:

  • • Rear-view mirror of vehicles
  • • Security mirrors in shops

Memory Trick: Vehicle mirrors → always small & upright image

Quick Comparison

Concave Mirror:

Can make big, small, real, virtual (ALL types)

Convex Mirror:

Always small & virtual

Refraction of Light

Refraction = Bending of light when it passes from one medium to another

Why Refraction Happens?

Key Reason:

Speed of light changes in different media

Refractive Index (n)

n = Speed in vacuum / Speed in medium

High refractive index → light slows more → more bending

Bending Rules

Rarer → Denser Medium

Light bends towards normal

Example: Air → Water

Denser → Rarer Medium

Light bends away from normal

Example: Water → Air

Memory Trick: "R-D-T" (Rarer → Denser → Towards)

"Speed change → direction change"

Lenses

Convex Lens (Converging)

Produces:

Mostly real & inverted images (except when object close → virtual magnified)

Uses:

  • • Magnifying glass
  • • Camera lens
  • • Microscope & Telescope
  • • Eye correction for hypermetropia (far-sightedness)

Memory Trick: Convex = Converging

Concave Lens (Diverging)

ALWAYS Produces:

  • • Virtual
  • • Erect
  • • Diminished

Uses:

  • • Spectacles for myopia (near-sightedness)
  • • Peepholes in doors

Memory Trick: Concave = Cave = spreads light (diverging)

Real vs Virtual Images (SSC Frequently Asks)

Type of Image Characteristics Examples
Real Image Inverted, can be obtained on screen Formed by concave mirror & convex lens
Virtual Image Erect, cannot be captured on screen Plane mirror, convex mirror, concave lens

Critical Angle & Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

Total Internal Reflection occurs when light travels from denser to rarer medium at angle > critical angle

Conditions for TIR

1. Medium:

Light travels from denser → rarer medium

2. Angle:

Angle of incidence > Critical angle

Critical Angle:

Minimum angle for TIR to occur

Memory Trick: "Denser → Rarer + Big angle"

Uses of TIR

Optical Fibers:

Internet cables, medical endoscopes

Diamond Sparkle:

Due to multiple TIR inside diamond

Mirage:

Hot air near ground → light bends → illusion of water

Important Formulas (Quick Revision)

Mirror Formula

1/f = 1/v + 1/u

f = focal length, v = image distance, u = object distance

Lens Formula

1/f = 1/v – 1/u

For lenses (sign convention important)

Magnification

m = hᵢ/hₒ = v/u

hᵢ = image height, hₒ = object height

Memory Tricks

Plane Mirror:

"VESLO" (Virtual, Erect, Same size, Laterally inverted, Object=Image)

Concave Mirror:

Can make ALL types of images

Convex Mirror:

Always small & virtual

Convex Lens:

Converging (like focus)

Concave Lens:

Diverging (spreads)

TIR:

"Denser → Rarer + Big angle"

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. What type of image is formed in a plane mirror?

Virtual & Erect

2. Which mirror is used in headlights?

Concave mirror

3. Rear-view mirror is which type?

Convex mirror

4. Convex mirror produces which type of image?

Virtual, erect, diminished

5. When does light bend towards normal?

When going from rarer to denser medium

6. Which lens is used to correct Myopia?

Concave lens

7. Which lens is used as a magnifying glass?

Convex lens

8. What is refraction?

Bending of light when passing between different media

9. Real images are always?

Inverted

10. Mirage is due to which phenomenon?

Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

Conclusion

Mastering Light: Reflection & Refraction is crucial for SSC physics preparation. These concepts form the foundation of optics and consistently appear in competitive exams.

Key Takeaways

Reflection: I = R (angle of incidence = angle of reflection)
Plane Mirror: VESLO properties
Concave Mirror: Uses - shaving, dentist, solar cooker
Convex Mirror: Always virtual, erect, diminished
Refraction: Bending due to speed change
Convex Lens: Converging, for hypermetropia
Concave Lens: Diverging, for myopia
TIR: Denser→Rarer + angle > critical angle
