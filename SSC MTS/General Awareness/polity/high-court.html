High Courts of India

Indian Polity - State Judiciary - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction (Articles 214–231, Part VI)

High Courts are the highest judicial bodies at the state level in India. They serve as appellate courts for lower courts and protect citizens' rights within their jurisdiction.

Chief Justice + Other Judges

Jurisdiction & Powers of High Courts (MOST IMPORTANT)

1. Original Jurisdiction

Cases that start directly in High Court:

Election disputes of MPs & MLAs
Marriage, divorce, wills cases
Civil cases above certain value
2. Appellate Jurisdiction

Hears appeals from lower courts:

District Courts
Family Courts
Civil & Criminal Courts
Revenue Courts
3. Writ Jurisdiction (Article 226)

High Courts can issue writs for:

Fundamental Rights
Any other legal rights

Wider than SC: SC (Article 32) only for FRs

4. Supervisory Jurisdiction (Article 227)

High Court supervises functioning of:

All lower courts
Tribunals within jurisdiction

Ensures proper administration of justice

5. Power of Judicial Review

Can strike down state laws violating:

Constitution
Fundamental Rights

Part of Basic Structure Doctrine

6. Public Interest Litigation (PIL)

High Courts accept PILs for:

Environmental issues
Public safety
Human rights violations
Government inaction

Writs Issued by High Courts

High Courts issue the same 5 writs as Supreme Court under Article 226

Habeas Corpus

"Produce the body" - against illegal detention

Mandamus

"We command" - to perform public duty

Certiorari

"To be certified" - quash lower court orders

Prohibition

"To forbid" - prevent lower court excess jurisdiction

Quo-Warranto

"By what authority" - challenge illegal appointments

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. High Courts are mentioned in which Article of Constitution?

Article 214

2. At what age do High Court judges retire?

62 years

3. Who appoints High Court judges?

President of India

4. Under which Article can High Courts issue writs?

Article 226

5. How is writ jurisdiction of High Court different from Supreme Court?

HC can issue writs for FRs + other rights, SC only for FRs

6. Which High Court serves multiple states?

Guwahati HC, Bombay HC, Punjab & Haryana HC, etc.

7. How are High Court judges removed?

By President on recommendation of Supreme Court

8. Which Article gives High Court supervisory power over lower courts?

Article 227

9. Under which jurisdiction can High Court start cases directly?

Original Jurisdiction

10. Which is the oldest High Court in India?

Calcutta High Court (established 1862)

Conclusion

High Courts play a crucial role in India's judicial system as the highest courts at the state level. Their wide-ranging powers, especially under Article 226, make them accessible protectors of citizens' rights.

Key Takeaways

Articles: 214-231 in Part VI cover High Courts
Appointment: President appoints after consulting CJI, Governor, HC CJ
Retirement: 62 years, removal by President on SC recommendation
Jurisdiction: Original, Appellate, Writ (Article 226), Supervisory (227), Judicial Review
Writs: Same 5 as SC but wider scope (FRs + other rights)
Shared HCs: Bombay, Guwahati, Punjab & Haryana serve multiple states
Memory Tricks: "H M C P Q" for writs, "O A W S J" for jurisdiction, "65-62" for retirement
