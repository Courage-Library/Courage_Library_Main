UPSC (Union Public Service Commission)

Indian Polity - Constitutional Body - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction (Articles 315–323)

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) is India's premier recruiting agency for central government Group A and B posts. It is a constitutional body established to ensure impartial and merit-based recruitment.

Functions of UPSC (SSC's Favorite)

UPSC conducts examinations for recruitment to:

IAS
IPS
IFS
NDA
CDS
CAPF AC
Engineering Services
Geoscientist
1. Conduct Examinations

Recruits for All India Services and Central Group A & B services through competitive exams.

2. Advisory Role

Advises government on recruitment rules, promotions, transfers, and disciplinary actions.

3. Direct Recruitment

Handles direct recruitment through interviews and examinations for various posts.

4. Departmental Promotions

Conducts departmental promotions for central government services.

5. Assist State Governments

Provides assistance to state governments when requested (with President's approval).

6. Annual Report

Submits annual report to President, who places it before Parliament with memorandum.

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. UPSC is established under which Article of the Constitution?

Article 315

2. What is the tenure of UPSC Chairman?

6 years or until 65 years of age (whichever earlier)

3. How are UPSC members removed?

By President of India

4. Who appoints the UPSC Chairman?

President of India

5. UPSC submits its annual report to?

President of India

6. UPSC is a ______ body.

Constitutional body

7. Expenses of UPSC are charged from?

Consolidated Fund of India

8. Which Article deals with functions of UPSC?

Article 320

9. UPSC advises government on which matters?

Recruitment rules, promotions, transfers, disciplinary actions

10. UPSC recruits for which categories of services?

All India Services and Central Group A & B services

Conclusion

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) plays a vital role in maintaining the merit-based recruitment system for India's civil services. Its constitutional status ensures independence and impartiality in selecting the nation's administrative leadership.

Key Takeaways

Articles: 315-323 establish and regulate UPSC
Composition: Chairman + members, appointed by President
Tenure: 6 years or 65 years age limit ("6-65 Rule")
Removal: Only by President, on specific grounds
Functions: Conduct exams, advisory role, promotions, disciplinary matters
Independence: Expenses from Consolidated Fund, fixed tenure
UPSC vs SSC: Constitutional vs non-constitutional, Group A/B vs Group B/C
Memory Tricks: "6-65 Rule", "315 to 323 = UPSC highway", "Only President removes"
