CAG — Comptroller & Auditor General of India

Indian Polity - Constitutional Authority - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction (Articles 148–151)

The Comptroller & Auditor General (CAG) of India is the constitutional authority responsible for auditing all government expenditures. Often called the "Guardian of Public Money", CAG ensures transparency and accountability in government financial operations.

Independence of CAG (SSC's Favorite)

Constitution provides multiple safeguards to ensure CAG's independence:

No Easy Removal

Can only be removed through impeachment by Parliament (like SC judge).

Salary Protection

Salary fixed and cannot be reduced during tenure.

Consolidated Fund

Salary charged from Consolidated Fund of India (not voted by Parliament).

No Re-employment

Not eligible for further government employment after retirement.

Service Conditions

Service conditions cannot be changed to disadvantage after appointment.

Administrative Independence

Has independence in recruitment and administration of audit department.

Independence Memory Trick

"No Removal, No Reduction, No Re-appointment"

Duties & Functions of CAG (VERY IMPORTANT)

CAG audits accounts of:

Union Government
State Governments
PSUs
Defence
Government Companies
Government Receipts
1. Union Government Accounts

Audits all ministries, departments, and central government schemes.

2. State Government Accounts

Audits all state government expenditures and receipts.

3. Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs)

Audits government-owned corporations like ONGC, NTPC, SAIL, BSNL, Railways.

4. Defence Expenditure

Audits military purchases and defence spending (highly sensitive area).

5. Government Receipts

Audits tax collections, customs, GST, and other government revenues.

6. Government Companies

Audits companies where government has 51% or more shareholding (Companies Act 2013).

Types of Audits by CAG

Compliance Audit

Checks whether laws, rules, and regulations were followed in financial transactions.

Focus: Legal compliance

Financial Audit

Verifies accuracy and fairness of financial statements and accounts.

Focus: Accuracy of accounts

Performance Audit

Checks whether money was spent efficiently and achieved intended results ('value for money').

Focus: Efficiency and effectiveness

CAG Reports & Public Accounts Committee (PAC)

CAG Reports Flow (Article 151)

1

CAG submits reports to:

President (for Union) / Governor (for State)

2

Reports placed before:

Parliament (Union) / State Legislature (State)

3

Examined by:

Public Accounts Committee (PAC)

4

PAC Action:

Questions government, recommends corrective actions

CAG & PAC Relationship

PAC is a parliamentary committee that examines CAG reports.

PAC Composition: 22 members (15 LS + 7 RS)
Chairperson: Usually from Opposition party
Function: Examines CAG reports, questions government officials
CAG's Role: Acts as friend, philosopher, guide to PAC

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. CAG is mentioned in which Article of the Constitution?

Article 148

2. Who appoints the Comptroller & Auditor General of India?

President of India

3. What is the tenure of CAG?

6 years or 65 years of age (whichever earlier)

4. CAG can be removed like a ______.

Supreme Court Judge (impeachment by Parliament)

5. Which institutions does CAG audit?

Central govt, state govts, PSUs, defence, government companies

6. CAG reports are examined by which parliamentary committee?

Public Accounts Committee (PAC)

7. CAG salary is charged from which fund?

Consolidated Fund of India

8. CAG cannot be appointed for any further government job after retirement. True or False?

True

9. CAG submits its reports to?

President (for Union) / Governor (for State)

10. What are the three types of audits conducted by CAG?

Compliance, Financial, and Performance audits

Conclusion

The Comptroller & Auditor General of India is a cornerstone of financial accountability in Indian democracy. As the supreme auditor, CAG ensures that public funds are used efficiently, effectively, and in accordance with laws.

Key Takeaways

Articles: 148-151 cover CAG provisions
Appointment: By President, tenure 6 years or 65 years
Removal: Like SC judge (impeachment by Parliament)
Independence: Salary from Consolidated Fund, no re-employment
Audits: Union, state, PSUs, defence, government companies
Audit Types: Compliance, Financial, Performance (C-F-P)
Reports: To President/Governor, examined by PAC
Memory Tricks: "6-65 Rule", "CAG audits, PAC investigates", "148-Appoint, 149-Audit, 151-Report"
