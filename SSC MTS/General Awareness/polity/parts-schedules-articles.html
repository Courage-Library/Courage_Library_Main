Parts, Schedules & Important Articles of Indian Constitution

Indian Polity - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction

Understanding the structure of the Indian Constitution - its Parts, Schedules, and key Articles - is essential for SSC exams. This chapter provides a comprehensive overview with memory tricks and exam-focused information.

SSC frequently asks about total numbers of Parts/Schedules, important Article numbers, and specific schedule contents.

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. How many Parts are there in the Indian Constitution?

25 Parts

2. Which schedule contains the Official Languages of India?

8th Schedule

3. Fundamental Duties are mentioned in which Article?

Article 51A

4. Anti-defection law is contained in which Schedule?

10th Schedule

5. Which Articles deal with Emergency provisions?

Articles 352, 356, and 360

6. Article 21 of the Constitution provides which right?

Right to Life and Personal Liberty

7. Which Article deals with the amendment of the Constitution?

Article 368

8. Right to Education is mentioned in which Article?

Article 21A

9. Which schedule contains the Union, State, and Concurrent Lists?

7th Schedule

10. Who called Article 32 the "Heart and Soul of the Constitution"?

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Conclusion

Mastering the structure of the Indian Constitution - its Parts, Schedules, and key Articles - is crucial for SSC exams. This knowledge helps in answering direct questions and understanding the constitutional framework.

Key Takeaways

Parts: 25 total, focus on 1-5 (Union), 6 (State), 9/9A (Local), 18 (Emergency)
Schedules: 12 total, remember 7th (Lists), 8th (Languages), 10th (Anti-defection)
Fundamental Rights: Articles 12-35, especially 14, 19, 21, 32
Emergency: Articles 352 (National), 356 (State), 360 (Financial)
Amendment: Article 368 for constitutional changes
Duties: Article 51A added by 42nd Amendment
Memory Tricks: Use acronyms and patterns for quick recall during exams

This is one of the highest-scoring topics in SSC MTS, GD, and CHSL exams.
