Preamble of the Indian Constitution

Indian Polity - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction

The Preamble is the introductory statement to the Constitution, often called its "identity card." It outlines the philosophy, objectives, and fundamental values of the Indian Constitution.

SSC frequently asks about keywords, 42nd Amendment changes, objectives, and historical aspects of the Preamble.

Complete Text of the Preamble

"WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA,

having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a

SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC

and to secure to all its citizens:

JUSTICE

social, economic and political;

LIBERTY

of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship;

EQUALITY

of status and of opportunity;

and to promote among them all

FRATERNITY

assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation;

IN OUR CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY this twenty-sixth day of November, 1949, do HEREBY ADOPT, ENACT AND GIVE TO OURSELVES THIS CONSTITUTION."

PART 1: Key Words of the Preamble

S
1. SOVEREIGN

India is an independent nation with complete authority over its internal and external affairs.

No external power can control India's decisions.

S
2. SOCIALIST

Added by 42nd Amendment (1976). Focus on reducing inequalities and welfare state.

Mixed economy with public and private sectors.

S
3. SECULAR

Added by 42nd Amendment (1976). Equal respect for all religions, no state religion.

State maintains equal distance from all religions.

D
4. DEMOCRATIC

Government by the people, through elected representatives.

Universal adult franchise, free and fair elections.

R
5. REPUBLIC

Head of state is elected, not hereditary. President is elected for a fixed term.

No monarchy, political sovereignty with people.

Memory Trick: "SSSDR"

Sovereign – Socialist – Secular – Democratic – Republic

Remember: "Sardar Patel Said 'Democracy Rules'"

PART 2: Objectives of the Preamble

1. JUSTICE
Social Justice:

Equal social status, removal of discrimination.

Economic Justice:

Reduction of economic inequalities, fair distribution.

Political Justice:

Equal political rights, participation in governance.

2. LIBERTY
Thought:

Freedom to form independent opinions.

Expression:

Freedom to express views (Article 19).

Belief, Faith & Worship:

Freedom of religion (Articles 25-28).

3. EQUALITY
Status:

No discrimination based on caste, religion, gender.

Opportunity:

Equal chances in education, employment, public offices.

Before Law:

Article 14 guarantees equality before law.

4. FRATERNITY
Brotherhood:

Sense of unity and brotherhood among citizens.

Dignity of Individual:

Respect for personal dignity and rights.

Unity & Integrity:

'Integrity' added by 42nd Amendment (1976).

Memory Trick: "JLEF"

Justice – Liberty – Equality – Fraternity

Remember: "Just Love Every Friend"

PART 3: Important Facts & Amendments

Source of the Preamble
Inspired by:

Preamble of the United States Constitution

Difference:

Indian Preamble is more detailed and comprehensive

42nd Amendment (1976)

Added three key words to the Preamble:

Socialist
Secular
Integrity

Also known as "Mini Constitution" due to extensive changes

Legal Status & Amendment
Amendability:

Preamble can be amended (Kesavananda Bharati case, 1973)

Basic Structure:

Basic structure cannot be altered through amendments

Enforceability:

Not directly enforceable in courts (declaratory)

Important Dates
Adopted on:

26 November 1949

Came into force:

26 January 1950

42nd Amendment:

1976 (during Emergency)

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. From which country is the Indian Preamble inspired?

United States of America

2. Which amendment added "Socialist" and "Secular" to the Preamble?

42nd Amendment (1976)

3. What does "Republic" mean in the Preamble?

Head of state is elected, not hereditary

4. Is the Preamble part of the Indian Constitution?

Yes (Supreme Court clarified in 1973)

5. Which words were added to the Preamble by the 42nd Amendment?

Socialist, Secular, and Integrity

6. When was the Preamble adopted?

26 November 1949

7. When did the Preamble come into effect?

26 January 1950

8. What does "Sovereign" mean in the Preamble?

India is an independent nation with complete authority

9. What types of Justice are mentioned in the Preamble?

Social, Economic, and Political Justice

10. What does Fraternity ensure according to the Preamble?

Dignity of individual and Unity & Integrity of the Nation

Conclusion

The Preamble encapsulates the essence and philosophy of the Indian Constitution. It serves as a guiding light for interpretation and reflects the aspirations of the people of India.

Key Takeaways

Keywords: Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic
Objectives: Justice, Liberty, Equality, Fraternity
42nd Amendment: Added Socialist, Secular, Integrity (1976)
Source: Inspired by US Constitution but more detailed
Dates: Adopted 26 Nov 1949, Enforced 26 Jan 1950
Legal Status: Part of Constitution, amendable but basic structure protected
Memory Tricks: "SSSDR" for keywords, "JLEF" for objectives
