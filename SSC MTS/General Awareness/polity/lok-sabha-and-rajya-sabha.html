Lok Sabha & Rajya Sabha (Composition & Powers)

Indian Polity - Parliament - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Overview of the Parliament (Articles 79–122, Part V)

The Parliament of India is the supreme legislative body. It consists of:

1

President of India

Constitutional Head

2

Lok Sabha

House of the People

3

Rajya Sabha

Council of States

Lok Sabha (Lower House) - Directly Elected & More Powerful

Composition

Feature Details
Maximum Strength552
Present Strength543 elected members (2 nominated seats abolished by 104th Amendment)
Tenure5 years
ElectionDirect election by people
Minimum Age25 years
Presiding OfficerSpeaker (elected by Lok Sabha)
Seats Distribution530 from States, 20 from Union Territories

Powers of Lok Sabha (Most Important)

1. Money Bill Power (Exclusive)

Lok Sabha alone can introduce and pass Money Bills. Rajya Sabha can only delay for 14 days, not reject.

2. Controls Council of Ministers

Government is responsible only to Lok Sabha. Can remove through no-confidence motion or cut motions.

3. Prime Minister Membership

Prime Minister must be from Lok Sabha (or must win a seat soon after appointment).

4. Impeachment Powers

Can initiate impeachment of President and remove Council of Ministers.

5. Budget Control

More powerful in budget matters and financial legislation.

6. Joint Sitting Presiding

In case of deadlock, Joint Session is presided by the Speaker of Lok Sabha.

Rajya Sabha (Upper House) - Permanent House

Composition

Feature Details
Maximum Strength250
Present Strength245
Elected Members233 (States + UTs)
Nominated Members12 (by President)
Tenure6 years
Retirement Cycle1/3 members retire every 2 years
Minimum Age30 years
ChairmanVice-President of India

Powers of Rajya Sabha

1. Permanent House

Cannot be dissolved (continuing chamber).

2. Special Powers (VERY IMPORTANT)
Article 249: Power to allow Parliament to make laws on State List (requires 2/3rd majority).
Article 312: Creation of All India Services like IAS, IPS, IFS (requires 2/3rd majority).
3. Equal Powers with Lok Sabha

In Constitutional Amendment Bills, Ordinary Bills, Impeachment of President.

Removal of Vice-President can be initiated ONLY in Rajya Sabha.

4. Money Bill Delay

Can delay Money Bill for 14 days (but cannot reject it).

Memory Tricks

Lok Sabha Trick

"L = Larger power, Lower age, Lives 5 years"

Lower House → Higher powers. Minimum age 25 years, tenure 5 years.

Rajya Sabha Trick

"R = Retiring House, Requires 30 years of age"

Permanent but 1/3 retires every 2 years. Minimum age 30 years.

Money Bill Trick

"MONEY → MUST start in LOK SABHA"

Money Bills originate only in Lok Sabha. Rajya Sabha can only delay.

Rajya Sabha Special Powers

"R S → Resolution & Services"

Resolution → Article 249 (State List legislation)

Services → Article 312 (All India Services)

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. What is the maximum strength of Lok Sabha?

552

2. How many members of Rajya Sabha are nominated by the President?

12

3. What is the tenure of Rajya Sabha?

6 years (permanent house with 1/3 retiring every 2 years)

4. Who presides over Rajya Sabha?

Vice-President of India

5. Which House controls the Government?

Lok Sabha

6. Money Bill can be introduced only in which House?

Lok Sabha

7. What is Rajya Sabha's special power under Article 249?

Allow Parliament to legislate on State List subjects

8. Who presides over a Joint Session of Parliament?

Speaker of Lok Sabha

9. What is the minimum age for Lok Sabha membership?

25 years

10. Rajya Sabha is also called?

Council of States

Conclusion

Understanding the composition and powers of Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha is crucial for SSC exams. This topic covers the core structure of India's parliamentary system and is a high-scoring area.

Key Takeaways

Lok Sabha: Lower House, 552 max strength, 5-year term, controls government
Rajya Sabha: Upper House, 250 max strength, permanent, special powers under Articles 249 & 312
Money Bill: Exclusive to Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha can only delay
Age: Lok Sabha - 25 years, Rajya Sabha - 30 years
Presiding Officers: Lok Sabha - Speaker, Rajya Sabha - Vice-President
Joint Session: Presided by Speaker of Lok Sabha
Memory Tricks: "L = Larger power, Lower age, Lives 5 years" and "R S → Resolution & Services"
