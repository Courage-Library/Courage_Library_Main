Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSPs)

Indian Polity - Articles 36–51 - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction

Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSPs) are constitutional guidelines for the government to establish a just, fair, and welfare society. Contained in Part IV (Articles 36–51), they represent the socio-economic goals of the Indian Constitution.

SSC frequently asks about DPSP categories, specific articles, amendments, and comparisons with Fundamental Rights.

Three Categories of DPSPs

S
Socialistic Principles

Aim: Social & economic justice

Welfare state (Art 38)
Equal pay for equal work (Art 39)
Free legal aid (Art 39A)
Right to work (Art 41)
Living wage (Art 43)

Memory: "WE WORK WITH JUSTICE"

G
Gandhian Principles

Aim: Reflect Mahatma Gandhi's ideas

Village panchayats (Art 40)
Cottage industries (Art 43)
Khadi & rural development
Cow slaughter prohibition (Art 48)
SC/ST welfare (Art 46)

Memory: "GANDHI → VILLAGE, COW, KHADI"

L
Liberal-Intellectual Principles

Aim: Modern democratic values

Uniform Civil Code (Art 44)
Protect monuments (Art 49)
Judiciary-executive separation (Art 50)
International peace (Art 51)
Environment protection (Art 48A)

Memory: "U–M–J–P" → Uniform, Monuments, Judiciary, Peace

Important Amendments Related to DPSPs

42nd Amendment (1976)

Added three important articles:

Article 39A: Free legal aid
Article 43A: Worker participation in industries
Article 48A: Environment protection

Also called "Mini Constitution" due to extensive changes

44th Amendment (1978)

Modified Article 39 to emphasize:

Securing adequate means of livelihood
Distribution of community resources
Prevention of concentration of wealth

Strengthened socialistic principles

86th Amendment (2002)

Shifted focus of Article 45:

Original: Free & compulsory education (6–14 years)
Changed to: Early childhood care (0–6 years)
Education Right: Moved to Article 21A as Fundamental Right

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. In which Part of the Constitution are DPSPs contained?

Part IV

2. From which country's constitution were DPSPs inspired?

Ireland

3. Which Article deals with Uniform Civil Code (UCC)?

Article 44

4. Are DPSPs enforceable in courts?

No, they are non-justiciable

5. Village Panchayats are mentioned in which Article?

Article 40

6. Free legal aid is provided under which Article?

Article 39A

7. Environment protection is mentioned in which Article?

Article 48A

8. Which amendment added Article 48A (Environment)?

42nd Amendment (1976)

9. Prohibition of cow slaughter is mentioned in which Article?

Article 48

10. Promotion of international peace is mentioned in which Article?

Article 51

Conclusion

Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSPs) represent the socio-economic philosophy of the Constitution, guiding the state towards establishing a welfare society. While not enforceable like Fundamental Rights, they provide the framework for progressive legislation and governance.

Key Takeaways

Part IV, Articles 36–51: Contain all DPSPs
Three Categories: Socialistic, Gandhian, Liberal-Intellectual
Non-justiciable: Not enforceable in courts (Article 37)
Inspired by: Irish Constitution
Important Articles: 38 (Justice), 39 (Equal pay), 40 (Panchayats), 44 (UCC)
42nd Amendment: Added 39A, 43A, 48A in 1976
Memory Trick: "WEALTHY VILLAGES WORK WITH JUSTICE, UNIFORM PEACE & ENVIRONMENT"
