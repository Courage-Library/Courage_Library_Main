Speaker of Lok Sabha & Parliamentary Committees

Indian Polity - High-Yield Topic for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Speaker of Lok Sabha (Articles 93–97)

The Speaker is the presiding officer of Lok Sabha, responsible for maintaining order and ensuring proper conduct of parliamentary business.

Election & Tenure

Elected

By Lok Sabha members from among themselves

5 Years

Tenure (till Lok Sabha dissolves)

Removal

By majority resolution of Lok Sabha

Impartial

Must act neutrally after election

Important Powers & Functions

1. Presides over Lok Sabha

Maintains order and discipline during sessions.

2. Decides Money Bill (Article 110)

Speaker's decision on whether a bill is a Money Bill is final. VERY IMPORTANT for SSC.

3. Anti-Defection Law

Decides on disqualification of MPs under 10th Schedule.

4. Presides Joint Sitting

Chairs Joint Session of Parliament (Article 108).

5. Chairs Key Committees

Public Accounts Committee, Business Advisory Committee, Rules Committee.

6. Parliamentary Proceedings

Allows "Zero Hour" & "Question Hour". Certifies Money Bills before sending to Rajya Sabha.

Deputy Speaker & Pro-tem Speaker

Deputy Speaker

Election: Elected by Lok Sabha members
Role: Acts when Speaker is absent
Convention: Usually from opposition party
Importance: Ensures continuity in presiding functions

Pro-tem Speaker

Appointment: Appointed by President
Temporary Role: Serves until regular Speaker is elected
Functions: Administers oath to newly elected MPs, conducts election of Speaker
Convention: Usually the oldest MP

Super Memory Trick for Speaker Powers

"MONEY – DEFECTION – JOINT – ORDER"

Money Bill decision
Disqualification under Anti-Defection
Joint Sitting presiding
Maintaining order in House

Parliamentary Committees

Parliamentary Committees are groups of MPs that conduct detailed examination of specific matters, helping Parliament in its legislative and oversight functions.

Standing Committees (Permanent)

Exist throughout the year, perform continuous oversight.

Public Accounts Committee (PAC)
Estimates Committee
Committee on Public Undertakings (COPU)
Business Advisory Committee

Ad hoc Committees (Temporary)

Formed for specific purposes, dissolve after completing task.

Joint Parliamentary Committee (JPC)
Select Committee
Inquiry Committees

Ad hoc Committees (Temporary)

Joint Parliamentary Committee (JPC)

Formed by both Houses for special investigations.

Bofors scam investigation
2G spectrum scam
Coca Cola–Pepsi pesticide issue

High-power investigative body

Select Committee

Formed by one House to examine a particular bill.

Examines specific legislation
Presents detailed report
Dissolves after task completion
Inquiry Committees

Formed for urgent matters of national importance.

Time-bound investigations
Specific terms of reference
Ad-hoc nature

Committee Memory Trick

"PAC–CAG, Estimates–LS Only, COPU–PSU, JPC–Scams"

PAC examines CAG reports
Estimates Committee: Lok Sabha members only
COPU examines PSUs
JPC investigates scams

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who decides whether a Bill is a Money Bill?

Speaker of Lok Sabha

2. Who presides over Joint Sitting of Parliament?

Speaker of Lok Sabha

3. Who appoints the Pro-tem Speaker?

President of India

4. Which committee examines CAG reports?

Public Accounts Committee (PAC)

5. Which is the largest Parliamentary Committee?

Estimates Committee (30 members)

6. The Chairperson of PAC is usually from?

Opposition party

7. How many members are nominated in Rajya Sabha?

12 (by President)

8. JPC is formed by?

Both Houses of Parliament

9. Anti-defection decisions in Lok Sabha are taken by?

Speaker

10. Minimum age for Lok Sabha membership?

25 years

Conclusion

Understanding the Speaker's powers and Parliamentary Committees is essential for SSC exams. This topic covers the operational framework of Parliament and is frequently tested.

Key Takeaways

Speaker: Elected by LS, decides Money Bill, presides Joint Sitting
Pro-tem Speaker: Temporary, appointed by President
PAC: Examines CAG reports, chairperson from opposition
Estimates Committee: Largest (30 members), LS only
JPC: Joint committee for special investigations
Memory Trick: "MONEY – DEFECTION – JOINT – ORDER"
Committee Trick: "PAC–CAG, Estimates–LS Only, COPU–PSU, JPC–Scams"
