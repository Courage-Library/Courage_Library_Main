Making of the Indian Constitution

Indian Polity - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction

The Indian Constitution is the supreme law of India, outlining the framework for governance, rights of citizens, and structure of government. Understanding its making is crucial for SSC's polity section.

SSC frequently asks about Constituent Assembly formation, key committees, adoption dates, and sources of constitutional provisions.

PART 1: Formation of the Constituent Assembly

Historical Background

First Proposed By: M.N. Roy (1934)
Congress Demand: 1935 session officially demanded
Formation Under: Cabinet Mission Plan, 1946
Elections Held: July 1946
First Meeting: 9 December 1946
Total Members: 389 (originally), 299 (after partition)

PART 4: Constitution Making Process

1
Ideas Collection (1946–1948)

Committees submitted reports, B.N. Rau prepared the initial draft based on discussions and international constitutions.

2
Drafting Committee Formation (29 August 1947)

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar appointed Chairman of the Drafting Committee, responsible for preparing the Draft Constitution.

3
Draft Published (February 1948)

Draft Constitution published for public feedback, received approximately 7,600 amendment suggestions.

4
Debates & Discussions (1947–1949)

Constituent Assembly debated every article for 2 years, 11 months, and 18 days with intense discussions.

5
Constitution Adopted (26 November 1949)

Constitution adopted by Constituent Assembly, signed by 284 members. Celebrated as Constitution Day.

6
Constitution Enforced (26 January 1950)

Constitution came into effect, India became a Sovereign Democratic Republic. Celebrated as Republic Day.

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who first proposed the idea of a Constituent Assembly for India?

M.N. Roy (1934)

2. Under which plan was the Constituent Assembly formed?

Cabinet Mission Plan, 1946

3. Who was the President of the Constituent Assembly?

Dr. Rajendra Prasad

4. Who chaired the Drafting Committee of the Constitution?

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

5. When was the Indian Constitution adopted?

26 November 1949

6. When did the Constitution come into force?

26 January 1950

7. From which country were Fundamental Rights borrowed?

United States of America

8. From which country were DPSPs borrowed?

Ireland

9. From which country were Emergency Provisions borrowed?

Germany (Weimar Constitution)

10. How many members originally constituted the Constituent Assembly?

389 members (reduced to 299 after partition)

Conclusion

The making of the Indian Constitution represents a remarkable democratic exercise. Understanding this process is essential for SSC exams as it forms the foundation of Indian polity.

Final Summary

Constituent Assembly: Formed under Cabinet Mission Plan, 1946
Key Persons: Dr. Rajendra Prasad (President), Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (Drafting Committee)
Timeline: 9 Dec 1946 (first meeting) to 26 Jan 1950 (enforcement)
Sources: Borrowed features from 10+ countries worldwide
Committees: Drafting Committee most important (Ambedkar as Chairman)
Adoption: 26 Nov 1949 (Constitution Day)
Duration: 2 years, 11 months, 18 days of detailed deliberation
