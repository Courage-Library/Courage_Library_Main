Legislative Assembly & Legislative Council

Indian Polity - State Legislature - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

State Legislature (Articles 168–212, Part VI)

Similar to Parliament at the Centre, States have their own legislature. The structure varies between unicameral (one house) and bicameral (two houses) systems.

LA

Legislative Assembly

Vidhan Sabha (Lower House)

Directly elected, more powerful

LC

Legislative Council

Vidhan Parishad (Upper House)

Only in some states, indirectly elected

Legislative Assembly (Vidhan Sabha) - Lower House

Composition & Basic Facts

Feature Details
Minimum Strength60 members
Maximum Strength500 members
MembersDirectly elected by people
Presiding OfficerSpeaker
Tenure5 years (can be dissolved earlier by Governor)
Minimum Age25 years
Special CasesGoa, Sikkim, Mizoram have less than 60 members (special provisions)

Powers of Legislative Assembly

1. Money Bill Power (Exclusive)

Money Bills can be introduced ONLY in Vidhan Sabha. Legislative Council cannot reject them.

2. Controls Council of Ministers

Chief Minister is responsible only to Vidhan Sabha. Can remove government through no-confidence motion.

3. Ordinary Legislation

Makes ordinary laws. Final authority in bicameral states.

4. Presidential Election

MLAs participate in election of President of India.

5. Governor's Role

Plays indirect role in Governor removal process.

6. Budget Approval

Approves state budget and financial matters.

Memory Trick for Assembly

"Assembly = A = Actual Power, Age 25, Approves Money Bills"

Actual Power over government
Age 25 for membership
Approves Money Bills exclusively

Legislative Council (Vidhan Parishad) - Upper House

Composition & Basic Facts

Feature Details
Minimum Strength40 members
Maximum StrengthCannot exceed 1/3rd of Assembly strength
Tenure6 years (permanent house)
Retirement Cycle1/3rd members retire every 2 years
Minimum Age30 years
Presiding OfficerChairman
NatureCannot be dissolved (continuing chamber)

Composition Breakdown (Indirect Election)

1/3

MLAs

Elected by members of Legislative Assembly

1/3

Local Bodies

Municipalities, Panchayats

1/12

Teachers

Electoral college of teachers

1/12

Graduates

Electoral college of graduates

1/6

Nominated

By Governor (literature, art, science, social work)

Powers of Legislative Council

1. No Government Control

Cannot form or remove government. Chief Minister is NOT responsible to the Council.

2. Limited Money Bill Power

Cannot reject Money Bills. Can only delay for 14 days.

3. Advisory Role in Legislation

Can consider and discuss ordinary bills, but Assembly has final authority.

4. Permanent House

Continues permanently, cannot be dissolved like the Assembly.

Memory Trick for Council

"Council = C = Continuous House, Consults but cannot Control Govt"

Continuous House (permanent)
Consults on legislation
Cannot control government
Cannot reject Money Bills

States with Legislative Council (VERY IMPORTANT)

Only 6 states in India have a bicameral legislature with both Legislative Assembly and Legislative Council.

Uttar Pradesh

Most populous state

Bihar

Maharashtra

Southern state

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which is the Lower House of State Legislature?

Legislative Assembly (Vidhan Sabha)

2. What is the minimum age for Vidhan Sabha membership?

25 years

3. What is the minimum age for Vidhan Parishad membership?

30 years

4. What is the duration of Legislative Assembly?

5 years

5. Which House of State Legislature cannot be dissolved?

Legislative Council

6. Legislative Council can delay Money Bill for how long?

14 days

7. Who decides whether a State Bill is a Money Bill?

Speaker of Legislative Assembly

8. Which Article deals with creation/abolition of Legislative Council?

Article 169

9. How many states in India have Legislative Councils?

6 states

10. Name any two states with bicameral legislature.

Uttar Pradesh & Bihar / Maharashtra & Karnataka / Andhra Pradesh & Telangana

Conclusion

Understanding the structure and powers of State Legislature is crucial for SSC exams. This topic covers the legislative framework at the state level and is frequently tested.

Key Takeaways

Legislative Assembly: Lower House, 5-year term, 25 years age, controls government
Legislative Council: Upper House, 6-year term, 30 years age, permanent house
Money Bills: Exclusive to Assembly, Council can only delay 14 days
States with Council: 6 states (UB MKT trick)
Council Composition: MLTG N trick for indirect election
Important Article: 169 for creation/abolition of Council
Memory Tricks: "A = Actual Power, Age 25" for Assembly, "C = Continuous House" for Council
