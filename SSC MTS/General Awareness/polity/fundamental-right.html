Fundamental Rights (Articles 12–35)

Indian Polity - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction

Fundamental Rights are the cornerstone of Indian democracy, enshrined in Part III (Articles 12–35) of the Constitution. These rights protect individual liberties and ensure justice, equality, and freedom for all citizens.

SSC consistently asks 2–4 questions from Fundamental Rights, focusing on specific articles, writs, and landmark cases.

The Six Fundamental Rights

Inspired by USA Bill of Rights | Enforceable in Courts

1
Right to Equality

Articles 14–18

Equality before law, no discrimination, abolition of untouchability
2
Right to Freedom

Articles 19–22

Speech, assembly, movement, life & liberty, protection against arrest
3
Right Against Exploitation

Articles 23–24

Prohibits human trafficking, forced labor, child labor
4
Right to Freedom of Religion

Articles 25–28

Freedom of conscience, manage religious institutions, no religious taxes
5
Cultural & Educational Rights

Articles 29–30

Protect culture/language, minorities can establish educational institutions
6
Right to Constitutional Remedies

Article 32

"Heart & Soul" - Approach Supreme Court for rights enforcement
Memory Trick: "E–F–X–R–C–R"

Equality – Freedom – Exploitation – Religion – Culture – Remedies

Easy way to remember all 6 Fundamental Rights in sequence

PART 1: Right to Equality (Articles 14–18)

Article 14

Equality before law and equal protection of laws

Same law applies to everyone
Article 15

Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth

Reservations allowed for SC/ST/OBC, women, backward classes
Article 16

Equality of opportunity in public employment

Reservations in government jobs for underprivileged groups
Article 17

Abolition of untouchability

Practicing untouchability is a punishable offence
Article 18

Abolition of titles

No titles like Raja, Maharaja
Allowed: Military & academic titles (Dr., Lt., Prof.)
Memory Trick for Articles 14–18

"Law – Discrimination – Job – Touch – Titles"

14 (Law) – 15 (Discrimination) – 16 (Job) – 17 (Touch) – 18 (Titles)

PART 2: Right to Freedom (Articles 19–22)

Article 19 – Six Fundamental Freedoms

19(1)(a): Speech & Expression
19(1)(b): Peaceful Assembly
19(1)(c): Form Associations/Unions
19(1)(d): Freedom of Movement
19(1)(e): Freedom of Residence
19(1)(g): Freedom of Profession

Restrictions: Sovereignty, security, public order, morality, friendly relations

Article 20

Protection in criminal cases

No ex-post facto law
No double jeopardy
No self-incrimination
Article 21

Right to Life & Personal Liberty (Most Important)

Right to privacy
Right to clean environment
Right to reputation
Right to livelihood
Article 21A

Right to Education

Free & compulsory education (6–14 years)
Added by 86th Amendment (2002)
Article 22

Protection against arrest

No arrest without reason
Right to lawyer
Produced before magistrate within 24 hours
Memory Trick for Articles 19–22

"6 – Criminal – Life – Arrest"

19 (6 freedoms) – 20 (Criminal protection) – 21 (Life) – 22 (Arrest)

PART 3 & 4: Rights Against Exploitation & Freedom of Religion

Right Against Exploitation (Articles 23–24)

Article 23

Prohibits:

Human trafficking
Forced labor
Begar (unpaid work)
Article 24

Prohibits child labor

No child below 14 years can work in:

Mines
Factories
Hazardous occupations

Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25–28)

Article 25

Freedom of conscience & free profession, practice, and propagation of religion

Article 26

Right to manage religious affairs & institutions

Article 27

No tax for promotion of any particular religion

Article 28

No religious instruction in government educational institutions

Exploitation Trick

"23 = Slavery, 24 = Child labour"

Religion Trick

"P–M–T–S" → Pray, Manage, Tax, Schools

25 (Pray) – 26 (Manage) – 27 (Tax) – 28 (Schools)

PART 5 & 6: Cultural Rights & Constitutional Remedies

Cultural & Educational Rights (Articles 29–30)

Article 29

Protection of interests of minorities

Right to conserve culture, language, script
Article 30

Right of minorities to establish educational institutions

Religious & linguistic minorities
Can administer their institutions
Government aid without discrimination
Memory Trick

"Culture → 29; Colleges → 30"

Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar called this the "Heart & Soul of the Constitution"

Direct approach to Supreme Court for enforcement of Fundamental Rights

Five Writs under Article 32
Habeas Corpus: "Produce the body" – release from illegal detention
Mandamus: "We command" – order public authority to perform duty
Certiorari: "To be certified" – quash lower court order
Prohibition: "To prohibit" – stop lower court from exceeding jurisdiction
Quo Warranto: "By what authority?" – challenge public office holder
Writs Memory Trick

"H M C P Q" → "HIMACHAL PRADESH QUEEN"

Articles 33–35 (Special Provisions)

Article 33

Parliament can restrict Fundamental Rights for armed forces, intelligence, etc.

Article 34

Restriction on rights during martial law in any area

Article 35

Parliament's power to make laws for giving effect to Fundamental Rights

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Which Article abolishes untouchability?

Article 17

2. Right to Education is under which Article?

Article 21A (Added by 86th Amendment)

3. Which Article is called the "Heart & Soul of the Constitution"?

Article 32 (Right to Constitutional Remedies)

4. Which Article guarantees freedom of speech and expression?

Article 19(1)(a)

5. Which writ is used for release from unlawful detention?

Habeas Corpus

6. Which Article prohibits employment of children below 14 years in hazardous jobs?

Article 24

7. Which Article gives freedom to manage religious institutions?

Article 26

8. Which Article protects cultural and educational rights of minorities?

Article 29 & 30

9. Which Fundamental Right can be directly enforced in the Supreme Court?

Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)

10. Which Articles deal with the Right to Equality?

Articles 14 to 18

Conclusion

Fundamental Rights form the bedrock of Indian democracy, ensuring individual liberties and protecting citizens from arbitrary state action. Mastering these rights is crucial for SSC exams and understanding the constitutional framework.

Key Takeaways

6 Rights: Equality (14-18), Freedom (19-22), Against Exploitation (23-24), Religion (25-28), Cultural (29-30), Remedies (32)
Most Important: Article 21 (Right to Life), Article 32 (Heart & Soul)
Special Articles: 21A (Education), 17 (Untouchability), 24 (Child Labor)
Writs: Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Certiorari, Prohibition, Quo Warranto
Memory Tricks: "E–F–X–R–C–R" for rights, "H M C P Q" for writs
Justiciable: All rights enforceable through courts (Article 32)
Amendments: 86th added Article 21A (Right to Education)
