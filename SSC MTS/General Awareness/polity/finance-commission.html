Finance Commission of India

Indian Polity - Constitutional Body - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction (Article 280)

The Finance Commission of India is a constitutional body that determines the financial relations between the central government and state governments. It plays a crucial role in maintaining fiscal federalism in India.

Functions of Finance Commission (SSC's Most Important)

Finance Commission recommends distribution of:

Central Taxes to States
Grants-in-Aid to States
Resources to Local Bodies
1. Distribution of Net Tax Proceeds

Recommends how taxes collected by Centre should be shared with States (vertical devolution).

2. Grants-in-Aid to States (Article 275)

Recommends central assistance to states, especially to those with revenue deficits.

3. Financial Stability Measures

Suggests measures to improve financial stability of states.

4. Centre-State & Local Body Distribution

Recommends distribution of resources between Centre, States, and Local Bodies (Panchayats & Municipalities).

5. Financial Position Evaluation

Evaluates financial position of both Centre and States.

6. Other Financial Matters

Reviews other financial matters referred by President.

Criteria for Tax Distribution (SSC Loves This)

Finance Commission uses various parameters to decide how much tax share each state receives:

Income Distance

Distance of state's per capita income from highest per capita income state. Higher distance = higher share.

Population

Based on population data (1971 or 2011 census). Larger population = higher share.

Area

Larger geographical area = higher share to cover administrative costs.

Forest & Ecology

States with larger forest cover get additional share for environmental conservation.

Tax Effort

Rewards states that show better tax collection efficiency.

Demographic Performance

States with lower population growth rate get rewarded.

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Finance Commission is established under which Article?

Article 280

2. How often is the Finance Commission constituted?

Every 5 years

3. How many members are in the Finance Commission?

5 (1 Chairman + 4 members)

4. Who appoints the Finance Commission?

President of India

5. Which Finance Commission recommended 41% tax devolution to states?

15th Finance Commission

6. Who was the Chairman of the 15th Finance Commission?

N. K. Singh

7. Grants-in-aid to states are mentioned in which Article?

Article 275

8. Which body decides tax sharing between Centre and States?

Finance Commission

9. Which population census was used by the 15th Finance Commission?

2011 Census

10. Finance Commission submits its report to?

President of India

Conclusion

The Finance Commission of India plays a pivotal role in maintaining fiscal federalism by ensuring fair distribution of financial resources between the Centre and States. Its constitutional status and regular reconstitution every 5 years make it a key institution in India's financial architecture.

Key Takeaways

Article 280: Establishes Finance Commission
Composition: 5 members (Chairman + 4), appointed by President
Tenure: Constituted every 5 years
Functions: Tax distribution, grants-in-aid, local body funding
Criteria: Income, Population, Area, Forest, Tax-effort (I P A F T)
15th FC: N.K. Singh Chairman, 41% tax devolution, 2011 census
Reports: Submitted to President, placed before Parliament
Memory Tricks: "₹5 Finance Commission", "Finance = Every Five", "D G F L S" for functions
