Election Commission of India (ECI)

Indian Polity - Constitutional Body - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction (Article 324)

The Election Commission of India (ECI) is an autonomous constitutional body responsible for conducting free, fair, and transparent elections in India. It is one of the most important constitutional bodies for Indian democracy.

324

Article 324

Gives full control of elections to ECI

Historical Evolution
Originally: Only 1 Chief Election Commissioner (1950)
After 1989: CEC + 2 Election Commissioners
Reason: To ensure more transparency and collective decision-making

Removal Process
CEC: Removed like a Supreme Court Judge (impeachment by Parliament)

Functions & Powers of ECI (SSC's Favorite)

ECI conducts elections to:

Lok Sabha
Rajya Sabha
State Assemblies
State Councils
President
Vice-President
1. Voter List Management

Prepares and updates electoral rolls, ensures no bogus voters.

2. Conduct Elections

Scheduling, polling booths, counting of votes, declaration of results.

3. Allot Election Symbols

Reserved symbols for national/state parties, free symbols for others.

4. Enforce Model Code of Conduct

Prevents misuse of power by ruling party, maintains fairness during elections.

5. Monitor Political Spending

Sets limits on campaign expenses, checks misuse of government money.

6. Ensure Free & Fair Elections

Deploys central forces, maintains law and order during elections.

7. Recognize Political Parties

Classifies parties as National, State, or Registered parties.

8. Conduct By-Elections

When a seat becomes vacant between general elections.

9. Quasi-Judicial Powers

Decides disputes between political parties, freezes or allots symbols.

Important Election Terms (SSC Must Know)

EVM (Electronic Voting Machine)

Used for voting since 2000. Electronic device for casting votes.

VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail)

Shows printed slip for 7 seconds, increases transparency, introduced in 2013.

EPIC (Elector Photo Identity Card)

Voter ID card issued by ECI to eligible voters.

Model Code of Conduct (MCC)

Rules for political parties during elections. Examples: No hate speech, no misuse of government funds.

NOTA (None of the Above)

Introduced in 2013, allows voters to reject all candidates.

Booth Level Officer (BLO)

Local officer responsible for managing voter list and polling station.

Important Articles Related to Elections (SSC Must)

Article 324: Powers of Election Commission of India
Article 325: One general electoral roll (no discrimination)
Article 326: Elections based on adult suffrage (18 years)
Article 327: Parliament's power to make election laws
Article 328: State legislature's power for state elections
Article 329: Courts cannot interfere during elections

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Election Commission of India is a ______ body.

Constitutional body

2. Which Article deals with the Election Commission of India?

Article 324

3. Who appoints the Chief Election Commissioner?

President of India

4. What is the tenure of an Election Commissioner?

6 years or 65 years of age (whichever earlier)

5. Chief Election Commissioner can be removed like a ______.

Supreme Court Judge (impeachment by Parliament)

6. How many members are there in the Election Commission of India?

3 (1 CEC + 2 Election Commissioners)

7. Which system shows a printed slip for 7 seconds during voting?

VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail)

8. What does NOTA stand for?

None of the Above

9. Who allots election symbols to political parties?

Election Commission of India

10. On what basis are elections held in India?

Universal Adult Franchise (18 years and above)

Conclusion

The Election Commission of India is the cornerstone of Indian democracy, ensuring free, fair, and transparent elections. Its autonomous status and wide-ranging powers make it one of the most important constitutional bodies.

Key Takeaways

Article 324: Supreme power to ECI for conducting elections
Composition: 3 members (CEC + 2 ECs), appointed by President
Tenure: 6 years or 65 years age limit
Removal: CEC like SC judge, ECs on CEC recommendation
Functions: Voter lists, conduct elections, MCC enforcement, symbol allotment
Important Terms: EVM, VVPAT, EPIC, NOTA, MCC, BLO
Articles: 324-329 cover various election-related provisions
Memory Tricks: "3 C's → CEC + 2 Commissioners", "A.S.P.E.C.T.", "324 → 24x7 Election Control"
