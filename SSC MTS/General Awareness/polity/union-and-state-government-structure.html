Union & State Government Structure

Indian Polity - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction

Understanding the structure of Union and State Governments is fundamental for SSC exams. This chapter covers the constitutional framework, key positions, legislative bodies, and their inter-relationships as per the Indian Constitution.

SSC frequently asks about Union vs State structure, bicameral states, legislative powers, and specific constitutional articles.

Collective responsibility

Union Legislature - Parliament (Article 79)

Lok Sabha (Lower House)
Strength: 543 elected + 2 nominated
Term: 5 years (unless dissolved earlier)
Presiding Officer: Speaker
Special Power: Money Bills originate only here
Rajya Sabha (Upper House)
Strength: 245 (233 elected + 12 nominated)
Term: Permanent House (1/3 retire every 2 years)
Presiding Officer: Vice-President (Chairman)
Special Role: Represents States & Union Territories

Vidhan Sabha (Legislative Assembly)
Strength: Varies by state (60-500 members)
Term: 5 years (unless dissolved earlier)
Presiding Officer: Speaker
Role: Main law-making body
Vidhan Parishad (Legislative Council)
Strength: 1/3 of Assembly strength (max 1/3 of total)
Term: Permanent House (1/3 retire every 2 years)
Presiding Officer: Chairman
Role: Advisory, review legislation

Important Rule

If there is a conflict between Union and State laws on Concurrent List subjects, Union Law prevails.

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who is the real executive head of India?

Prime Minister

2. Who appoints the Governor of a state?

President of India

3. Which Article deals with the President of India?

Article 52

4. What is the upper house of Parliament called?

Rajya Sabha

5. What is the upper house of State Legislature called?

Vidhan Parishad or Legislative Council

6. How many states in India have bicameral legislature?

6 states

7. Who is the head of the State Government?

Chief Minister

8. What are the three components of Parliament?

President, Lok Sabha, and Rajya Sabha

9. What is Vidhan Sabha?

Legislative Assembly (Lower House of State Legislature)

10. Who heads the Rajya Sabha?

Vice-President of India (as ex-officio Chairman)

Conclusion

Mastering the structure of Union and State Governments provides a solid foundation for understanding Indian polity. This knowledge is crucial for answering direct questions and comprehending the federal structure of India's governance.

Key Takeaways

Union Govt: Part V, President (nominal), PM (real), Parliament bicameral
State Govt: Part VI, Governor (nominal), CM (real), mostly unicameral
Bicameral States: 6 states (UP, Bihar, Maharashtra, Karnataka, AP, Telangana)
Legislative Lists: Union (national), State (local), Concurrent (both)
Articles: 52 (President), 153 (Governor), 79 (Parliament), 168 (State Legislature)
Judiciary: Supreme Court (Union), High Court (State)
Memory Tricks: "P-P-P" for Union, "G-C-A" for State, "UB MKT" for bicameral states
