Indus Valley Civilization (IVC)

The Oldest Urban Culture of India - Complete Notes for SSC MTS Exams

Introduction – Why Should You Learn This?

Before India had kings like Mauryas or Guptas, there existed a civilization so advanced that it had drainage systems more modern than some today.

That was the Indus Valley Civilization, also called the Harappan Civilization.

It existed 4,500+ years ago (approx. 2500 BCE – 1500 BCE), but still gives us lessons on planning, hygiene, art, and trade. For SSC MTS, 1–2 questions are almost certain from this topic.

1. Important Features of Indus Valley Civilization

Town Planning – The Most Important Feature

The cities of IVC were planned like today’s smart cities.

Key Points (Exam Focus):

  • Cities built on grid pattern (roads cut at 90°)
  • Houses made of baked bricks
  • Drainage system was underground
  • Cities had citadel (upper fortified part) and lower town

Memory Trick

GRID = Harappan City ID

  • Grid roads
  • Regular drains
  • Interconnected houses
  • Double towns (Citadel + Lower town)

2. Major Sites and Their Unique Findings

This is the MOST repeated part in exams.

Harappa (Punjab, Pakistan)

Findings:

  • Granaries
  • Wells and drains
  • Cemetery R

Memory Trick:

"H for Harappa, H for Huge Granary."

Mohenjo-daro (Sindh, Pakistan)

Findings:

  • Great Bath (most important)
  • Granary
  • Assembly Hall
  • Bronze dancing girl
  • Statue of bearded priest

Memory Trick:

"Mohenjo-daro = Most Bath & Most Bronze."

Dholavira (Gujarat)

Findings:

  • Water conservation tanks
  • Stadium-like structures
  • Signboard with script

Memory Trick:

"D-hola = Dhol (drum) = needs water → Water reservoirs."

Lothal (Gujarat)

Findings:

  • Dockyard (first in the world)
  • Beads & ornaments factory

Memory Trick:

"Lothal → Local Port."

Kalibangan (Rajasthan)

  • Fire altars
  • Ploughed field

Banawali (Haryana)

  • Barley cultivation
  • Terracotta toys

Rakhigarhi (Haryana)

  • Largest IVC site in India
  • Human skeletons found

3. Agriculture

Key Points:

  • Wheat, barley grown
  • Rice found at Lothal
  • No evidence of iron
  • Tools made of copper & bronze

Memory Trick:

"WBR"

Wheat, Barley, Rice (only at Lothal)

4. Art and Craft

Pottery

Red pottery with black designs

Statues

  • Dancing Girl (bronze) → Mohenjo-daro
  • Bearded Priest → Mohenjo-daro

Seals

  • Made of steatite
  • Unicorn seal is common

Trick: "Seal = Stea-lite → Steatite stone."

5. Trade & Economy

  • They traded with Mesopotamia
  • Used weights & measures
  • Used seals for trade
  • No evidence of coins

6. Script

  • Written from right to left
  • Still undeciphered

Important Note:

The Indus script remains one of the great unsolved mysteries in archaeology.

7. Religion

  • No temples found
  • Worshipped nature
  • Proto-Shiva (Pashupati Seal)
  • Mother Goddess

Key Insight:

The Pashupati Seal shows a figure in yoga posture, suggesting early forms of Hindu practices.

8. Decline of Indus Valley Civilization

Reasons not fully confirmed but possible theories include:

Floods
Drought
Earthquakes
Invasion

Climatic change
