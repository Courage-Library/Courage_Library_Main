National Movements (1905–1947)

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Freedom Struggle

Overview: Three Phases of Freedom Struggle

This chapter is SUPER IMPORTANT for SSC exams. Every year questions come from Partition of Bengal, Swadeshi Movement, Gandhian movements, Quit India, INA, and Independence Acts.

1905–1919

Phase 1: 1905–1919 (Moderates & Extremists Era)

1. Partition of Bengal (1905)

  • Viceroy: Lord Curzon
  • Stated reason: "Better administration"
  • Actual reason: Divide Hindus & Muslims (Divide and Rule policy)
  • Result: Massive public protest → Swadeshi Movement

Anti-Partition Movement began, marking the rise of mass nationalism in India.

2. Swadeshi Movement (1905–1907)

Aim: Use Indian goods, boycott British goods

Bonfires of foreign cloth
Promotion of khadi
National schools
Rise of Extremists

Key Leaders (Lal–Bal–Pal)

Lala Lajpat Rai

Bal Gangadhar Tilak

Bipin Chandra Pal

The Swadeshi Movement marked the transition from petitioning to mass agitation.

3. Surat Split (1907)

Congress split between Moderates (Gopal Krishna Gokhale) and Extremists (Tilak) over Swadeshi methods.

4. Muslim League (1906)

Founded in Dhaka to protect Muslim political interests.

5. Home Rule Movement (1916)

  • Founded by Bal Gangadhar Tilak (Poona) and Annie Besant (Madras)
  • Slogan: "Home Rule is my birthright!"
  • Aim: Self-government for India

6. Lucknow Pact (1916)

Agreement between Indian National Congress and Muslim League for Hindu-Muslim unity and constitutional reforms.

7. Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (1919)

Introduced Dyarchy in provinces – division of subjects between elected ministers and British officials.

Phase 2: 1919–1942 (Gandhian Era)

1. Rowlatt Act (1919)

  • Gave British power to arrest and jail anyone without trial
  • Gandhi called it "Black Act"
  • Led to nationwide protests

2. Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (1919)

  • General Dyer opened fire on peaceful gathering in Amritsar
  • Hundreds killed, thousands injured
  • Deep anger across India, turning point in freedom struggle

3. Non-Cooperation Movement (1920–22)

Gandhi's first mass movement aiming to:

  • Boycott schools, colleges, courts
  • Boycott foreign cloth
  • Renounce British titles
  • Supported by Khilafat Movement for Hindu-Muslim unity

Why it Ended

Chauri Chaura Incident (1922) – violent clash where police station was burnt, leading Gandhi to withdraw the movement.

4. Simon Commission (1927)

All members were British – no Indian representation.

Nationwide protests with slogan:

"Simon Go Back!"

The commission led to the Nehru Report (1928) demanding Dominion Status for India.

5. Dandi March & Civil Disobedience (1930)

  • Gandhi walked 240 miles from Sabarmati to Dandi
  • Broke salt law by making salt from seawater
  • Started Civil Disobedience Movement

Civil Disobedience = Breaking unjust laws

Salt laws
Forest laws
Tax laws
Government regulations

6. Round Table Conferences (1930-32)

  • Three conferences between Indian leaders and British
  • Gandhi attended Second Round Table Conference (1931)
  • Failed to reach agreement on India's constitutional future

7. Government of India Act (1935)

  • MOST IMPORTANT constitutional reform before independence
  • Introduced Provincial Autonomy
  • Provided for Federal system (never implemented)
  • Basis for India's Constitution

Phase 3: 1942–1947 (Final Phase of Freedom)

1. Quit India Movement (1942)

  • Launched by Gandhi on 8 August 1942
  • Slogan: "Do or Die"
  • Demand: Immediate end to British rule
  • British arrested all Congress leaders immediately

Movement turned violent as leaders were arrested. People attacked government symbols – post offices, police stations, railways.

2. Indian National Army (INA)

  • Founded by: Subhash Chandra Bose
  • Also called Azad Hind Fauj
  • Slogan: "Give me blood, I will give you freedom."
  • Fought alongside Japan against British in World War II

INA's military campaign and subsequent trials of INA soldiers created massive public sympathy and weakened British authority.

3. Naval Mutiny (1946)

Royal Indian Navy revolted in Bombay. British realized Indian armed forces were no longer loyal.

4. Cabinet Mission (1946)

British mission to frame a constitution for India. Proposed grouping of provinces but rejected by Congress and Muslim League.

5. Mountbatten Plan (1947)

Last Viceroy Lord Mountbatten proposed partition of India into India and Pakistan.

6. Indian Independence Act (1947)

  • Passed by British Parliament in July 1947
  • Created two independent dominions: India and Pakistan
  • India became independent on 15 August 1947
  • Pakistan on 14 August 1947

Lord Mountbatten became first Governor-General of independent India.

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who started the Swadeshi Movement?

Extremists (Lal-Bal-Pal) in response to Partition of Bengal

2. Who gave the slogan "Home Rule is my birthright"?

Bal Gangadhar Tilak

3. Who gave the slogan "Do or Die"?

Mahatma Gandhi (Quit India Movement, 1942)

4. When did the Jallianwala Bagh massacre happen?

13 April 1919

5. Who visited India in 1927-28 as part of the Simon Commission?

All-British commission, led to "Simon Go Back" protests

6. Who organized the Indian National Army (INA)?

Subhash Chandra Bose

7. Dandi March was part of which movement?

Civil Disobedience Movement (1930)

8. Who was the last Viceroy of India?

Lord Mountbatten

Conclusion

From 1905 to 1947, India's freedom struggle evolved through distinct phases: the rise of extremism with Swadeshi, the mass mobilization under Gandhi, and the final push through Quit India, INA, and constitutional negotiations. Understanding this progression is key to scoring well in SSC history questions.

Key Takeaways for SSC Exams

Partition of Bengal (1905) → Swadeshi Movement
Gandhian movements (Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience, Quit India)
Important acts (Rowlatt, Government of India Act 1935, Independence Act 1947)
Key leaders (Tilak, Gandhi, Bose) and their slogans
Constitutional developments (Simon Commission, Cabinet Mission)
Final phase events (INA, Naval Mutiny, Partition)

This comprehensive understanding guarantees success in SSC MTS, GD, CHSL history questions.
