Marathas – Shivaji & His Administration

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL Exams - Medieval India

Introduction

The Marathas emerged in the 17th century as the strongest power in western India. Their greatest leader was:

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Founder of the Maratha Empire | Symbol of bravery, good governance, discipline & justice

This topic is very important for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL because Shivaji's administration and achievements frequently appear in exams.

1. Early Life of Shivaji

  • Born: 1630 at Shivneri Fort
  • Father: Shahji Bhonsle
  • Mother: Jijabai
  • Guru: Dadaji Kondadev

From childhood, Shivaji was trained in:

Guerrilla warfare
Administration
Leadership
Horse riding

2. Rise of Shivaji – Key Events

1. Capture of Torna Fort (1646)

First fort captured at age 16!

2. Defeated Afzal Khan

A general sent by Bijapur. Shivaji killed him using Tiger Claws (Wagh Nakh).

3. Attack on Shaista Khan

Shaista Khan was a Mughal viceroy in Deccan. Shivaji attacked him at night and cut his fingers.

4. Escape from Agra (1666)

Shivaji escaped from Emperor Aurangzeb's custody by hiding inside sweet baskets.

5. Coronation at Raigad (1674)

Shivaji became Chhatrapati (King) at Raigad Fort.

3. Shivaji's Administration (VERY IMPORTANT)

Shivaji created a strong, organized, and corruption-free administration.

A. Central Administration (Ashta Pradhan – 8 Ministers)

Shivaji appointed Ashta Pradhan (Council of Eight Ministers)

Minister Name Role
Prime Minister Peshwa Head of administration
Finance Minister Amatya Revenue & accounts
Foreign Affairs Sumant Diplomacy & correspondence
Military Chief Senapati Army head
Religious Officer Panditrao Religious matters
Chief Justice Nyayadhish Law & justice
Internal Security Dabir Intelligence
Private Secretary Sachiv Royal letters & orders

Memory Trick

"P-A-S-S-P-N-D-S"

"PASS PND-S" → Ashta Pradhan list

B. Revenue Administration

1. Chauth

  • 25% tax collected from neighboring kingdoms
  • In return, Shivaji provided protection

2. Sardeshmukhi

  • Additional 10% tax charged from conquered areas

These were NOT taxes on farmers but on rival states.

C. Military Administration

1. Guerrilla Warfare (Ganimi Kava)

Hit-and-run tactics
Surprising enemies
Knowledge of terrain

2. Strong Navy

Shivaji is called Father of Indian Navy.

Built sea forts like:

Sindhudurg
Vijaydurg

3. Fort Strategy

Built and repaired 300+ forts including:

Raigad
Pratapgad
Torna
Rajgad

Administrative Divisions

Division Head
Province Prant
District Taraf
Village Mauza

Local headman = Patil

Village accountant = Kulkarni

Shivaji fought multiple Mughal commanders but never surrendered his independence!

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who founded the Maratha Empire?

Shivaji Maharaj

2. What is "Chauth"?

25% tax collected for protection

3. Who was Shivaji's Prime Minister?

Peshwa

4. Which warfare method was used by Shivaji?

Guerrilla Warfare

5. Where was Shivaji crowned?

Raigad Fort

6. Shivaji is known as the father of?

Indian Navy

7. How many ministers were there in Shivaji's council?

Eight (Ashta Pradhan)

Conclusion

Shivaji was not just a warrior but an excellent administrator, planner, and nation-builder. His efficient systems (especially Ashta Pradhan, Chauth–Sardeshmukhi, Guerrilla warfare) are highly important for SSC exams.
