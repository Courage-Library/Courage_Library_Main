Indian Independence Act 1947

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Modern History & Polity

Introduction

The Indian Independence Act 1947 was passed by the British Parliament based on the Mountbatten Plan. This historic Act formally ended British rule in India and created two independent nations.

SSC MTS, GD, CHSL frequently ask direct questions from this Act. This is a must-learn topic for easy scoring.

India Independence

15 August 1947

Pakistan Independence

14 August 1947

Background of the Act

Preceding Events

  • Cabinet Mission Plan (1946) failed to keep India united
  • Communal violence increased between Hindus and Muslims
  • Partition became politically unavoidable
  • Lord Mountbatten proposed the Partition Plan
  • British Parliament passed the Act swiftly

Key Document

The Act was based on:

Mountbatten Plan (June 1947)

Also called the Partition Plan

Key Features of Indian Independence Act 1947

1. Creation of Two Independent Dominions

India

15 August 1947

Independence Day

Pakistan

14 August 1947

Independence Day

2. Complete End of British Rule

All British control and interference in Indian affairs ended. India became fully sovereign in internal and external matters.

3. Abolition of Viceroy's Office

Viceroy was replaced by Governor-General appointed by the British King but acting on advice of Indian ministers.

First Governor-General

Lord Mountbatten

First Indian Governor-General

C. Rajagopalachari (1948)

4. Constituent Assemblies Empowered

The Constituent Assemblies of India and Pakistan became sovereign law-making bodies, free from British control, to frame their respective constitutions.

5. Princely States Became Independent

Princely states were given three options:

  • Join India
  • Join Pakistan
  • Remain independent

Important princely states that faced decisions: Hyderabad, Junagarh, Kashmir

6. India Ceased to be a British Colony

India became a sovereign nation within the British Commonwealth. The British Parliament could no longer legislate for India.

7. Provincial Autonomy

Provincial governments became fully independent and no longer under British control. They could make laws for their provinces.

8. Temporary Powers of Governor-General

Until the new constitutions were framed, the Governor-General and provincial governors continued to function with emergency powers to ensure smooth transition.

9. Choice for Civil Servants & Army Officers

All government employees and military personnel could choose whether to serve in India or Pakistan, ensuring administrative continuity in both nations.

10. End of "Emperor of India" Title

The British King stopped using the title "Emperor of India," symbolizing the complete end of British imperial authority over India.

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. In which year was the Indian Independence Act passed?

1947 (Passed on 18 July 1947)

2. When did India become independent?

15 August 1947

3. Who was the last Viceroy of India?

Lord Mountbatten

4. Who was the first Indian Governor-General of independent India?

C. Rajagopalachari (1948)

5. Which Act created India and Pakistan as independent nations?

Indian Independence Act 1947

6. What options were given to princely states under the Act?

Join India, Join Pakistan, or Remain independent

7. Who replaced the Viceroy after independence?

Governor-General

8. What happened to the title "Emperor of India"?

It was abolished under the Act

Conclusion

The Indian Independence Act 1947 marked the formal end of British rule in India and created two sovereign nations. It transferred power to Indian leaders, empowered Constituent Assemblies, and set the stage for constitution-making and nation-building.
