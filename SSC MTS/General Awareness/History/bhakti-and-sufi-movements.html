Bhakti & Sufi Movement

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL Exams - Medieval India

Introduction

From the 7th–17th century, India saw two spiritual movements that changed society. This is one of the most important & easiest topics in Medieval History. SSC MTS, GD, CHSL often ask 2–3 questions from it.

1. Bhakti Movement

Rise of devotion (Hindu saints)

2. Sufi Movement

Rise of love and humanity (Muslim saints)

Both movements opposed:

  • Caste system
  • Rituals
  • Blind traditions
  • Religious conflict

Both movements promoted:

  • Love
  • Equality
  • Brotherhood
  • Devotion

Part 1: Bhakti Movement

What is Bhakti?

Bhakti = Personal devotion to God with love and faith

No need for priests, rituals, or caste barriers.

1. Origin of Bhakti Movement

It began in South India (7th–9th century). The first Bhakti saints were:

Alvars

Devotees of Vishnu

Nayanars

Devotees of Shiva

These groups started the early Bhakti tradition.

2. Features of Bhakti Movement

Devotion is more important than knowledge
All humans are equal
God can be reached through love
No need for rituals or costly sacrifices
Supported by saints and poets
Rejected caste discrimination

3. Important Bhakti Saints

A. South Indian Bhakti Saints

Shankaracharya (Advaita Philosophy)
  • Believed in non-dualism (Advaita)
  • God & soul are the same
  • Established 4 mathas (monasteries)
Ramanujacharya (Vishisht Advaita)
  • Believed the soul is different from God but connected
  • Promoted devotion to Vishnu
Basavanna (Lingayat Movement)
  • Opposed caste system
  • Worshipped Shiva (Lingayats)

B. North Indian Bhakti Saints

Kabir (One of the most important)
  • Teachings: "God is one"
  • Rejected temples, mosques, castes
  • Simple poems → Dohe
  • Followers → Kabir Panth
Guru Nanak
  • Founder of Sikhism
  • Believed in equality
  • Promoted "Naam Japna, Vand Chhakna, Kirat Karna"

Mirabai

Devotee of Krishna, wrote devotional songs

Ravidas

Saint from lower caste, equality and brotherhood

Tulsidas

Wrote Ramcharitmanas, devotee of Rama

Surdas

Devotee of Krishna, wrote "Sursagar"

Important Themes of Bhakti Movement

"God is one"

"Love & devotion is the path"

"Humans are equal"

"Remove caste system"

"Reject rituals"

Part 2: Sufi Movement

What is Sufism?

Sufism is a mystical form of Islam that focuses on: Love, Peace, Service, Brotherhood, Music & meditation

"To love people is to love God."

Important Sufi Orders (Silshilas)

1. Chishti Order (MOST IMPORTANT)

Founder in India: Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti (Ajmer)

Teachings:
  • Love
  • Peace
  • Feeding the poor
  • Avoid politics
Famous saints:
Nizamuddin Auliya
Baba Farid
Salim Chishti
Qutubuddin Bakhtiyar Kaki

2. Suhrawardi Order

  • Founder: Bahauddin Zakariya
  • More involved in politics

3. Naqshbandi Order

  • Focused on Islamic law
  • Became important during Mughal period

Practices of Sufis

Sama (music)
Zikr (recitation)
Meditation
Service to humanity
Simple living
Writing poetry

Places where they lived → Khanqahs (rest houses)

Sufi Saints & Their Teachings

Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti

  • "Garib Nawaz"
  • Tomb at Ajmer = major pilgrimage site

Nizamuddin Auliya

  • Saint of Delhi
  • Famous for Sufi music (Qawwali)

Baba Farid

  • Known for wise sayings
  • Punjabi poet

Salim Chishti

  • Lived during Akbar's time

Bhakti vs Sufi – Exam Table

Feature Bhakti Sufi
Religion Hindu Islam

Caste Opposed Opposed
Rituals Rejected Rejected
Music Bhajans Qawwali
House Temple/Gurudwara Khanqah
Famous Kabir, Nanak Chishti, Auliya

Quick Revision Points

Bhakti Movement:

  • Started in South India
  • Alvars = Vishnu saints, Nayanars = Shiva saints
  • Kabir preached against all religions
  • Guru Nanak founded Sikhism

Sufi Movement:

  • Chishti order → love, peace
  • Sufis lived in Khanqahs
  • Sufi Saints: Chishti, Farid, Auliya

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who founded the Sikh religion?

Guru Nanak

2. "God is one" was preached by?

Kabir

3. Which Sufi saint is called "Garib Nawaz"?

Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti

4. Bhakti Movement in North India reached height during?

Medieval period

5. What are Alvars?

Vishnu saints

6. Chishti order belongs to?

Sufi Movement

Conclusion

Bhakti and Sufi movements played a major role in: Ending caste barriers, Promoting equality, Uniting Hindus & Muslims, Strengthening Indian culture.
