Social Reformers – Raja Ram Mohan Roy & Swami Vivekananda

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL - Social & Religious Reform Movements

Why Social Reforms Were Needed?

During the 19th century, Indian society suffered from numerous social evils that required urgent reform. Reformers like Raja Ram Mohan Roy and Swami Vivekananda emerged to transform society through education, rational thinking, and spiritual awakening.

This chapter is HIGHLY IMPORTANT for SSC exams. Questions frequently come from Brahmo Samaj, Sati abolition, Ramakrishna Mission, and contributions of these reformers.

1. Raja Ram Mohan Roy – Father of Modern India

Born

1772, Bengal

Known As

Father of Modern India

Major Contribution

Brahmo Samaj (1828)

1. Founded Brahmo Samaj (1828)

A socio-religious movement promoting:

  • Belief in One God (Monotheism)
  • No idol worship
  • Equality and rational thinking
  • Opposition to caste discrimination

Memory Trick

"Brahmo = Brahman + Monotheism"

One God only, no idol worship

2. Abolition of Sati (1829)

Sati = practice where widows were forced to burn on their husband's funeral pyre.

Raja Ram Mohan Roy campaigned strongly against this evil practice.

Finally abolished by Lord William Bentinck in 1829

MOST EXPECTED SSC QUESTION

Who played the main role in banning Sati?

Raja Ram Mohan Roy

3. Fight Against Social Evils

Opposed Caste System

Supported Widow Remarriage

Opposed Child Marriage

Promoted Women's Education

4. Promoted Western Education

Supported modern learning including:

English Language
Science
Modern Learning
Journalism

5. Newspapers Started

Sambad Kaumudi

Published in Bengali (1821)

Mirat-ul-Akbar

Published in Persian (1822)

Memory Trick for Raja Ram Mohan Roy

"B–S–N–W"

B

Brahmo Samaj

S

Sati Abolished

N

Newspapers

W

Women's Rights

2. Swami Vivekananda – Messenger of Indian Spirituality

Born

1863, Kolkata

Real Name

Narendranath Datta

Guru

Ramakrishna Paramhansa

Founded

Ramakrishna Mission (1897)

1. Founded Ramakrishna Mission (1897)

Based on the teachings of his guru Ramakrishna Paramhansa.

Mission focuses on:

Education
Health Services
Social Uplift
Spiritual Service

Motto of Ramakrishna Mission

"Atmano Mokshartham Jagat Hitaya Cha"

(For one's own salvation and for the welfare of the world)

2. Famous Chicago Speech (1893)

His speech at the World Parliament of Religions in Chicago made him world famous.

He began with:

"Sisters and brothers of America..."

This opening won the hearts of the audience and introduced Indian spirituality to the West.

Frequently Asked in SSC

Where did Vivekananda deliver his famous speech?

Chicago, USA (1893)

3. Spread of Vedanta Philosophy

Explained Hindu philosophy (Vedanta, Yoga) to the Western world, making Indian spirituality globally recognized.

4. Promotion of Nationalism

Inspired Indian youth with powerful messages:

"Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached."
"You are the creator of your own destiny."

5. Emphasis on Youth Power

Believed India's future depends on the strength and energy of youth. Called for youth awakening and national regeneration.

6. Encouraged Physical & Mental Strength

"You will be nearer to Heaven through football than through the study of the Gita."

Emphasized the importance of physical strength along with spiritual growth.

7. Important Books by Vivekananda

Raja Yoga

Jnana Yoga

Practical Vedanta

Lectures from Colombo to Almora

Books

Comparison: Raja Ram Mohan Roy vs Swami Vivekananda

Feature Raja Ram Mohan Roy Swami Vivekananda
Era Early 19th century Late 19th century
Main Focus Social-religious reforms Spiritual awakening & youth empowerment
Organization Founded Brahmo Samaj (1828) Ramakrishna Mission (1897)
Major Social Issue Sati abolition Vedanta philosophy & nationalism
Approach Rational & modern thinking Spiritual & practical strength
Known As Father of Modern India Messenger of Indian Spirituality

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who founded the Brahmo Samaj?

Raja Ram Mohan Roy (1828)

2. In which year was Sati abolished?

1829 (by Lord William Bentinck)

3. Who founded the Ramakrishna Mission?

Swami Vivekananda (1897)

4. Where did Vivekananda deliver his famous 1893 speech?

Chicago, USA (World Parliament of Religions)

5. What was Swami Vivekananda's original name?

Narendranath Datta

6. Which newspaper was started by Raja Ram Mohan Roy?

Sambad Kaumudi (Bengali) and Mirat-ul-Akbar (Persian)

7. Who is called the "Father of Modern India"?

Raja Ram Mohan Roy

8. Who was Swami Vivekananda's guru?

Ramakrishna Paramhansa

Conclusion

Both Raja Ram Mohan Roy and Swami Vivekananda played transformative roles in shaping modern India: Raja Ram Mohan Roy reformed society through rational thinking and social justice, while Swami Vivekananda reformed minds through spiritual awakening and national pride.
