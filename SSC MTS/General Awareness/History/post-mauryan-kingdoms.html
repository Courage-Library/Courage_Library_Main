Post-Mauryan Kingdoms – Kushana & Sunga Era

Complete Notes for SSC MTS Exams - Ancient India

Introduction

After the decline of the Mauryan Empire in 185 BCE, India broke into several regional kingdoms. Two of the MOST IMPORTANT post-Mauryan dynasties for SSC exams are:

Sunga Dynasty

Kushana Dynasty

Let's learn them easily with stories, tricks, timelines & exam-focused facts.

Part 1: Sunga Dynasty (185 BCE – 75 BCE)

1. How did the Sunga Dynasty begin?

After Ashoka's death, Mauryan Empire weakened. The last Mauryan king Brihadratha was killed by his army commander:

Pushyamitra Sunga

He founded the Sunga Dynasty in 185 BCE

Memory Trick:

"Sunga Surges after Sorrow" (Mauryan decline → Sunga rise)

2. Important Sunga Rulers

Pushyamitra Sunga (Founder)

  • Re-established Brahmanical traditions
  • Performed great Vedic ritual Ashvamedha Yajna
  • Faced Indo-Greek invasions
  • Patron of Sanskrit and Vedic culture

Agnimitra

  • Son of Pushyamitra
  • Main character in Kalidasa's play "Malavikagnimitram"

SSC has asked this many times.

3. Sunga Art & Culture

Architecture:

  • Sanchi Stupa: Repaired & enlarged during Sunga period
  • Bharhut Stupa: Famous for its gateways (toranas)

Sculptures:

  • Jataka tales beautifully carved
  • Language used → Sanskrit

4. Decline of Sunga Dynasty

Weakened rulers after Agnimitra
Internal conflicts
Last king Devabhuti killed by Vasudeva Kanva

Start of Kanva Dynasty

Stupas Sanchi & Bharhut renovated

Memory Trick

"Pushya Pushed the Mauryas away."

Pushyamitra Sunga ended Mauryan rule.

Part 2: Kushana Dynasty (1st–3rd Century CE)

The Kushanas built a powerful empire in North-Western India. Their rule marked the Golden Age of Trade, Art & Culture.

1. Who were the Kushanas?

  • They belonged to Central Asia (modern-day China region)
  • Part of a tribe called Yuezhi

2. Important Kushana Kings

Kujula Kadphises

  • Founder of Kushana Empire in India
  • Unified different Yuezhi tribes

Kanishka (MOST IMPORTANT)

The greatest Kushana ruler.

Reigned in 78 CE (Start of Shaka Era)

Capitals:
Purushapura (modern Peshawar)
Mathura (second capital)

3. Kanishka's Contributions

Religion

  • Patron of Mahayana Buddhism
  • Organized 4th Buddhist Council in Kashmir
  • Spread Buddhism to: China, Central Asia, Mongolia

Art & Culture

  • Developed Gandhara Art (mix of Greek + Indian style)
  • Buddha statues began to be made in human form
  • Famous for sculptures, coins, and inscriptions

Scholars in Kanishka's court

Very important for exam:

Charaka

Father of Indian Medicine (Charaka Samhita)

Ashvaghosha

Wrote Buddhacharita

Nagarjuna

Scholar of Mahayana Buddhism

Kushana Coins

  • Gold coins (most famous)
  • Images of Greek, Persian & Indian gods
  • Show prosperity & trade

Trade & Economy (Golden Period)

  • Flourishing Silk Route
  • Trade with Rome, China & Central Asia
  • Goods: spices, silk, carpets, cotton, horses

Shaka Era started by Strong king Sanishka (Kanishka)

(funny but works)

Exam Practice Questions

1. Who founded the Sunga Dynasty?

Pushyamitra Sunga

2. The character Agnimitra appears in which book?

Malavikagnimitram

3. Which king started the Shaka Era (78 CE)?

Kanishka

4. Gandhara Art is associated with which ruler?

Kanishka

5. 4th Buddhist Council was held under?

Kanishka

6. Sanchi Stupa was repaired during?

Sunga Dynasty

Conclusion

Post-Mauryan kingdoms kept India culturally rich and politically diverse. Remember:

Sungas

  • Sanskrit
  • Vedic revival
  • Sanchi repair

Kushanas

  • Kanishka
  • Buddhism
  • Gandhara Art
  • Gold coins

These points guarantee 2–4 marks in SSC MTS.
