Delhi Sultanate – Slave, Khilji, Tughlaq, Sayyid, Lodi

Complete Notes for SSC MTS Exams - Medieval India

Introduction

The Delhi Sultanate ruled India for 320 years (1206–1526 CE). It had five dynasties, each with powerful rulers, wars, reforms, victories, and blunders.

Let's study each dynasty with: Key rulers, Achievements, Architecture, Memory tricks, and SSC MTS exam questions

Quick Timeline

Slave Dynasty

1206–1290

Khilji Dynasty

1290–1320

Tughlaq Dynasty

1320–1414

Sayyid Dynasty

1414–1451

Lodi Dynasty

1451–1526

1. Slave Dynasty (1206–1290)

Also called: Mamluk Dynasty (Mamluk = Slave)

Founder: Qutbuddin Aibak

  • Slave of Muhammad Ghori
  • Established the dynasty in 1206
  • Built: Qutub Minar (started), Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque
  • Died while playing polo (chaugan)
  • Title: Lakh-Baksh (giver of lakhs)

Successor: Iltutmish (Most Important)

Achievements:

  • Completed Qutub Minar
  • Introduced Silver coin – Tanka
  • Introduced Copper coin – Jittal
  • Saved Delhi from Mongols
  • Gave Delhi Sultanate shape and stability

First Woman Ruler: Raziya Sultan

  • Daughter of Iltutmish
  • Brave and efficient
  • Faced opposition from nobles
  • Ruled for 4 years

Last Important Ruler: Balban

  • Strict ruler
  • Introduced Spy System
  • Theory of Kingship → "Zil-e-Ilahi" (King is God's shadow)
  • Broke the power of Turkish nobles

Memory Trick

Slave Dynasty = A-I-R-B

Aibak → Iltutmish → Raziya → Balban

Like "AIR-Bus"

2. Khilji Dynasty (1290–1320)

Founder: Jalaluddin Khilji

Peaceful ruler, no major achievements.

Greatest Ruler: Alauddin Khilji

Known for: Military reforms, Market reforms, Conquests

Military Achievements:

  • Defeated Mongols multiple times
  • Conquered South India using general Malik Kafur

Market Reforms (VERY IMPORTANT)

Fixed prices of essential goods

Set up government warehouses

Officers checked prices daily

Land Reforms

Increased land tax
Controlled nobles
No drinking
No celebrations

Memory Trick

Alauddin = All-Do-In

He did EVERYTHING: Army, Market, South India.

3. Tughlaq Dynasty (1320–1414)

They are known for big ideas, but bad execution.

1. Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq

  • Built Tughlaqabad Fort
  • Practical ruler

2. Muhammad bin Tughlaq (MOST IMPORTANT!)

Known as: "The Wisest Fool" - Brilliant plans but failed in execution

His 5 Famous Experiments (Exam's Favourite)

1. Capital Transfer

Shifted capital from Delhi → Daulatabad - Forced migration failed

2. Token Currency

Introduced bronze coins instead of silver - People forged coins → system collapsed

3. Tax Increase in Doab

Famine occurred - People suffered

4. Khorasan Expedition

Planned huge army → cancelled → waste of money

5. Qarachal Expedition

Sent army to Himalayas → many soldiers died

Overall: Genius ideas, poor planning → failures.

3. Firoz Shah Tughlaq

Opposite of MBT → Practical builder.

Achievements:

  • Built 40 gardens, 200 towns, 30 canals
  • Founded Firozabad
  • Built Firoz Shah Kotla
  • Abolished torture & reduced taxes
  • Patron of scholars

Memory Trick: Tughlaqs

  • Ghiyasuddin → Good start
  • Muhammad → Mad plans
  • Firoz → Fixer (builder)

4. Sayyid Dynasty (1414–1451)

Weakest of all dynasties

Key Points:

  • Founded by Khizr Khan
  • Continued Mongol pressure
  • No major achievements
  • Depended on nobles

Not many SSC questions from this dynasty.

5. Lodi Dynasty (1451–1526)

Last dynasty of Delhi Sultanate

Founder: Bahlol Lodi

  • Ruled from Delhi
  • Strengthened Afghan power

Sikandar Lodi

  • Strict ruler
  • Founded Agra city
  • Encouraged agriculture
  • Strict price control

Last Ruler: Ibrahim Lodi

  • Fought Babur in First Battle of Panipat (1526)
  • Defeated → Delhi Sultanate ended
  • Mughal Empire began

Memory Trick

Lodi = BSI

Bahlol → Sikandar → Ibrahim (Like "B-S-I" → easy order)

Architecture of Delhi Sultanate (Exam-Focused)

Dynasty Monuments
Slave Qutub Minar (Aibak/Iltutmish), Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque
Khilji Alai Darwaza
Tughlaq Tughlaqabad Fort, Firoz Shah Kotla
Lodi Tombs in Delhi

Sure-Shot MCQs (SSC MTS)

1. Qutub Minar was completed by?

Iltutmish

2. Who introduced market control system?

Alauddin Khilji

3. Who shifted capital to Daulatabad?

Muhammad bin Tughlaq

4. Who founded Agra?

Sikandar Lodi

5. Which battle ended the Delhi Sultanate?

First Battle of Panipat (1526)

6. Who started the Slave Dynasty?

Qutbuddin Aibak

7. Who built Tughlaqabad Fort?

Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq

Conclusion

Delhi Sultanate is full of: Strong rulers, Big reforms, Great architecture, Big mistakes (especially MBT!)
