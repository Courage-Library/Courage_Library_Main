Mahajanapadas & Buddhism–Jainism

Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL Exams - Ancient India

Introduction

This chapter is high-scoring and frequently asked in SSC MTS, GD, CHSL, Railways, and other competitive exams.

Let's make it super easy with stories, charts, and memory tricks!

Part 1: Mahajanapadas – The First 16 Kingdoms of India

What are Mahajanapadas?

Around 600 BCE, after the Later Vedic period, India saw the rise of many small states called Janapadas. Some of them expanded and became big kingdoms called:

Mahajanapadas (16 Great States)

This period is also called the Age of Second Urbanization because cities grew rapidly again after Indus Valley Civilization.

Magadha became the most powerful kingdom due to:

Fertile land

Iron ore availability

Strong armies

Smart rulers

Magadha eventually formed the Mauryan Empire.

Part 2: Buddhism – Life, Teachings & Importance

Founder: Gautama Buddha

  • Born: Lumbini (Nepal)
  • Real name: Siddhartha
  • Clan: Shakya
  • Father: Suddhodana (King of Kapilvastu)
  • Mother: Maya
  • Wife: Yasodhara
  • Son: Rahula

Memory Trick

"L-K-B"

  • Lumbini (Birth)
  • Kapilvastu (Childhood)
  • Bodh Gaya (Enlightenment)

Important Events of Buddha's Life

Event Place
Birth Lumbini
Enlightenment Bodh Gaya
First Sermon Sarnath
Death (Mahaparinirvana) Kushinagar

Buddha's Teachings – The Core

Four Noble Truths

  1. Life is full of suffering
  2. Desire causes suffering
  3. End desire → end suffering
  4. Follow the Eightfold Path

Eightfold Path

Right view, effort, speech, action, livelihood, resolve, mindfulness, concentration.

Middle Path

Avoid extremes → balanced life.

Key Principles

  • Karma & Rebirth
  • No caste system

Buddhist Councils

Council Location King
1st Rajgir Ajatashatru
2nd Vaishali Kalashoka
3rd Pataliputra Ashoka
4th Kashmir Kanishka

Spread of Buddhism

Emperor Ashoka played the biggest role by sending missionaries to Sri Lanka and other regions.

Part 3: Jainism – Founder, Teachings & Concepts

Founder: Mahavira

  • 24th Tirthankara
  • Born: Kundagrama (Vaishali)
  • Father: Siddhartha
  • Mother: Trishala
  • Attained Kaivalya (knowledge): Jrimbhikagrama
  • Death: Pawapuri

Key Teachings of Jainism

Five Great Vows

  1. Ahimsa (non-violence)
  2. Satya (truth)
  3. Asteya (no stealing)
  4. Brahmacharya (celibacy)
  5. Aparigraha (non-possession)

Karma Theory

Every action has consequences.

Core Belief

No belief in God as creator. Focus is on soul purification.

Jain Literature

  • Angas
  • Upangas
  • Language: Prakrit

Comparison Between Buddhism & Jainism (VERY IMPORTANT)

Feature Buddhism Jainism
Founder Gautama Buddha Mahavira
Birth Lumbini Vaishali

Memory Trick:

"BP-JP" → Buddha–Pali, Jain–Prakrit
