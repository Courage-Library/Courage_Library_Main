Mughal Empire – Akbar, Jahangir, Shah Jahan, Aurangzeb

Complete Notes for SSC MTS Exams - Medieval India

Introduction

The Mughal Empire ruled India for nearly 200 years, and their administration, culture, architecture, and policies shaped India deeply.

In SSC exams, 3–5 questions directly come from the Mughal Empire.

The Mughal Empire was founded by Babur in 1526 after defeating Ibrahim Lodi in the First Battle of Panipat.

The four Mughal rulers that SSC focuses MOST on are:

Akbar

Jahangir

Shah Jahan

Aurangzeb

1. Akbar (1556–1605)

Greatest ruler of Mughal Empire

Early Life

  • Became king at age 13
  • Real hero in his life: Bairam Khan, regent (guardian) who helped him rule initially

Major Policies & Achievements

1. Abolition of Taxes

Akbar removed:

Jaziya

Tax on non-Muslims

Pilgrimage tax

This made him popular among Hindus.

2. Mansabdari System (VERY IMPORTANT)

Introduced by Akbar. Each officer (Mansabdar) had two ranks:

Zat

Personal rank / salary

Sawar

Number of cavalry under him

Memory Trick:

"Z = Zinda (yourself)"

S = Soldiers

3. Religious Policy – Sulh-i-Kul

Means: Universal peace. Equal respect to all religions.

Akbar also started:

  • Din-i-Ilahi (a new religion with moral teachings)
  • Ibadat Khana (Hall of worship & discussions)

4. Rajput Policy

Akbar made alliance with Rajputs by:

Respecting their religion
Treating them equally
Marrying Rajput princess
(Jodha Bai)

Most Rajput kings (except Mewar) accepted his friendship.

5. Architecture

Built Fatehpur Sikri

Famous buildings:

Buland Darwaza
Diwan-i-Khas
Panch Mahal

Quick Revision: Akbar

Feature Fact
System Mansabdari
Rank Zat & Sawar
Tax abolished Jaziya & Pilgrimage
New faith Din-i-Ilahi
Capital shift Agra → Fatehpur Sikri
Title Akbar the Great

2. Jahangir (1605–1627)

Son of Akbar. Called: "Justice Lover" (Adil Emperor)

Symbol of justice → Gold chain of justice at Agra Fort.

1. Administration

Followed Akbar's policies. Ruled smoothly and peacefully.

2. Art & Painting

Jahangir loved: Nature, Birds, Flowers, Paintings

Mughal painting reached its peak during his rule.

3. Important Events

  • Captain Hawkins & Sir Thomas Roe (British) came to his court
  • Gave permission to English to trade in India

This later helped British become powerful.

4. Noor Jahan

  • His wife, very powerful
  • Issued coins in her name

Quick Revision: Jahangir

Feature Fact
Love Painting & nature
Symbol of Justice Golden chain
Wife Noor Jahan
British Arrival Thomas Roe

3. Shah Jahan (1628–1658)

Known as "The Engineer King" or "Prince of Builders"

His reign is considered the Golden Age of Mughal Architecture.

1. Architecture

Built:

Taj Mahal

Agra (in memory of Mumtaz)

Red Fort

Delhi

Jama Masjid

Delhi

Shalimar Garden

Lahore

2. Peacock Throne

A jeweled throne built by Shah Jahan—later taken by Nadir Shah.

Shah Jahan & Aurangzeb War

Aurangzeb imprisoned Shah Jahan in Agra Fort until his death.

Quick Revision: Shah Jahan

Feature Fact
Nickname Engineer King
Famous Monument Taj Mahal
Other Monuments Red Fort, Jama Masjid
Throne Peacock Throne
Capital Agra → Delhi

4. Aurangzeb (1658–1707)

Last strong Mughal ruler

Known for: Strict Islamic rule, Largest Mughal Empire, Religious conservatism

Major Policies

1. Re-imposed Jaziya

Tax on non-Muslims (removed by Akbar)

2. Prohibited Music & Dance

Considered them un-Islamic. Ended royal patronage of arts.

3. Hindu Temples

Destroyed some temples. Ordered restrictions during festivals.

4. Longest Reign

Ruled for 50 years (1658–1707)

Wars

  • Continued war with Marathas (Shivaji's successors)
  • Fought Sikhs, Jats, Rajputs
  • Deccan wars drained the treasury

Death

Died in 1707, after which Mughal Empire rapidly declined.

Quick Revision: Aurangzeb

Feature Fact
Tax Re-imposed Jaziya
Culture Banned music
Wars Marathas, Deccan
Religion Orthodox Muslim
Last strong ruler Yes

Most Expected SSC MTS Questions

1. Who started the Mansabdari system?

Akbar

2. Who built the Taj Mahal?

Shah Jahan

3. Who gave trade permission to the British?

Jahangir

4. Who banned music and dance?

Aurangzeb

5. Who founded Din-i-Ilahi?

Akbar

6. Fatehpur Sikri was built by?

Akbar

7. Golden age of Mughal architecture?

Shah Jahan

8. Chain of Justice was started by?

Jahangir

Conclusion

Remember the Mughal rulers in order:

A → J → S → A

Akbar → Jahangir → Shah Jahan → Aurangzeb

Each ruler's signature contribution:

Akbar

Administration + Tolerance + Mansabdari

Jahangir

Art + British entry

Shah Jahan

Architecture + Taj Mahal

Aurangzeb

Conservatism + Deccan wars

This chapter guarantees 4–5 marks in SSC MTS.
