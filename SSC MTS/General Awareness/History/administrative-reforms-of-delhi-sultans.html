Administrative Reforms of Delhi Sultans

Complete Notes for SSC MTS Exams - Medieval India

Introduction

Delhi Sultans ruled for 320 years, and during this period, they created some of the most organized administrative systems in Indian history.

This chapter is very important for SSC because it appears in both History and Polity sections.

Their administration was influenced by:

Turkish practices
Persian culture
Islamic laws
Local Indian traditions

The administration included:

  • Central administration
  • Military administration
  • Revenue & land system
  • Market regulation
  • Spy system
  • Provincial administration

1. Central Administration

Delhi Sultanate followed a monarchical system → King = supreme head, assisted by a council of ministers.

Department Head Function
Diwan-i-Wizarat Wazir Finance & Revenue
Diwan-i-Ariz Ariz Army & Military matters
Diwan-i-Insha Dabir State correspondence & letters
Diwan-i-Risalat Chief Sadr Religious matters & charities
Diwan-i-Qaza Qazi Judiciary

Memory Trick:

"W-A-I-R-Q" → "Wear Q"

Wazir – Ariz – Insha – Risalat – Qaza

2. Military Administration

The Sultans needed a strong army for: Preventing Mongol invasions, Expanding empire, Controlling rebellious nobles

Iltutmish

  • Introduced Iqta system
  • Gave land assignments to nobles instead of salary

Alauddin Khilji

  • Introduced standing army (regular paid army)
  • Introduced Dagh (branding of horses)
  • Introduced Chehra (soldier's identity card)
Memory Trick:

"D-C"

Dagh & Chehra are always together → Alauddin

Muhammad bin Tughlaq

  • Tried to create huge armies
  • Failed due to over-planning

3. Revenue Administration

Iqta System

Introduced by Iltutmish, strengthened by others.

How it worked:

  • Sultan gave land (Iqta) to nobles
  • Nobles (Iqtedars) collected revenue
  • Part kept for local administration
  • Rest sent to Sultan

Land Revenue Reforms

Alauddin Khilji

Increased land tax

Muhammad bin Tughlaq

Increased taxes in Doab region

Firoz Tughlaq

Built canals → boosted agriculture

4. Market Reforms (VERY IMPORTANT for SSC)

Reforms by Alauddin Khilji

Why Market Reforms?

To maintain large army
To keep low prices
To control nobles

Four Market Controls:

1. Price fixing

Prices of grains, clothes, horses, slaves were fixed

2. Grain Storehouses

Govt. built mandis & warehouses

3. Market Inspectors

Shahna-i-Mandi supervised markets

4. Strict Punishments

Cheating → severe punishment

5. Spy System

Balban

  • Established the strongest spy network
  • Agents were called "Barids"
  • Secret informers everywhere

Alauddin Khilji

  • Expanded spy system
  • Monitored nobles closely

6. Provincial Administration

The empire was divided into administrative levels:

Level Name Head
Province Iqtas / Subahs Muqti / Governor
District Shiq Shiqdar
Village Pargana Amil
Village group Gram Village headman

Key Terms (Regularly asked in SSC):

Muqti

Provincial governor

Amil

Revenue collector

Kotwal

Police officer of city

7. Judicial System

Based on Sharia (Islamic law).

Key posts:

Qazi-ul-Quzat

Chief judge of the empire

Qazi

District-level judge

Punishments were strict:

Cutting hands
Death penalty
Public punishments

8. Reforms by Specific Sultans (Super Important)

Iltutmish

  • Introduced Iqta system
  • Silver coin Tanka
  • Copper Jittal
  • Saved Delhi from Mongols
  • Built administration foundation

Balban

  • Strong spy system
  • Strict rule (blood & iron policy)
  • "Zil-e-Ilahi" concept
  • Ended power of Turkish nobles

Alauddin Khilji

  • Price control
  • Standing army
  • Dagh and Chehra system
  • Conquests of Deccan
  • Strict administration

Muhammad bin Tughlaq

Known for crazy experiments:

Token currency
Capital transfer
Increased taxes
Failed expeditions

Firoz Shah Tughlaq

  • Built canals, gardens, towns
  • Improved revenue system
  • Built hospitals
  • Soft policies
  • Repaired Qutub Minar

Sikandar Lodi

  • Founded Agra
  • Controlled prices
  • Improved agriculture

Sikandar Lodi

Delhi Sultans built one of the strongest administrative systems in medieval India.
