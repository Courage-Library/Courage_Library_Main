Mughal Administration & Culture

Complete Notes for SSC MTS Exams - Medieval India

Introduction

The Mughal Administration is one of the most organized systems in medieval world history, and Mughal Culture is the richest blend of Persian, Indian & Central Asian art.

SSC MTS often asks about:

Mansabdari system
Zabti revenue system
Important officials
Mughal paintings
Architecture
Literature
Music

Part 1: Mughal Administration

The Mughal Empire was highly centralised, yet flexible.

1. Emperor – Central Authority

The entire administration revolved around the Emperor, who had unlimited powers.

Titles used by Mughal emperors:

Shahenshah (King of Kings)
Badshah
Zil-e-Ilahi (Shadow of God)

2. Central Administration – Key Departments

Office Name Role
Prime Minister Wazir Revenue & administration
Finance Minister Diwan-i-Ala Accounts, treasury
Military Head Mir Bakshi Army, Mansabdars
Chief Justice Qazi-ul-Quzat Islamic law
Foreign Minister Mir Saman Royal household
Postal & Intelligence Barid Spy system

Memory Trick:

"W–D–M–Q–B = We Do Mughal Quality Business"

Easy way to remember all central officials

3. Mansabdari System (VERY IMPORTANT)

Introduced by Akbar

What is a Mansabdar?

A government officer who worked in administration + army.

Features:

  • Every mansabdar had a rank
  • Two numbers were assigned: Zat & Sawar

Two Types of Ranks:

Zat

Personal rank (status & salary)

Sawar

Number of cavalry he must maintain

Example: A mansabdar with Zat 5000 / Sawar 3000 = Big rank + large cavalry

4. Jagir System

  • Mansabdars did not receive salary in cash
  • Instead, they were given Jagirs (land) to collect revenue from
  • But they did NOT own the land
  • Jagirs were transferable

This ensured no mansabdar became too powerful.

5. Revenue System (Land Revenue)

Introduced by Todar Mal (Akbar's finance minister)

Zabti System

Based on survey & measurement of land:

  • Measured land with Bamboo Jaribs
  • Revenue fixed based on productivity
  • Land classified into: Polaj, Parauti, Chachar, Banjar

Revenue collected in:

Cash (mostly)

Sometimes in kind

Provincial Administration

The empire was divided into administrative levels:

Level Name Head
Province Suba Subedar
District Sarkar Faujdar
Pargana Pargana Amil
Village Gram Muqaddam

Memory Trick:

"Suba → Sarkar → Pargana → Gram"

(Top → bottom hierarchy)

Part 2: Mughal Culture

Mughal culture is a blend of Persian, Indian, Turkish, and Central Asian traditions.

1. Mughal Architecture (SSC's Hot Topic)

Akbar

Buildings:

Fatehpur Sikri
Buland Darwaza
Panch Mahal
Diwan-i-Khas

Style: Indo-Islamic architecture

Shah Jahan (Golden Age of Architecture)

Most important monuments:

Taj Mahal
Red Fort
Jama Masjid
Shalimar Bagh
Moti Masjid

Babur

Brought Persian-style gardens → Charbagh style

Jahangir

Loved gardens, Tomb of Itimad-ud-Daulah

Aurangzeb

Few buildings: Badshahi Mosque, Bibi ka Maqbara

2. Mughal Paintings

Akbar

  • Started Mughal School of Painting
  • Famous painters: Basawan, Daswanth, Abdus Samad

Jahangir (Peak of Mughal Painting)

  • Naturalism
  • Birds, flowers, animals
  • Artist: Ustad Mansur

Shah Jahan

  • Portrait painting
  • Miniature painting

3. Mughal Literature

Akbar

  • "Akbarnama" & "Ain-i-Akbari" written by Abul Fazl
  • "Tabaqat-i-Akbari" – Nizamuddin Ahmad

Jahangir

Autobiography → Tuzuk-i-Jahangiri

Shah Jahan

Court poets: Persian literature flourished

Aurangzeb

Religious texts, No patronage to music & literature

4. Mughal Music

Akbar

  • Tansen – greatest musician
  • Invented Raag Deepak & Raag Miyan ki Malhar

Jahangir & Shah Jahan

Supported music & artists

Aurangzeb

  • Banned music in court
  • But secretly listened (Fun fact!)

Memory Tricks to Remember Everything

Trick 1: A-J-S-A

Akbar → Jahangir → Shah Jahan → Aurangzeb

Trick 2: Architecture

A–J–S–A = F–T–Red/White–None

Akbar→Fatehpur, Jahangir→Tombs, Shah Jahan→Red/White, Aurangzeb→None

Trick 3: Literature

  • A = Abul Fazl
  • J = Jahangir's book
  • S = Poetry
  • A = Aura → Religious

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who introduced the Mansabdari system?

Akbar

2. Who wrote Akbarnama?

Abul Fazl

3. Which Mughal ruler built the Taj Mahal?

Shah Jahan

4. Which emperor wrote his own autobiography?

Jahangir

5. Who reimposed Jaziya tax?

Aurangzeb

6. Zabti revenue system was developed by?

Todar Mal

7. Who was the greatest Mughal painter?

Ustad Mansur (Jahangir's court)

8. Which king promoted music the most?

Akbar (Tansen)

Conclusion

Mughal Administration & Culture represent the peak of India's medieval governance and artistic excellence.

Remember the following to score full marks:

Mansabdari (Akbar)
Zabti (Todar Mal)
Mughal architecture
Akbarnama
Tuzuk-i-Jahangiri
Tansen, Ustad Mansur
