Vedic Age – The Birth of Indian Culture

Complete Notes for SSC MTS Exams - Ancient India

Introduction

After the decline of the Indus Valley Civilization, new groups called Aryans entered India around 1500 BCE. Their arrival began a new era called the Vedic Age, because people's knowledge came from sacred books called Vedas.

This period has high SSC MTS importance, especially questions comparing Early Vedic vs Later Vedic Age.

Two Phases of Vedic Age

Early Vedic Age

1500–1000 BCE

Later Vedic Age

1000–600 BCE

The difference between these two is VERY important for exams.

1. Sources of Vedic Age

The Vedas (Most Important)

There are 4 Vedas:

Veda Purpose
Rig Veda Oldest; hymns on nature gods
Sam Veda Music & chanting
Yajur Veda Rituals & sacrifices
Atharva Veda Magic, healing, household rituals

Trick to remember 4 Vedas:

"R-S-Y-A"

Rishi Saw Yagna Always

R

Rig

S

Sam

Y

Yajur

A

Atharva

2. Early Vedic Age (1500–1000 BCE)

Lifestyle & Economy

  • Pastoral → cattle herding
  • Horse, cows were most important
  • Cattle = main wealth

Memory Trick:

"Early Vedic people = Early Farmers of Cows."

Society

  • Family: Patriarchal
  • Women had respect
  • Education: Gurukula
  • Marriage: Monogamy common
  • No child marriage

Political System

  • Tribal society
  • King's power was limited
  • Two assemblies: Sabha and Samiti

Memory Trick:

"SABha & SAMIti = 'Suba-Sami' (like two brothers)."

Religion

  • Nature worship
  • No idol worship
  • Famous gods: Indra (War god), Agni (Fire), Varuna (Water), Surya (Sun)

3. Later Vedic Age (1000–600 BCE)

Now society becomes more complex, cities start forming, and rituals increase.

Economy

  • Agriculture becomes main occupation
  • Iron was discovered → better farming tools
  • Trade increased
  • Coins called Nishka were used

Society

  • Varna system becomes rigid

Brahmins

Priests

Kshatriyas

Warriors

Vaishyas

Traders

Shudras

Servants

Women's Decline

  • Women lost status
  • Child marriage started
  • No Vedic education for many women

Politics

  • Rule became king-centered
  • Big kingdoms called Mahajanapadas
  • Important officers: Purohita, Senapati, Treasurer

Religion

  • Sacrifices (yajnas) increased
  • New gods: Vishnu, Shiva, Prajapati
  • Rise of Upanishads → stressed knowledge over rituals

4. Comparison Table (MOST IMPORTANT for SSC EXAM)

Feature Early Vedic Later Vedic
Economy Cattle rearing Agriculture + iron tools
Society Flexible varna Rigid varna
Women High status Low status
Religion Nature worship Ritual worship + new gods
Politics Tribal Kingdoms (state formation)
Tools No iron Iron tools

Memory Trick:

"Early = Simple, Later = Complex."

Assemblies

Janapadas

Mahajanapadas

6. Quick Revision Points

  • Aryans entered India in 1500 BCE
  • Rig Veda = oldest book of India
  • Early Vedic = cattle, Later Vedic = agriculture
  • Iron came in Later Vedic Age
  • Varna system becomes rigid in Later Vedic Age
  • Upanishads belong to Later Vedic Age
