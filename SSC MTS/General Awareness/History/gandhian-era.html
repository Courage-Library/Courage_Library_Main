Gandhian Era Movements

Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience & Quit India - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Why Gandhi Became the National Leader?

Mahatma Gandhi returned to India in 1915 and introduced Satyagraha (Truth + Non-violence) as a new method of protest. He led three major mass movements that transformed India's freedom struggle.

SSC MTS asks MANY questions from Gandhian movements. Focus on causes, key events, and outcomes of each movement.

Non-Cooperation

1920–1922

First nationwide movement

Civil Disobedience

1930–1934

Dandi March & Salt Satyagraha

Quit India

1942

Final mass movement

1. Non-Cooperation Movement (1920–1922)

Reasons Behind the Movement

  • Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (1919) – British brutality
  • Rowlatt Act – "Black Act" allowing arrest without trial
  • Khilafat Issue – Support for Turkish Caliph
  • British disrespect for Indian rights and justice

India's first nationwide mass movement under Gandhi's leadership.

Aim of the Movement

To force British to give Swaraj (self-rule) through peaceful non-cooperation.

Methods of Protest

Boycott British schools
Use khadi, boycott foreign cloth
Boycott courts & councils
Return British titles

Participation

Students
Women
Middle-class
Peasants & Workers
Muslims (Khilafat leaders)

End of Movement: Chauri Chaura Incident (1922)

A mob burnt a police station in Chauri Chaura (Uttar Pradesh).

Gandhi stopped the movement because he strictly opposed violence.

MOST IMPORTANT SSC QUESTION

Why did Non-Cooperation Movement end?

Chauri Chaura Incident (1922)

Outcome of Non-Cooperation Movement

  • Increased national unity and awareness
  • Congress transformed into a mass-based organization
  • Gandhi emerged as the undisputed national leader
  • British shocked by nationwide peaceful protest
  • Khadi became symbol of Indian nationalism
  • Foundation laid for future mass movements

2. Civil Disobedience Movement (1930–1934)

Background

  • British monopoly on salt production and high salt tax
  • Simon Commission (1928) – no Indian members
  • Nehru Report rejected by British
  • Peasant suffering and economic depression

Started with the historic Dandi March – breaking the salt law.

Dandi March (Salt March) – 1930

  • Gandhi walked 240 miles (385 km) from Sabarmati Ashram to Dandi
  • Dates: 12 March – 6 April 1930
  • 78 volunteers accompanied him
  • On 6 April, Gandhi picked up salt from sea water, symbolically breaking the salt law

Memory Trick

"Gandhi + Salt = Civil Disobedience"

Salt became the symbol of protest against unjust British laws.

Features of Civil Disobedience Movement

Civil Disobedience = Breaking British Laws peacefully

Salt Laws

Forest Laws

Tax Laws

Liquor Laws

Massive participation by women, students, peasants, and merchants across India.

1st Round Table Conference (1930)

Congress did not attend. Failed to reach agreement.

2nd Round Table Conference (1931)

Gandhi attended after Gandhi-Irwin Pact. No agreement reached.

3rd Round Table Conference (1932)

Congress boycotted again. Led to Government of India Act 1935.

Poona Pact (1932)

Between Gandhi and Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.

Issue: Separate electorates for Depressed Classes.

Result: Joint electorate with reserved seats for Depressed Classes.

End of Civil Disobedience Movement

Movement gradually declined by 1934 due to:

  • British repression and arrests
  • Exhaustion of resources
  • Shift to constitutional politics

3. Quit India Movement (1942)

Background

  • World War II – British dragged India into war without consent
  • Cripps Mission (1942) failed – offered Dominion Status after war
  • Growing frustration with British rule
  • Demand for immediate independence

India's FINAL mass movement against British rule – the decisive push for independence.

Launch of Quit India Movement

  • Date: 8 August 1942
  • Place: Bombay (Gowalia Tank Ground)
  • Gandhi's slogan: "Do or Die"
  • Demand: Immediate end to British rule in India

"Do or Die"

Gandhi's call for final struggle

British arrested entire Congress leadership within hours of the speech.

Features of Quit India Movement

  • Entire Congress leadership arrested overnight
  • Movement became leaderless
  • Students, peasants, women took charge
  • Widespread strikes, protests, and sabotage

Parallel Governments Formed

Satara (Maharashtra)
Ballia (Uttar Pradesh)
Midnapore (Bengal)

Outcome of Quit India Movement

  • Shook the foundations of British rule in India
  • Demonstrated that Indians would no longer accept British domination
  • British realized they could not rule India without massive force
  • Paved the way for independence negotiations
  • Established that independence was inevitable
  • Inspired post-war independence movements across colonies

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Why did the Non-Cooperation Movement end in 1922?

Chauri Chaura Incident – violence at police station

2. "Do or Die" slogan was given by Gandhi during which movement?

Quit India Movement (1942)

3. When did the Civil Disobedience Movement begin?

1930 (with Dandi March on 12 March 1930)

4. Dandi March is associated with breaking which law?

Salt Law (British monopoly on salt production)

5. Which Round Table Conference did Gandhi attend?

Second Round Table Conference (1931)

6. What was the main demand of the Quit India Movement?

Immediate independence from British rule

7. Who were the leaders of the Khilafat Movement that supported Non-Cooperation?

Ali Brothers (Shaukat Ali and Mohammad Ali)

8. What was the Poona Pact (1932) about?

Agreement between Gandhi and Ambedkar on representation of Depressed Classes

Conclusion

The Gandhian era (1919–1942) transformed India's freedom struggle through peaceful Satyagraha, mass participation, and strategic non-violence. From boycotting British goods to breaking salt laws to the final "Do or Die" call, these movements demonstrated the power of organized people's resistance.

Exam Success Strategy

Memorize years and triggers of each movement
Associate key events (Dandi March = Salt Law)
Remember why each movement ended
Link slogans to correct movements

Mastering these three movements guarantees 5+ easy marks in SSC MTS, GD, CHSL history section.
