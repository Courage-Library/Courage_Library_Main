Arrival of Europeans

Portuguese, Dutch, French, British - Complete Notes for SSC MTS, GD, CHSL

Introduction: Why Europeans Came to India?

Europeans came mainly for spices, which were luxury items with high profit margins. This chapter is crucial for SSC exams as it covers trading companies, important battles, and colonial beginnings.

Memory Trick: "P D E F" → Portuguese → Dutch → English → French

1. Portuguese (1498) – First Europeans

Arrival of Portuguese

  • Vasco da Gama reached Calicut (Kozhikode) in 1498
  • Welcomed by Zamorin (King of Calicut)
  • Established first European sea route to India

Memory Trick

"Gama Came First"

Vasco da Gama → First European in India

Important Portuguese Centres

Goa became headquarters in 1530 and remained capital of Portuguese India

Goa (Capital)
Diu
Daman
Cochin
Bassein

Famous Governor: Albuquerque

  • Conquered Goa (1510)
  • Introduced "Blue Water Policy"
  • Encouraged intermarriage with Indians
  • Strengthened Portuguese naval power

Portuguese Contributions

  • Introduced tobacco, potatoes, tomatoes, pineapples
  • Introduced printing press to India
  • Longest European presence (till 1961)

2. Dutch (1605) – Netherlands

Arrival of Dutch

  • Dutch East India Company formed in 1602
  • First factory in India at Masulipatnam (1605)
  • Primarily interested in spice trade

Major Dutch Centres

Surat
Chinsura
Pulicat (First Capital)
Nagapattinam (Later Capital)

Decline of Dutch Power

Defeated by English in Battle of Bedara (1759)

Gradually lost influence to British East India Company

Memory Trick

"Dutch got Destroyed at Bedara"

3. British (1600) – English East India Company

Arrival of British

  • English East India Company founded in 1600 (Charter by Queen Elizabeth I)
  • First factory in India at Surat (1613)
  • Permission granted by Mughal Emperor Jahangir
  • Ambassador Sir Thomas Roe (1615) secured trading rights

Important British Centres

Surat (First)
Madras (Fort St. George)
Bombay (Portuguese Gift)
Calcutta (Fort William)

Key British Governors & Their Achievements

Governor Key Achievement Year/Event
Robert Clive Victory in Battle of Plassey 1757 - Foundation of British rule
Warren Hastings First Governor General of Bengal 1773 - Regulating Act
Lord Wellesley Subsidiary Alliance System 1798-1805
Lord Dalhousie Doctrine of Lapse 1848-1856 - Railways & Telegraph

4. French (1664) – Last Major European Power

Arrival of French

  • French East India Company established in 1664
  • Main competitor to British in South India
  • Focused on trade and territorial expansion

Important French Centres

Pondicherry (Capital)
Mahe
Karaikal
Chandernagore

Famous French Governor: Joseph François Dupleix

  • Governor of French territories in India (1742-1754)
  • Fought British for control of South India
  • Master of diplomacy and political intrigue

His policies led to the Carnatic Wars between French and British

Anglo-French Wars (Carnatic Wars)

Three Carnatic Wars fought between French and British (1746-1763)

  • French initially successful under Dupleix
  • British ultimately victorious
  • Final defeat at Battle of Wandiwash (1760)

Memory Trick

"French Were Washed at Wandiwash"

British victory ended French ambitions in India

SSC MTS Practice Questions

1. Who was the first European to reach India by sea?

Vasco da Gama (Portuguese, 1498)

2. Where did Vasco da Gama first land in India?

Calicut (Kozhikode)

3. Which European power came to India first?

Portuguese

4. What was the capital of French India?

Pondicherry

5. Who defeated the French at the Battle of Wandiwash?

British (1760)

6. Where was the first English factory established in India?

Surat (1613)

7. Who granted permission to the British to trade in India?

Jahangir (Mughal Emperor)

8. Which Portuguese governor conquered Goa?

Albuquerque (1510)

Conclusion

The arrival of Europeans marks the beginning of colonial rule in India. Mastering this chapter guarantees 4–5 easy marks in SSC MTS, GD, and CHSL exams. Focus on remembering the order of arrival, key battles, important governors, and trading centers.

Exam Focus Areas

  • Order of European arrival (P→D→E→F)
  • First factories and capitals
  • Key battles and their years
  • Famous governors and their achievements
  • Trading company formation years
  • Contributions of Portuguese
