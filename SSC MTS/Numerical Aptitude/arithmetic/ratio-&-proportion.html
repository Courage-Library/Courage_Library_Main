Ratio & Proportion

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

Introduction

The chapter Ratio & Proportion is one of the most scoring topics in SSC MTS, CHSL, and other competitive exams. It forms the base for many other chapters like Partnership, Time & Work, Time & Distance, and Ages.

What is a Ratio?

A ratio is the comparison of two quantities of the same kind by division.

Ratio = First Quantity / Second Quantity

It is written as a : b, where a and b are quantities of the same unit.

Example

If the age of Ram is 10 years and Shyam is 15 years,

then ratio of their ages = 10 : 15 = 2 : 3

Key Properties of Ratios

1. Ratios have no units

Both quantities must have the same unit.

2. Multiplying or dividing both terms by the same number doesn't change the ratio

Example: 2 : 3 = 4 : 6 = 6 : 9

3. If a : b = c : d, then a, b, c, d are said to be in proportion

What is Proportion?

When two ratios are equal, we say they are in proportion.

a : b :: c : d or a/b = c/d

Here, b and c are called means, and a and d are called extremes.

Product of means = Product of extremes

b × c = a × d

Example

If 2 : 3 :: 8 : 12 → (2×12 = 3×8) → both equal 24 → proportional

Types of Proportion

(A) Direct Proportion

If increase in one quantity causes an increase in the other, or decrease causes decrease, then the two quantities are said to be in Direct Proportion.

x₁/x₂ = y₁/y₂

Examples:

  • More men → more work
  • More money → more goods

Example Question:

If ₹200 gives 5 pens, how many pens for ₹400?

5/? = 200/400 → ? = 10 pens

(B) Inverse Proportion

If increase in one quantity causes a decrease in the other (and vice versa), then they are in Inverse Proportion.

x₁ × y₁ = x₂ × y₂

Examples:

  • More workers → Less days to complete work
  • More speed → Less time to cover distance

Example Question:

If 6 men can complete a work in 15 days, how many days will 10 men take?

6 × 15 = 10 × x → x = (6×15)/10 = 9 days

Compound Ratio

When multiple ratios are multiplied together:

If a : b, b : c, and c : d, then Compound Ratio = a : d.

Example

a : b = 2 : 3, b : c = 4 : 5

→ Compound ratio = (2×4) : (3×5) = 8 : 15

Continued Proportion

If a : b = b : c, then a, b, c are in continued proportion.

b² = a × c

Example

If a : b = 2 : 4 and b : c = 4 : 8

→ a : b : c = 2 : 4 : 8

Important Formulas for Ratio & Proportion

Concept Formula Example
Ratio a : b = a/b 2 : 3
Proportion a : b :: c : d → a/b = c/d 4/8 = 1/2
Direct Proportion x₁/x₂ = y₁/y₂ Speed ↑ → Distance ↑
Inverse Proportion x₁×y₁ = x₂×y₂ Speed ↑ → Time ↓
Mean Proportion b² = a×c b = √(a×c)

Partnership

Definition

When two or more persons invest money in a business and share the profit or loss, it is called a Partnership.

Types of Partnership

(a) Simple Partnership

All partners invest their money for the same period of time.

Profit or loss is shared in the ratio of their capitals.

Profit Ratio = Capital Ratio

Example:

A invests ₹10,000 and B invests ₹20,000

→ Profit Ratio = 10,000 : 20,000 = 1 : 2

(b) Time Partnership

When capitals are invested for different time periods, profits are shared according to (Capital × Time).

Profit Ratio = (Capital × Time)

Example:

A invests ₹5,000 for 12 months and B invests ₹8,000 for 6 months.

→ Ratio = (5000×12) : (8000×6) = 60,000 : 48,000 = 5 : 4

(c) Sleeping Partner

A partner who invests capital but does not take part in day-to-day business activities.

Admission or Retirement of a Partner

If a new partner joins, he gets profit according to his investment and time. If a partner leaves, his profit share stops from that point.

Important Partnership Formulas

Case Formula
Profit Sharing Profit ∝ Capital × Time
Equal Time Profit ∝ Capital
Equal Capital Profit ∝ Time
Partner's share (Capital × Time) / Sum of all (Capital × Time) × Total Profit

Example Questions (SSC MTS Level)

1. Direct Proportion

If 3 pencils cost ₹9, find the cost of 5 pencils.

→ (3/5) = (9/x) → x = 15

2. Inverse Proportion

8 workers can finish a job in 12 days. How many days will 6 workers take?

→ 8×12 = 6×x → x = 16 days

3. Partnership (Simple)

A & B invest ₹12,000 and ₹8,000 respectively. Find profit ratio.

→ 12,000 : 8,000 = 3 : 2

4. Partnership (Time)

A invests ₹6,000 for 12 months and B invests ₹9,000 for 8 months.

→ (6000×12) : (9000×8) = 72,000 : 72,000 = 1 : 1

5. Profit Share

A & B invest ₹10,000 and ₹20,000 for 6 and 3 months respectively. Total profit = ₹9000

→ A : B = (10,000×6) : (20,000×3) = 60,000 : 60,000 = 1 : 1

→ Each gets ₹4500

Tips for SSC MTS Exam

  • Always check if it's Direct or Inverse proportion.
  • Multiply capital and time for partnership problems.
  • Simplify ratios fully (reduce to smallest numbers).
  • Convert all months/years into the same unit.
  • Memorize base formulas — it saves time in the exam.
