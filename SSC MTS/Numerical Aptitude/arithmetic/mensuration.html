Mensuration

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

Introduction

Mensuration is the branch of mathematics that deals with the measurement of shapes and figures — their length, area, and volume. It is divided into 2D (Two-Dimensional) and 3D (Three-Dimensional) figures.

1. 2D Figures (Plane Figures)

2D shapes have length and breadth but no height. They have area and perimeter.

A. Square

All four sides equal.

All angles = 90°

Side = a

Formulas

Quantity Formula
Perimeter 4a
Area a²
Diagonal a√2

Example

If side = 8 cm, Area = 8² = 64 cm², Perimeter = 32 cm

B. Rectangle

Opposite sides equal and parallel.

All angles = 90°

Length = l, Breadth = b

Formulas

Quantity Formula
Perimeter 2(l + b)
Area l × b
Diagonal √(l² + b²)

Example

l = 10, b = 6 → Area = 60 cm², Perimeter = 32 cm

C. Circle

Radius = r, Diameter = 2r

Formulas

Quantity Formula
Circumference 2πr
Area πr²
Semi-circle Area (1/2)πr²
Semi-circle Perimeter πr + 2r

Example

r = 7 cm → Area = 22/7 × 7² = 154 cm², Circumference = 44 cm

D. Triangle

Base = b, Height = h

Formulas

Quantity Formula
Area ½ × b × h
Perimeter Sum of all sides
Heron's Formula √[s(s – a)(s – b)(s – c)]
where s = (a + b + c)/2

Special Triangles

Type Property Area
Equilateral All sides equal (√3/4)a²
Isosceles Two sides equal ½ × base × height
Right-angled One 90° angle ½ × base × perpendicular

Example

Equilateral triangle side = 6 cm → Area = (√3/4) × 6² = 9√3 cm²

2. 3D Figures (Solid Figures)

3D shapes have length, breadth, and height. They have Curved Surface Area (CSA), Total Surface Area (TSA), and Volume.

A. Cube

All edges equal.

Side = a

Formulas

Quantity Formula
Lateral Surface Area (LSA) 4a²
Total Surface Area (TSA) 6a²
Volume a³
Diagonal a√3

Example

If a = 5 cm → TSA = 6×25 = 150 cm², Volume = 125 cm³

B. Cuboid

Length = l, Breadth = b, Height = h

Formulas

Quantity Formula
LSA 2h(l + b)
TSA 2(lb + bh + hl)
Volume l × b × h
Diagonal √(l² + b² + h²)

Example

l = 10, b = 6, h = 4 → Volume = 240 cm³, TSA = 2(60 + 24 + 40) = 248 cm²

C. Cylinder

Radius = r, Height = h

Formulas

Quantity Formula
CSA 2πrh
TSA 2πr(h + r)
Volume πr²h

Example

r = 7 cm, h = 10 cm → Volume = 22/7 × 7² × 10 = 1540 cm³

D. Cone

Radius = r, Height = h

Slant height = l = √(r² + h²)

Formulas

Quantity Formula
CSA πrl
TSA πr(l + r)
Volume (1/3)πr²h

Example

r = 3 cm, h = 4 cm → l = 5

CSA = 3.14 × 3 × 5 = 47.1 cm²

Volume = 1/3 × 3.14 × 9 × 4 = 37.68 cm³

E. Sphere

Radius = r

Formulas

Quantity Formula
Surface Area 4πr²
Volume (4/3)πr³

Example

r = 7 cm → Surface Area = 4 × 22/7 × 49 = 616 cm²

Volume = 4/3 × 22/7 × 343 = 1436.7 cm³

F. Hemisphere

Half of a sphere.

Formulas

Quantity Formula
CSA 2πr²
TSA 3πr²
Volume (2/3)πr³

Important Relationships

Figure Relation
1 litre = 1000 cm³
1 m³ = 1000 litres
Volume ratio of cube and sphere of same radius 6 : 4π
Volume ratio of cone and cylinder of same base & height 1 : 3

Key Tips for SSC MTS

  • Always keep units same (cm, m, etc.).
  • For curved figures → focus on π (3.14 or 22/7).
  • For solids, volume = base area × height (general rule).
  • Convert volume ↔ capacity if required (1 litre = 1000 cm³).
  • Remember TSA = LSA + area of both ends.

You've completed Mensuration!

Courage Tip: Practice mensuration problems regularly, focusing on formula application and unit conversions. Remember the relationships between different 3D shapes and practice volume-capacity conversions. Visualize the shapes to better understand surface area calculations.
