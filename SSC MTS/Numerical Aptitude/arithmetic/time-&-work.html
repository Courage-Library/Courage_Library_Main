Time & Work

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

Introduction

The Time and Work chapter is one of the most important topics in SSC exams. It deals with finding how long it takes to complete a work individually or together, based on their efficiency or work rates.

1. Basic Concepts

Work

The amount of job or task to be completed

Time

Number of days/hours to complete work

Efficiency

Work done in one day/hour

Relation Between Work, Time & Efficiency:

Work = Time × Efficiency

Time = Work / Efficiency

Efficiency = Work / Time

Example 1:

A can do a piece of work in 10 days.

A's 1-day work = 1/10

That means A completes 1/10th of the work each day.

2. Combined Work

If A can do a work in x days and B in y days, then together they can complete the work in:

(x × y) / (x + y) days

and 1-day work together = 1/x + 1/y

Example 2:

A can do a work in 10 days and B in 15 days.

A's 1-day work = 1/10

B's 1-day work = 1/15

Together = (1/10 + 1/15) = (3 + 2)/30 = 5/30 = 1/6

They can finish in 6 days

Example 3 (Alternate Work):

A and B can do a work in 12 and 16 days respectively. They work alternately starting with A.

LCM = 48 (Total work)

A's 1-day work = 4, B's 1-day work = 3

Work done in 2 days = 7

48 ÷ 7 = 6 complete pairs (12 days → 42 work done)

Left work = 6 → A will do next (needs 1.5 days)

Total = 13.5 days

3. Work Efficiency

Efficiency ∝ 1/Time

If A : B efficiency = 3 : 2, Then Time ratio = 2 : 3

Example 4:

A is twice as efficient as B.

If A can finish in 10 days

B will take = (10 × 2) = 20 days

Example 5:

A and B can do a work in 12 and 15 days respectively.

Efficiency ratio = 15 : 12 = 5 : 4

A : B = 5 : 4

Example 6:

A is 50% more efficient than B.

Efficiency ratio = 3 : 2

Time ratio = 2 : 3

If B takes 18 days, A = 18 × (2/3) = 12 days

Work and Wages

Example 7:

A can do a piece of work in 10 days and B in 15 days. They earn ₹600 together.

Work done ratio = 1/10 : 1/15 = 3 : 2

So A gets ₹360 and B gets ₹240

Answer: A = ₹360, B = ₹240

4. Pipes & Cisterns

Inlet Pipe

Fills the tank (positive work)

Outlet Pipe

Empties the tank (negative work)

Example 8:

A pipe fills a tank in 12 hours, another empties it in 20 hours.

Time to fill = (12×20)/(20–12) = 240/8 = 30 hours

Example 9:

Two pipes fill a tank in 15 and 20 hours respectively.

Together = (15×20)/(15+20) = 300/35 = 8.57 hours

≈ 8 hrs 34 min

Example 10:

A pipe can fill a tank in 10 hours, outlet can empty it in 15 hours. If both are opened, how much part will be filled in 5 hours?

1 hour work = (1/10 – 1/15) = (3–2)/30 = 1/30

So in 5 hours = 5/30 = 1/6 of the tank

Example 11 (Multiple Pipes):

Three pipes A, B, and C can fill a tank in 12, 15, and 20 hours respectively. All opened together.

1 hour work = 1/12 + 1/15 + 1/20

= (5 + 4 + 3)/60 = 12/60 = 1/5

Time = 5 hours

6. Key Shortcuts & Tricks

If A is x% more efficient than B, Time ratio = 100 : (100 + x)

If A is x% less efficient than B, Time ratio = (100 + x) : 100

Work done ∝ Efficiency × Time

For alternate work questions, always find LCM to get total work

7. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Always treat filling as positive work, emptying as negative work

Don't forget to take LCM of days when combining workers

If a pipe or person works for part of the time, adjust accordingly

Never mix efficiency ratios with time ratios incorrectly

Practice Questions

1. A can complete a work in 15 days and B in 20 days. In how many days will they complete the work together?

2. A pipe can fill a tank in 6 hours and another can empty it in 8 hours. If both are opened, how long will it take to fill the tank?

3. A is 25% more efficient than B. If B can complete a work in 20 days, how long will A take?

4. Three workers A, B, C can complete a work in 10, 12, and 15 days respectively. They work together for 2 days, then C leaves. How long will A and B take to complete the remaining work?

5. A and B working together can complete a work in 8 days. If A alone can do it in 12 days, how long will B take alone?
