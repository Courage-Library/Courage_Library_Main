Time, Speed & Distance

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

1. Basic Concept

Time, Speed, and Distance are fundamentally related quantities used in daily life and competitive exams.

Relationship Formula

Speed = Distance / Time

Time = Distance / Speed

Distance = Speed × Time

Unit Conversion

1 km = 1000 m

1 hour = 60 minutes = 3600 seconds

1 km/hr = (5/18) m/s

1 m/s = (18/5) km/hr

Example 1

If a car travels 90 km in 3 hours,

then Speed = 90 / 3 = 30 km/hr

Example 2

If a train moves at 54 km/hr, find its speed in m/s.

54 × (5/18) = 15 m/s

Answer: 15 m/s

Concept of Average Speed

General Formula

Average Speed = Total Distance / Total Time

Special Case: Same Distance at Two Speeds

Average Speed = (2 × x × y) / (x + y)

Example 3

A man travels to a place at 40 km/h and returns at 60 km/h.

Average speed = (2 × 40 × 60) / (40 + 60) = 48 km/h

Answer: 48 km/h

2. Relative Speed

When two objects move in the same or opposite direction, their relative speed helps in finding how fast they approach or separate.

Relative Speed Formulas

Motion Type Relative Speed
Same direction (A – B) km/hr
Opposite direction (A + B) km/hr

Example 4

Two trains move in opposite directions at 40 km/h and 60 km/h.

Relative speed = 40 + 60 = 100 km/h

Answer: 100 km/h

Example 5

Two people run on a circular track of 400 m at speeds 6 m/s and 4 m/s in the same direction.

Relative speed = 6 – 4 = 2 m/s

Time to meet = 400 / 2 = 200 seconds

Answer: 200 seconds

3. Boats & Streams

When a boat moves in a river, its effective speed changes depending on the direction of flow.

Basic Formulas

Downstream Speed = B + S

Upstream Speed = B - S

Example 6

Boat speed = 10 km/h, Stream speed = 2 km/h

Downstream = 12 km/h, Upstream = 8 km/h

Finding Boat and Stream Speeds

B = (Downstream + Upstream) / 2

S = (Downstream - Upstream) / 2

Example 7

Downstream = 15 km/h, Upstream = 5 km/h

B = (15 + 5)/2 = 10 km/h

S = (15 - 5)/2 = 5 km/h

Answer: B=10 km/h, S=5 km/h

Example 8

A boat goes 24 km downstream in 3 hours and returns in 4 hours.

Downstream = 24/3 = 8 km/h

Upstream = 24/4 = 6 km/h

B = (8 + 6)/2 = 7 km/h

S = (8 - 6)/2 = 1 km/h

Answer: B=7 km/h, S=1 km/h

4. Trains

Train problems are based on relative speed and length concepts.

Key Formulas

Time to pass a pole = Length of Train / Speed

Time to pass a platform = (Train Length + Platform Length) / Speed

Example 9

A train 120 m long passes a pole in 6 seconds. Find its speed.

Speed = 120 / 6 = 20 m/s

= 20 × (18/5) = 72 km/h

Answer: 72 km/h

Example 10

A train 180 m long passes a platform 120 m long in 12 seconds.

Speed = (180 + 120) / 12 = 25 m/s

= 25 × (18/5) = 90 km/h

Answer: 90 km/h

When Two Trains Cross Each Other

Opposite direction: Relative speed = (A + B)

Same direction: Relative speed = |A - B|

Time = Sum of lengths / Relative speed

Example 11

Two trains 100 m and 120 m long move in opposite directions at 54 km/h and 36 km/h.

Relative speed = 54 + 36 = 90 km/h = 25 m/s

Time = (100 + 120) / 25 = 8.8 seconds

Answer: 8.8 seconds

Example 12

Two trains of equal length cross each other in 12 seconds at speed 45 km/h and 63 km/h in opposite directions.

Relative speed = 108 km/h = 30 m/s

Let each train length = x

2x / 30 = 12 → x = 180 m

Answer: 180 m

5. Key Tips for SSC MTS

  • Convert all speeds to same units before solving.
  • For meeting or crossing, use relative speed.
  • For average speed, always use total distance ÷ total time.
  • Boats & streams are direct formula-based; avoid confusion.
  • In train problems, use length + relative speed properly.

Courage Tip: Practice time, speed and distance problems regularly, focusing on unit conversions and identifying the right formula for each scenario. For relative speed problems, always determine whether objects are moving in the same or opposite directions first.
