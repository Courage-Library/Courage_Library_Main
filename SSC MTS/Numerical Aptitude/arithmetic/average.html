Average

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

Definition

An average is the value that represents a group of numbers by their central or middle value. It shows equal distribution of total quantity among all members.

Basic Formula:

Average = Sum of all quantities / Number of quantities

Example 1:

Find the average of 5, 10, 15, 20, and 25.

Average = (5 + 10 + 15 + 20 + 25) / 5 = 75 / 5 = 15

Answer = 15

Important Points

If all the numbers are equal, their average is the same number.

Addition/Subtraction Effect:

If the same number is added or subtracted from all terms, the average changes by the same number.

Example: Average of 10, 20, 30 is 20.

Add 5 to each → 15, 25, 35 → New average = 25.

Multiplication/Division Effect:

If all the terms are multiplied or divided by the same number, the average also changes accordingly.

Multiply by 2 → Average × 2

Divide by 2 → Average ÷ 2

Special Averages:

First n natural numbers

(n + 1) / 2

First n even numbers

(n + 1)

First n odd numbers

n

Weighted Average

When different quantities carry different importance or weights, we use weighted average instead of simple average.

Formula:

Weighted Average = ∑(wᵢ × xᵢ) / ∑wᵢ

where xᵢ = value of each item, wᵢ = corresponding weight

Example 2:

Find the average marks of a student who scored:

Maths

80 (weight 3)

Science

70 (weight 2)

English

60 (weight 1)

Weighted Average = (80×3 + 70×2 + 60×1) / (3+2+1)

= (240 + 140 + 60) / 6 = 440 / 6 = 73.33

Weighted average = 73.33

Shortcut Example:

When two items are mixed in the ratio a : b, their average value = (ax₁ + bx₂) / (a + b)

Example 3:

The average age of 25 men is 30 years and of 15 women is 20 years. Find the average age of the group.

Average age = (25×30 + 15×20) / (25+15)

= (750 + 300) / 40 = 1050 / 40 = 26.25

Average age = 26.25 years

Missing Term Problems

These are problems where one or more values are missing, but the average and other values are given.

Type 1: Finding Missing Value

If average of n numbers is A, then Sum of all numbers = A × n

Hence, Missing number = Required Sum − Sum of known numbers

Example 4:

Average of 5 numbers is 40. Four numbers are 45, 35, 25, 55. Find the missing number.

Sum of all = 40 × 5 = 200

Sum of known = 45 + 35 + 25 + 55 = 160

Missing number = 200 – 160 = 40

Answer: 40

Type 2: When One Term is Replaced

(New average − Old average) × Number of terms = New term − Old term

Example 5:

The average of 10 numbers is 40. If one number 60 is replaced by 30, find the new average.

Change = (New - Old) ÷ n = (30 - 60) ÷ 10 = -30 ÷ 10 = -3

New average = 40 - 3 = 37

Answer: New average = 37

Example 6:

The average of 5 consecutive even numbers is 20. Find the largest number.

Let numbers be: (20 - 4), (20 - 2), 20, (20 + 2), (20 + 4)

Largest number = 24

Answer: 24

Key Tips for SSC MTS

Convert every "average" question into a sum-based equation

For mixture or group questions, use weighted average

For missing term problems, use the sum of n × average concept

For consecutive numbers, middle term = average

Quick Revision Formulas:

Basic Average

Sum / Number of terms

Weighted Average

∑(wᵢ × xᵢ) / ∑wᵢ

Missing Number

(n × Average) - Sum of known

Practice Questions

1. The average of 6 numbers is 25. If one number is excluded, the average becomes 20. Find the excluded number.

2. The average weight of 10 students is 45 kg. When a new student joins, the average becomes 46 kg. Find the weight of the new student.

3. Find the average of first 15 natural numbers.

4. The average of 8 numbers is 12. If each number is multiplied by 3, find the new average.

5. In a class of 30 students, average marks are 60. If 5 students with average 40 leave, find the new average.
