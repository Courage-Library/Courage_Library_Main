Data Interpretation (DI)

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

Introduction

Data Interpretation (DI) is one of the most scoring topics in SSC MTS and other competitive exams. It tests your ability to understand, analyze, and interpret data given in various forms — such as Tables, Bar Graphs, and Line Graphs.

1. Basic Concepts

What is Data Interpretation?

Data Interpretation means extracting meaningful information from given numerical data and answering questions based on it.

You must:

  • Read data carefully
  • Understand units and categories
  • Use basic arithmetic (%, ratio, average, etc.)
  • Apply logical reasoning

2. Types of Data Presentation

A. Tables

Data is presented in rows and columns — similar to a spreadsheet.

Example:

Year Company A (in ₹ Lakhs) Company B (in ₹ Lakhs)
2019 120 100
2020 150 120
2021 180 160
2022 210 190

Questions Based on the Table:

1. What is the total income of Company A from 2019 to 2022?

= 120 + 150 + 180 + 210 = 660 lakhs

2. In which year did Company B earn maximum income?

→ 2022 (₹190 lakhs)

3. What is the ratio of total income of A to B over the years?

A : B = 660 : (100 + 120 + 160 + 190 = 570)

= 660 : 570 = 22 : 19

4. What is the percentage increase in A's income from 2019 to 2022?

= ((210 – 120)/120) × 100 = 75%

Tips for Table DI:

  • Add or compare row/column values quickly.
  • Focus on difference, ratio, and percentage change.
  • Avoid calculation mistakes — approximate where possible.

B. Bar Graphs

Bar graphs represent data using rectangular bars — either vertical or horizontal. The length of the bar represents the value of the quantity.

Example:

The bar graph shows the number of students (in hundreds) enrolled in different courses of a college.

Course Students (in hundreds)
Science 6
Arts 4
Commerce 5
Law 3

Questions:

1. Total students

= (6 + 4 + 5 + 3) × 100 = 1800 students

2. Ratio of Science to Arts

= 6 : 4 = 3 : 2

3. Percentage of Commerce students

= (5/18) × 100 = 27.78%

Tips for Bar Graphs:

  • Identify what each axis represents (x = category, y = value).
  • Note the scale carefully (e.g. 1 bar = 100 students).
  • For comparison questions, observe bar height differences.

C. Line Graphs

A line graph represents data points connected by lines. It shows trends or changes over time — e.g. increase or decrease in production, sales, population, etc.

Example:

The line graph shows the production (in tons) of a factory from 2018 to 2022.

Year Production (tons)
2018 200
2019 240
2020 300
2021 270
2022 330

Questions:

1. In which year was the production highest?

→ 2022 (330 tons)

2. What was the percentage increase from 2018 to 2020?

= ((300 – 200)/200) × 100 = 50%

3. What is the average production for 5 years?

= (200 + 240 + 300 + 270 + 330) / 5 = 268 tons

4. In which year did the production fall compared to the previous year?

→ 2021 (from 300 → 270)

Tips for Line Graphs:

  • Observe upward (increase) or downward (decrease) trends.
  • Use slope comparison for quick difference estimation.
  • Focus on points where lines intersect — often used in questions.

3. Common Calculations Used in DI

Concept Formula
Percentage (Value / Total) × 100
Ratio Value₁ : Value₂
Average Sum of Values / Number of Values
Difference Larger – Smaller
Growth Rate ((New – Old) / Old) × 100

4. Key Tricks to Score in SSC MTS

  • Read question carefully before calculating.
  • Round off large numbers for approximation.
  • Focus on percentage & ratio comparisons.
  • If a question seems lengthy — skip temporarily and revisit later.
  • Always verify units (e.g. ₹, %, 1000s, Lakhs).

You've completed Data Interpretation!

Courage Tip: Practice different types of DI questions regularly, focusing on quick calculations and pattern recognition. Always start by understanding the data structure and units before attempting calculations. Time management is crucial in DI sections.
