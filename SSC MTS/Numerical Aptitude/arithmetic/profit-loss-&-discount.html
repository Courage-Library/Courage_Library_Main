Profit, Loss & Discount

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

Basic Concepts

When a shopkeeper buys and sells goods, he either gains or loses money. These concepts are based on Cost Price (C.P.), Selling Price (S.P.), and Marked Price (M.P.).

1. Important Terms

(i) Cost Price (C.P.)

The price at which an article is purchased.

(ii) Selling Price (S.P.)

The price at which an article is sold.

(iii) Marked Price (M.P.)

The price tagged or printed on the article before discount.

(iv) Discount

Reduction given on the Marked Price (M.P.).

(v) Profit (Gain)

When S.P. > C.P.

Profit = S.P. − C.P.

(vi) Loss

When S.P. < C.P.

Loss = C.P. − S.P.

2. Formulas

Concept Formula
Profit % (Profit / C.P.) × 100
Loss % (Loss / C.P.) × 100
S.P. (for profit) C.P. × (1 + Profit %/100)
S.P. (for loss) C.P. × (1 - Loss %/100)
C.P. (for profit) (S.P. × 100) / (100 + Profit %)
C.P. (for loss) (S.P. × 100) / (100 - Loss %)

3. Relation Between M.P., Discount & S.P.

S.P. = M.P. - Discount

or

S.P. = M.P. × (1 - Discount %/100)

For Successive Discount:

Net Discount % = A + B - (A × B)/100

where A and B are two discount percentages

4. Important Points

Profit or loss is always calculated on C.P.

Discount is always calculated on M.P.

If there is both discount and profit, first find S.P., then compare it with C.P.

If an article is sold at x% gain and another at x% loss, there is always a net loss.

Net Loss % = x²/100

For three successive discounts A%, B%, C%:

Net Discount % = A + B + C - (AB + BC + CA)/100 + ABC/10000

5. Examples

Example 1:

C.P. = ₹500, S.P. = ₹600

Profit = 600 - 500 = ₹100

Profit % = (100 / 500) × 100 = 20%

Answer: Profit 20%

Example 2:

C.P. = ₹1,200, S.P. = ₹960

Loss = 1,200 - 960 = ₹240

Loss % = (240 / 1,200) × 100 = 20%

Answer: Loss 20%

Example 3 (Finding S.P. for Profit):

C.P. = ₹800, Profit = 25%

S.P. = 800 × (1 + 25/100) = 800 × 1.25 = ₹1,000

Answer: S.P. = ₹1,000

Example 4 (Finding C.P. for Profit):

S.P. = ₹840, Profit = 20%

C.P. = (840 × 100) / (100 + 20) = 84000 / 120 = ₹700

Answer: C.P. = ₹700

Example 5 (Discount):

M.P. = ₹1,000, Discount = 20%

S.P. = 1,000 × (1 - 20/100) = ₹800

Answer: S.P. = ₹800

Example 6 (Successive Discounts):

Two successive discounts of 20% and 10% are given on M.P. ₹1,000.

Net Discount % = 20 + 10 - (20×10)/100 = 30 - 2 = 28%

So, S.P. = 1,000 × (1 - 28/100) = ₹720

Answer: S.P. = ₹720

Example 7 (Gain after Discount):

A shopkeeper marks goods 40% above C.P. and gives 20% discount. Find his gain %.

Let C.P. = ₹100

Then M.P. = ₹140

Discount = 20% of 140 = ₹28

S.P. = 140 - 28 = ₹112

Profit = 112 - 100 = ₹12

Profit % = (12 / 100) × 100 = 12%

Answer: 12% Profit

Example 8 (Net Loss):

A sells a phone at 10% profit and B sells it to C at 10% loss. Find overall gain or loss %.

Net effect % = (10 - 10 - (10×10)/100) = -1%

Answer: 1% Loss

6. Successive Discounts Shortcut

If three discounts are A%, B%, and C%, then:

Net Discount % = A + B + C - (AB + BC + CA)/100 + ABC/10000

7. Key Tips for SSC MTS

Always compare S.P. and C.P. to decide profit or loss

Never confuse discount % (on M.P.) with profit % (on C.P.)

Use net discount formula for back-to-back discounts

For gain/loss chain transactions, apply percentage change formula:

Net % Change = A + B + (A × B)/100

Practice Questions

1. A shopkeeper buys an article for ₹400 and sells it for ₹500. Find his profit percentage.

2. If the marked price is ₹800 and discount is 25%, find the selling price.

3. Two successive discounts of 10% and 20% are given on an article. Find the net discount percentage.

4. A sells to B at 20% profit and B sells to C at 10% profit. Find the overall profit percentage.

5. An article marked 50% above cost price is sold at 20% discount. Find the actual profit percentage.
