Simple & Compound Interest

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

Introduction

Money lent or borrowed for a certain time is called Principal (P). The extra amount paid or received for using that money is called Interest (I). The rate at which interest is charged per year on the principal is called Rate (R), and the period of time is called Time (T).

1. Simple Interest (S.I.)

Definition

When interest is calculated only on the principal, it is called Simple Interest (S.I.).

Formula

S.I. = (P × R × T) / 100

Amount (A) = P + S.I.

Example 1

Find the S.I. on ₹5,000 for 3 years at 10% per annum.

Solution:

S.I. = (5000 × 10 × 3) / 100 = 1500

Answer: ₹1,500

Amount = 5000 + 1500 = 6500

Example 2

In how many years will ₹2,000 become ₹2,800 at 10% per annum?

Solution:

S.I. = 2800 - 2000 = 800

T = (100 × 800) / (2000 × 10) = 4 years

Answer: 4 years

2. Compound Interest (C.I.)

Definition

When interest is calculated on both principal and the accumulated interest, it is called Compound Interest.

Formula (Annual Compounding)

A = P(1 + R/100)T

C.I. = A - P

Example 3

Find the compound interest on ₹5,000 for 2 years at 10% per annum.

Solution:

A = 5000(1 + 10/100)2 = 5000 × 1.12 = 5000 × 1.21 = 6050

C.I. = 6050 - 5000 = 1050

Answer: ₹1,050

Formula for Different Time Periods

Half-Yearly:

A = P(1 + R/(2×100))2T

Quarterly:

A = P(1 + R/(4×100))4T

Example 4 (Half-Yearly)

Find C.I. on ₹8,000 for 1 year at 10% p.a. compounded half-yearly.

Solution:

A = 8000(1 + 10/(2×100))2×1 = 8000(1.05)2 = 8000 × 1.1025 = 8820

C.I. = 8820 - 8000 = 820

Answer: ₹820

3. Difference Between S.I. and C.I.

Basis Simple Interest Compound Interest
Calculation Only on Principal On Principal + Interest
Formula (P × R × T)/100 P(1 + R/100)T - P
Growth Linear Exponential
Equal Interest Each Year Yes No
Amount P + S.I. P + C.I.

Example 5 (Difference between S.I. & C.I.)

Find difference between S.I. and C.I. on ₹10,000 for 2 years at 10% p.a.

Solution:

S.I. = (10000 × 10 × 2)/100 = 2000

C.I. = 10000(1 + 10/100)2 - 10000 = 10000(1.21 - 1) = 2100

Difference = 2100 - 2000 = 100

Answer: ₹100

Shortcut for Difference (for 2 years)

Difference = P × (R/100)2

Useful for small time periods.

4. Installment-Based Problems

When money is borrowed and repaid through equal installments, each installment includes part of principal + interest.

Formula (When C.I. is charged on Remaining Amount)

P = x[((1 + R/100)n - 1) / ((R/100)(1 + R/100)n)]

x = P × [(R/100)(1 + R/100)n / ((1 + R/100)n - 1)]

Example 6

A man borrows ₹9,600 at 10% p.a. compounded annually and agrees to pay in 3 equal yearly installments. Find the installment amount.

Solution:

x = 9600 × [0.10 × (1.10)3 / ((1.10)3 - 1)]

x = 9600 × [0.1331 / 0.331] = 9600 × 0.402 = ₹3,859.2

Answer: ₹3,859.20 per year

Example 7 (Practical Case)

A person takes a loan of ₹20,000 at 8% interest p.a. compounded annually to be repaid in 2 equal installments. Find each installment.

Solution:

x = 20000 × [0.08 × (1.08)2 / ((1.08)2 - 1)]

x = 20000 × [0.09312 / 0.1664] = 20000 × 0.5595 = ₹11,190

Answer: ₹11,190

5. Key Points for SSC MTS

  • Always check if interest is simple or compound before applying the formula.
  • For short terms (1–2 years), the difference between C.I. and S.I. is very small.
  • If compounding is half-yearly, divide R by 2 and multiply T by 2.
  • Installment-based questions always apply C.I. logic, not S.I.
  • For 2 years difference: Difference = P × (R/100)2

Courage Tip: Practice interest problems regularly, focusing on identifying whether to use simple or compound interest formulas. For installment problems, remember the specific formulas and always verify if compounding is annual, half-yearly, or quarterly.
