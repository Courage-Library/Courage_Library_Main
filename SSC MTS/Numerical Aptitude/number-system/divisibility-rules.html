Divisibility Rules

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

Introduction

Divisibility rules help you quickly check whether one number is divisible by another without actual division. They are extremely useful for simplification, factorization, HCF-LCM, and number system questions in SSC MTS and other competitive exams.

Basic Idea

If a number divides another number exactly (i.e., without leaving any remainder), then the second number is said to be divisible by the first.

Divisible Example

36 ÷ 9 = 4 (no remainder)

36 is divisible by 9

Not Divisible Example

37 ÷ 9 = 4 remainder 1

37 is not divisible by 9

Divisibility Rules (1 to 10)

1. Divisibility by 1

Every number is divisible by 1.

Example: 65 ÷ 1 = 65

2. Divisibility by 2

A number is divisible by 2 if its last digit is even (0, 2, 4, 6, 8).

Example: 124 → last digit 4 → divisible by 2

Example: 237 → last digit 7 → not divisible

3. Divisibility by 3

If the sum of all digits of a number is divisible by 3, then the number is divisible by 3.

Example: 243 → 2 + 4 + 3 = 9 → 9 ÷ 3 = 3 → divisible

Example: 247 → 2 + 4 + 7 = 13 → not divisible

4. Divisibility by 4

A number is divisible by 4 if the last two digits form a number divisible by 4.

Example: 3,416 → last two digits = 16 → 16 ÷ 4 = 4 → divisible

Example: 8,513 → last two digits = 13 → not divisible

5. Divisibility by 5

A number is divisible by 5 if its last digit is 0 or 5.

Example: 105 → ends with 5 → divisible

Example: 123 → ends with 3 → not divisible

6. Divisibility by 6

A number is divisible by 6 if it is divisible by both 2 and 3.

Example: 132 → even, sum of digits = 6 → divisible by 3 → divisible

Example: 135 → sum of digits = 9 → divisible by 3 but not even → not divisible

7. Divisibility by 7

Multiply the last digit by 2 and subtract it from the remaining number. If the result is divisible by 7 (including 0), then the number is divisible by 7.

Example: 203 → last digit = 3 → 20 − (3×2) = 20 − 6 = 14 → divisible by 7

Example: 206 → last digit = 6 → 20 − 12 = 8 → not divisible

8. Divisibility by 8

A number is divisible by 8 if the last three digits form a number divisible by 8.

Example: 10,624 → last three digits = 624 → 624 ÷ 8 = 78 → divisible

Example: 12,362 → 362 ÷ 8 = 45.25 → not divisible

9. Divisibility by 9

If the sum of all digits is divisible by 9, then the number is divisible by 9.

Example: 4,131 → 4+1+3+1=9 → divisible

Example: 1,456 → 1+4+5+6=16 → not divisible

10. Divisibility by 10

A number is divisible by 10 if its last digit is 0.

Example: 540 → ends with 0 → divisible

Example: 245 → ends with 5 → not divisible

Divisibility Rules (11 to 20)

11. Divisibility by 11

If the difference between the sum of digits at odd and even places is 0 or divisible by 11, then the number is divisible by 11.

Example: 2,035 → (2+3) − (0+5) = 5 − 5 = 0 → divisible

Example: 4,715 → (4+1) − (7+5) = 5 − 12 = -7 → not divisible

12. Divisibility by 12

A number is divisible by 12 if it is divisible by both 3 and 4.

Example: 3,312 → last two digits = 12 → divisible by 4, sum of digits = 9 → divisible by 3 → divisible by 12

13. Divisibility by 13

Multiply the last digit by 9 and add it to the remaining number. If the result is divisible by 13, then the original number is divisible by 13.

Example: 351 → last digit 1 × 9 = 9 → 35 + 9 = 44 → 44 ÷ 13 = 3.38 → not divisible

Example: 1,053 → last digit 3 × 9 = 27 → 105 + 27 = 132 → 132 ÷ 13 = 10.15 → divisible

14. Divisibility by 14

A number is divisible by 14 if it is divisible by both 2 and 7.

Example: 196 → even, 196 ÷ 7 = 28 → divisible by 14

Example: 105 → not even → not divisible

15. Divisibility by 15

A number is divisible by 15 if it is divisible by both 3 and 5.

Example: 450 → ends with 0 → divisible by 5, sum = 9 → divisible by 3 → divisible by 15

16. Divisibility by 16

If the last four digits form a number divisible by 16, the whole number is divisible by 16.

Example: 65,552 → last four digits = 5552 → 5552 ÷ 16 = 347 → divisible

17. Divisibility by 17

Multiply the last digit by 5, then subtract it from the remaining number. If the result is divisible by 17, then the number is divisible by 17.

Example: 221 → 22 − (1×5) = 17 → divisible

18. Divisibility by 18

A number is divisible by 18 if it is divisible by both 2 and 9.

Example: 234 → even, sum = 9 → divisible by 9 → divisible by 18

19. Divisibility by 19

Multiply the last digit by 2, add it to the rest of the number. If result divisible by 19, then the number is divisible.

Example: 1,026 → last digit 6 × 2 = 12 → 102 + 12 = 114 → 114 ÷ 19 = 6 → divisible

20. Divisibility by 20

If the last digit is 0 and the tens digit is even, the number is divisible by 20. (Or simply: last two digits must be 00, 20, 40, 60, or 80.)

Example: 1,340 → last two digits = 40 → divisible

Example: 2,160 → last two digits = 60 → divisible

Divisibility Rules (21 to 25)

21. Divisibility by 21

A number is divisible by 21 if it is divisible by both 3 and 7.

Example: 1,323 → sum of digits = 9 → divisible by 3, last digit 3×2=6 → 132−6=126 → divisible by 7 → divisible by 21

22. Divisibility by 22

A number is divisible by 22 if it is divisible by both 2 and 11.

Example: 1,254 → even, (1+5) − (2+4) = 6 − 6 = 0 → divisible by 11 → divisible by 22

23. Divisibility by 23

Multiply the last digit by 7, add it to the rest. If result divisible by 23 → original number is divisible.

Example: 2,530 → last digit 0×7=0 → 253+0=253 → 253÷23=11 → divisible

24. Divisibility by 24

A number is divisible by 24 if it is divisible by both 3 and 8.

Example: 9,312 → last three digits = 312 ÷ 8 = 39, sum of digits = 15 ÷ 3 = 5 → divisible by 24

25. Divisibility by 25

A number is divisible by 25 if its last two digits are 00, 25, 50, or 75.

Example: 325 → ends with 25 → divisible

Example: 785 → ends with 85 → not divisible

You've completed Divisibility Rules!

Courage Tip: Practice divisibility rules regularly, focusing on the most commonly used ones (2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11). Remember that composite numbers can be broken down into their prime factors for easier divisibility checks.
