Simplification & BODMAS

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

1. Introduction

In SSC MTS and other government exams, Simplification questions test your calculation speed, accuracy, and understanding of basic arithmetic rules.

To solve these quickly and correctly, you must follow the BODMAS rule and know how to handle fractions, decimals, roots, powers, and operations efficiently.

Let's go step by step.

2. What is Simplification?

Simplification means reducing a mathematical expression into its simplest form using basic operations like Addition (+), Subtraction (−), Multiplication (×), and Division (÷).

Example:

Simplify → 5 + 3 × 2 = ?

As per rule, multiply first → 3 × 2 = 6

Then add → 5 + 6 = 11

Answer = 11

3. The BODMAS Rule

The BODMAS rule tells the correct order of mathematical operations. Each letter stands for a specific operation:

Letter Meaning Operation
B Brackets ( ), { }, [ ]
O Orders Powers, Roots (like ², √)
D Division ÷
M Multiplication ×
A Addition +
S Subtraction −

Rule: Always solve in the order B → O → D → M → A → S

4. Example of BODMAS

Example 1:

Simplify: 8 + (6 ÷ 2 × 3) – 4

Inside brackets first: 6 ÷ 2 = 3

Then × 3 = 9

Expression becomes: 8 + 9 – 4 = 13

Answer = 13

Example 2:

Simplify: 25 – [5 × (4 – 2)]

Inside inner bracket: (4 – 2) = 2

Then multiply: 5 × 2 = 10

Finally: 25 – 10 = 15

Answer = 15

5. Types of Brackets

There are three types of brackets used in simplification:

( ) – Round Brackets

Solve first

{ } – Curly Brackets

Solve second

[ ] – Square Brackets

Solve third

Example:

Simplify: [8 – {3 + (6 – 2)}]

Inner ( ) → (6 – 2) = 4

{3 + 4} = 7

[8 – 7] = 1

Answer = 1

6. Common Operations Used in Simplification

(a) Fractions

When simplifying fractions, find the LCM of denominators to add or subtract.

Example: Simplify: ½ + ⅓ = ?

LCM of 2 and 3 = 6

(3 + 2)/6 = 5/6

(b) Decimals

Align decimal points properly or convert into fractions for accuracy.

Example: Simplify: 1.5 + 2.35 + 0.15 = 4.00

(c) Powers and Roots

2³ = 8

√49 = 7

(3²)² = 3²×² = 3⁴ = 81

Example: Simplify: (2³ + 3²) ÷ 5 = (8 + 9) ÷ 5 = 17 ÷ 5 = 3.4

(d) Percentages

Convert percentages to fractions or decimals.

20% = 1/5

50% = ½

75% = ¾

Example: Simplify: 50% of 60 + 25% of 40

(½ × 60) + (¼ × 40) = 30 + 10 = 40

(e) Reciprocals

If x = ½, then reciprocal = 2/1 = 2

7. Short Tricks for Simplification

Shortcut Explanation
Multiply before Add/Subtract Always apply BODMAS order
Use LCM for Fractions Common base simplifies faster
Convert Percent to Fraction Makes calculation simpler
Remove Brackets step by step Inner to outer
Check signs carefully (+/−) Common mistake area

8. Important Concept: Approximation (for Speed)

In SSC MTS, some questions are designed for fast estimation rather than exact calculation.

Example:

Find approx value of √81.9 × 3.2

√81.9 ≈ 9

9 × 3.2 = 28.8

So, approx = 29

9. Practice Questions

1. Simplify: 12 ÷ (3 × 2) + 4 – 1 = ?

2. Simplify: [25 – {6 + (8 ÷ 4)}] = ?

3. Simplify: (3² + 4²) ÷ 5 = ?

4. Simplify: (15% of 200) + (25% of 80) = ?

5. Simplify: 3 + 6 × (5 + 4) ÷ 3 – 7 = ?

10. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding before multiplying

Ignoring brackets

Misplacing decimal points

Forgetting to convert % into fractions

11. Key Takeaways

BODMAS defines the correct order of operations

Always solve brackets first, then powers, then ÷, ×, +, −

Understand fractions, decimals, and percentages well

Practice is the key to speed and accuracy in SSC MTS
