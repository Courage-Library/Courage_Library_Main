LCM & HCF

Essential Concepts for Delhi Police & SSC Exams

1. Introduction

In arithmetic and number system topics, LCM (Least Common Multiple) and HCF (Highest Common Factor) are two of the most important concepts. They are used to solve problems related to time, work, speed, and fractions in SSC and other government exams.

Let's understand them one by one with easy examples.

2. What is HCF (Highest Common Factor)?

Definition:

The HCF (also called GCD - Greatest Common Divisor) of two or more numbers is the largest number that divides all of them exactly (without leaving any remainder).

Example:

Find the HCF of 12 and 16.

Factors of 12

1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

Factors of 16

1, 2, 4, 8, 16

Common factors

1, 2, 4

HCF = 4

3. What is LCM (Least Common Multiple)?

Definition:

The LCM of two or more numbers is the smallest number that is exactly divisible by all of them.

Example:

Find the LCM of 12 and 16.

Multiples of 12

12, 24, 36, 48, 60, 72...

Multiples of 16

16, 32, 48, 64...

LCM = 48

4. Relationship Between LCM and HCF

For any two numbers a and b: LCM × HCF = a × b

Example:

For 12 and 16:

HCF = 4, LCM = 48

LCM × HCF = 48 × 4 = 192

Product of numbers = 12 × 16 = 192

Verified

5. Methods to Find HCF and LCM

(a) Prime Factorization Method

Write each number as a product of prime factors.

For HCF, take the lowest powers of common prime factors.

For LCM, take the highest powers of all prime factors.

Example:

Find HCF and LCM of 18 and 24.

18 = 2 × 3²

24 = 2³ × 3¹

HCF = 2¹ × 3¹ = 6

LCM = 2³ × 3² = 72

(b) Division Method (For HCF)

Divide the larger number by the smaller number.

Then divide the divisor by the remainder.

Repeat until the remainder is 0.

The last divisor is the HCF.

Example:

Find HCF of 36 and 60.

60 ÷ 36 = remainder 24

36 ÷ 24 = remainder 12

24 ÷ 12 = remainder 0

So, HCF = 12

Then, using relation:

LCM = (a × b) / HCF = (36 × 60) / 12 = 180

6. LCM and HCF of Fractions

HCF of Fractions

HCF of Numerators / LCM of Denominators

LCM of Fractions

LCM of Numerators / HCF of Denominators

Example:

Find HCF and LCM of 2/3, 4/5, and 6/7.

HCF Calculation:

HCF of numerators (2, 4, 6) = 2

LCM of denominators (3, 5, 7) = 105

HCF = 2 / 105

LCM Calculation:

LCM of numerators (2, 4, 6) = 12

HCF of denominators (3, 5, 7) = 1

LCM = 12 / 1 = 12

7. LCM and HCF of Decimals

Convert decimals into whole numbers by removing the decimal point.

Find the LCM or HCF as usual.

Put the decimal point back according to the smallest decimal place.

Example:

Find LCM of 0.6, 1.2, and 1.8

Convert to whole numbers (multiply by 10): 6, 12, 18

LCM of 6, 12, 18 = 36

Now divide by 10 → 3.6

8. Important Shortcuts

Case Shortcut Formula
Two numbers given LCM × HCF = Product of numbers
Three numbers Product of numbers = HCF × LCM × (Product of unique common factors)
Fractions As given above
Decimals Remove decimal → Find LCM/HCF → Reinsert decimal

9. Practice Questions (for SSC MTS)

1. Find LCM and HCF of 15, 20, and 30.

2. The HCF and LCM of two numbers are 8 and 120 respectively. If one number is 24, find the other.

3. Find the smallest number which when divided by 12, 18, 24 leaves a remainder of 9 each time.

4. Find the HCF and LCM of 1.5, 2.5, and 3.5.

5. Find the LCM of ¾, ⅖, and ⅗.

10. Key Takeaways

HCF = Greatest number that divides all

LCM = Smallest number divisible by all

Formula: LCM × HCF = Product of the numbers

Useful for problems on time, work, and ratio questions in SSC MTS, CHSL, and CGL exams
