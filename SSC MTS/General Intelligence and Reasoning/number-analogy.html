Number Analogy

SSC MTS Exam Section

Concept

Number Analogy means identifying a mathematical relationship between two given numbers and applying the same logic on the next pair.

Example:

8 : 64 :: 9 : ?

→ 8² = 64

→ 9² = 81

Answer = 81

Exam Tip: Always check square/cube patterns first as they are most common in SSC exams.

Major Patterns used in SSC

Type Examples
Addition / Subtraction 10 : 15 (+5)
Multiplication 6 : 48 (×8)
Square / Cube 7 : 49 (square) / 3 : 27 (cube)
Prime Numbers 5 : 11 pattern prime based
Factor / Multiple based 12 : 6 (half)
Mixed logic (square + addition / × then + etc)

Quick Identification Tricks

Step 1

Check square / cube first (most common)

Step 2

If small numbers → most likely basic arithmetic

Step 3

If random numbers → look for prime / divisibility pattern

Step 4

Last step → try mixed pattern (× then + something)

Solved Examples (Short Answer)

5 : 25 :: 6 : ?

→ square → 36

4 : 8 :: 7 : ?

→ ×2 → 14

3 : 27 :: 2 : ?

→ cube → 8

11 : 13 :: 17 : ?

→ next prime → 19

Quick Recap

Number analogy = logic + apply same relation

Check in this order: Square → Cube → × / ÷ → + / – → Prime → Mixed

Keep calculations simple and fast

Most common: Square patterns in SSC exams
