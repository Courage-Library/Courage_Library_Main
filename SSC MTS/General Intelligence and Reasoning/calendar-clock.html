Calender & Clock

Odd Days / Time Gain-Loss - SSC MTS Exam Section

Concept

This chapter checks calculation logic based on dates, days, leap year concept, and clock time gain/loss. No need of deep calculations… basic short formula based questions asked in MTS.

Exam Tip: Focus on basic formulas for odd days and clock gain/loss. These are quick-scoring questions in SSC MTS.

Important Points – Calendar

Concept Key Idea
Normal year 365 days → 1 odd day
Leap year 366 days → 2 odd days
Leap Year rule divisible by 4 (except centuries → must be divisible by 400)

Odd Days Formula:

Total days % 7 = Odd Days

Example: If odd days = 3 → Day shifts 3 days ahead.

Important Points – Clock (Time Gain / Loss)

Concept Key Formula
Time gained every day total gain per day / actual time to get 1 hour difference
Time lost everyday total loss per day / actual time to get 1 hour difference

Example Concept:

If a watch gains 5 mins/day → To gain 60 mins → 60/5 = 12 days.

Solved Examples

Calendar Example 1:

How many odd days in 200 years?

200 = (100*2) odd days → 200 years = 5 odd days

Calendar Example 2:

If today is Wednesday, after 10 days it will be?

10 % 7 = 3 → Wednesday + 3 = Saturday

Clock Example:

A watch loses 4 mins per day. In how many days will it lose 1 hour?

= 60 / 4 = 15 days

Practice Questions

1

Leap year has how many odd days?

Options: a) 1 b) 2 c) 3 d) 0

Remember: Leap year = 366 days

View Answer

b) 2

Leap year has 366 days = 2 odd days

2

If today is Thursday, what day will it be after 16 days?

Options: a) Friday b) Sunday c) Monday d) Saturday

Use: Total days % 7 = Odd days

View Answer

d) Saturday

16 % 7 = 2 → Thursday + 2 = Saturday

3

A clock gains 3 mins per day. How many days to gain 1 hour?

Options: a) 15 days b) 20 days c) 18 days d) 12 days

Use formula: 60 / gain per day

View Answer

b) 20 days

60 / 3 = 20 days

4

A normal year has how many odd days?

Options: a) 1 b) 2 c) 3 d) 0

Remember: Normal year = 365 days

View Answer

a) 1

Normal year has 365 days = 1 odd day

5

A watch loses 10 mins per day. In how many days will it be 1 hour slow?

Options: a) 4 b) 5 c) 6 d) 7

Use formula: 60 / loss per day

View Answer

c) 6

60 / 10 = 6 days

Quick Recap

Leap year = divisible by 4 (century must be divisible by 400)

Normal year = 1 odd day | Leap year = 2 odd days

Odd days → day shifting logic

Clock gain/loss → 60 mins / (gain-loss per day)

Previous year Questions

1

If 22 Feb is Tuesday, what day will be 1 March?

Options: a) Monday b) Wednesday c) Thursday d) Friday

Year: 2020 | Shift: 1

View Answer

b) Wednesday

22 Feb (Tue) → 1 March = 7 days later → Tuesday + 7 days = Wednesday

2

A clock gains 2 minutes per day. In how many days will it gain 30 minutes?

Options: a) 10 days b) 15 days c) 20 days d) 30 days

Year: 2021 | Shift: 2

View Answer

b) 15 days

30 / 2 = 15 days

3

Which year is a leap year?

Options: a) 1900 b) 2000 c) 2100 d) 2200

Year: 2022 | Shift: 1

View Answer

b) 2000

Century years must be divisible by 400. 2000 ÷ 400 = 5 (exact)
