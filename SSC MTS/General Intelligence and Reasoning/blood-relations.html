Blood Relations

Family Tree & Puzzle Based - SSC MTS Exam Section

Concept

Blood relations questions test your ability to read relationships (father, mother, son, daughter, aunt, uncle, cousin, etc.) and apply those links in trees or short puzzles.

Two Common Formats in SSC MTS:

Direct family-tree style

(given a diagram or statements)

Puzzle-based sentences

(statements like "A is B's father's sister's son")

Exam Tip: Always build from the person mentioned (usually the first person) and move step-by-step. Translate words into a simple family map.

Common Relation Terms & Quick Symbols

F = Father

M = Mother

S = Son

D = Daughter

B = Brother

Sis = Sister

H = Husband

W = Wife

g-f = Grandfather

g-m = Grandmother

un = Uncle

a = Aunt

cous = Cousin

P = Parent

C = Child

Fast Rules / Formulas (Useful Shortcuts)

Father's brother = Paternal uncle

Mother's brother = Maternal uncle

Son of my uncle = Cousin

If X is father of Y and Z is son of Y → X is grandfather of Z

Father's sister = Aunt

Mother's sister = Aunt

Daughter of my aunt = Cousin

If A is B's mother's brother → A is B's maternal uncle

Solved Examples (Short Explanation)

Statement: "A is the mother of B. B is the father of C." Who is A to C?

→ A → mother of B; B → father of C → A is grandmother of C

Answer: Grandmother

"P is Q's brother. Q is R's father." Who is P to R?

→ P is brother of R's father → Uncle

Answer: Uncle

"X is the daughter of Y's father." Who is X to Y?

→ Y's father's daughter could be Y herself or Y's sister. Usually implies another female → Sister

Answer: Sister

Practice Questions

1

A is the mother of B. B is the brother of C. How is C related to A?

Options: A) Son B) Daughter C) Grandson D) Child

B is A's child; C is B's sibling → C is also A's child

View Answer

D) Child

2

P is Q's father. R is P's sister. How is R related to Q?

Options: A) Aunt B) Mother C) Sister D) Cousin

P is Q's father; P's sister = Q's aunt

View Answer

A) Aunt

3

A is B's brother. C is B's son. How is C related to A?

Options: A) Nephew B) Son C) Cousin D) Uncle

C is son of A's brother → C is Nephew

View Answer

A) Nephew

4

M is the mother of N. N is the sister of O. O is the father of P. How is M related to P?

Options: A) Grandmother B) Mother C) Aunt D) Sister

O is father of P; O is child of M → M is grandmother

View Answer

A) Grandmother

5

S is the brother of T. T is the wife of U. How is S related to U?

Options: A) Brother-in-law B) Son C) Uncle D) Cousin

S is brother of wife of U → S is Brother-in-law

View Answer

A) Brother-in-law

Quick Recap

Parse the sentence left to right; map relations stepwise

Use symbols (F, M, S, D) and draw a tiny tree when unsure

For "mother's brother" → maternal uncle; "father's sister" → aunt

Children of siblings = cousins
