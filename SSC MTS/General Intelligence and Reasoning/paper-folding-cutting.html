Paper Folding & Cutting

Symmetry Patterns - SSC MTS Exam Section

Concept

A paper is folded and then cuts/holes are made on it. You have to mentally unfold the paper and identify how the cuts/holes will appear when opened fully. Logic depends on symmetry — fold multiplies the cut pattern.

Key Point:

Wherever you cut before unfolding, duplicates symmetrically when unfolded.

Exam Tip: Always count the number of folds first - each fold doubles the pattern when unfolded.

What to Observe

Condition What happens after unfold
Single fold pattern doubles once
Double fold pattern doubles twice (×4)
Fold both sides symmetric in both axes
Cut on fold line pattern appears on that same exact axis when opened

Rules to Solve Faster

Step 1

First identify how many folds

Step 2

Each fold multiplies pattern count

Step 3

Focus on location of the cut relative to fold line (center / side / corner)

Example:

If paper folded into 2 and single hole cut → when opened = 2 holes

Solved Examples (Short)

Paper folded into half (vertical). A hole punched near top right (after fold).

When opened → appears top right + top left both sides

Paper folded twice (vertical + horizontal). One cut near folded corner.

After opening → 4 cuts forming square symmetry

Practice Questions

1

Paper folded once → hole cut → how many holes after opening?

Options: a) 1 b) 2 c) 3 d) 4

Single fold doubles the pattern

View Answer

b) 2

Single fold = 1 hole × 2 = 2 holes

2

Paper folded twice → 1 hole → after opening?

Options: a) 2 b) 3 c) 4 d) 1

Double fold multiplies pattern by 4

View Answer

c) 4

Double fold = 1 hole × 2 × 2 = 4 holes

3

If a paper is folded vertically and hole is cut at bottom right after fold, when opened where will the second hole appear?

Options: a) bottom left b) top right c) top left d) centre

Vertical fold creates left-right symmetry

View Answer

a) bottom left

Vertical fold: bottom right → bottom left (mirror across vertical axis)

4

If folding is double side, what happens to cuts?

Options: a) double b) multiply in 4 c) no change d) disappear

Double fold means two folds

View Answer

b) multiply in 4

Double fold = 2 folds = pattern × 2 × 2 = ×4

5

More folds = ?

Options: a) less cuts b) same cuts always c) more duplication d) cannot say

Each fold increases pattern multiplication

View Answer

c) more duplication

More folds = more symmetrical duplication of pattern

Quick Recap

Folding multiplies cut pattern

Single fold = 2 copies, double fold = 4 copies

Identify fold direction first

Symmetry is the base logic
