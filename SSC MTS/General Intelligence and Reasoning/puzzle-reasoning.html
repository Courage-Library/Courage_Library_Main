Puzzle Reasoning

Seating & Logic - SSC MTS Exam Section

Concept

Puzzle reasoning tests your ability to place people/objects according to given conditions (who sits where, who owns what, sequence orders). Seating arrangements are the most common format — linear or circular — plus small logic puzzles (conditions, matching, yes/no).

Key Strategy:

Clear, stepwise mapping of statements is the key to solving puzzles quickly.

Exam Tip: Always draw diagrams for seating arrangements. They help visualize positions and avoid confusion.

Common Seating Types & Patterns

Linear (single row) — positions from left/right; notice facing direction

Circular — positions around a circle; use clockwise/anticlockwise

Two-row — front/back relationships

Rectangular / square — corner vs side positions matter

Mixed attributes puzzles — seating + colors + pets + food etc

Fast Solving Rules (Strategy)

Step 1

Read all statements once. Mark definite positions first

Step 2

Fill in relative positions (A is left of B, between X and Y, etc.)

Step 3

Use diagram: draw row or circle and label possible slots

Step 4

Use elimination for multi-attribute puzzles (grid/box method)

Step 5

For circular: fix one person to avoid rotation ambiguity

Step 6

Track facing direction: if facing you, left/right swap

Solved Examples (Short Reasons)

Linear, facing north: A sits left of B, C sits right of B. Order left→right?

If facing north left/right are natural → A B C

Circular, 4 persons facing centre: P to right of Q, R opposite P.

Fix Q at top, then P at Q's right → P on right, R opposite P → R on left, last at bottom

Two-row: 3 in front row facing north, 3 behind facing south. X sits behind Y opposite Z.

Translate to diagram: place Y front, X behind same column, Z opposite X → deduce positions

Practice Questions

1

Five friends A, B, C, D, E sit in a row facing north. B is immediate left of C. D sits at extreme right. A sits right of B. Who sits at extreme left?

Options: A) A B) B C) E D) C

Place D at right, B left of C, A right of B → deduce order

View Answer

D) C

Order: C B A E D → extreme left = C

2

Seven students stand in a line. T is 3rd from left. U is 2nd from right. How many between T and U if total 7?

Options: A) 3 B) 4 C) 2 D) 5

Calculate positions and count people between

View Answer

C) 2

T position=3, U position=6 → between = 2 (positions 4 & 5)

3

Row of 8 facing north: M is 4th from left. N is 3rd from right. How many between M and N?

Options: A) 0 B) 1 C) 2 D) 3

Calculate positions using left/right formulas

View Answer

B) 1

Positions: M=4, N=6 (since 8-3+1=6) → between = 1

4

In a row facing north: P sits to left of Q, R sits between Q and S, T sits at extreme left. Who is immediate left of Q?

Options: A) P B) R C) S D) T

Use T extreme left, arrange to satisfy conditions

View Answer

A) P

Order: T P Q R S → immediate left of Q = P

5

Linear puzzle with attributes: Four students — A,B,C,D — each read different book (Math, Eng, Sci, Hist). Given A read Science, B not Math, C read History. Who read English?

Options: A) A B) B C) C D) D

Use elimination method for attributes

View Answer

D) D

A=Science, C=History, B=English or Math but B≠Math → B=English, D=Math

Quick Recap

Draw diagram immediately (row or circle)

Fix one person in circular problems to remove rotation ambiguity

Mark facing direction — it changes left/right orientation

Place definite positions first, then fill relative ones

Options: A) P B) Q C) R D) T

B) Q

Order: Q R P T S → extreme left = Q

2

Eight people sit around a table facing centre. A is opposite E. B is left of A. C is right of E. Who is opposite B?

Options: A) C B) D C) E D) Cannot say

Year: 2021 | Shift: 2

View Answer

A) C

Fix positions: A opposite E, B left of A, C right of E → C opposite B

3

Six friends stand in a circle. M is 2nd to the left of N. O is between N and P. Who is 3rd to right of O?

Options: A) M B) N C) P D) Cannot say

Year: 2022 | Shift: 1

View Answer

A) M

Arrange: M 2nd left of N, O between N and P → 3rd right of O = M
